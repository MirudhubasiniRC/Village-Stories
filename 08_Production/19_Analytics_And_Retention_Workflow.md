# Analytics And Retention Workflow

## Purpose

This manual turns YouTube Analytics into a production feedback loop for Village
Stories. Retention data diagnoses clarity, pacing, packaging, and audio — it does
not authorize distress packaging, brainrot cutting, or false hooks.

## Guiding Principle

**Measure honestly. Change one variable. Keep the series identity.**

If retention is low, first ask whether viewers understood the opening, heard the
subject, and received the promise made by the title and thumbnail. Faster cuts
are only one possible remedy — and never the default.

---

## Pre-Production Viral Reference Review

Every new story, including a one-line idea supplied by the producer, must be
reviewed before production using:

1. the channel's latest relevant analytics;
2. current public winners in the same niche;
3. the standing refinement gate in
   `07_Episode_System/16_Growth_Sprint_Mode.md`.

Record:

- reference channel/video and date checked;
- public views, runtime, title promise, and visible story structure;
- repeated patterns across multiple winners;
- important counterexamples or weak performers;
- which structural lesson applies to the proposed story;
- what will remain distinctly Village Stories.

Public performance data cannot reveal private retention, swipe, traffic-source,
or subscriber-attribution data. Therefore write **“supported pattern”**, not
“proven cause,” unless Village Stories' own analytics verifies the mechanism.

The required output is:

```text
Supplied story
→ benchmark diagnosis
→ missing retention/emotion/ASMR elements
→ refined story spine
→ hook options + sound anchors
→ runtime cohort
→ canon/originality check
```

Do not proceed to prompts until this output exists, unless the producer
explicitly locks the story against refinement.

## Monetization Targets

Official YouTube eligibility routes:

| Access | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Expanded YPP (eligible regions) | 500 subscribers + 3 public uploads in 90 days + either 3M valid public Shorts views in 90 days or 3,000 public watch hours in 12 months |
| Full ad revenue | 1,000 subscribers + either 10M valid public Shorts views in 90 days or 4,000 public watch hours in 12 months |

Shorts-feed watch time does not count toward the 4,000-hour route.

**Policy gate:** YouTube may deny monetization to channels that appear
mass-produced, generic, repetitive, or templated. Growth experiments must remain
original and meaningfully varied. See `07_Episode_System/16_Growth_Sprint_Mode.md`.

## Metrics To Capture (every published video)

Capture within 48–72 hours, then again at day 7 and day 28.

| Metric | Why it matters | Village Stories target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CTR (impressions → views) | Packaging appeal | Improve when packaging is weak |
| Intro retention (first 30s) | Hook clarity | Above channel typical for format |
| Average view duration / % viewed | Overall hold | Rising trend over last 10 videos |
| Retention dips | Confusion, repetition, weak audio, broken promise | Diagnose and fix next episode |
| Retention spikes / rewatches | What people love | Expand, do not bury later |
| Shorts viewed vs swiped away | First-second Short hook | Improve first frame + first sound |
| Traffic source | Discovery vs returning | Separate Shorts feed from Browse/Search |

Official YouTube note: a strong intro usually means the first 30 seconds matched
thumbnail/title expectation and kept interest. Weak intros often mean packaging
misalignment or a delayed premise — not “not enough drama.”

## Diagnosis Rules

| Observation | Likely cause | First fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High CTR · steep early drop | Packaging overpromises or opening delays | Rewrite first 15s · align title/thumbnail |
| Low CTR · decent retention | Packaging weak | New thumbnail/title tests |
| Dip on establishing / walking | Padding | Compress geography · cut dead holds |
| Dip on dialogue | Unclear or soft voice | Re-mix voice forward |
| Dip on “pretty” wide | No new information | Replace with active subject |
| Spike on pour / stir / splash | Strong ASMR | Feature earlier and louder |
| Spike on emotion | Relationship truth | Do not cut before reaction lands |
| Shorts swipe in first 1–2s | No immediate subject/action | Open on action frame 1 |

## Retention-First Production Targets

### Shorts (9:16)

- Hook in **0–1 second** with active subject
- New information every **1–3 seconds**
- One central action only
- Primary ASMR audible immediately
- Complete resolution before end card

### Long-form (16:9)

- Active promise by **0–12 seconds**
- No calendar-first open
- Meaningful change every **4–8 seconds** of ordinary coverage
- ASMR process can hold longer **only while sound/action still changes**
- Compress walks, setup, and repeated beats

### Not allowed as “retention tactics”

- Unexplained child distress as scroll-stop
- Fake cliffhangers / Part-2 bait
- Constant micro-cuts with no readable cause
- Music stingers replacing story
- Loud ambience covering the hero action

## Experiment Protocol

1. Write one hypothesis (example: “Subject-first pour ASMR in first 3s raises intro retention”).
2. Change **one** primary variable (opening, audio mix, thumbnail, or cut density).
3. Publish and wait for 48–72h data.
4. Record result in `11_Templates/Analytics_Review_Template.md`.
5. Promote winners into Lessons Learned; discard losers without rewriting canon identity.

Minimum evidence before adopting a permanent rule: **3 videos** of the same format showing the same direction.
