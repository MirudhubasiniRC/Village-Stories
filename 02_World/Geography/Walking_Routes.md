# Walking Routes

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent walking routes used throughout the Village Stories universe.

These routes connect every major location within the village and ensure that characters move naturally through the world.

Walking paths should never feel random or created for the convenience of a story.

Every journey should reinforce the audience's mental map of the village.

---

# Philosophy

People usually take the path they have walked for years.

Children develop shortcuts.

Farmers follow familiar routes to the fields.

Neighbours naturally pass one another on shared paths.

Walking routes should feel lived-in rather than designed.

---

# Primary Village Route

This is the main public route used by villagers and visitors.

```text
Village Entrance
        │
Sacred Banyan Tree
        │
Main Village Path
        │
Village Square
        │
Family Entrance Path
        │
Family House Compound
```

This is the busiest pedestrian route in the village.

---

# River Route

A short stone path connects the village to the river.

```text
Sacred Banyan Tree
        │
Stone Path
        │
River Ghat
        │
Stone Bridge
```

Typical users:

* Women washing clothes
* Children swimming
* Fishermen
* Villagers bathing
* Families collecting water
* Festival processions

---

# Children's Routes

Children move more freely than adults.

Common routes include:

* Family House ↔ River Ghat
* Family House ↔ Banyan Tree
* Family House ↔ Village Square
* Family House ↔ Primary School
* Primary School ↔ Playground
* Village Square ↔ Small Grocery Store (errands with adults)
* River Ghat ↔ Stone Bridge
* Pond ↔ Paddy Fields

Children may stop to explore, play, climb trees, or interact with animals along these routes.

---

```text

        │
Village Square
        │
West Path
        │
Small Grocery Store
```

Typical users:

* Mother
* Neighbours

* Younger Daughter accompanying Mother
* Father for practical purchases

---

# Festival Ground Route

A celebration and gathering route:

```text
Family House / Village Square
        │
West / South-West Path
        │
Festival Ground
```

Typical users:

* Whole families during festivals
* Neighbours and elders
* Vendors on festival days
* Children during celebrations
* Procession groups arriving from shrine/village-centre directions

On ordinary days the same path may be used as a quiet shortcut across open land.

# Seasonal Changes

Walking routes remain the same throughout the year.

However, conditions change.

### Summer

* Dry paths
* Dust
* Firm ground

### Monsoon

* Wet earth
* Small puddles
* Slippery stones
* Mud beside paths

### Post-Monsoon

* Lush vegetation
* Firm but slightly damp paths
* Harvest activity

The path never changes location—only its condition.

# Storytelling Opportunities

Walking routes naturally create opportunities for encounters.

Examples include:

* Meeting a neighbour.
* Seeing the postman arrive.
* Helping someone carry goods.
* Greeting elders.
* Spotting wildlife.
* Discovering fallen mangoes.
* Watching children play.

These moments should emerge naturally rather than feel scripted.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Permanent route locations.
* Logical travel order.
* Consistent entrances to buildings.
* Natural movement between locations.
* Established connections between public and private spaces.

Never:

* Teleport characters.
* Invent shortcuts through buildings.
* Change route locations.
* Skip important connecting areas without a narrative reason.

---

# Canon Authority

All character movement throughout the Village Stories universe should follow the walking routes defined in this document.

New paths may only be introduced if they are added permanently to the world and documented here.

---

# Guiding Principle

Every walk should strengthen the audience's understanding of the village.

By repeatedly following the same natural paths, viewers gradually learn the geography of the world and begin to feel that they, too, know their way around Village Stories.
