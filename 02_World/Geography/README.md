# Geography README

## Purpose

The Geography folder defines the permanent physical layout of the Village Stories universe.

It establishes where every major landmark, road, water body, and natural feature exists in relation to one another.

This folder is the geographical source of truth for the entire project.

Every story, image, animation, and camera movement should respect the geography established here.

---

# Geography Philosophy

The village is a real place with a fixed layout.

People should be able to mentally navigate through it.

Returning viewers should gradually recognize locations, understand walking routes, and become familiar with the village just as they would with a real hometown.

The geography should never feel random or redesigned between episodes.

---

# Folder Structure

## Village_Map.md

Defines the complete layout of the village.

Shows how every permanent location connects to the others.

This is the master geographical reference.

---

## Orientation.md

Defines the permanent compass directions.

Includes:

* North
* South
* East
* West
* Sunrise direction
* Sunset direction

This document is used to maintain consistent navigation and support lighting continuity.

---

## Walking_Routes.md

Defines how people naturally move through the village.

Includes:

* Main walking paths.
* Connections between locations.
* Logical travel routes.
* Areas that are rarely used.
* Everyday movement patterns.

Characters should always travel using these established routes.

---

## River_System.md

Defines every permanent water feature.

Includes:

* River.
* River Ghat.
* Village Pond.
* Irrigation Canal.
* Water flow.
* Seasonal water behaviour.

Water should always behave consistently throughout the series.

---

## Landmarks.md

Defines important permanent landmarks.

Examples include:

* Sacred Banyan Tree.
* Vinayagar Shrine.
* River Ghat.
* Stone bridge.
* Large gathering trees.
* Historic features.

Landmarks help viewers orient themselves within the village.

---

# Geographic Continuity

Permanent locations should never move.

Maintain consistent:

* Relative distances.
* Walking directions.
* Land elevations.
* Water locations.
* Roads and paths.
* Neighbourhood placement.

The audience should always understand where they are.

---

# Natural Expansion

As the universe grows, new locations may be introduced.

Examples include:

* A blacksmith's workshop.
* Village school.
* Weekly market area.
* Bus stop.
* Rice mill.
* Potter's house.

Every new location must:

* Fit naturally within the existing geography.
* Connect logically to established routes.
* Avoid contradicting previous episodes.

The village should grow organically.

---

# Relationship With Other Folders

This folder defines **where places exist**.

It does not describe:

* Buildings in detail.
* Characters.
* Objects.
* Stories.
* Festivals.
* Occupations.

Those topics belong in their respective folders.

---

# Canon Authority

If any story, prompt, image, or animation conflicts with the geography defined here, the geography should be considered correct unless an intentional world update has been made.

The village layout should remain stable throughout the lifetime of the project.

---

# Guiding Principle

A believable world begins with believable geography.

Every path, river, field, house, and landmark should feel as though it has existed for generations and will continue to exist long after each story has ended.
