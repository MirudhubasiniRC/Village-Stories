# Orientation

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent geographical orientation of the Village Stories universe.

It establishes the official compass directions, the orientation of every major landmark, and how locations relate to one another.

Unlike the Lighting Style document, this file does **not** describe lighting behaviour or colour. Its purpose is purely geographical.

Every story, storyboard, image, animation, and map must respect the orientation defined here.

---

# Permanent Compass

```text
                     NORTH
                       ↑
WEST ◄─────────────────┼─────────────────► EAST
                       │
                       ↓
                     SOUTH
```

This compass never changes.

All maps, locations, and walking directions are based on this orientation.

---

# Village Orientation

The village naturally expands from north to south.

The northern side welcomes visitors.

The southern side gradually becomes agricultural land.

The eastern side is defined by the river.

The western side contains future expansion areas and open village land.

---

# North

The northern area is the public gateway into the village.

Permanent locations include:

* Village Entrance
* Sacred Banyan Tree
* Vinayagar Shrine

This is where:

* Visitors arrive.
* Villagers welcome guests.
* Community announcements occur.
* Travellers begin entering the village.

---

# East

The eastern side is the village's water edge.

Permanent locations include:

* River
* River Ghat
* Stone Steps
* River Bank

The eastern side is closely associated with:

* Bathing
* Washing clothes
* Fetching water
* Fishing
* Morning gatherings
* Children's play

---

# South

The southern side gradually becomes quieter.

Permanent locations include:

* Coconut Grove
* Paddy Fields
* Village Pond
* Irrigation Canal
* Open Farmland

Agriculture dominates this region.

---

# West

The western side contains the Small Grocery Store and open land reserved for future village trades growth.

Established:

* Small Grocery Store
* Barber Under the Big Tree
* Blacksmith Forge
* Carpenter Workshop
* Potter Clay Yard

Potential future locations nearby include:

* Weekly Market

The western edge remains less densely developed than the village centre, aside from the grocery, open-air barber stand, and traditional forge.

---

# North-East

The north-east corridor near the Village Entrance approach contains:

* Tea Stall

Still reserved nearby:

* Bus Stop

---

# East of Village Centre

East of the residential/village-centre routes contains:

* Primary School
* Playground

---

# South-West

The south-west contains the village's open celebration field:

* Festival Ground

Still reserved nearby:

* Temple Fair Area
* Cattle Fair

The Festival Ground remains open land that fills only during celebrations.

---

# Family House Orientation

The Family House has one permanent orientation.

## Front

Faces East.

The Front Garden and Front Thinnai face the sunrise.

Visitors approach from the Main Village Path before turning into the family entrance path.

---

## Rear

Faces West.

The Rear Veranda, Kitchen, Well, and Vegetable Garden occupy the western side of the house.

This side naturally connects household life with farming activities.

---

## Left Side

Faces North.

This side visually connects the family home with the village.

---

## Right Side

Faces South.

This side gradually opens toward the agricultural landscape.

---

# Relationship Between Major Landmarks

The Village Entrance is the northernmost public landmark.

The Sacred Banyan Tree and Vinayagar Shrine stand immediately south of the entrance.

The River Ghat lies directly east of the Banyan Tree, connected by a short stone pathway.

The Main Village Path begins south of the Banyan Tree.

The Village Square forms the social centre of the residential area.

The Family House is located slightly east of the Main Village Path and sits within its own compound.

Behind the Family House are:

* Water Well
* Vegetable Garden
* Cow & Goat Shed
* Coconut Grove
* Paddy Fields
* Village Pond
* Irrigation Canal

These relationships remain permanent.

# Navigation Language

Villagers naturally navigate using familiar places rather than compass directions.

Examples include:

* "Near the Banyan Tree."
* "At the River Ghat."
* "Behind the house."
* "Across the bridge."
* "By the well."
* "Towards the fields."
* "Near the pond."

Dialogue and narration should favour these natural references over north, south, east, and west.

---

# Orientation Rules

Always maintain:

* Fixed compass directions.
* East-facing Family House.
* River east of the Banyan Tree.
* Agricultural land south of the residential area.
* Consistent approach to the Family House.
* Permanent relationships between landmarks.

Never:

* Rotate buildings.
* Mirror the village.
* Reverse the compass.
* Relocate permanent landmarks.
* Change established orientations.

---

# Canon Authority

This document is the official orientation reference for the Village Stories universe.

If any future illustration, story, map, or animation conflicts with this orientation, this document takes precedence unless the world itself is intentionally redesigned.

---

# Guiding Principle

A consistent sense of direction helps transform the village from a setting into a believable place.

As viewers return episode after episode, they should instinctively know where they are, where they are going, and how every familiar landmark connects to the rest of the village.
