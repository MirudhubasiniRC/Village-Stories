# Landmarks

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent landmarks of the Village Stories universe.

Landmarks help both the villagers and the audience understand where they are within the village.

Unlike ordinary locations, landmarks become visual anchors that repeatedly appear throughout the series and gradually build familiarity.

Each landmark listed here has its own detailed document within the **Locations** folder.

---

# What Is A Landmark?

A landmark is a permanent feature that:

* Never changes location.
* Is easily recognizable.
* Helps people navigate.
* Has cultural, historical, practical, or emotional significance.
* Appears naturally across many stories.

Landmarks become part of the identity of the village.

# Using Landmarks In Stories

Characters naturally refer to landmarks instead of compass directions.

Examples include:

* "Meet me at the Banyan Tree."
* "I'll be at the River Ghat."
* "He's crossing the bridge."
* "The children are near the pond."

This reflects authentic village navigation and helps viewers become familiar with the world.

---

# Visual Continuity

Every landmark must maintain its established identity.

Maintain:

* Location.
* Scale.
* Architecture.
* Materials.
* Surroundings.
* Atmosphere.
* Purpose.

Seasonal changes may affect appearance, but the landmark itself should always remain recognizable.

---

# Canon Authority

This document defines the permanent landmarks of the Village Stories universe.

No landmark may be moved, renamed, or significantly redesigned unless the change is intentionally documented as a permanent update to the world.

---

# Guiding Principle

Great stories take place in memorable places.

The landmarks of Village Stories should become as familiar to viewers as the recurring characters themselves.

Over time, audiences should instantly recognize each landmark and associate it with the emotions, memories, and everyday life that unfold there.
