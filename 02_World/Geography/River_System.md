# River System

## Purpose

This document defines every permanent water body within the Village Stories universe and explains how they are connected.

The river system is more than geography—it supports daily life, farming, wildlife, traditions, and community.

Every episode should treat the river system as one continuous, interconnected natural feature.

---

# Philosophy

Water is one of the foundations of village life.

People gather around water.

Children play near water.

Farmers depend on water.

Animals drink from water.

Festivals honour water.

The river system should always feel alive and essential to the village.

---

# Water Network

The Village Stories universe contains three permanent water bodies.

```text
River
   │
River Ghat
   │
(Flows naturally downstream)
   │
Village Pond
   │
Irrigation Canal
```

Each has its own identity and purpose.

---

# The River

## Identity

The river is the largest natural water body in the village.

It flows throughout the year, although its depth and speed change with the seasons.

The river is clean, calm, and suitable for everyday village life.

---

## Purpose

The river serves as:

* Community gathering place.
* Bathing location.
* Clothes washing area.
* Fishing spot.
* Children's recreation area.
* Festival ritual location.
* Source of natural beauty.

---

## Appearance

Maintain:

* Natural flowing freshwater.
* Sandy and rocky riverbed.
* Gentle current.
* Native vegetation along the banks.
* Large shade trees.
* Birds and dragonflies.
* Small fish visible in clear water.

The river should never feel industrial or polluted.

---

# River Ghat

The River Ghat is the village's primary public access to the river.

It is one of the most frequently visited locations in the entire series.

---

## Permanent Features

Maintain:

* Wide stone steps descending into the river.
* Weathered natural stone.
* Moss on lower steps.
* Hand-cut stone edges.
* Stone sitting areas.
* Shade from nearby trees.
* Rope tied to a tree for children to swing into the water (when appropriate).
* Flat stones for washing clothes.
* Gentle slope into deeper water.

---

## Everyday Activities

Naturally include:

* Washing clothes.
* Bathing.
* Children swimming.
* Learning to swim.
* Fishing.
* Washing vegetables.
* Filling brass pots.
* Sitting and talking.
* Relaxing during evenings.
* Watching the river.

Not every activity should occur every time, but the Ghat should rarely feel empty.

---

# Stone Bridge

The bridge connects both sides of the river.

Maintain:

* Traditional stone construction.
* Low height above the water.
* No railings or simple stone edges.
* Weathered appearance.
* Moss in damp areas.

The bridge is primarily used by pedestrians and livestock.

---

# Village Pond

The pond is separate from the river.

It serves the agricultural side of the village.

---

## Purpose

Supports:

* Irrigation.
* Wildlife.
* Livestock.
* Fishing.
* Water storage.

The pond is generally quieter than the River Ghat.

---

## Appearance

Maintain:

* Lotus flowers.
* Water lilies.
* Reeds.
* Frogs.
* Dragonflies.
* Birds.
* Gentle reflections.

The pond should feel peaceful.

---

# Irrigation Canal

The canal carries water toward the agricultural land.

Maintain:

* Slow-moving water.
* Earthen banks.
* Small crossings where needed.
* Grass growing along the edges.
* Seasonal water variation.

The canal is practical rather than social.

---

# Seasonal Behaviour

## Summer

* River level slightly lower.
* Pond level reduced.
* Canal still flowing.
* Exposed rocks along the river.
* Dry grasses near the banks.

---

## Monsoon

* River becomes fuller.
* Stronger current.
* Pond reaches maximum capacity.
* Canal carries abundant water.
* Rich green vegetation.

Flooding should remain gentle unless a story specifically requires otherwise.

---

## Post-Monsoon / Harvest

* Healthy water levels.
* Clear reflections.
* Lush vegetation.
* Stable irrigation.
* Abundant wildlife.

---

# Wildlife

Typical river life includes:

* Small freshwater fish.
* Frogs.
* Dragonflies.
* Kingfishers.
* Egrets.
* Ducks (occasionally).
* Butterflies.
* Water insects.

Wildlife should vary naturally throughout the year.

---

# Audio Identity

The river contributes constantly to the village soundscape.

Possible sounds include:

* Flowing water.
* Gentle splashes.
* Wet footsteps.
* Clothes being washed.
* Birds.
* Children's laughter.
* Fishing activity.
* Wind through riverside trees.

The sound should always feel calm and authentic.

---

# Story Opportunities

The river naturally supports stories involving:

* Family bathing.
* Children learning to swim.
* Lost objects.
* Fishing trips.
* Village conversations.
* Seasonal changes.
* Festival rituals.
* Quiet reflection.
* Animal encounters.

The river should frequently appear throughout the series because it is one of the emotional centres of village life.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Permanent river location.
* River east of the Banyan Tree.
* River Ghat connected by the stone path.
* Stone bridge location.
* Pond south of the agricultural fields.
* Canal connected to the pond.
* Natural water flow.

Never:

* Relocate water bodies.
* Change the direction of water flow.
* Replace the Ghat.
* Introduce modern structures.
* Allow water to disappear without a story-based reason.

---

# Canon Authority

This document defines the official water system of the Village Stories universe.

All future stories, locations, prompts, and animations involving rivers, ponds, canals, or water-related activities must conform to these standards.

---

# Guiding Principle

The river system is the lifeblood of the village.

It nourishes the land, brings people together, supports daily life, and creates countless opportunities for memorable stories.

Viewers should come to recognize the sound, appearance, and atmosphere of the river as one of the defining identities of Village Stories.
