# Blacksmith Forge

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the village Blacksmith's open-sided traditional forge.

It is where farming tools, knives, metal fittings, and practical household items are made or repaired.

---

# Location

The forge is permanently located west of the Village Centre, in the reserved trades area beyond the Small Grocery Store and near the west-side path.

It connects naturally to farmers, the Barber Under the Big Tree, and future carpenter/potter locations without relocating the established village map.

---

# Identity

The forge is a modest open-sided work shelter, not an industrial factory.

Maintain:

* Country tile roof on sturdy timber posts.
* Open work frontage facing the path.
* Packed-earth floor darkened near the hearth.
* Brick-and-clay forge hearth.
* Hand bellows.
* Anvil on solid wooden block.
* Tool rack and iron scrap area.
* Water bucket or cooling trough.
* No modern power tools or industrial machinery.

---

# Daily Life

The Blacksmith heats, shapes, sharpens, and repairs:

* Sickles.
* Hoes.
* Small farming blades.
* Knives.
* Metal fittings.
* Cart-related metal repairs when appropriate.

Farmers arrive with worn tools, wait at a respectful distance from the hot work area, and carry repaired tools home.

---

# Atmosphere and Sounds

* Bellows breathing.
* Fire crackle.
* Hammer on hot metal.
* Anvil ring.
* Water hiss during cooling.
* Low practical conversation.
* Smoke rising through roof gaps.

---

# Seasonal Behaviour

Summer work is hottest and most heat-conscious. Monsoon work emphasizes keeping forge materials dry. Pre-planting and harvest periods increase tool repair demand.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* West-side open-sided tile-roof forge.
* Hand bellows, hearth, anvil, and packed-earth floor.
* Traditional hand-tool work.
* No enclosed industrial workshop or modern machinery.
