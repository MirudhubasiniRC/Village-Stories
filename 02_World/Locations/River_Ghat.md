# River Ghat

## Purpose

The River Ghat is the village's primary connection to the river and one of the most frequently visited locations in the Village Stories universe.

It is a place where daily life naturally unfolds.

Unlike the Sacred Banyan Tree, which represents faith and reflection, the River Ghat represents community, routine, laughter, and the rhythm of village life.

It should feel welcoming from sunrise until evening.

---

# Identity

The River Ghat is not merely a set of steps leading to water.

It is an outdoor living space where generations of villagers have gathered every day.

Children learn to swim here.

Women wash clothes while sharing stories.

Men bathe before beginning work.

Fishermen prepare their nets.

Travellers wash away the dust of the road.

Many friendships begin and continue here.

The River Ghat should always feel alive without ever feeling crowded.

---

# Location

The River Ghat lies immediately east of the Sacred Banyan Tree.

A short stone pathway connects the shrine to the ghat.

A small stone bridge stands slightly downstream.

The River Ghat is the village's primary public access to the river.

# Stone Steps

Maintain:

* Wide hand-cut granite steps.
* Gentle descent into the river.
* Approximately 12–18 broad steps.
* Lower steps naturally darker from constant contact with water.
* Small patches of moss on permanently wet areas.
* Rounded edges from decades of use.
* Safe, gradual slope suitable for children and elders.

The steps should feel handcrafted and timeless.

---

# Riverside Platforms

Along the upper edge of the ghat are several flat stone platforms.

These are used for:

* Washing clothes.
* Drying clothes temporarily.
* Cleaning vegetables.
* Resting.
* Sitting and talking.
* Placing brass water pots.

These platforms should appear naturally worn from generations of daily use.

---

# Trees

Large native trees provide shade throughout the day.

Maintain:

* Banyan influence nearby.
* Neem trees.
* Tamarind tree.
* Wild shrubs.
* Thick grasses.
* Flowering seasonal plants.

Shade is one of the defining characteristics of the River Ghat.

---

# The River

The river should always appear:

* Clean.
* Fresh.
* Calm.
* Gently flowing.
* Transparent enough to reveal stones beneath shallow water.

The current is gentle enough for bathing and children learning to swim.

Only during heavy monsoon should the river appear noticeably stronger.

---

# Everyday Activities

Morning

* Bathing.
* Fetching water.
* Washing clothes.
* Prayer before work.
* Fishermen preparing.

Afternoon

* Children swimming.
* Women chatting while washing clothes.
* Livestock drinking.
* Travellers resting.

Evening

* Villagers relaxing.
* Children splashing.
* Quiet conversations.
* Watching the sunset reflect on the river.

The River Ghat should feel different throughout the day while remaining familiar.

---

# Fishing

Fishing is a regular part of village life.

Typical scenes include:

* Bamboo fishing rods.
* Small woven fish baskets.
* Hand-thrown nets.
* Fresh catch kept in brass or bamboo containers.

Fishing should remain small-scale and traditional.

---

# Washing Clothes

Clothes washing is one of the signature activities of this location.

Maintain:

* Flat washing stones.
* Brass and clay water pots.
* Cotton sarees.
* Cotton veshtis.
* Children's clothing.
* Natural soap where appropriate.
* Clothes spread on rocks or grass to dry temporarily.

This activity should appear naturally throughout the series.

---

# Children

The River Ghat is one of the children's favourite places.

Activities include:

* Learning to swim.
* Floating banana stems.
* Skipping stones.
* Catching tiny fish.
* Building sand structures.
* Collecting smooth pebbles.
* Playing with the family dog.

Adults naturally supervise younger children.

---

# Wildlife

Common wildlife includes:

* Kingfishers.
* Egrets.
* Herons.
* Ducks (occasionally).
* Dragonflies.
* Butterflies.
* Frogs.
* Small freshwater fish.
* River snails.

Wildlife should enrich the atmosphere without dominating the scene.

---

# Seasonal Behaviour

## Summer

* Lower water level.
* Warm stones.
* Clear water.
* Exposed sandbars.
* Children spend more time swimming.

---

## Monsoon

* Fuller river.
* Faster current.
* Darker water.
* Wet stone steps.
* Lush riverside vegetation.

Swimming becomes more cautious.

---

## Post-Monsoon / Harvest

* Healthy water level.
* Bright green surroundings.
* Clear reflections.
* Active wildlife.

This is the most visually vibrant season.

# Camera Notes

Useful compositions include:

* Wide establishing shot of the entire ghat.
* View from the stone steps looking across the river.
* View from the river toward the ghat.
* Low-angle shot from the waterline.
* Framing through overhanging tree branches.
* View toward the Banyan Tree along the stone pathway.
* Medium shots showing daily village activity without crowding.

Foreground elements such as water ripples, leaves, brass pots, or drying clothes should often be used to add depth.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Stone pathway from the Banyan Tree.
* Wide granite steps.
* Washing platforms.
* Calm natural river.
* Stone bridge downstream.
* Native trees.
* Traditional materials.
* Open community atmosphere.

Never:

* Add concrete embankments.
* Install railings.
* Introduce modern structures.
* Pollute the river.
* Remove the stone steps.
* Change the river's location.

---

# Canon Authority

This document is the permanent reference for the River Ghat.

Every future story, storyboard, image prompt, animation, and audio design involving the River Ghat must conform to this document unless a permanent canonical change is intentionally introduced.

# Guiding Principle

The River Ghat is where the village breathes together.

It is a place where work becomes conversation, routine becomes memory, and ordinary moments become the stories that define village life.
