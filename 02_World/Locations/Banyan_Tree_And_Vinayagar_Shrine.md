# Banyan Tree And Vinayagar Shrine

## Purpose

The Sacred Banyan Tree and Vinayagar Shrine form the spiritual heart of the Village Stories universe.

Located near the village entrance, this is the first landmark visitors encounter and one of the most recognizable places in the series.

It is a place of worship, rest, conversation, celebration, and quiet reflection.

Every return to this location should immediately feel familiar.

# Identity

This location is not a temple building.

It is a centuries-old banyan tree that naturally became the village's sacred gathering place.

Beneath its vast canopy sits a humble Vinayagar shrine that has served generations of villagers.

The space belongs equally to faith, community, and everyday life.

It should always feel peaceful, welcoming, and deeply rooted in tradition.

---

# Location

Situated immediately south of the Village Entrance.

A short stone pathway branches east from the shrine and leads directly to the River Ghat.

The Main Village Path continues south toward the neighbourhood.

The Banyan Tree naturally becomes the crossroads between:

* The village entrance.
* The river.
* The residential area.

# The Banyan Tree

The tree is the oldest living feature in the village.

Maintain:

* Massive trunk with natural curves.
* Thick aerial roots hanging from mature branches.
* Wide circular canopy providing abundant shade.
* Dense green leaves with botanical-level detail.
* Weathered bark with natural texture.
* Small patches of moss and lichen.
* Fallen leaves scattered naturally.

The tree should immediately communicate age, permanence, and protection.

---

# Circular Stone Platform (Thinnai)

The tree is surrounded by a permanent raised circular platform.

Maintain:

* Stone and lime construction.
* Approximately knee height.
* Smooth, rounded edges from decades of use.
* Circular opening allowing the trunk to grow naturally.
* Comfortable seating all around.
* Clean but weathered appearance.

This is one of the most common resting places in the village.

---

# Vinayagar Shrine

The shrine sits beneath the eastern side of the Banyan canopy, facing west toward villagers arriving from the Main Village Path.

Maintain:

* **Circular stone platform** (round stage) — see Canon Visual Reference above.
* **Hand-carved stone Vinayagar idol at centre** of platform, at banyan trunk base.
* Modest stone pedestal / offering ledge before the idol.
* **Brass standing oil lamps** on either side on the platform.
* Fresh flowers and garlands whenever appropriate.
* Turmeric and kumkum markings.
* Incense holder and small brass plate for offerings when story requires.
* Optional brass bell near shrine — not mandatory in every frame.

**Primary identity:** open banyan shade + round platform + central stone god.

**Do not default to:** a separate four-pillar tiled canopy box dominating the frame (not the approved visual identity).

The shrine should remain humble and deeply authentic.

It is not a large temple.

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Exact location.
* Massive canopy.
* Circular stone platform.
* Hand-carved Vinayagar idol.
* Shrine orientation.
* Brass lamps.
* Brass bell.
* Stone pathway to the River Ghat.
* Mature aerial roots.
* Natural weathered appearance.
* **Sacred platform boundary:** people pray from the path or foot of steps; **Family Dog and other animals stay on ground below the round platform**, never beside the idol on the stage.

Never:

* Move the tree.
* Reduce its size.
* Replace the shrine.
* Modernize the architecture.
* Add electric lighting.
* Add concrete structures.
* Remove the circular platform.
* Place the Family Dog (or any animal) **on the circular sacred platform** beside the Vinayagar idol.

# Guiding Principle

The Sacred Banyan Tree and Vinayagar Shrine represent the soul of the village.

Long before the current generation was born, people gathered beneath these branches.

Long after every individual story ends, the tree will still stand, quietly watching over the life of the village.
