# Barber Under the Big Tree

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the village Barber's open-air work location within the Village Stories universe.

It is a simple veranda-style barber setup beneath a large shade tree, not a walled modern shop.

---

# Location

The Barber's place is permanently located west of the Village Centre, along the path toward the future trades area and near the Small Grocery Store route.

It occupies the map's reserved barber position without adding a permanent Barber Shop building.

---

# Identity

The location is an open, familiar, early-1990s village service point.

Maintain:

* Large mature shade tree.
* Packed-earth ground.
* Small raised veranda/platform or simple work edge beneath the canopy.
* One practical wooden barber chair or sturdy simple chair.
* Wall mirror secured to a nearby tree-safe frame or modest veranda post.
* Small wooden box for scissors, combs, cloth, and razor equipment.
* Water vessel and clean cloth.
* No salon building, glass frontage, electric styling chairs, or modern signage.

---

# Daily Life

The Barber works here during daylight, especially:

* Before festivals.
* On quieter farming afternoons.
* Before school events.
* When Father, Elder Brother, or other villagers need a haircut.

Customers wait in shade, exchange quiet news, and leave when finished. The location never becomes a loud gossip set-piece.

---

# Atmosphere and Sounds

* Leaves moving overhead.
* Scissors snipping.
* Comb through hair.
* Barber cloth rustling.
* Low conversation.
* Bicycle bell or path footsteps.
* Birds in the tree.

---

# Seasonal Behaviour

Summer:

* Dense shade is valuable.
* Midday work slows.

Monsoon:

* Work pauses or shifts beneath temporary rain cover.
* Ground becomes damp and service resumes when practical.

Festival periods:

* More haircuts.
* Longer but orderly waiting.
* Clean cloth and extra preparation.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* West-side permanent tree/veranda setting.
* One modest open-air barber workspace.
* No enclosed modern salon.
* No permanent commercial construction beyond the simple work setup.
