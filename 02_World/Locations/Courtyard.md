# Courtyard (Mutram)

## Purpose

The Courtyard (Mutram) is the heart of the Family House.

It is the central space that connects every major room while bringing sunlight, fresh air, rain, moonlight, and nature into the home.

Unlike the Front Thinnai, which connects the family to the village, the Courtyard connects the family to one another.

Many of the most intimate and memorable moments of Village Stories should naturally unfold here.

---

# Identity

The Courtyard is not an empty open space.

It is the family's everyday living area.

It changes constantly throughout the day.

Morning sunlight slowly crosses the floor.

Children play while Mother works nearby.

Fresh vegetables dry in one corner.

Rain falls into the open sky during monsoon.

Moonlight quietly fills the house at night.

The Courtyard should always feel alive, peaceful, and deeply lived in.

---

# Location

The Courtyard is located at the exact centre of the Family House.

It connects directly to:

* Front Thinnai
* Bedroom
* Kitchen
* Small Interior Passage
* Puja Corner

Every important movement inside the house naturally passes through this space.

# Architecture

Maintain:

* Traditional open-to-sky mutram.
* Nearly square layout.
* Red oxide flooring.
* Thick surrounding walls.
* Wooden roof around the perimeter.
* Four large wooden support pillars.
* Wide roof overhang around the opening.
* Continuous walking corridor around the open centre.

The open sky is the defining feature of the Courtyard.

---

# Central Rain Drain

At the centre lies a permanent circular granite rain drain.

Maintain:

* Circular carved granite.
* Slightly recessed.
* Decorative drainage grooves.
* Rainwater flowing naturally underground.
* Moss forming only during prolonged wet periods.

The drain should become a recognizable visual feature throughout the series.

---

# Sunlight Behaviour

Morning

* Sunlight enters from the eastern side.
* Long soft shadows.
* Warm golden light.

Afternoon

* Bright overhead illumination.
* Balanced natural light.

Evening

* Warm western glow reaching the opposite side.
* Long shadows stretching across the floor.

The movement of sunlight should visually indicate the passage of time.

---

# Moonlight Behaviour

During clear nights:

* Moonlight enters through the open roof.
* Soft silver illumination.
* Gentle shadows from wooden pillars.
* Quiet, peaceful atmosphere.

The Courtyard should feel magical without becoming unrealistic.

---

# Rain Behaviour

During monsoon:

Rain falls only through the open central roof.

Maintain:

* Water falling naturally.
* Circular splash patterns.
* Ripples around the granite drain.
* Wet red oxide floor near the centre.
* Dry walking corridor beneath the surrounding roof.
* Water flowing smoothly into the underground drain.

Rain scenes here should become a signature visual of the series.

---

# Permanent Objects

The Courtyard permanently includes:

* Four wooden pillars.

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Open-to-sky centre.
* Four wooden pillars.
* Red oxide floor.
* Central granite rain drain.
* Wooden roof surrounding the opening.
* Connections to every major room.
* Traditional architecture.

Never:

* Cover the roof opening.
* Install skylights.
* Add modern furniture.
* Remove the rain drain.
* Change the room connections.
* Replace red oxide flooring.
