# Stone Bridge

## Purpose

The Stone Bridge is the permanent crossing over the village river.

Although modest in size, it is an important transition point connecting the River Ghat with the eastern fields and future expansion areas.

It is also one of the village's most scenic viewpoints.

---

# Identity

The bridge is not a grand structure.

It is a simple village bridge built generations ago by local craftsmen using large granite blocks.

Over time, thousands of footsteps have worn its surface smooth.

It represents connection—between people, places, and daily routines.

---

# Location

The bridge stands a short distance downstream (south) of the River Ghat.

It crosses the river at one of its narrower sections.

The bridge is visible from the River Ghat and partially visible from the Sacred Banyan Tree during certain wide shots.

---

# Overall Layout

```text
               River Flow
                    ↓

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

       Granite Stone Bridge

==============================

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

         East River Bank
```

The bridge is aligned roughly east-west, allowing people to cross the river from one bank to the other.

---

# Structure

Maintain:

* Traditional granite construction.
* Flat walking surface.
* Slight natural arch (very subtle).
* Thick stone foundation.
* Weathered appearance.
* Hand-cut stone blocks.
* No modern concrete.
* No painted surfaces.

The bridge should feel as though it has existed for decades.

---

# Width

Wide enough for:

* Two people walking side by side.
* A bicycle.
* A bullock cart.
* Livestock crossing.

It should never feel oversized.

---

# Bridge Edges

Maintain:

* Low stone parapet walls.
* Rounded edges from years of use.
* Moss growing in damp areas.
* Small wild plants in cracks.

The parapets are functional but low enough to preserve open views of the river.

---

# Surface

The walking surface should include:

* Smooth worn granite.
* Slight colour variation.
* Occasional small patches of moss after rain.
* Fallen leaves.
* Fine dust during summer.

It should always appear naturally aged.

---

# Everyday Activities

The bridge naturally supports:

* Villagers walking.
* Children stopping to watch fish.
* Cyclists crossing.
* Farmers leading cattle.
* People greeting each other.
* Travellers entering or leaving the village.
* Quiet moments overlooking the river.

It is a place of movement, but people often pause here.

---

# Scenic Viewpoint

Standing on the bridge offers views of:

* River Ghat.
* Sacred Banyan Tree.
* River.
* Fishing activity.
* Birds.
* River reflections.
* Sunrise over the water.
* Evening light on the river.

The bridge should occasionally be used as a contemplative location.

---

# Wildlife

Often seen nearby:

* Kingfishers.
* Egrets.
* Dragonflies.
* Butterflies.
* Frogs beneath the bridge.
* Fish visible through clear water.

Wildlife should appear naturally.

---

# Seasonal Behaviour

## Summer

* Dry stone surface.
* Warm granite.
* Lower river level.
* Clear water.

---

## Monsoon

* Damp stones.
* Moss becomes greener.
* Fuller river.
* Stronger current beneath the bridge.

The bridge remains usable throughout the year.

---

## Post-Monsoon / Harvest

* Clean stone.
* Fresh vegetation.
* Bright reflections.
* Pleasant walking conditions.

---

Typical sounds include:

* Flowing water.
* Birdsong.
* Footsteps on stone.
* Bicycle wheels.
* Soft conversations.
* Wind through riverside trees.
* Distant laughter from the River Ghat.

The bridge should always sound peaceful.

# Story Opportunities

The Stone Bridge naturally supports stories involving:

* Travellers arriving.
* Children observing wildlife.
* Quiet conversations.
* Chance encounters.
* Watching the river after rain.
* Carrying harvests.
* Returning home at sunset.

It often serves as a gentle transition between scenes.

---

# Camera Notes

Useful compositions include:

* Wide shot showing the entire bridge across the river.
* Side profile with flowing water beneath.
* View from the bridge toward the River Ghat.
* Low-angle shot from the riverbank.
* Reflection of the bridge in calm water.
* Characters framed against the open sky while crossing.

The bridge is ideal for transition shots between locations.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Granite construction.
* Low stone parapets.
* Weathered appearance.
* East-west crossing.
* Downstream position relative to the River Ghat.
* Traditional craftsmanship.
* Natural surroundings.

Never:

* Replace with concrete.
* Add metal railings.
* Install modern lighting.
* Widen the bridge.
* Relocate it.
* Decorate it permanently.

---

# Canon Authority

This document is the permanent reference for the Stone Bridge.

Every future story, storyboard, image prompt, and animation must preserve its structure, location, and identity unless a permanent canonical change is intentionally introduced.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Stone Bridge is a quiet connector.

It is crossed countless times without ceremony, yet it links people, places, and memories.

Its simplicity is part of its beauty, and its familiarity should make viewers feel that they have crossed it many times before.
