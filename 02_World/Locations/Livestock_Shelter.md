# Livestock Shelter

## Purpose

The Livestock Shelter is the permanent home for the family's cows, calf, goats, chickens, and rooster.

It provides protection from rain, summer heat, and nighttime predators while supporting the family's daily farming and household life.

The shelter is an essential part of the family's self-sufficient lifestyle.

---

# Philosophy

The Livestock Shelter is clean, practical, and well cared for.

It is never industrial or overcrowded.

The animals are considered part of the family and are treated with kindness and respect.

The shelter should always feel peaceful, natural, and lived in.

---

# Location

The Livestock Shelter is located behind the Backyard beside the Vegetable Garden.

It connects naturally with:

- Backyard
- Vegetable Garden
- Compost Area
- Path toward River Ghat

The family can reach the shelter within a few steps from the Kitchen.

# Structure

Maintain:

- Wooden support posts.
- Traditional country tile roof.
- Open sides for natural ventilation.
- Packed earth floor.
- Stone feeding troughs.
- Wooden gates.
- Bamboo partitions where appropriate.
- Natural fencing.

Avoid modern concrete livestock buildings.

# Family Livestock

The family permanently owns:

- 2 Adult Cows
- 1 Calf
- 2 Goats
- 8–12 Native Free-Range Chickens
- 1 Rooster

The Family Dog frequently visits the shelter but does not live here.

---

# Adult Cows

The family owns two gentle native cows.

They provide:

- Fresh milk.
- Organic manure.

Typical behaviour includes:

- Eating hay.
- Chewing cud.
- Resting peacefully.
- Slowly walking around the shelter.
- Responding calmly to Mother and Father.
- Nuzzling the calf.
- Wearing simple traditional neck bells.

The cows should always appear healthy, relaxed, and well cared for.

---

# Calf

The young calf is naturally curious and playful.

Typical behaviour includes:

- Staying close to its mother.
- Exploring the shelter.
- Running playful circles.
- Watching the Family Dog.
- Following Mother or Daughter with curiosity.
- Nudging family members affectionately.

The calf brings youthful energy and warmth to the shelter.

---

# Goats

The family owns two friendly goats.

Typical behaviour includes:

- Browsing leaves.
- Standing on rocks or logs.
- Following each other.
- Gentle bleating.
- Exploring nearby vegetation.
- Watching family members curiously.

They remain calm around the family.

---

# Chickens

The family keeps:

- 8–12 Native Free-Range Chickens
- 1 Rooster

Unlike the larger animals, chickens roam freely during daylight hours.

They naturally explore:

- Backyard
- Vegetable Garden edges
- Kitchen surroundings
- Open yard
- Around the Well Utility Area

Typical behaviour includes:

- Pecking insects.
- Scratching the soil.
- Dust bathing.
- Searching for seeds.
- Following Mother or Father while they work.

At sunset they naturally return to the Chicken Coop.

---

# Rooster

The rooster:

- Crows before sunrise.
- Watches over the flock.
- Patrols the shelter.
- Perches on fences or low branches.
- Alerts the chickens when strangers or animals approach.

Its morning crow is one of the village's signature sounds.

---

# Family Dog

The Family Dog regularly visits the Livestock Shelter.

Typical behaviour includes:

- Resting nearby.
- Watching over the animals.
- Playing gently with the calf.
- Following Father during feeding time.
- Escorting Mother while she carries fodder.
- Walking beside Daughter while she feeds the chickens.
- Alerting the family to unfamiliar visitors or stray animals.

The livestock trust the dog completely.

The dog never chases or frightens the family's animals.

# Bullock Cart

The family's traditional wooden bullock cart is stored beside the Hay Storage under a simple tiled lean-to roof.

It is used for:

- Carrying harvested crops.
- Transporting hay and fodder.
- Bringing firewood.
- Moving farming tools.
- Occasional family travel within the village.

When not in use, the cart remains clean, shaded, and protected from rain.

The wooden wheels, yoke, and frame should show gentle signs of regular use while remaining well maintained.

# Seasonal Animal Behaviour

## Summer

- Animals rest in the shade.
- Water troughs are refilled more frequently.
- Chickens search for cooler soil beneath trees.

## Monsoon

- Animals spend more time inside the shelter.
- Rain falls on the tiled roof.
- The Family Dog often rests beneath the shelter roof.
- Chickens remain close to the coop during heavy rain.

## Post-Monsoon

- Fresh grazing becomes plentiful.
- The calf becomes more playful.
- Goats spend longer browsing nearby vegetation.
- Chickens find many insects after rainfall.
