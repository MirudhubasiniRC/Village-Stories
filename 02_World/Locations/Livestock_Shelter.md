# Livestock Shelter

## Purpose

The Livestock Shelter is the permanent home for the family's cows, calf, goats, chickens, and rooster.

It provides protection from rain, summer heat, and nighttime predators while supporting the family's daily farming and household life.

The shelter is an essential part of the family's self-sufficient lifestyle.

---

# Philosophy

The Livestock Shelter is clean, practical, and well cared for.

It is never industrial or overcrowded.

The animals are considered part of the family and are treated with kindness and respect.

The shelter should always feel peaceful, natural, and lived in.

---

# Location

The Livestock Shelter is located behind the Backyard beside the Vegetable Garden.

It connects naturally with:

- Backyard
- Vegetable Garden
- Compost Area
- Path toward River Ghat

The family can reach the shelter within a few steps from the Kitchen.

Maintain:

### Hens — three recurring types (always include in reference flock)

1. **Brown hen** — solid warm **cinnamon-brown** feathers, soft painterly
   shading, small red comb, round gentle body, head down pecking seeds.
2. **White hen** — **pure white** feathers with subtle painterly **grey-blue**
   shading in plumage, small red comb, peaceful pecking posture.
3. **Speckled hen** — **white, brown, and black mottled** spotted pattern —
   distinctive recurring identifier; gentle busy movement pecking ground.

### Flock scale and style

- Slim native village chicken proportions — NOT broiler chickens, NOT giant
  cartoon birds.
- Same Ghibli-inspired **digital anime** finish on feathers, ground, walls,
  and plants — entire frame painted, not photoreal birds on photo background.
- Healthy, clean, well cared for; gentle village activity only.

# Seasonal Animal Behaviour

## Summer

- Animals rest in the shade.
- Water troughs are refilled more frequently.
- Chickens search for cooler soil beneath trees.

## Monsoon

- Animals spend more time inside the shelter.
- Rain falls on the tiled roof.
- The Family Dog often rests beneath the shelter roof.
- Chickens remain close to the coop during heavy rain.

## Post-Monsoon

- Fresh grazing becomes plentiful.
- The calf becomes more playful.
- Goats spend longer browsing nearby vegetation.
- Chickens find many insects after rainfall.
