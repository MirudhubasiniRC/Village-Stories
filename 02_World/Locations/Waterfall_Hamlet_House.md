# Waterfall Hamlet House

## Purpose

This is Maternal Paati's permanent home in the northern hill hamlet outside the
central village boundary.

Authority: `CANON-2026-001`.

## Geography

- North of the Village Entrance.
- Reached by following the existing northern dirt road, then taking a permanent
  north-west upland fork.
- Approximately 20–25 minutes from the Main Family House in dry weather and
  30–35 minutes during safe rain.
- The house sits **very close beside the waterfall**, approximately **25–40 m
  laterally from the falling water**, on a raised stable rock terrace — never
  beneath, behind, or over the cascade.
- The waterfall is clearly visible from the front veranda, cooking veranda, and
  forecourt without requiring a distant landscape shot.
- The arrival path does not cross the waterfall or fast-moving stream.

The cascade feeds a rocky upland stream that joins the established village river
upstream, beyond the central map boundary.

## Visual Identity

Maintain:

- modest one-storey lime-plastered house;
- weathered red clay-tile roof with deep rain eaves;
- dark wooden pillars and doors;
- covered veranda large enough for family eating;
- raised stone or packed-earth plinth against runoff;
- sheltered clay stove;
- coconut husk and firewood storage alcove;
- brass, clay, wood, and woven household materials;
- wet dark stone, ferns, moss, banana, areca, and native hill greenery;
- waterfall large and unmistakably visible beside the house from exterior and
  veranda compositions;
- a natural rock shoulder and dense rooted vegetation separating the house
  terrace from direct spray and runoff;
- optional shallow rock overhang sheltering one outer veranda edge without
  turning the home into a cave.

The home is cared for and dignified. Heavy rain may dampen an ordinary working
bundle of firewood, but the house must not be portrayed as collapsing, destitute,
or dangerously exposed.

Apply the Main Family House **90s Lived-In Lock** spirit here too: slightly
weathered lime plaster, age-varied terracotta tiles, lived-in veranda props —
never resort, villa, marble, glass sliders, or spotless show-home emptiness.

## Weather Behaviour

During heavy rain:

- roof runoff falls in clear curtains beyond the veranda;
- packed-earth edges darken and form shallow puddles;
- the waterfall becomes louder and fuller;
- fine waterfall mist remains behind the planted boundary and may catch soft
  light around the veranda background;
- the raised house floor remains safe and dry;
- the arrival track is muddy but usable only when an adult judges it safe;
- the fast stream remains off the required route.

## Audio Identity

Primary location sounds:

- layered waterfall roar at a controlled background level;
- rain on clay tiles;
- roof-edge runoff;
- wet footsteps;
- leaf drip;
- clay-stove fire;
- coconut husk crackle;
- cooking vessel and wooden-pick contact.

The waterfall must not mask dialogue or the current tactile ASMR subject. During
cooking close-ups, lower the broad waterfall bed and lead with flame, batter,
sizzle, and utensil contact.

## Camera Geography

Approved:

- parallax reveal from rain-dark foreground leaves to house and waterfall;
- low leading or side-tracking arrival along the safe path;
- veranda framing with cool rain outside and warm stove light inside;
- close, large oblique waterfall background while preserving the lateral safety
  offset;
- macro fire/cooking inserts;
- gentle arc or slow pullback for the family eating payoff.

Avoid:

- house balanced on a cliff;
- characters standing beneath the waterfall;
- children beside a torrent;
- impossible aerial geography;
- repeated empty waterfall beauty shots that delay the story;
- camera crossing the stream without a documented route.

## Story Uses

Natural uses include:

- family visits to Maternal Paati;
- wet-weather cooking;
- carrying a covered food or dry-fuel bundle;
- learning safe household tasks;
- listening to rain and water from the veranda;
- small intergenerational traditions.

## Continuity Rules

Always:

- arrive from the northern upland path;
- keep the waterfall clearly visible immediately beyond the safe planted and
  rock boundary;
- preserve the 25–40 m lateral offset and raised terrace relationship;
- retain the raised plinth, veranda, sheltered stove, and fuel alcove;
- preserve heavy-rain adult accompaniment for the children;
- distinguish this home from Lakshmi Paati's N-04 flower house.

Never:

- teleport from the Main Family House;
- use the village Stone Bridge as the waterfall approach;
- move the waterfall into the central village;
- connect the cascade downstream in a way that reverses established river flow;
- make the route require a dangerous crossing.
