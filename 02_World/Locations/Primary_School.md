# Primary School

## Purpose

The Primary School is the village's permanent childhood learning landmark within the Village Stories universe.

It is where Elder Brother and other village children study, play, form friendships, and carry the rhythm of school days into family life.

Every return to this location should feel instantly recognizable as an early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu government or village primary school—humble, orderly, and full of young life.

---

# Philosophy

The school is not a modern private campus.

It is a modest village school built for practical learning, discipline, play, and community pride.

Children arrive dusty from walking paths.

Teachers are respected.

Bells, slate boards, morning assembly, and open-ground play define the place more than decoration.

The school should always feel sincere, sunlit, and permanently part of the village map.

---

# Location

The Primary School is permanently located **East of the Village Centre**, along a short branch from the Main Village Path / neighbourhood routes.

It connects naturally to:

* Village Neighbourhood
* Village Square route
* Playground immediately beside or behind the school
* Walking paths used by children each school morning
* Routes back toward Family House

Elder Brother attends this school.

Younger Daughter may visit only in age-appropriate story contexts (events, accompanying family, future readiness), remaining clearly younger.

The school must never be relocated.

---

# Identity

Maintain early-1990s authenticity:

* Simple masonry or brick-and-lime classrooms
* Country tile or asbestos-sheet era roofing appropriate to humble village schools of the period
* Painted walls in practical government-school colours (cream, pale green, or soft blue washes)
* Blackboards with chalk
* Wooden desks/benches or floor seating depending on classroom
* Tamil and basic lessons atmosphere
* Hand-painted school name board
* National flag post for assembly
* No smart boards
* No glass corporate architecture
* No modern plastic playscapes as identity

# Architecture

Maintain:

* Single-storey classrooms
* Veranda or corridor running along classroom fronts where traditional
* Wooden doors and simple window openings
* Black-painted blackboard walls or mounted boards
* Chalk dust traces
* Basic wooden teacher table and chair
* Student benches or neat floor mats according to classroom continuity once chosen
* School bell (hand bell or metal hanging bell)
* Notice board with handwritten notices
* Drinking water pot / tap area appropriate to period village school
* Separate simple toilet/outbuilding if shown, kept discreet and period-true

Avoid:

* Multi-floor modern blocks
* AC rooms
* Digital displays
* Branded international school aesthetics

---

# Playground

The Playground is permanently attached to the school compound on the east-side school grounds.

Maintain:

* Open packed-earth play area
* Sparse grass patches
* One or more shade trees at the edge
* Simple play possibilities: running, hopscotch-like markings scratched in dust, ball play, kabaddi-like open games
* No brightly coloured modern plastic play equipment as default identity

Optional humble period elements if needed:

* Simple metal/wood swing frame only if kept worn and village-real
* Never a commercial play park

Children's laughter here should become a recurring audio signature of daytime village life.

---

# Classroom Atmosphere

Inside classrooms:

* Chalk smell
* Soft murmuring before teacher begins
* Slates or notebooks according to lesson
* Charts of alphabets, numbers, or simple civic posters from the era
* Ceiling fans only if continuity establishes them; otherwise natural ventilation
* Open windows to trees and compound light

Learning should feel earnest and local.

---

# Daily Rhythm

## Morning Arrival

* Children walking in small groups
* Metal tiffin boxes
* Slate/notebook bags or cloth bags
* Bicycles for older students/teachers leaning near compound
* Assembly near flag post

## School Hours

* Lessons
* Short breaks
* Playground bursts of energy
* Water drinking lines
* Teacher movement between rooms

## Closing Bell

* Sudden release of voices
* Children running toward gate
* Dust rising
* Walk home along village paths
* Elder Brother returning toward Family House route

## After Hours / Holidays

* Quiet compound
* Occasional caretaker presence
* Birds in shade trees
* Empty playground stillness
