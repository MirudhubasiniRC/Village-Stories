# Small Grocery Store

## Purpose

The Small Grocery Store is the village's everyday provisions landmark within the Village Stories universe.

It is where families buy rice, lentils, oil, spices, soap, matches, and other household essentials that cannot always be grown or made at home.

Every return to this location should feel instantly familiar as an early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu petty shop / provisions store—compact, trustworthy, and socially warm.

---

# Philosophy

The grocery store is not a supermarket.

It is a small owner-run shop where goods are measured, wrapped, and remembered by habit.

Customers are known by face and family.

Credit may exist through trust.

Conversation happens while sugar is weighed.

The shop should always feel practical, intimate, and permanently local.

---

# Location

The Small Grocery Store is permanently located **West of the Village Centre**, among the emerging trades and provisions side of the village.

It connects naturally to:

* Routes from Village Square
* Neighbourhood houses
* Future nearby trades (Blacksmith, Carpenter, Potter, Barber) without requiring them to exist yet in full detail
* Walking paths used by Mother and neighbours for household purchases

Mother is a natural recurring customer.

Father may buy practical items when passing.

Elder Brother may be sent for small errands with clear instructions.

Younger Daughter may accompany Mother and watch with curiosity.

The store must never be relocated.

---

# Identity

Maintain early-1990s authenticity:

* Narrow shopfront opening onto a path or small open edge
* Raised platform / thinnai-like step where people can sit briefly
* Wooden shelves packed with tins, jars, and packets of the era
* Hanging biproducts: bunches, rope, brooms, or simple household goods
* Hand-painted Tamil signboard
* Weighing scale
* Paper cones / newspaper wrapping habits of the period
* Glass jars of sweets and loose items
* No supermarket aisles
* No barcode scanners
* No giant refrigerated glass walls as identity
* No modern mall packaging dominance

# Shop-Street Visual Lock (CANON-2026-005)

When the grocery appears with path / neighbouring trades (establishing or
approach shots), match viral early-1990s village street language:

**Always:**

* Narrow packed-earth lane — not a wide asphalt boulevard.
* Open shutter shopfront; raised sit-step; counter + scale readable.
* Shelves packed with jars, tins, gunny, hanging brooms / rope / goods.
* Hand-painted **Tamil** signboard (no Devanagari default).
* Neighbouring shop edges or tea-stall path language when the wide includes street.
* Lived-in paint wear at counter lean-edge.

**Never:**

* Supermarket aisles, glass mall fronts, franchise boards, LED strip glow.
* Empty “show” shelves or luxury café seating.
* Wide modern highway retail strip.

# Architecture

Maintain:

* Single small shop room
* Open shutter front during business hours
* Wooden or metal shutter closed at night
* Country tile or simple sheet roof consistent with nearby village buildings
* Cream / pale blue / aged white wall wash
* Worn paint near the counter edge from years of leaning arms
* Dimmer rear storage corner
* Small electric bulb or tube light practical to early 1990s if electrified; otherwise warm lantern logic for late dusk closing only

Avoid:

* Glass curtain walls
* Franchise storefronts
* LED strip aesthetics
* AC duct visible modernity

# Daily Rhythm

## Morning

* Shutter opens
* Fresh sweeping
* First customers after tea/breakfast rhythm
* Mother-type household buyers
* Soft measuring and wrapping sounds

## Midday

* Slower browsing
* Neighbour conversations at the step
* Children sent on tiny errands
* Owner's quiet waiting

## Evening

* Busier household restocking
* Farmers returning with small needs
* Stronger social energy
* Last sales before dusk

## Night

* Shutter closed
* Quiet western path
* Calendar and shelves hidden behind wood/metal

# Seasonal Behaviour

## Summer

* Oil and soap demand steady
* Customers pause longer on shaded step
* Dust at threshold

## Monsoon

* Damp footprints at entrance
* Careful grain protection
* People shelter briefly under shop overhang while buying
* Earth smell mixes with spice smell

## Festival periods

* Extra sugar, ghee-related needs if stocked, camphor, incense

# Buying Culture

Authentic behaviours:

* Asking by measure (a quarter, half kilo)
* Trusting the shopkeeper's weighing
* Brief talk about family and weather
* Carrying goods in cloth bag or paper packet
* Children memorizing a list from Mother
* Returning empty bottles/cans when relevant

Shopping is relational, not anonymous.
