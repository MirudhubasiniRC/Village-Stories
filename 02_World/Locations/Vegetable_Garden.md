# Vegetable Garden

## Purpose

The Vegetable Garden is the family's primary source of fresh vegetables, herbs, and cooking ingredients.

It represents self-sufficiency, seasonal living, and the family's close relationship with nature.

Nearly every cooking story begins here before moving into the Kitchen.

---

# Philosophy

The Vegetable Garden is not decorative.

It is a productive family garden that provides fresh ingredients for everyday meals.

Every plant has a purpose.

The garden changes naturally with the seasons while remaining healthy and carefully maintained.

---

# Location

The Vegetable Garden is located directly behind the Backyard.

It connects naturally with:

- Backyard
- Kitchen
- Well Utility Area
- Livestock Shelter
- Compost Area
- Path leading toward the River Ghat

Fresh vegetables are carried directly from the garden into the Kitchen.

# Ground

Maintain:

- Rich brown soil.
- Narrow walking paths.
- Small earthen borders.
- Healthy mulch.
- Natural irrigation channels.

Avoid concrete pathways.

---

# Garden Structure

The garden consists of several permanent growing areas.

These include:

- Leafy vegetables.
- Root vegetables.
- Fruiting vegetables.
- Climbing vegetables.
- Herbs.
- Banana plants.

Each section remains in approximately the same location.

# Seasonal Changes

Summer

- Frequent watering.
- Bright sunlight.
- Mature vegetables.

Monsoon

- Rapid growth.
- Rich green leaves.
- Damp soil.
- Healthy vines.

Post-Monsoon

- Peak harvest.
- Dense vegetation.
- Colourful produce.

Dry Season

- Slower growth.
- Greater dependence on well water.

---

# Daily Activities

Common activities include:

- Harvesting vegetables.
- Watering plants.
- Removing weeds.
- Checking plant health.
- Collecting herbs.
- Planting seedlings.
- Adding compost.
- Inspecting flowers and fruits.

The garden is visited several times each day.

---

# Harvesting

Mother harvests only what the family needs.

She:

- Selects ripe vegetables.
- Uses traditional baskets.
- Avoids damaging plants.
- Leaves young vegetables to mature.

Harvesting should feel careful and respectful.

---

# Father

Father regularly:

- Prepares soil.
- Repairs supports.
- Builds trellises.
- Plants seasonal crops.
- Carries compost.
- Maintains irrigation.

Heavy garden work is usually performed by Father.

---

# Mother

Mother regularly:

- Harvests vegetables.
- Collects herbs.
- Inspects plant health.
- Waters delicate plants.
- Teaches Daughter about gardening.

The Vegetable Garden is one of Mother's favourite places.

---

# Daughter

Daughter learns gardening by helping Mother.

Typical activities include:

- Carrying baskets.
- Watering plants.

---

# Family Dog

The Family Dog occasionally:

- Walks beside Mother.
- Watches birds.
- Rests in the shade.
- Follows the family through the garden.

The dog does not damage crops.

---

# Chickens

Free-range chickens naturally visit the garden edges.

Typical behaviour:

- Searching for insects.
- Pecking the soil.
- Dust bathing outside planting beds.
- Following Mother or Father.

They should not destroy the family's crops.

# Ambient Life Rule

The Vegetable Garden should never appear static.

Background life may include:

- Butterflies moving between flowers.
- Bees visiting blossoms.
- Leaves swaying gently.
- Birds landing briefly.
- Chickens crossing nearby.
- The Family Dog resting under shade.
- Gentle breeze moving vines.

The audience should feel that the garden is continuously growing and living.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Healthy productive plants.
- Traditional village gardening methods.
- Seasonal vegetables.
- Natural soil.
- Narrow earthen paths.
- Strong connection with the Kitchen.
- Daily family use.

Never:

- Introduce ornamental landscaping.
- Use modern greenhouse structures.
- Leave the garden neglected.
- Overcrowd the space with unnecessary decorations.
