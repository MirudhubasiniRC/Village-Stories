# Puja Corner

## Purpose

The Puja Corner is the spiritual heart of the Family House.

It is a small, sacred space where the family begins important days, expresses gratitude, seeks blessings, and quietly reflects.

Unlike the Sacred Banyan Tree & Vinayagar Shrine, which belongs to the entire village, this space belongs only to the family.

It should always feel intimate, peaceful, and deeply respected.

---

# Identity

The Puja Corner is not a separate room.

It is a dedicated prayer alcove opening directly into the Courtyard, allowing natural light, fresh air, and the sounds of the home to gently enter.

It is humble rather than elaborate.

Everything here has purpose.

Nothing is decorative for decoration's sake.

---

# Location

The Puja Corner is permanently located along the northern wall of the Courtyard.

It is visible from the Courtyard but slightly recessed, creating a quiet and respectful space without isolating it from everyday family life.

It remains easily accessible to every member of the household.

# Architecture

Maintain:

* Recessed wall alcove.
* White lime-plastered walls.
* Red oxide flooring.
* Simple wooden shrine platform.
* No doors.
* No curtains.
* Open connection with the Courtyard.
* Soft natural lighting.

The space should remain visually simple.

---

# Shrine

Maintain:

* Small wooden shrine fixed to the wall.
* Traditional carved craftsmanship.
* One or more framed family deities appropriate to a traditional South Indian Hindu household.
* Small brass idols where appropriate.
* Fresh flowers.
* Sandalwood paste.
* Kumkum.
* Turmeric.
* Sacred ash (Vibhuti).
* Incense holder.

The arrangement should remain neat and respectful.

---

# Permanent Objects

These permanently belong here:

* Brass kuthuvilakku (standing oil lamp).
* Small brass hand bell.
* Brass incense holder.
* Brass camphor plate.
* Matchbox stored discreetly.
* Small brass water vessel.
* Flower plate.
* Cotton wicks.
* Oil container.
* Prayer books wrapped in cloth.
* Wooden prayer stool.
* Small woven sitting mat.

Every object should appear well cared for.

---

# Daily Routine

Morning

* Fresh flowers offered.
* Oil lamp lit.
* Incense burned.
* Brief family prayer.
* Tulsi leaves or flowers placed before the shrine.

Afternoon

* Lamp usually extinguished.
* Quiet space.
* Fresh flowers remain.

Evening

* Oil lamp lit again.
* Incense.
* Short evening prayer.
* Bell rung gently.

The routine should feel natural rather than ceremonial.

---

# Festival Behaviour

During festivals:

* Additional flower garlands.
* Extra brass lamps.
* Banana leaves nearby.
* Fruits offered.
* Coconut.
* Traditional sweets.
* Decorative kolam extending into the Courtyard.

The Puja Corner becomes brighter but never overcrowded.

---

# Environmental Behaviour

Morning

* Soft eastern light reaching the alcove indirectly.
* Gentle breeze from the Courtyard.
* Faint incense smoke drifting upward.

Evening

* Warm glow from the oil lamp.
* Soft moving shadows.
* Quiet atmosphere.

Monsoon

* Diffused cool light.
* Fresh smell of rain entering from the Courtyard.
* Calm reflective mood.

Nature should gently complement the sacred atmosphere.

---

# Audio Identity

Typical sounds include:

* Brass bell.
* Crackling oil lamp.
* Soft chanting.
* Birds from the Courtyard.
* Wind.
* Rain during monsoon.
* Quiet household activity in the background.

The Puja Corner should never feel completely silent; instead, it should feel peacefully connected to the living home.

# Camera Notes

Useful compositions include:

* View from the Courtyard into the alcove.
* Close-up of the brass lamp flame.
* Soft focus through incense smoke.
* Hands placing flowers.
* Reflections on polished brass.
* Morning light illuminating the shrine.
* Family praying together from behind.

Avoid dramatic or exaggerated camera angles.

The sacred atmosphere should be communicated through simplicity.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Recessed alcove.
* Wooden shrine.
* Brass kuthuvilakku.
* Brass bell.
* Fresh flowers when appropriate.
* Open connection to the Courtyard.
* White walls.
* Red oxide floor.

Never:

* Convert it into a separate room.
* Introduce electric decorative lighting.
* Add modern religious décor.
* Remove the brass lamp.
* Fill the alcove with unnecessary objects.

# Guiding Principle

The Puja Corner reminds the family to pause.

In a home filled with everyday work, laughter, cooking, rain, and conversation, this small space offers a quiet moment of gratitude before life continues.

Its beauty lies not in grandeur, but in its simplicity, devotion, and constancy across generations.
