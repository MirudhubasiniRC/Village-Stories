# Village Square

## Purpose

The Village Square is the social heart of the Village Stories universe.

Unlike the Sacred Banyan Tree, which serves as the village's spiritual centre, the Village Square serves as its everyday civic centre.

It is where people naturally meet, exchange news, rest, help one another, and experience the rhythms of village life.

Almost every resident passes through this space at some point during the day.

---

# Identity

The Village Square is not a formal plaza.

It is a naturally widened section of the Main Village Path that gradually became the village's common meeting place over generations.

There are no walls or gates.

Life simply slows down here.

Children stop to play.

Neighbours pause to chat.

Travelling vendors set down their baskets.

The postman distributes letters.

People naturally gather before continuing on their way.

---

# Location

The Village Square is located along the Main Village Path within the residential neighbourhood.

It lies between the Sacred Banyan Tree and the Family House.

Nearly every resident passes through this location daily.

---

# Ground

Maintain:

* Packed earth surface.
* Fine village dust during summer.
* Firm mud after rain.
* Small patches of grass along the edges.
* Naturally worn footpaths.
* No paving blocks or concrete.

The ground should reflect decades of everyday use.

---

# Central Shade Tree

The square contains a mature native shade tree.

Maintain:

* Broad canopy.
* Strong trunk.
* Dense leaves.
* Seasonal flowers where appropriate.
* Comfortable shade throughout much of the day.

Unlike the Sacred Banyan Tree, this tree is simply part of everyday village life.

---

# Stone Seating

Around the tree are simple stone benches.

Maintain:

* Hand-cut granite.
* Naturally weathered.
* Comfortable height.
* No decorative carving.

Villagers frequently sit here throughout the day.

---

# Surrounding Houses

The square is bordered by traditional homes.

These houses belong to recurring neighbours and should remain permanently positioned.

Each house should have:

* Whitewashed walls.
* Terracotta roof.
* Front veranda.
* Kolam at the entrance when appropriate.
* Clay pots.
* Flowering plants.
* Traditional wooden doors.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Open earth square.
* Central shade tree.
* Granite seating.
* Surrounding traditional houses.
* Main Village Path passing through.
* Community atmosphere.
* Traditional architecture.

Never:

* Add modern shops.
* Add permanent concrete structures.
* Introduce motor traffic as a dominant feature.
* Remove the central tree.
* Change the location of neighbouring houses.

---

# Canon Authority

This document is the permanent reference for the Village Square.

Every future story, storyboard, image prompt, and animation involving the Village Square must preserve its layout, atmosphere, and identity unless a permanent canonical change is intentionally introduced.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Village Square is where everyday life naturally intersects.

People arrive with different purposes, but almost everyone leaves having shared a greeting, a smile, a conversation, or a small moment that strengthens the feeling of community.

It is the village's living room beneath the open sky.
