# Kitchen

## Purpose

The Kitchen is the working heart of the Family House and one of the most important locations in the Village Stories universe.

It is where meals are prepared, traditions are preserved, practical knowledge is passed between generations, and many of the series' most memorable moments naturally unfold.

Unlike modern kitchens built around appliances, this kitchen is built around traditional craftsmanship, efficient workflow, natural light, and a close relationship with the surrounding environment.

Because cooking is central to the series, the Kitchen serves as one of the primary storytelling locations.

# Identity

The Kitchen is a traditional 1990s South Indian village kitchen.

Everything inside exists for a purpose.

Every workstation has a permanent location.

Every cooking activity follows a logical sequence.

Every object has an established home.

The Kitchen should always feel:

* Warm
* Welcoming
* Functional
* Smoke-aged
* Naturally illuminated
* Spacious
* Organized
* Deeply connected to nature

It should never appear cluttered, modernized, or artificially designed.

# Core Design Philosophy

The Kitchen is designed around four guiding principles.

## Workflow

Cooking should always follow a logical sequence from ingredient preparation to serving.

---

## Tradition

Traditional tools, materials, and cooking methods remain central to every activity.

---

## Nature

The Kitchen remains visually connected to the outdoors through natural light, ventilation, and the Garden Window.

The audience should frequently see changing weather, wildlife, plants, and family life beyond the window.

---

## Simplicity

Nothing is decorative without purpose.

Every object exists because it contributes to daily cooking.
