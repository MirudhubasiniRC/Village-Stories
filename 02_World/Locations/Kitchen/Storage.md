# Kitchen Storage

## Purpose

This document defines every permanent storage location within the Family Kitchen.

Storage is organized according to traditional cooking workflow rather than appearance.

Every ingredient, vessel, and cooking material has a permanent home, allowing efficient movement during meal preparation.

Nothing should ever appear or disappear without a logical source.

---

# Storage Philosophy

The Kitchen follows four permanent storage principles.

* Frequently used items remain closest to the stove.
* Heavy objects stay low.
* Fragile objects stay protected.
* Food remains separated from cooking fuel.

Every storage area supports the natural cooking workflow.

---

# Storage Zones

The Kitchen permanently contains the following storage areas.

* Firewood Storage
* Daily Firewood Basket
* Brass Vessel Shelf
* Clay Pot Shelf
* Iron Cookware Area
* Dry Ingredient Shelf
* Spice Shelf
* Water Storage
* Vegetable Basket Area
* Banana Leaf Storage
* Cleaning Shelf

Each zone has a permanent position.

---

# Firewood Storage

## Location

North-west corner beside the Raised Clay Stove.

## Purpose

Stores cooking fuel.

Maintain:

* Raised wooden rack.
* Firewood stacked horizontally.
* Small kindling.
* Coconut husks.
* Dried coconut fronds.
* Palm leaf bundles.

Wood should always remain dry and neatly arranged.

---

# Daily Firewood Basket

A bamboo basket permanently sits beside the stove.

Purpose:

* Holds enough wood for one cooking session.
* Reduces movement while cooking.
* Keeps the floor tidy.

The basket is refilled whenever needed.

---

# Brass Vessel Shelf

## Location

North wall.

Above working height.

## Purpose

Stores polished brass cooking vessels.

Top Shelf

* Festival vessels.
* Rarely used serving items.

Middle Shelf

* Everyday cooking pots.
* Water vessels.
* Medium bowls.

Lower Shelf

* Small bowls.
* Ladles.
* Serving utensils.

Brass items should always appear clean and polished.

---

# Clay Pot Shelf

## Location

Adjacent to the Brass Shelf.

## Purpose

Stores earthen cookware.

Maintain:

* Curry pots.
* Rice pot.
* Buttermilk pot.
* Fermentation pots.
* Pickle jars.
* Storage jars.

Clay cookware should be grouped by size.

---

# Iron Cookware Area

Heavy cookware occupies the lowest shelf.

Maintain:

* Iron kadai.
* Dosa tawa.
* Heavy roasting pan.
* Uruli.

Heavy cookware should never be stored overhead.

---

# Dry Ingredient Shelf

This shelf permanently stores staple cooking ingredients.

Maintain:

* Rice container.
* Toor dal.
* Urad dal.
* Moong dal.
* Chana dal.
* Tamarind.
* Jaggery.
* Rock salt.

Ingredients should be stored in traditional containers.

---

# Spice Shelf

Frequently used spices remain within easy reach of the stove.

Typical contents:

* Mustard seeds.
* Cumin.
* Fennel.
* Black pepper.
* Fenugreek.
* Turmeric.
* Red chilli powder.
* Coriander powder.
* Curry leaves (fresh when available).

Spices should remain organized in small brass, steel, or glass containers appropriate to the 1990s.

---

# Water Storage

## Location

Near the Rear Veranda entrance.

Purpose:

Fresh water brought directly from the Well.

Maintain:

* Large brass water pot.
* Clay drinking water pot.
* Brass tumbler.
* Copper vessel.

Water containers should always appear clean.

---

# Vegetable Basket Area

Fresh vegetables from the garden are placed here before preparation.

Typical contents:

* Tomatoes.
* Brinjal.
* Drumsticks.
* Okra.
* Greens.
* Onions.
* Garlic.
* Ginger.

Vegetables should appear seasonal.

---

# Banana Leaf Storage

Clean banana leaves are stored flat on a wooden rack.

Purpose:

* Serving meals.
* Festival cooking.
* Food wrapping.

Leaves should always remain fresh.

---

# Cleaning Shelf

Contains everyday cleaning supplies.

Maintain:

* Coconut fibre scrub.
* Ash container.
* Natural soap.
* Cleaning cloths.
* Small bucket.

Modern cleaning products should not appear.

---

# Storage Workflow

Permanent sequence:

Ingredients arrive

↓

Stored

↓

Prepared

↓

Cooked

↓

Served

↓

Cleaned

↓

Returned to storage

Nothing should remain randomly placed after cooking.

---

# Environmental Behaviour

The storage areas should show subtle signs of daily use.

Examples:

* Slight water marks beneath pots.
* Fine flour dust near grain containers.
* Curry leaf fragrance around the spice shelf.
* Firewood scent near the stove.
* Fresh vegetables changing with the seasons.

The Kitchen should always feel actively used.

---

# Camera Anchors

Useful compositions include:

* Brass vessels reflecting morning light.
* Rows of clay pots.
* Firewood stacked neatly.
* Vegetables in woven baskets.
* Hands selecting spices.
* Water pot beside the Rear Veranda.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Permanent storage locations.
* Organized shelves.
* Traditional containers.
* Clean appearance.
* Logical workflow.

Never:

* Store plastic containers prominently.
* Mix firewood with food storage.
* Change shelf positions.
* Introduce modern cabinets.
