# Kitchen Continuity

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent continuity rules for the Family Kitchen.

Its purpose is to preserve consistency across every story, storyboard, image prompt, animation, and future production document.

Unlike the other Kitchen documents, this file contains rules rather than descriptions.

When uncertainty exists, this document takes precedence.

---

# Canon Status

The Family Kitchen is a permanent canonical location.

Its architecture, layout, workflow, atmosphere, and visual identity remain consistent throughout the Village Stories universe unless an intentional permanent story event changes them.

Temporary changes made for a specific story do not alter the Kitchen's canon.

---

# Permanent Orientation

Always maintain:

* East → Courtyard
* West → Rear Veranda
* North → Cooking Wall
* South → Garden Window

Never rotate or mirror the Kitchen.

---

# Permanent Architecture

The following architectural elements never change.

* White lime-plastered walls.
* Red oxide flooring.
* High sloping Mangalore tiled roof.
* Exposed timber rafters.
* Large Garden Window.
* North Ventilation Window.
* Courtyard Door.
* Rear Veranda Door.

These elements remain fixed.

---

# Permanent Work Zones

Maintain the same locations for:

* Raised Clay Stove.
* Firewood Storage.
* Brass Vessel Shelf.
* Clay Pot Shelf.
* Water Storage.
* Preparation Area.
* Traditional Grinding Area.
* Open Working Area.

Workstations must never move.

---

# Permanent Circulation

Movement through the Kitchen always follows:

Rear Veranda

↓

Preparation Area

↓

Traditional Tools

↓

Raised Clay Stove

↓

Serving

↓

Courtyard

Characters should not teleport between workstations.

---

# Permanent Object Placement

Objects should always return to their established locations after use.

Examples:

* Wooden ladles beside the stove.
* Ammikkal at the grinding area.
* Firewood in the storage rack.
* Clay pots on the clay shelf.
* Brass vessels on the brass shelf.
* Water pots near the Rear Veranda.

Temporary use does not permanently change storage.

---

# Environmental Continuity

Always maintain:

* Natural daylight.
* Cross ventilation.
* Outdoor visibility.
* Seasonal weather.
* Gentle airflow.
* Realistic steam.
* Subtle smoke.

The Kitchen should always feel connected to nature.

---

# Lighting Continuity

Preferred lighting order:

1. Daylight.
2. Firelight.
3. Oil lamp.
4. Moonlight.

Avoid introducing strong artificial lighting.

---

# Camera Continuity

Always preserve:

* Garden Window compositions.
* Clay Stove compositions.
* Traditional working angles.
* Calm pacing.
* Gentle movement.
* Natural framing.

Avoid dramatic visual changes between episodes.

---

# Character Behaviour

Family members should already know the Kitchen.

Their movements should appear practiced and efficient.

They should not hesitate while searching for tools or ingredients.

Children gradually learn the workflow through observation and supervised participation.

Visitors may occasionally ask questions or require guidance.

---

# Tool Usage

Traditional tools should always be used for the tasks they are designed for.

Do not substitute modern appliances unless a story explicitly introduces them as a major canonical change.

Traditional methods remain the default.

---

# Cooking Behaviour

Cooking should always appear:

* Calm.
* Methodical.
* Safe.
* Traditional.
* Efficient.

Meals should rarely appear rushed.

The Kitchen should communicate patience.

---

# Cleanliness

The Kitchen is always maintained.

Normal signs of daily use include:

* Steam.
* Light smoke staining.
* Water droplets.
* Ash near the firebox.
* Worn work surfaces.

However:

* Dirty dishes should not accumulate unnecessarily.
* Food waste should not remain scattered.
* Work areas should be cleaned after cooking.

The Kitchen should feel lived in rather than messy.

---

# Seasonal Continuity

Seasonal conditions should affect the Kitchen.

Examples:

Summer

* Bright sunlight.
* Dry atmosphere.

Monsoon

* Rain visible through the Garden Window.
* Cooler colours.
* Increased steam.

Post-Monsoon

* Fresh vegetation.
* Pleasant daylight.

Seasonal changes should never contradict the World canon.

---

# Relationship With Other Locations

The Kitchen maintains permanent relationships with:

* Courtyard.
* Rear Veranda.
* Water Well.
* Vegetable Garden.
* Family House.

Their positions never change.

---

# Relationship With Objects

The Kitchen defines where objects belong.

Individual object specifications belong within:

```text
04_Objects/
```

The Kitchen should reference object files rather than duplicate their information.

---

# Canonical Exceptions

Temporary changes are allowed only when required by a story.

Examples:

* Festival decorations.
* Extra cooking vessels.
* Guests helping.
* Harvest produce temporarily stored.

After the story concludes, the Kitchen returns to its canonical state.

---

# Future Expansion

New Kitchen elements may be added only if they:

* Fit the 1990s setting.
* Support traditional cooking.
* Do not contradict existing documentation.
* Preserve established workflow.

Existing canonical elements should not be removed without a permanent story event.

---

# Authority

If multiple documents contain conflicting information:

Priority order:

1. Kitchen/Continuity.md
2. Kitchen.md
3. Other Kitchen documents.
4. Story-specific notes.

This document resolves continuity conflicts.

---

# Canon Authority

This document is the final authority governing the Family Kitchen.

Every future story, storyboard, image prompt, animation, production asset, and AI-generated scene must follow these continuity rules unless an intentional canonical change is introduced.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Family Kitchen should feel like a real place that exists even when no one is cooking.

Every object has a home.

Every movement has a purpose.

Every meal follows familiar traditions.

Across hundreds of episodes, viewers should feel they know this Kitchen as well as the family themselves.
