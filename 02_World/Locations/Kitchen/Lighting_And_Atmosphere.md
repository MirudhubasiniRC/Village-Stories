# Kitchen Lighting & Atmosphere

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent environmental characteristics of the Family Kitchen.

It establishes how natural light, weather, air movement, temperature, sounds, aromas, and seasonal changes influence the Kitchen throughout the day and across the year.

Rather than describing cooking, this document defines how the Kitchen feels.

---

# Philosophy

The Kitchen should never feel like an enclosed room.

It should feel alive.

Light changes.

Wind moves through the windows.

Rain becomes visible outside.

Steam rises.

Birds sing.

Cooking aromas drift into the backyard.

Nature should always be present.

---

# Primary Light Source

The Kitchen is illuminated primarily by natural daylight.

The most important light source is the **Large Garden Window**.

This window defines the visual identity of the Kitchen.

Artificial lighting should remain minimal.

---

# Morning Lighting

Typical characteristics:

* Soft golden sunlight.
* Long shadows.
* Fresh atmosphere.
* Bright eastern bounce light entering from the Courtyard.
* Gentle southern daylight through the Garden Window.

The Kitchen should feel calm and full of promise.

---

# Midday Lighting

Characteristics:

* Bright natural daylight.
* Balanced illumination.
* Minimal harsh shadows.
* Clear visibility across the entire room.

This is the brightest period of the day.

---

# Afternoon Lighting

Characteristics:

* Warm sunlight entering through the Garden Window.
* Rich highlights on brass vessels.
* Soft shadows across the floor.
* Warm reflections on clay pots.

Afternoon lighting creates a relaxed atmosphere.

---

# Evening Lighting

Before sunset:

* Warm golden light.
* Long shadows.
* Soft orange tones.

After sunset:

* Brass oil lamp.
* Clay stove firelight.
* Gentle ambient darkness.

Evening scenes should feel intimate rather than bright.

---

# Night Lighting

Only traditional light sources are used.

Maintain:

* Brass oil lamp.
* Clay stove flames.
* Moonlight entering through the Garden Window when appropriate.

Avoid brightly lit night scenes.

Darkness is part of the atmosphere.

---

# Seasonal Lighting

## Summer

Maintain:

* Bright sunlight.
* Warm colour temperature.
* Dry atmosphere.
* Strong shadows.

---

## Monsoon

Maintain:

* Soft diffused daylight.
* Cool colour tones.
* Moist atmosphere.
* Visible rainfall outside.
* Increased steam visibility.

Rain should transform the mood of the Kitchen.

---

## Post-Monsoon

Maintain:

* Clear skies.
* Fresh greenery.
* Crisp daylight.
* Pleasant temperatures.

The Kitchen feels especially vibrant during this season.

---

# Air Movement

Natural airflow permanently enters through:

* Garden Window.
* Courtyard Door.
* Rear Veranda Door.

Warm air exits through:

* Roof ventilation.
* North Ventilation Window.

Curtains, hanging cloths, and light smoke should respond gently to this airflow.

---

# Rain Behaviour

Rain should become one of the Kitchen's signature visual elements.

During rainfall:

* Rain visible beyond the Garden Window.
* Water droplets on leaves.
* Mist drifting through the backyard.
* Damp breeze entering the Kitchen.
* Steam becoming more noticeable.
* Clay stove providing warmth.

The contrast between warm cooking and cool rain should create emotional comfort.

---

# Steam Behaviour

Steam rises naturally from:

* Rice.
* Curries.
* Boiling water.
* Herbal drinks.
* Milk.

Maintain:

* Gentle movement.
* Soft diffusion.
* Interaction with natural light.
* Greater visibility during cool weather.

Steam should never appear exaggerated.

---

# Smoke Behaviour

Smoke should remain subtle.

Maintain:

* Thin wisps.
* Natural upward movement.
* Gradual dispersal.
* Soft interaction with sunlight.

The Kitchen should never appear filled with smoke.

---

# Temperature

The Kitchen should communicate warmth without discomfort.

Viewers should feel:

* Warm fire.
* Cool breeze.
* Comfortable working conditions.

The environment should always feel balanced.

---

# Fragrance Identity

The Kitchen should suggest familiar traditional aromas.

Typical fragrances include:

* Wood fire.
* Fresh curry leaves.
* Coconut.
* Roasted spices.
* Tamarind.
* Fresh coriander.
* Boiling rice.
* Rain on earth during monsoon.

These aromas should be implied visually rather than stated repeatedly.

---

# Audio Identity

Characteristic sounds include:

* Firewood crackling.
* Bubbling curry.
* Birds outside.
* Butterflies fluttering.
* Wind through trees.
* Wooden ladles.
* Clay lids.
* Brass vessels.
* Rainfall.
* Crickets at night.
* Distant temple bell.

These sounds define the Kitchen's ASMR personality.

---

# Relationship With The Garden

The Garden Window constantly connects the Kitchen with outdoor life.

Possible background moments:

* Butterflies passing.
* Chickens wandering.
* Family Dog waiting.
* Father returning.
* Children playing.
* Banana leaves moving.
* Rain beginning.
* Coconut trees swaying.

These events should support the scene without distracting from cooking.

---

# Environmental Storytelling

The environment should quietly tell its own story.

Examples:

* Steam drifting into cool air.
* Sunlight moving across the floor.
* Rain collecting on leaves.
* Wind lifting a cotton curtain.
* Shadows changing with time.

The Kitchen should never feel visually static.

---

# Camera Atmosphere

Lighting should support:

* Warm close-ups.
* Steam illuminated by sunlight.
* Firelight beneath clay pots.
* Silhouettes during sunset.
* Rain framed by the Garden Window.
* Moonlight entering during quiet night scenes.

The atmosphere should always reinforce the emotional tone of the story.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Natural lighting.
* Seasonal consistency.
* Visible airflow.
* Outdoor connection.
* Warm interior.
* Subtle smoke.
* Soft steam.

Never:

* Use bright artificial lighting.
* Fill the Kitchen with dense smoke.
* Ignore changing weather.
* Disconnect the Kitchen from the outdoors.

# Guiding Principle

The Kitchen should feel as though nature is cooking alongside the family.

Sunlight, rain, wind, steam, birdsong, and the warmth of the clay stove should work together to create a place where every meal feels comforting, timeless, and unmistakably part of village life.
