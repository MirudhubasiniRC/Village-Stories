# Field Rest Area (Neem · Kayiru Kattil · Field Well)

## Purpose

This document defines the family's **field-work rest landmark** at the paddy edge — the place where Father pauses during agricultural labour, washes at a simple field well, and rests in neem shade.

It is **not** the backyard **Well Utility Area** behind the Kitchen. That well serves the house; this well serves **fields and harvest work**.

Every story, image prompt, animation, and production document involving mid-field rest, face-washing after labour, or the kayiru kattil under the neem tree should reference this location.

# Identity

The Field Rest Area is a **practical farmer pause point**, not a temple or public ghat.

It belongs to the rhythm of honest labour: transplanting, harvesting, carrying baskets, washing mud from face and hands, letting the dog rest in shade before walking home.

It should feel peaceful, open, and rooted in generations of field work.

# Major features

## Neem tree

* Large native South Indian neem — medicine and shade (`Nature/Flora.md`).
* Wide canopy; fallen leaves natural on ground.
* Primary shade for the kattil.

## Kayiru kattil (field rest cot)

* Traditional **woven rope charpai** on four wooden legs.
* Village term: **kayiru kattil** (also spoken as *kayathu kattil* in some households).
* Used for brief rest between field tasks — not a bedroom cot.
* Family Dog may lie here calmly; never as comedy damage to crops.

## Field well

* Simple **agricultural / field-edge well** — stone or brick rim, wooden pulley frame, rope.
* For washing hands, face, and feet during field work; drawing water for nearby tasks when needed.
* Distinct from `Water_Well.md` (household Well Utility Area behind Kitchen).
* No electric pump house as default identity.

# Typical activities

* Face and hand washing after paddy or garden labour.
* Resting on kattil in neem shade.
* Setting down harvest baskets (onion bamboo baskets, etc.) at well edge.
* Dog waiting while Father finishes washing.
* Brief pause before walking home toward Vinayagar shrine path or compound.
