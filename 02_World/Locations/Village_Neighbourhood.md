# Village Neighbourhood

## Purpose

The Village Neighbourhood defines the permanent residential community of the Village Stories universe.

It establishes where the recurring families live, how the homes are arranged, and how the neighbourhood functions as a living community.

This document describes **the place**.

The people who live here are defined separately in the Character folder.

---

# Identity

The Village Neighbourhood is a peaceful 1990s South Indian farming community where several families have lived for generations.

Everyone knows one another.

Children freely visit neighbouring homes.

Families regularly exchange food, help during difficult times, celebrate festivals together, and naturally become part of each other's daily lives.

The neighbourhood should always feel warm, welcoming, and deeply connected.

---

# Location

The neighbourhood lies along both sides of the Main Village Path.

It begins shortly after the Village Square and continues toward the Family House.

The Main Family lives within the neighbourhood rather than apart from it.

# Architectural Style

Every house should share the same traditional architectural language while maintaining its own identity.

Maintain:

* Whitewashed lime-plastered walls.
* Terracotta tiled roof.
* Wooden doors.
* Raised front thinnai.
* Small front yard.
* Clay water pots.
* Flowering plants.
* Fruit trees where appropriate.
* Traditional rainwater drainage.
* Mud or red oxide floors.

No house should appear modern or out of place.

---

# Front Yards

Most homes naturally include:

* Morning kolam.
* Tulsi plant.
* Clay flower pots.
* Coconut broom.
* Wooden stool.
* Drying firewood.
* Children's sandals.
* Small woven baskets.
* Traditional storage pots.

Each yard should feel personal while remaining authentic to the village.

---

# Shared Community Spaces

The neighbourhood itself functions as one shared living environment.

Residents naturally gather:

* Along the Main Village Path.
* At the Village Square.
* On front thinnais.
* Beneath roadside trees.
* Outside neighbouring homes.

These spaces encourage everyday interaction.

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Permanent house locations.
* House IDs.
* Traditional architecture.
* Whitewashed walls.
* Terracotta roofs.
* Raised front thinnais.
* Community atmosphere.
* Open front yards.
* Walking connections between homes.

Never:

* Relocate a permanent house.
* Replace traditional homes with modern buildings.
* Add large compound walls.
* Isolate families from one another.
* Remove a house without permanently updating the village canon.

---

# Canon Authority

This document is the permanent reference for the residential geography of the Village Stories universe.

Every recurring household must occupy the same home throughout the series unless an intentional canonical event permanently changes the village.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Village Neighbourhood should feel like a place where every home has a history, every family belongs, and every doorway leads to another story.

As viewers return to the village, they should begin to recognize not only the houses, but also the lives unfolding within them.
