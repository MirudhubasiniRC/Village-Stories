# Potter Clay Yard

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the village Potter's traditional clay-working yard.

---

# Location

The Clay Yard is permanently located west of the Village Centre in the trades area, near the Carpenter Workshop and Blacksmith Forge routes.

---

# Identity

Maintain:

* Traditional olai-leaf (palm-leaf) roof working shelter.
* Open packed-earth clay yard.
* Hand-operated potter's wheel.
* Clay soaking and preparation area.
* Wooden shelves or boards for drying pots.
* Outdoor kiln separate from the palm-leaf roof for fire safety.
* Stacked firewood and finished-pot area.
* No modern ceramic factory or electric kiln.

---

# Daily Life

The Potter forms, dries, fires, and sells practical clay items: water pots, cooking pots, storage jars, oil lamps, and festival diyas.

---

# Atmosphere and Sounds

* Potter's wheel turning.
* Wet clay handled by palms.
* Pots set on drying boards.
* Kiln crackle and firing smoke.
* Birds, wind, and west-side path activity.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain palm-leaf roof, outdoor kiln, hand wheel, clay yard, and traditional firing process.

Never create a tiled factory, electric kiln, modern showroom, or power-driven production line.

The Clay Yard should feel like earth becoming useful: wet clay, patient hands, sun drying, and fire completing the work.
