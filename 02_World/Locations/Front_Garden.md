# Front Garden

## Purpose

The Front Garden is the welcoming face of the Family House.

It forms the transition between the public village and the family's private home.

Every visitor, neighbour, family member, and traveller first experiences the house through this garden.

It should immediately communicate warmth, care, and the family's close relationship with nature.

---

# Identity

Unlike a decorative garden, this is a living family space.

Every plant has a purpose.

Some provide flowers for prayer.

Some provide ingredients for cooking.

Some offer shade.

Others simply make the home beautiful.

The garden should feel mature, naturally grown over many years, and lovingly maintained.

---

# Location

The Front Garden is permanently located between the Main Village Path and the Front Thinnai.

It is the easternmost part of the family property.

Morning sunlight reaches this area before any other part of the house.

---

# Overall Layout

```
                 Main Village Path
                        │
              Wooden Entrance Gate
                        │
             Packed Earth Entrance Path
                        │
        Flower Beds      │      Flower Beds

     Jasmine Plants      │      Hibiscus Plants

        Tulsi Shrine      │      Butterfly Plants

                        │

                 Front Thinnai
```

Nothing should feel perfectly symmetrical.

The garden should look naturally developed over time.

---

# Entrance Gate

Maintain:

* Handmade wooden gate.
* Low wooden fence on both sides.
* Simple wooden latch.
* Weathered finish.
* Flowering creepers growing nearby.

The gate usually remains open during the day unless livestock need to be kept inside.

---

# Entrance Path

Maintain:

* Packed earth.
* Slightly worn by years of walking.
* Grass growing along the edges.
* Small natural stones.
* Fallen flowers.
* Leaves after windy days.

The path should gently guide visitors toward the Front Thinnai.

---

# Flower Garden

Flowers are an essential part of the home's identity.

Maintain a mixture of:

* Jasmine.
* Hibiscus.
* Kanakambaram (Firecracker Flower).
* Marigold.
* Rose (one or two traditional bushes).
* Butterfly-attracting wildflowers.

These flowers are used for:

* Daily prayer.
* Hair decoration.
* Festival decoration.
* Natural beauty.

Flowers should bloom differently throughout the seasons.

---

# Sacred Tulsi

One raised Tulsi platform permanently exists within the Front Garden.

Maintain:

* Lime-plastered square pedestal.
* Healthy Tulsi plant.
* Small oil lamp space.
* Fresh water offered daily.
* Occasional flower offerings.

The Tulsi is part of the family's daily routine.

---

# Trees

The Front Garden should include one medium-sized shade tree.

Recommended:

* Indian Gooseberry (Amla)
  or
* Guava

The tree should:

* Provide gentle shade.
* Attract birds.
* Produce seasonal fruit.
* Never block the house façade.

---

# Butterflies & Pollinators

The Front Garden naturally attracts:

* Butterflies.
* Honey bees.
* Dragonflies after rain.
* Small birds.

Their presence should subtly enrich the environment.

---

# Daily Life

Morning

* Mother collects flowers.
* Tulsi worship.
* Sweeping.
* Fresh kolam at the entrance.
* Children leaving for school.

Afternoon

* Dog resting in the shade.
* Butterflies visiting flowers.
* Neighbours stopping briefly.
* Light gardening.

Evening

* Family returning home.
* Soft conversations near the gate.
* Children playing before dinner.
* Oil lamp near the Tulsi.

---

# Seasonal Behaviour

## Summer

* Bright flowering plants.
* Dry pathway.
* Deep morning sunlight.
* Warm earthy colours.

---

## Monsoon

* Rich green foliage.
* Rain droplets on leaves.
* Wet earth.
* Fresh flower growth.
* Butterflies after rainfall.

---

## Post-Monsoon / Harvest

* Most colourful season.
* Full flowering plants.
* Healthy vegetation.
* Fresh fragrance.

---

# Environmental Behaviour

Always include naturally when appropriate:

* Bees moving between flowers.
* Butterflies.
* Fallen petals.
* Gentle breeze moving leaves.
* Morning dew.
* Light drifting flower fragrance.
* Birds visiting the tree.

The garden should never appear static.

---

# Audio Identity

Common sounds include:

* Bees.
* Butterflies fluttering softly.
* Birds singing.
* Leaves moving in the breeze.
* Footsteps along the entrance path.
* Children entering and leaving.
* Dog resting nearby.

The atmosphere should feel peaceful and welcoming.

---

# Story Opportunities

The Front Garden naturally supports:

* Welcoming visitors.
* Flower gathering.
* Festival decoration.
* Children returning from school.
* Quiet conversations.
* Receiving deliveries.
* Morning routines.
* Family farewells.
* Village greetings.

It often serves as the transition between the public village and the private home.

---

# Camera Notes

Useful compositions include:

* Looking from the Village Path toward the house.
* Looking outward from the Front Thinnai.
* Flowers framing the foreground.
* Butterflies passing through the frame.
* Morning sunlight illuminating the entrance.
* Low-angle shots through flower beds.
* The house partially hidden behind plants for depth.

The garden should make the house feel inviting before viewers even enter it.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Wooden entrance gate.
* Earthen pathway.
* Flower beds.
* Tulsi platform.
* Medium shade tree.
* Open relationship with the Front Thinnai.
* Traditional planting style.

Never:

* Add ornamental concrete landscaping.
* Introduce modern garden decorations.
* Replace flowers with lawns.
* Install paving stones.
* Remove the Tulsi platform.
* Introduce exotic ornamental plants inconsistent with a 1990s South Indian village.

---

# Canon Authority

This document is the permanent reference for the Front Garden.

Every future storyboard, image prompt, animation, and story involving the front of the Family House must preserve this layout and atmosphere unless a permanent canonical change is intentionally introduced.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Front Garden is the home's first smile.

Before a character speaks, before a meal is cooked, and before a story begins, the garden quietly welcomes everyone with flowers, birdsong, and the feeling that this is a home cared for with love.
