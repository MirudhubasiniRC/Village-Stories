# Bedroom

## Purpose

The Bedroom is the family's private resting space.

It is where the family sleeps, recovers from illness, stores their most personal belongings, and experiences many of the quietest moments of everyday life.

Unlike the Courtyard, which is active throughout the day, the Bedroom represents comfort, privacy, healing, and rest.

It should always feel calm, airy, and peaceful.

---

# Identity

This is not a modern bedroom.

It is a traditional 1990s South Indian village bedroom designed for simplicity, comfort, and natural ventilation.

During the day it feels spacious and uncluttered.

At night it transforms into a warm, intimate sleeping space.

Nothing inside should feel luxurious.

Everything should feel practical, well cared for, and lovingly maintained.

---

# Location

The Bedroom is permanently located on the northern side of the Courtyard.

It is accessed directly from the Courtyard.

The east-facing window behind the bed receives the first soft sunlight every morning, following the world's fixed orientation.

# Architecture

Maintain:

* White lime-plastered walls.
* Red oxide flooring.
* Sloping tiled roof.
* Exposed wooden rafters.
* Thick masonry walls.
* High ceiling for ventilation.
* One large east-facing wooden window.
* One solid wooden entrance door opening toward the Courtyard.

The room should always feel naturally cool.

---

# Wooden Cot

The cot is the room's primary piece of furniture.

Maintain:

* Traditional teak wooden cot.
* Handcrafted headboard.
* Natural woven coir support.
* Cotton mattress.
* White cotton bedsheet.
* Thin cotton blanket folded neatly.
* Two cotton pillows.

The cot remains permanently beneath the east-facing window.

---

# Storage

Permanent furniture includes:

* Wooden clothes chest.
* Wooden clothes rack.
* Small wooden stool.
* Brass mirror mounted on the wall.
* Small wall shelf.
* Folded spare bedding.

Storage should remain minimal and tidy.

---

# Personal Objects

Typical personal belongings include:

* Folded sarees.
* Folded veshtis.

* Hair comb.
* Coconut oil bottle.
* Small brass container.
* Family photographs (very few).

# Window

The window is one of the defining features of the Bedroom.

Maintain:

* Large wooden shutters.
* Cotton curtains.
* East-facing orientation.
* Morning sunlight.
* Fresh airflow.
* View toward the Front Garden.

The window should frequently appear in compositions.
