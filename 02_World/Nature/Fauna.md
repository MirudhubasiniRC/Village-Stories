# Fauna

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent animal life of the Village Stories universe.

It establishes the domestic animals, birds, insects, aquatic life, reptiles, amphibians, and small wild animals that naturally inhabit the village and its surrounding landscape.

Every story, image prompt, storyboard, and animation should use this document as the authoritative reference for wildlife.

---

# Philosophy

Animals are part of everyday village life.

They are not decorations.

They live alongside the villagers, sharing the same environment.

Some animals work with people.

Some provide food.

Some pollinate plants.

Some simply add life and movement to the world.

Wildlife should always feel natural and never staged.

---

# Animal Categories

The Village Stories universe contains:

* Domestic Animals
* Birds
* Insects
* Amphibians
* Reptiles
* Fish
* Small Wild Animals

Each category behaves according to the seasons, weather, and time of day.

---

# Domestic Animals

## Family Dog

Purpose:

* Companion.
* Property guardian.
* Family member.

Behaviour:

* Follows family members.
* Waits patiently near the Kitchen.
* Rests beneath shade trees.
* Greets Father returning home.
* Plays gently with children.

The Family Dog should appear frequently throughout the series.

---

## Cows

Purpose:

* Milk.
* Agriculture.
* Village life.

Behaviour:

* Grazing.
* Resting beneath trees.
* Returning home during evening.

---

## Goats

Purpose:

* Livestock.

Behaviour:

* Browsing shrubs.
* Following village paths.
* Curious but calm.

---

## Chickens

Purpose:

* Eggs.
* Natural village activity.

# Butterflies

Butterflies are one of the visual signatures of Village Stories.

Common colours:

* Yellow.
* White.
* Orange.
* Blue.

Behaviour:

* Visiting flowers.
* Flying through gardens.
* Crossing the Garden Window.
* Appearing after rainfall.

They should frequently appear in peaceful outdoor scenes.

---

# Bees

Purpose:

* Pollination.

Behaviour:

* Around flowers.
* Vegetable gardens.
* Fruit trees.

Maintain realistic activity.

---

# Dragonflies

Most common:

* Post-Monsoon.
* Near water.

Behaviour:

* Flying above fields.
* Hovering near ponds.

---

# Fireflies

Appear during:

* Warm evenings.
* Monsoon.
* Cool nights.

Use sparingly for magical but realistic atmosphere.

---

# Frogs

Most active during:

* Monsoon.
* Near ponds.
* Wet fields.

Behaviour:

* Calling after rain.
* Hiding among vegetation.

---

# Fish

Commonly found in:

* Canal.

* Natural ecosystem.

---

# Reptiles

Occasionally include:

* Garden lizards.
* Skinks.

# Small Wild Animals

Typical animals:

* Palm squirrels.
* Field mice.
* Rabbits (rare).
* Mongooses.

---

# Seasonal Behaviour

Summer

* Animals seek shade.
* Reduced midday activity.

Monsoon

* Frogs increase.
* Dragonflies increase.
* Birds active after rain.

Post-Monsoon

* Maximum wildlife activity.
* Butterflies abundant.
* Bees active.

Harvest Season

* Birds gather near fields.
* Livestock more visible.

Cool Season

* Calm mornings.
* Active daytime birds.

---

# Relationship With The Family

Animals naturally interact with daily life.

Examples:

* Dog waiting outside the Kitchen.
* Chickens following Mother.
* Cows returning in the evening.
* Birds singing during breakfast.
* Butterflies around the Vegetable Garden.

Animals should support everyday life rather than distract from it.

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Native South Indian wildlife.
* Seasonal behaviour.
* Natural movement.
* Appropriate habitats.
* Respectful interaction with villagers.

Never:

* Introduce exotic animals.
* Overcrowd scenes with wildlife.
* Humanize animals unrealistically.
* Add wildlife that contradicts the environment.

# Guiding Principle

The animals of the village are quiet companions to everyday life.

Whether it is a dog waiting patiently by the Kitchen, butterflies dancing among flowers, or birds greeting the sunrise, wildlife should gently enrich the world, making the village feel alive even when no one is speaking.
