# Fauna

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent animal life of the Village Stories universe.

It establishes the domestic animals, birds, insects, aquatic life, reptiles, amphibians, and small wild animals that naturally inhabit the village and its surrounding landscape.

Every story, image prompt, storyboard, and animation should use this document as the authoritative reference for wildlife.

---

# Philosophy

Animals are part of everyday village life.

They are not decorations.

They live alongside the villagers, sharing the same environment.

Some animals work with people.

Some provide food.

Some pollinate plants.

Some simply add life and movement to the world.

Wildlife should always feel natural and never staged.

---

# Animal Categories

The Village Stories universe contains:

* Domestic Animals
* Birds
* Insects
* Amphibians
* Reptiles
* Fish
* Small Wild Animals

Each category behaves according to the seasons, weather, and time of day.

# Domestic Animals

## Family Dog

Breed:

* German Shepherd.

Purpose:

* Companion.
* Property guardian.
* Family member.

Behaviour:

* Follows family members.
* Waits patiently near the Kitchen.
* Rests beneath shade trees.
* Greets Father returning home.
* Plays gently with Elder Brother and Younger Daughter.
* Watches the compound calmly without aggression.

Appearance notes:

* Classic rural German Shepherd look — **black-and-tan**, fully fluffy long coat.
* Intelligent, loyal, and healthy from village life.
* Never styled as a police, military, show, or attack dog.

The Family Dog should appear frequently throughout the series.

## Chickens

Purpose:

* Eggs.
* Natural village activity.

The family keeps **native free-range village chickens** — **one rooster**
(orange-gold hackles, forest-green iridescent tail) and three recurring hens
(**cinnamon-brown, pure white, speckled mottled**). Locked in
`Family_Chicken_Canon_Design_Reference.png`. Ankle-to-shin height on adults.

Behaviour:

* Scratching soil and pecking seeds.
* Following insects.
* Walking through yards.
* Gathering near food preparation.

They frequently appear around the Family House — including **packed-earth Front
Thinnai / Front Garden edge** (pecking and roaming), Backyard, Well Utility Area,
Vegetable Garden, and Livestock Shelter during daylight.

## Ducks

**Lakeside lock (CANON-2026-010):** when the Family House **lake** is visible,
show **ducks** on the water or lake edge (2–5 birds typical) — swimming, dabbling,
or walking the muddy rim. Native village ducks — brown/white mix OK; calm, not
cartoon mascots.

Also appear near:

* Canal.
* Paddy field edges after rain.

Do not leave a readable lake empty of all waterfowl in establishing or feed
exteriors.

# Birds

Common species include:

* House Sparrow
* Common Myna
* Asian Koel
* White-throated Kingfisher (near water)
* Green Bee-eater
* Cattle Egret
* Indian Pond Heron (lake/paddy)
* Rock Pigeon
* Crow
* Parakeet

**Outdoor density lock:** at least one bird cue when outdoors in daylight —
perch on jackfruit/mango/thatch eave, flight across lake, or ground sparrows —
unless the shot is extreme macro food-only.

Birds should always match the environment.

---

# Living Environment Density (CANON-2026-010)

Viral village Shorts feel alive because **animals and place scenery share the
frame** with the story — not because of separate nature documentaries.

**When the shot is outdoors / homestead / lake (ep_004+):**

| Must usually show | Where |
| --- | --- |
| Chickens pecking | Yard, thinnai edge, garden path |
| Ducks | On lake or lake edge when water is in frame |
| Birds | Trees, eaves, flight, or ground |
| Cows / goats | Shelter / feed beats; soft bg OK otherwise |
| Family Dog | When story casts him |
| Place scenery | Lake + hills + green path + fruit trees as locked |

**Cut-cut Shorts:** weave life into story shots (background ducks while feeding
cows; sparrow on jackfruit branch during harvest). Do **not** add 8 empty
ambience-only clips — keep density **inside** the causal cuts.

Animals support everyday life; they never steal the hook.

# Seasonal Behaviour

Summer

* Animals seek shade.
* Reduced midday activity.

Monsoon

* Frogs increase.
* Dragonflies increase.
* Birds active after rain.

Post-Monsoon

* Maximum wildlife activity.
* Butterflies abundant.
* Bees active.

Harvest Season

* Birds gather near fields.
* Livestock more visible.

Cool Season

* Calm mornings.
* Active daytime birds.

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Native South Indian wildlife.
* Seasonal behaviour.
* Natural movement.
* Appropriate habitats.
* Respectful interaction with villagers.

Never:

* Introduce exotic animals.
* Overcrowd scenes with wildlife.
* Humanize animals unrealistically.
* Add wildlife that contradicts the environment.
