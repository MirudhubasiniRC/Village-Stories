# Seasons

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent seasonal cycle of the Village Stories universe.

Every season influences the environment, weather, lighting, farming, cooking, wildlife, village life, clothing, and daily routines.

All future stories, image prompts, animations, and production documents should reference this document before defining environmental conditions.

---

# Philosophy

The village changes naturally throughout the year.

The changes are gradual rather than dramatic.

Trees bloom.

Fields grow.

Rain transforms the landscape.

Cooking adapts to the weather.

Birds behave differently.

People change their daily routines.

The audience should feel the passing of the seasons without needing to be told.

---

# Annual Seasonal Cycle

The Village Stories universe permanently follows this cycle.

```text
Early Summer

↓

Peak Summer

↓

Southwest Monsoon

↓

Post-Monsoon

↓

Harvest Season

↓

Cool Season

↓

Back to Early Summer
```

This cycle repeats every year.

# Early Summer

## General Feeling

The village begins to warm after the cool season.

The land remains green.

Vegetation is healthy.

The atmosphere feels fresh and comfortable.

* Bright mornings.
* Warm afternoons.
* Clear skies.
* Soft golden evenings.

## Weather

* Pleasant mornings.

## Landscape

* Healthy gardens.

# Peak Summer

## General Feeling

The hottest period of the year.

Village activity slows during midday.

Morning and evening become the busiest times.

---

## Lighting

Maintain:

* Strong sunlight.
* Bright blue sky.
* Sharp shadows.

---

## Weather

Typical conditions:

* Hot afternoons.
* Dry air.
* Dust.
* Occasional wind.

---

## Landscape

Maintain:

* Dry pathways.
* Slightly faded grass.
* Lower water levels.
* Healthy trees providing shade.

---

## Agriculture

Typical activities:

* Irrigation.
* Early morning field work.
* Water conservation.

---

## Wildlife

Animals seek shade.

Bird activity reduces during midday.

---

## Cooking

Typical foods:

* Buttermilk.
* Fresh curd.
* Cooling drinks.
* Light meals.

---

* Lighting.
* Sky colour.
* Wind.
* Rain.
* Water levels.
* Crops.
* Trees.
* Flowers.
* Wildlife.
* Village routines.
* Cooking.

---

# Kitchen Influence

Season directly affects the Kitchen.

Examples:

Summer

* Brighter light.
* Less steam.

Monsoon

* Rain visible through the Garden Window.
* More steam.
* Fire feels warmer.

Post-Monsoon

* Fresh vegetables.
* Bright natural light.

The Kitchen should always reflect the current season.

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Correct seasonal lighting.
* Appropriate crops.
* Realistic weather.
* Seasonal wildlife.
* Seasonal recipes.
* Consistent vegetation.

Never:

* Mix conflicting seasonal characteristics.
* Ignore seasonal influence on village life.
* Change seasons abruptly without narrative reason.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the seasonal cycle of the Village Stories universe.

Every story, image prompt, animation, storyboard, and production document must follow these seasonal characteristics unless a specific canonical event states otherwise.

---

# Guiding Principle

The seasons are the village's heartbeat.

Without changing the people or the places, they quietly transform everything else—the light, the land, the food, the sounds, the weather, and the feeling of home.
