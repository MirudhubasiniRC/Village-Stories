# Festivals

## Purpose

This document defines the traditional festivals celebrated within the Village Stories universe.

It establishes how festivals influence village life, family activities, decorations, food, clothing, music, community gatherings, and the overall atmosphere.

Festivals should enrich the world naturally without changing its core identity.

---

# Philosophy

Festivals are moments when everyday village life becomes slightly more colourful, joyful, and communal.

They are celebrations of gratitude, faith, family, harvest, and tradition.

The village does not become extravagant.

Instead, familiar places become brighter through simple decorations, shared meals, and community participation.

---

# Festival Principles

Always maintain:

* Traditional South Indian celebrations.
* Community participation.
* Family gatherings.
* Handmade decorations.
* Traditional clothing.
* Home-cooked food.
* Temple-centered activities.

Avoid excessive modern decorations or commercial celebrations.

---

# Major Festivals

The Village Stories universe recognizes the following major celebrations.

* Pongal
* Deepavali
* Tamil New Year
* Vinayagar Chaturthi
* Navaratri
* Karthigai Deepam

Smaller local temple festivals may also occur.

---

# Pongal

## Theme

Harvest.

Gratitude.

Prosperity.

Family.

---

## Village Atmosphere

Maintain:

* Fresh kolam.
* Sugarcane bundles.
* Turmeric plants.
* Decorated cattle.
* Harvested fields.
* Community celebration.

---

## Family Activities

Typical activities:

* Cleaning the home.
* Preparing the courtyard.
* Cooking Sweet Pongal.
* Cooking Ven Pongal.
* Family gathering.
* Offering thanks.

---

## Kitchen

The Kitchen becomes especially active.

Maintain:

* Multiple dishes.
* Larger clay pots.
* Banana leaves.
* Traditional sweets.
* Fresh harvest produce.

---

# Deepavali

## Theme

Light.

Joy.

Family.

New beginnings.

---

## Village Atmosphere

Maintain:

* Oil lamps.
* Clean homes.
* New clothes.
* Families visiting one another.

Firecrackers should remain subtle and not dominate the atmosphere.

---

## Food

Typical dishes:

* Murukku.
* Laddu.
* Mysore Pak.
* Mixture.
* Payasam.

---

# Tamil New Year

Theme:

Fresh beginnings.

Maintain:

* Home cleaning.
* Mango leaves.
* Flowers.
* Traditional meals.
* Family prayers.

The day should feel peaceful rather than crowded.

---

# Vinayagar Chaturthi

Because the village already contains the Sacred Banyan Tree and Vinayagar Shrine, this festival has special significance.

Maintain:

* Flower decorations.
* Fresh kolam.
* Coconut offerings.
* Fruits.
* Modakam.
* Community prayers.

The Shrine becomes the visual centre of the celebration.

---

# Navaratri

Maintain:

* Traditional dolls.
* Flowers.
* Evening prayers.
* Visiting neighbours.
* Sundal preparation.

The atmosphere remains calm and devotional.

---

# Karthigai Deepam

Theme:

Light.

Maintain:

* Rows of oil lamps.
* Warm evening glow.
* Family gathering.
* Quiet celebration.

This festival creates one of the most beautiful nighttime environments in the series.

---

# Local Temple Festival

Occasionally the village celebrates a local temple festival.

Possible activities:

* Procession.
* Traditional music.
* Community meal.
* Decorative arches.
* Flower garlands.

These events should remain modest and authentic.

---

# Decorations

Typical decorations include:

* Kolam.
* Mango leaf garlands.
* Banana stems.
* Jasmine flowers.
* Marigold garlands.
* Oil lamps.
* Fresh flowers.

Plastic decorations should be avoided.

---

# Clothing

Festival clothing includes:

Men

* White veshti.
* Clean cotton shirt.

Women

* Traditional sarees.
* Fresh flowers in hair.
* Simple jewellery.

Children

* Traditional festive clothing.
* Bright colours.

---

# Food

Festivals naturally feature:

* Sweet Pongal.
* Ven Pongal.
* Payasam.
* Vadai.
* Murukku.
* Appam.
* Adhirasam.
* Kozhukattai.
* Banana leaf meals.

Recipes should reflect the specific festival.

---

# Community Behaviour

Festivals encourage:

* Visiting neighbours.
* Sharing sweets.
* Helping elders.
* Temple gatherings.
* Preparing food together.
* Children playing outdoors.

The village feels especially united.

---

# Nature During Festivals

Natural elements remain important.

Examples:

* Flowering plants.
* Banana leaves.
* Fresh mango leaves.
* Morning sunlight.
* Evening oil lamps.
* Gentle breeze.

Nature continues to frame the celebration.

---

# Audio Identity

Typical sounds:

* Temple bell.
* Traditional devotional songs.
* Children laughing.
* Birds.
* Cooking.
* Quiet conversations.

Festivals should remain peaceful rather than noisy.

# Camera Opportunities

Recurring compositions:

* Oil lamps after sunset.
* Flower garlands.

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Traditional celebrations.
* Handmade decorations.
* Community participation.
* Seasonal appropriateness.
* Respectful religious practices.

Never:

* Introduce modern commercial festival decorations.
* Overcrowd scenes.
* Turn festivals into spectacle.
* Contradict established village culture.
