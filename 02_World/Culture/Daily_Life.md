# Daily Life

## Purpose

This document defines the everyday rhythm of life in the Village Stories universe.

It describes the ordinary activities performed by families, neighbours, farmers, children, elders, and village workers throughout the day.

These routines create the feeling that the village continues living even when the story focuses on only one family.

Every story, image prompt, storyboard, and animation should naturally reflect these daily patterns.

---

# Philosophy

Village life is built around routine.

People rarely hurry.

Most activities follow habits developed over generations.

Simple tasks—sweeping the yard, drawing water, cooking meals, tending gardens, feeding animals, greeting neighbours—form the heart of everyday life.

The beauty of the village comes from these repeated rhythms.

---

# Daily Rhythm

Every day generally follows this progression.

```text
Pre-Dawn

↓

Morning Preparation

↓

Work & School

↓

Midday Meal

↓

Afternoon Chores

↓

Evening Gathering

↓

Night Rest
```

Although individual stories may focus on one activity, the wider village should continue following this rhythm.

---

# Pre-Dawn

Typical activities:

* Oil lamps lit in some homes.
* Early farmers waking.
* Quiet prayer.
* Preparing the first fire in the Kitchen.
* Fetching fresh water if needed.

The village remains peaceful.

---

# Early Morning

The village gradually comes alive.

Common activities:

* Sweeping front yards.
* Drawing kolam.
* Watering plants.
* Feeding cows, goats, and chickens.
* Collecting vegetables.
* Preparing breakfast.
* Children getting ready for school.
* Farmers preparing tools.
* Neighbours greeting one another.

Birdsong fills the village.

---

# Morning

The busiest working period.

Typical activities:

* Farming.
* Gardening.
* Cooking lunch.
* Washing clothes.
* Drying grains.
* Grinding spices.
* Carrying produce.
* Village shop opening.
* School lessons.

Most adults remain occupied.

---

# Midday

The pace slows.

Typical activities:

* Lunch preparation.
* Family meal.
* Short rest.
* Animals resting in shade.
* Quiet household work.

The hottest hours encourage slower movement.

---

# Afternoon

Activity gradually increases again.

Typical activities:

* Gardening.
* Harvesting vegetables.
* Collecting firewood.
* Cleaning tools.
* Preparing evening meal.
* Children returning from school.
* Homework.
* Light household repairs.

The atmosphere feels relaxed.

---

# Evening

The social heart of the village.

Typical activities:

* Tea preparation.
* Neighbours chatting.
* Temple bell.
* Livestock returning.
* Children playing.
* Family members gathering.
* Dinner preparation.
* Quiet walks through the village.

Warm light and gentle conversation define this time.

---

# Night

The village becomes peaceful.

Typical activities:

* Dinner.
* Storytelling.
* Prayer.
* Cleaning the Kitchen.
* Preparing for the next day.
* Sleep.

Only soft natural sounds remain.

---

# Family Life

Most households follow similar routines.

Maintain:

* Shared meals.
* Cooperative cooking.
* Household chores divided naturally.
* Respect for elders.
* Children learning through observation.

Daily life should feel collaborative.

---

# Neighbour Relationships

Neighbours naturally interact throughout the day.

Examples:

* Borrowing ingredients.
* Sharing vegetables.
* Exchanging recipes.
* Offering help during harvest.
* Casual conversations.
* Visiting without formal invitation.

Relationships are warm and familiar.

---

# Children's Daily Life

Typical activities include:

* School.
* Homework.
* Outdoor games.
* Helping parents.
* Feeding animals.
* Collecting flowers.
* Watching cooking.
* Learning village traditions.

Children spend more time outdoors than indoors.

---

# Elderly Life

Older villagers often:

* Rest beneath trees.
* Tell stories.
* Watch children play.
* Offer advice.
* Prepare traditional remedies.
* Participate in temple activities.

Their presence adds continuity to village life.

---

# Farming Life

Agriculture shapes the village schedule.

Daily activities include:

* Field preparation.
* Irrigation.
* Harvesting.
* Transporting produce.
* Repairing tools.
* Caring for livestock.

The exact work changes with the seasons.

---

# Household Chores

Common chores include:

* Sweeping.
* Washing utensils.
* Washing clothes.
* Fetching water.
* Preparing food.
* Cleaning the home.
* Maintaining the garden.
* Drying spices.
* Folding clothes.

These tasks repeat naturally throughout the week.

---

# Food Culture

Meals are central to daily life.

Typical rhythm:

Morning

Breakfast.

Midday

Main family meal.

Evening

Dinner prepared slowly.

Tea or coffee naturally connects different parts of the day.

---

# Nature's Presence

Everyday life remains closely connected to nature.

Examples:

* Watching approaching rain.
* Picking fresh curry leaves.
* Listening to birds.
* Resting beneath trees.
* Collecting flowers.
* Walking through gardens.

Nature is part of daily routine rather than a separate activity.

---

# Social Behaviour

Village life encourages:

* Hospitality.
* Patience.
* Cooperation.
* Respect.
* Simplicity.
* Gratitude.

Arguments and conflict are uncommon in ordinary daily life.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Calm daily rhythm.
* Seasonal influence.
* Family cooperation.
* Strong neighbour relationships.
* Respect for traditional routines.

Never:

* Introduce rushed urban lifestyles.
* Ignore household responsibilities.
* Depict the village as inactive during the day.
* Break established cultural behaviour without narrative reason.

# Guiding Principle

The true beauty of village life is found in ordinary moments repeated with care.

A meal cooked together, a neighbour stopping to chat, children playing beneath a banyan tree, or birds greeting the sunrise may seem simple, yet these everyday rhythms create the timeless feeling that defines Village Stories.
