# Food Repertoire And Audience Bridge

## Purpose

Village Stories is set in **early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu**, but the channel
serves viewers from **South India, North India, other Indian states, and the
USA/diaspora**.

This document locks how we keep the **home and village visually Tamil-authentic**
while allowing **South + North dishes** that broaden discovery without turning
the world into a generic “pan-India AI village.”

Authority: `CANON-2026-004`.

---

## Core Rule

| Layer | Lock |
| --- | --- |
| **Place / architecture / clothes / language of world** | Early-1990s rural **Tamil Nadu** — thinnai, Mangalore tiles, clay stove, ammikkal, lived-in lock |
| **Dishes Mother may cook** | **South + North** home foods that a Tamil village family of the era could plausibly make or learn |
| **Titles / SEO** | May use familiar North/search names (**kheer, roti, samosa, dal**) when the on-screen dish matches |
| **What we never do** | Relocate the house to Punjab/UP aesthetics; default tandoor courtyard; Hindi-village set dressing as the permanent world |

**Vibe bridge (what both audiences feel):** clay-stove fire, brass vessels, milk
boil, tadka crackle, monsoon/rain, family care, kids tasting, Dog under shelter —
not “North set” vs “South set.”

---

## South dishes (home base)

Natural defaults for the Kitchen:

- Idli, dosa, kuzhi paniyaram, appam (when story fits)
- Sambar, rasam, poriyal, kootu
- Coconut chutney (ammikkal), gunpowder / podi
- Filter coffee, buttermilk
- Rice payasam / pal payasam, sweet pongal
- Tamarind rice, lemon rice, curd rice
- Banana-leaf serving when story justifies

---

## North / shared dishes (approved for this kitchen)

Allowed when the episode promise is that dish. Cooked on the **same clay stove /
Tamil kitchen tools** unless a story-justified temporary setup is documented.

| Dish (search-friendly name) | Village Stories on-screen truth |
| --- | --- |
| Rice kheer | Rice payasam / pal payasam in milk + jaggery or sugar + cardamom |
| Poori / puri | Deep-fried wheat poori Mother learned / makes for special mornings |
| Aloo sabzi | Simple potato curry as side for poori |
| Samosa | Home samosa on clay stove / kadai — festival or rainy snack |
| Dal tadka | Soft dal with mustard/jeera-style tadka in this kitchen’s vessels |
| Roti / phulka | Occasional tawa roti when story needs North comfort (not every episode) |
| Halwa | Rava / carrot / wheat-style sweet on special days |
| Chai | Masala or plain tea — already village-natural |

**Use carefully / rarely:**

- Paneer dishes — only if milk/paneer source is story-clear; not weekly default.
- Full tandoor naan — do **not** build a permanent tandoor into the Family House
  without a separate canon decision. Prefer tawa/kadai methods.

---

## How “North + South vibes” appear on screen

**Do:**

- Keep Tamil house, garden, thinnai, clothing, Mother braid + mallipoo.
- Let the **food** carry North familiarity (kheer pour, poori puff, samosa fry).
- Use English + familiar dish names in titles for multi-state / USA discovery.
- Mix episodes across weeks: one South staple, one shared/North comfort sweet or
  snack — variety without relocating the village.

**Do not:**

- Redesign the house as a North courtyard set for one kheer video.
- Add Devanagari shopboards, snow, desert, or Punjabi village dress as default.
- Claim the family “moved to North India.”
- Fake petromax powercut + matar paneer clones as the only format.

---

## Episode planning rule

Before locking a dish for an episode, confirm:

1. Specific food promise in the opening.
2. Relationship need (promise, guest, festival, tired Father, kids’ ask).
3. Macro ASMR steps that belong to that dish.
4. Tamil kitchen continuity (clay stove, brass/steel, ammikkal when grinding).
5. Title can say the search name; story stays in this world.

## Guiding Principle

The **village stays Tamil**. The **menu can travel**.  
Viewers from North and South should both feel at home in the sounds of the
stove — without the architecture lying.
