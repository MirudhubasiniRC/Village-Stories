# Occupations

## Purpose

This document defines the traditional occupations found within the Village Stories universe.

It establishes the livelihoods, skills, tools, daily routines, and social roles of the villagers.

These occupations help create a believable rural economy and provide authentic background activity for stories.

---

# Philosophy

The village is largely self-sustaining.

Most occupations are closely connected to the land, traditional craftsmanship, or services supporting everyday life.

Work is respected regardless of profession.

Occupations are not merely jobs—they are part of the village's cultural identity.

---

# Occupational Principles

Maintain:

* Traditional skills.
* Community cooperation.
* Honest work.
* Practical knowledge.
* Respect for craftsmanship.
* Seasonal influence.

Avoid modern urban professions unless introduced through a specific story.

---

# Agriculture

## Farmers

The most common occupation in the village.

Typical responsibilities:

* Preparing fields.
* Planting crops.
* Irrigation.
* Harvesting.
* Caring for livestock.
* Maintaining farm equipment.

Common crops include:

* Paddy
* Groundnut
* Finger millet
* Sesame
* Vegetables

Farming activities follow the Agricultural Calendar.

---

# Dairy Farmers

Responsibilities:

* Milking cows.
* Caring for cattle.
* Preparing fodder.
* Delivering milk.

Morning and evening are their busiest periods.

---

# Vegetable Growers

Maintain home gardens and small farms.

Typical produce:

* Tomatoes.
* Brinjal.
* Okra.
* Drumsticks.
* Greens.
* Chillies.

Fresh vegetables are often shared with neighbours.

---

# Traditional Crafts

## Potter

Produces:

* Clay pots.
* Water vessels.
* Cooking pots.
* Lamps.
* Storage jars.

Work increases before festivals.

---

## Carpenter

Builds and repairs:

* Doors.
* Windows.
* Furniture.
* Farming tools.
* Kitchen equipment.

Woodworking is done using traditional hand tools.

---

## Blacksmith

Produces and repairs:

* Farming implements.
* Knives.
* Aruvamanai blades.
* Iron cookware repairs.

The blacksmith plays an important support role in village life.

---

## Weaver

Produces:

* Cotton cloth.
* Towels.
* Traditional household textiles.

May also repair woven items.

---

## Tea Stall Owner

Operates the roadside Tea Stall.

Location:

* Tea Stall (North-East, near Entrance / Bus Stop corridor)

Typical work:

* Preparing tea and filter coffee.
* Serving simple snacks.
* Maintaining the stall stove and glasses.
* Welcoming regular customers.

The Tea Stall is a daily social pause for farmers, elders, and travellers.

# Temple Service

Temple caretakers may:

* Maintain the shrine.
* Prepare lamps.
* Arrange flowers.
* Assist during festivals.

---

# Household Work

Many household responsibilities include:

* Cooking.
* Cleaning.
* Gardening.
* Food preservation.
* Childcare.
* Caring for elders.

These tasks are essential to village life and should be portrayed with equal importance.

---

# Seasonal Occupations

Certain activities increase during specific seasons.

Examples:

Harvest Season

* Grain collection.
* Transporting produce.
* Drying crops.

Monsoon

* Irrigation management.
* Tool repair.
* Indoor crafts.

Festival Season

* Flower preparation.
* Pottery.
* Decorative work.

Occupational activity should reflect the active season.

---

# Community Cooperation

Villagers naturally assist one another.

Examples:

* Sharing farm equipment.
* Helping during harvest.
* Lending tools.
* Repairing homes.
* Carrying heavy loads.

Community support is a defining characteristic of the village.

---

# Daily Rhythm

Most occupations follow:

Morning

Preparation and active work.

Midday

Meal and rest.

Afternoon

Continuation of work.

Evening

Return home, family time, and preparation for the next day.

This rhythm aligns with the broader village schedule.

---

# Storytelling Opportunities

Occupations naturally support stories involving:

* Harvests.
* Building and repairs.
* Preparing for festivals.
* Teaching children.
* Delivering letters.
* Sharing produce.
* Helping neighbours.

Work should often become a quiet background element rather than the main focus.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Traditional occupations.
* Seasonal work patterns.
* Respectful portrayal of labour.
* Community cooperation.

Never:

* Introduce unrealistic industries.
* Replace traditional occupations with modern equivalents without narrative justification.
* Depict work as disconnected from the village environment.

# Guiding Principle

The occupations of the village are more than ways of earning a living.

They represent generations of knowledge, craftsmanship, and cooperation, quietly sustaining the community while preserving the traditions that define Village Stories.
