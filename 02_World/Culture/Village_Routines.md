# Village Routines

## Purpose

This document defines the collective rhythm of the village throughout the day.

Rather than focusing on individual families, it describes how the entire village naturally changes as people begin work, children leave for school, farmers return home, livestock move through the streets, and the community gradually becomes active or quiet.

These routines provide a living background for every story.

---

# Philosophy

The village is always alive.

Even when the story follows only one family, the wider community continues its daily rhythm.

Smoke rises from distant kitchens.

Children walk to school.

Farmers work in the fields.

Birds fill the morning air.

Neighbours greet each other.

The audience should always feel that life continues beyond the camera.

---

# Daily Village Cycle

The village follows a predictable daily rhythm.

```text id="d7tbvo"
Pre-Dawn

↓

Sunrise

↓

Morning Activity

↓

Midday Quiet

↓

Afternoon Activity

↓

Evening Gathering

↓

Night Rest
```

This pattern repeats every day unless interrupted by weather, festivals, or special events.

---

# Pre-Dawn

Village Character:

Quiet.

Peaceful.

Sleeping.

Typical observations:

* A few oil lamps glowing.
* Early farmers waking.
* Temple area calm.
* Light breeze.
* Crickets.
* Dogs resting.

Movement is minimal.

---

# Sunrise

Village Character:

The village begins to awaken.

Typical observations:

* Roosters calling.
* Birdsong increasing.
* Front yards swept.
* Kolam being drawn.
* Smoke rising from kitchens.
* Water being drawn from wells.
* Temple bell.

The transition should feel gradual.

---

# Morning Activity

Village Character:

The busiest part of the day.

Typical observations:

* Farmers walking to fields.
* Children leaving for school.
* Women preparing meals.
* Livestock moving.
* Village shop opening.
* Neighbours greeting one another.
* Bicycles occasionally passing.
* Vegetable baskets being carried.

The village feels energetic but never rushed.

---

# Midday Quiet

Village Character:

The village slows naturally.

Typical observations:

* Reduced movement.
* Families eating lunch.
* Animals resting in shade.
* Empty pathways.
* Gentle breeze.
* Distant farming activity.

This is the calmest daytime period.

---

# Afternoon Activity

Village Character:

Activity slowly returns.

Typical observations:

* Children returning from school.
* Garden work.
* Washing clothes.
* Preparing evening meals.
* Livestock grazing.
* Village conversations increasing.

The pace remains relaxed.

---

# Evening Gathering

Village Character:

The social heart of the village.

Typical observations:

* Tea preparation.
* Temple bell.
* Families outdoors.
* Children playing.
* Farmers returning.
* Cows walking home.
* Neighbours chatting.
* Warm cooking aromas.

The village feels welcoming.

---

# Night Rest

Village Character:

Quiet.

Comfortable.

Safe.

Typical observations:

* Oil lamps.
* Families indoors.
* Crickets.
* Occasional dog bark.
* Soft wind.
* Moonlight.

Movement becomes minimal.

---

# Weekly Rhythm

Although each day follows a similar pattern, some weekly variations naturally occur.

Examples:

* Weekly market visits.
* Washing larger household items.
* Garden maintenance.
* Community cleaning.
* Temple gatherings.

These should appear occasionally rather than daily.

---

# Seasonal Influence

The village rhythm changes with the seasons.

Summer

* Earlier work.
* Longer midday rest.
* Evening activity increases.

Monsoon

* Outdoor work depends on rainfall.
* Indoor cooking increases.
* Fewer children playing outside during heavy rain.

Harvest Season

* Increased field activity.
* More community cooperation.
* Larger gatherings.

Cool Season

* Comfortable outdoor activity.
* Longer evening conversations.

# Background Activity

When the camera focuses on one location, background activity may naturally include:

* A neighbour walking past.
* Chickens crossing the road.
* Children carrying school bags.
* Someone cycling through the village.
* Farmers transporting vegetables.
* Smoke rising from distant houses.
* Cows grazing.
* Women carrying water pots.

These moments should feel incidental rather than staged.

---

# Community Behaviour

Villagers naturally:

* Greet one another.
* Offer assistance.
* Share seasonal produce.
* Exchange conversations.
* Respect elders.
* Watch over children.
* Care for community spaces.

The village functions as a close-knit community.

---

# Environmental Continuity

Village routines should always reflect:

* Time of day.
* Active season.
* Current weather.
* Agricultural calendar.

Background activity should never contradict these conditions.

---

# Storytelling Influence

Village routines help create:

* Realistic establishing shots.
* Believable background activity.
* Seasonal atmosphere.
* Social authenticity.
* Smooth scene transitions.

The audience should feel the village continues living beyond the immediate story.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Gradual daily transitions.
* Consistent community behaviour.
* Seasonal influence.
* Appropriate background activity.
* Calm village pace.

Never:

* Present the village as empty without narrative reason.
* Introduce busy urban behaviour.
* Contradict the established daily rhythm.

# Guiding Principle

A living village is never silent.

Even when the story follows one family, countless ordinary moments continue around them—smoke rising from distant kitchens, children laughing in the lanes, cows returning home at sunset, and neighbours sharing quiet conversations. These unseen routines give the Village Stories universe its sense of life and continuity.
