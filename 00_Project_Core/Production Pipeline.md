# PRODUCTION_PIPELINE.md

# Village Stories Production Pipeline

## Purpose

This document defines the standard production workflow for every Village Stories episode.

Every episode should follow this pipeline unless an intentional exception is documented.

The objective is to produce consistent, high-quality episodes while preserving continuity, historical authenticity, and production efficiency.

---

# Production Philosophy

Do not create episodes by improvising.

Instead:

Research → Plan → Verify → Produce → Review → Publish

Every stage should build upon the previous one.

---
# Phase 0 — Inspiration Library

## Objective

Continuously collect authentic ideas, memories, observations, and research before they are needed.

The Inspiration Library is the creative fuel for the entire Village Stories universe. It should be updated regularly, even when no episode is currently being produced.

Never begin story development from an empty page if inspiration already exists in the library.

---

## Sources of Inspiration

### Real Village Life

* Childhood memories
* Family experiences
* Elders' stories
* Everyday village routines
* Local traditions
* Seasonal activities

---

### Historical Research

Collect forgotten aspects of 1990s South Indian village life.

Examples:

* The village postman delivering inland letters and money orders
* Cycle repair shops
* Bullock cart transport
* Public telephone (STD/ISD) booths
* Radio news broadcasts
* Kerosene ration shops
* Weekly village markets
* Traditional farming methods
* Handmade household tools

---

### Nature

Observe and document:

* Rain patterns
* River flow
* Paddy growth stages
* Birds
* Butterflies
* Dragonflies
* Coconut trees
* Village sounds
* Seasonal flowers

---

### Human Emotions

Capture simple emotional moments such as:

* Helping a neighbour
* Waiting for someone
* Caring for a sick family member
* Cooking together
* Watching the first rain
* Sharing food
* Childhood curiosity
* Quiet acts of kindness

---

### Everyday Details

Collect the small moments that make the village feel alive.

Examples:

* Clothes drying on a line
* Oil lamp being lit at dusk
* Wet slippers outside the house
* Steam rising from freshly cooked rice
* Bicycle bells
* Temple bells
* River conversations
* Children chasing dragonflies
* A dog sleeping on the thinnai
* Smoke drifting from a clay stove

---

### Visual References

Store inspiration such as:

* Old family photographs
* Traditional architecture
* Village landscapes
* Clothing references
* Farming tools
* Historical advertisements
* Old calendars
* Vintage packaging

---

### Story Seeds

Every interesting idea should be saved, even if it is only one sentence.

Examples:

* The postman brings unexpected news.
* Children race paper boats during the first monsoon rain.
* Father teaches his son to swim at the river ghat.
* Mother prepares food for a neighbour after a difficult harvest.
* The village gathers beneath the banyan tree before a festival.

Do not judge ideas when collecting them. Evaluation happens later during story development.

---

## Rule

The Inspiration Library is never complete.

Every new observation, memory, research finding, historical detail, or emotional moment should be added so the creative library becomes richer over time.

A larger Inspiration Library leads to more authentic stories and a more believable village.


# Phase 1 — Story Development

### Objective

Create a meaningful story before thinking about visuals.

### Steps

* Select an emotional theme.
* Determine the season.
* Choose the primary location(s).
* Decide which characters appear.
* Define the beginning.
* Define the emotional ending.

Output:

* Story Outline
* Episode Goal

---

# Phase 2 — Canon Verification

Before writing any prompts:

Verify:

* Character continuity
* Location continuity
* Timeline continuity
* Seasonal continuity
* Historical accuracy

If conflicts exist:

Resolve them before continuing.

---

# Phase 3 — Research

Consult only the documents needed.

Examples:

Need a postman?

→ Research → Communication

Need Pongal?

→ Culture → Festivals

Need monsoon farming?

→ Research → Agriculture

Need river scene?

→ Locations → River Ghat

Never rely on memory when documentation exists.

---

# Phase 4 — Storyboard

Break the story into cinematic shots.

Each shot should have:

* Purpose
* Camera size
* Camera movement
* Characters
* Location
* Emotional objective

Output:

Complete storyboard.

---

# Phase 5 — Image Prompt Creation

Generate production-ready image prompts.

Each prompt must respect:

* Canon
* World Layout
* Character Bible
* Location Bible
* Lighting
* Season
* Camera rules

---

# Phase 6 — Image Generation

Generate images.

Review each image for:

* Character accuracy
* Lighting consistency
* Environment continuity
* Object placement
* Historical authenticity

Regenerate only if necessary.

---

# Phase 7 — Animation Prompt Creation

For every image:

Create animation instructions.

Maintain:

* Exact continuity
* Natural movement
* Slow pacing
* Camera consistency

---

# Phase 8 — Animation Production

Animate each shot.

Verify:

* Motion quality
* Continuity
* Timing
* Camera movement

---

# Phase 9 — Audio Production

Produce:

* ASMR
* Ambient sounds
* Foley
* Voice-over (if required)

Audio should support the emotion rather than dominate it.

---

# Phase 10 — Editing

Assemble the episode.

Check:

* Shot rhythm
* Story pacing
* Emotional flow
* Audio balance
* Transitions

---

# Phase 11 — Quality Assurance

Before publishing:

Complete every checklist.

Verify:

* Canon
* Continuity
* Character consistency
* Historical accuracy
* Visual quality
* Audio quality

Nothing should be published without QA.

---

# Phase 12 — Publishing

Prepare:

* Thumbnail
* Title
* Description
* Tags
* Upload schedule

Publish.

---

# Phase 13 — Episode Archive

After publishing:

Store:

* Story
* Storyboard
* Prompts
* Final assets
* Thumbnail
* SEO
* Lessons learned

Every episode becomes part of the permanent production archive.

---

# Continuous Improvement

After each episode, ask:

* What worked?
* What felt authentic?
* What can improve?
* What new world detail was introduced?
* Should the Canon or Research Library be updated?

Every episode should improve both the series and the production system itself.

---

# Guiding Principle

The production pipeline exists to protect consistency.

The goal is not simply to produce more episodes.

The goal is to produce episodes that feel as though they all belong to the same living world, no matter when they are created.
