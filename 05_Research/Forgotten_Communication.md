# Forgotten Communication

## Purpose

This document defines practical, early-1990s village communication habits.

## Everyday Communication

* A visitor calls from the gate or path rather than sending a message.
* News travels through the tea stall, school gate, grocery store, shrine, and neighbours.
* Children carry short verbal messages only when safe and appropriate.
* Handwritten notes may travel through a schoolchild, bicycle rider, or known visitor.
* The postman brings letters, postcards, and money orders on a regular route; a delivery can be a meaningful but ordinary event.
* Announcements may be made in person or through a modest festival loudspeaker.

## Avoid

* Mobile phones, messaging apps, social-media behaviour, or instant communication expectations.
* Modern public-address systems presented as everyday permanent infrastructure.

## Story Use

Delayed information should create gentle human scenes—someone walking over, waiting at the thinnai, or asking a neighbour—not artificial suspense.
