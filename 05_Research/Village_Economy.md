# Village Economy

## Purpose

This reference supports believable everyday exchange without reducing village life to transactions.

## Everyday Patterns

* Families buy small quantities from the Grocery Store as needed.
* Farm income changes with planting, harvest, rainfall, and crop condition.
* Tools are repaired before replacement.
* Tea-stall purchases are small, familiar, and often part of conversation.
* Barter, mutual help, borrowing, and returning favours may occur naturally between trusted neighbours.

## Story Rule

Money concerns can create modest practical stakes—saving for school needs, repairing a tool, sharing after harvest—but never exploit hardship for spectacle.

## Avoid

Modern banking, digital payment, supermarkets, branded consumer culture, and unrealistic abundance of purchased goods.
