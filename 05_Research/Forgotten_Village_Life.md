# Forgotten Village Life

## Purpose

This document collects small, repeatable early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu details that can enrich Village Stories scenes without distracting from the characters.

Use only details compatible with established canon.

---

# Morning Details

* A swept earth courtyard before the day's work begins.
* Fresh kolam at entrances where the location and family routine establish it.
* Metal vessels being rinsed and set out to dry.
* Cows, goats, chickens, and the Family Dog responding to household movement.
* A bicycle bell or cart wheel sound arriving from the village path.
* Steam, woodsmoke, and the first cooking sounds from kitchens.

---

# Household Habits

* Cotton towels and shoulder cloths drying in sunlight.
* Brass and steel vessels cleaned after use and returned to their logical shelf.
* Grain, lentils, and spices kept in practical containers.
* Small household repairs made before an object is replaced.
* Visitors welcomed first onto the thinnai or a shaded sitting place.

---

# Work and Travel

* Farming tools carried by hand or in baskets rather than treated as background props.
* Bullock carts used for hay, harvest, and local practical transport.
* Bicycles leaned against trees, walls, and verandas, without formal parking racks.
* People travelling by foot along familiar paths, often greeting those they pass.
* Tradespeople repairing and reusing tools whenever practical.

---

# Evening Details

* Cooler light changing the colour of packed earth, tile, and field water.
* Cattle being settled and tools returned to storage.
* Lamps, lanterns, or limited electric light used according to location context.
* Conversation moving toward the thinnai, tea stall, or courtyard.
* Insects, distant birds, bicycle bells, and quiet household sounds replacing daytime work noise.

---

# Avoid

* Modern plastic-dominant households.
* Smartphones, LED lighting, contemporary vehicles, or branded consumer imagery.
* Overcrowding every scene with “village” props.
* Treating poverty, tradition, or animals as visual spectacle.

---

# Continuity Rule

Every researched detail must have a practical owner, location, time, and reason to appear.

---

# Guiding Principle

The village becomes believable through small useful actions, not through a collection of decorative nostalgia.
