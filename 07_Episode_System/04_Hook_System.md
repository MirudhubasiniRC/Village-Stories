# Hook System

## First Seconds

Begin with a visible change, question, or meaningful action—not a generic introduction.

Examples:

* Mother notices an empty spice jar while cooking.
* Father finds an old object that brings back a memory.
* The Family Dog waits at the gate for the postman.
* Heavy rain begins while work is unfinished.
* Elder Brother and Younger Daughter both want the same small thing.
* A neighbour arrives with an unexpected need.
* A rare flower blooms in the garden.

## Next-Short Hook

End a Short with an honest unresolved thread:

* “Tomorrow they will try the repaired tool.”
* A letter remains unopened until the family gathers.
* The siblings still need to make peace.
* Mother has found a clue to Father's old memory.

Never imply danger that the next episode cannot justify.
