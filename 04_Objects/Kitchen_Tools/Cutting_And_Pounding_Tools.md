# Cutting and Pounding Tools

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Aruvamanai, Ural & Ulakkai, and Stone Rolling Pin used in the Family Kitchen.

---

# Aruvamanai — Traditional Floor Cutter

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Low, stable wooden base.
* Upright curved iron blade fixed at one end.
* Hand-made, slightly worn wood surface.
* Clean sharpened blade with natural darkened metal character.
* No plastic, modern chopping-board styling, or counter-mounted cutter.

## Permanent Location

Stored beneath the Kitchen preparation table; brought out for use and returned after cleaning.

## Use and Safety

Used seated on the floor for vegetables, greens, coconut pieces, and other preparation as established by Kitchen canon.

The cutter remains stable. Children do not use it without direct adult guidance.

## Audio Identity

* Vegetable tap against blade.
* Wood base contact.
* Small basket movement.

---

# Ural & Ulakkai — Mortar and Pestle

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Heavy stone or dense hardwood mortar, squat and deep.
* Long matching pestle, hand-worn and balanced.
* Natural matte material surface.
* No polished decorative carved form.

## Use

Pounding pepper, garlic, dry spices, and rice-related ingredients in small quantities.

## Handling

The pestle is lifted and lowered with controlled rhythm. It must never appear oversized or dangerously swung.

## Audio Identity

* Deep rhythmic thud.
* Spice crush.
* Stone or wood resonance.

---

# Stone Rolling Pin

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Short, dense smooth stone roller.
* Compact stone board or stable rolling surface when in use.
* Dark neutral stone colour, subtly worn.

## Use

Flattening dough or spice mixtures when a recipe requires it.

It is a secondary tool, never a substitute for the grinding stones.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Aruvamanai as a floor-level upright-blade cutter.
* Ural & Ulakkai as a heavy matched mortar-and-pestle pair.
* Rolling pin as stone, not modern plastic or polished metal.

Never replace these with modern knives, food processors, or industrial tools unless a future canon document specifically introduces them.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Family Kitchen's cutting and pounding tools.

---

# Guiding Principle

These tools should communicate careful physical cooking: cut with respect, pound with rhythm, and prepare ingredients by hand.
