# Cooking Vessels

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Family Kitchen's cooking vessels.

They are distinct from hand tools and remain compatible with the Raised Clay Stove's three-burner system.

---

# Clay Pots

Maintain:

* Terracotta curry pots in varied stable sizes.
* Rice pot, buttermilk pot, fermentation pot, and pickle/storage jars as defined by Kitchen Storage.
* Rounded hand-made forms, matte clay surface, natural heat-darkening only near use.
* No glazed decorative cookware or modern non-stick appearance.

Clay pots are handled carefully and stored grouped by size.

---

# Iron Kadai and Dosa Tawa

Maintain:

* Heavy black iron kadai with stable rounded bowl.
* Flat heavy iron dosa tawa.
* Dark seasoned surfaces, never glossy non-stick coating.
* Stored low in the Iron Cookware Area due to weight.

---

# Brass Vessels and Uruli

Maintain:

* Warm brass cooking and serving vessels.
* Heavy rounded uruli for recipes requiring a broad shallow vessel.
* Natural patina, cleaned and polished more carefully for festivals.

---

# Idli and Steaming Vessels

Maintain:

* Period-appropriate metal steaming vessel.
* Nested idli plates with simple round depressions.
* Stable stove-compatible form.
* No electric steamer or plastic insert.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always distinguish clay, iron, and brass by material, weight, colour, and cooking use.

Never replace the permanent vessel set with aluminium-coated modern cookware, non-stick sets, or electric appliances.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Family Kitchen cooking vessels.

---

# Guiding Principle

Cooking vessels should make the stove feel alive: clay holding slow heat, iron taking direct flame, and brass marking care and celebration.
