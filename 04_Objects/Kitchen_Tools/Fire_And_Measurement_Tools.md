# Fire and Measurement Tools

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Iron Tongs, Traditional Measuring Cups, and Clay and Brass Serving Ladles used in the Family Kitchen.

---

# Iron Tongs

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Simple forged iron tongs.
* Long enough to safely manage hot lids, embers, or clay-pot adjustments.
* Dark iron surface with heat patina.
* No plastic grip, chrome kitchen-tool finish, or modern spring mechanism.

## Use

Used carefully near the Raised Clay Stove, never as an all-purpose utensil.

## Audio Identity

* Light metal tap.
* Iron contact with pot or stove edge.

---

# Traditional Measuring Cups

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Small traditional metal measuring cups in stable nested sizes.
* Brass, tinned metal, or other period-appropriate simple metal surface.
* Gently worn but clean.
* No modern printed metric plastic cups.

## Use

Measuring rice, lentils, spices, flour, and other ingredients by familiar household quantities.

They support Mother's practiced cooking, not laboratory-style precision.

---

# Clay Serving Ladles

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Hand-formed clay ladles for suitable serving or cooking tasks.
* Warm terracotta tone.
* Slight handmade irregularity.
* Carefully handled to avoid breakage.

---

# Brass Serving Ladles

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Warm aged brass tone.
* Long handle and rounded serving bowl.
* Simple functional form.
* Soft patina from use, polished lightly for special occasions when appropriate.

## Use

Serving food or ritual/festival dishes when a more formal traditional tool is natural.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Forged iron tongs.
* Small nested traditional metal measuring cups.
* Distinct clay and brass serving ladles.

Never merge them into generic stainless-steel modern utensils or introduce plastic measuring sets.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Family Kitchen's fire and measurement tools.

---

# Guiding Principle

These tools should reveal the Kitchen's quiet precision: heat handled safely, ingredients measured by memory, food served with care.
