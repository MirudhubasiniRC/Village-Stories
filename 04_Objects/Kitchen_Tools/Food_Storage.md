# Food Storage

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Family Kitchen food-storage objects.

The Kitchen Storage document establishes their zones; this file locks their physical materials, form, and continuity.

---

# Rice and Grain Containers

Maintain:

* Large lidded traditional metal or food-safe storage bins for rice.
* Smaller metal or clay containers for dals and grains.
* Clearly different sizes based on household quantity.
* Matte used surfaces, clean lids, no branded packaging walls.

Dry staples remain protected from heat, dampness, and pests.

---

# Pickle and Spice Containers

Maintain:

* Clay pickle jars with fitted lids where appropriate.
* Small metal tins or jars for frequently used spices.
* Traditional glass or metal containers for dry ingredients.
* No modern plastic organiser aesthetic.

Spice containers remain near the stove only when Kitchen Storage canon allows.

---

# Vegetable and Coconut Storage

Maintain:

* Bamboo onion baskets.
* Garlic bundles hung in a dry ventilated area.
* Coconut storage in a cool basket or shelf zone.
* Small hanging baskets for drying or safe temporary holding.

Objects must appear practical, breathable, and naturally varied by season.

---

# Tamarind, Jaggery, and Dry Goods

Maintain:

* Wrapped or lidded storage suitable to the ingredient.
* Simple reusable cloth, clay, glass, or metal containment.
* Clear separation from firewood and cleaning materials.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain traditional container logic: heavy staples low, dry ingredients protected, vegetables ventilated, spices reachable.

Never introduce modern plastic pantry containers, branded packaged-food dominance, or random storage placement.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Family Kitchen food-storage objects.

---

# Guiding Principle

Food storage should make the Kitchen feel prepared without looking staged: every ingredient has a place because every meal begins before the stove is lit.
