# Serving and Dining

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Family Kitchen serving and dining objects.

It defines recurring materials and forms; meal staging must continue to follow family, food, and location canon.

---

# Banana Leaves

Maintain:

* Fresh clean banana leaves for traditional serving occasions.
* Natural deep-green veins and hand-cut edges.
* Used for festivals, guests, special meals, and occasions where canon makes them natural.
* Never rendered as plastic leaf plates.

---

# Everyday Plates and Bowls

Maintain:

* Stainless-steel plates and small bowls for ordinary family meals.
* Practical, lightly used, clean reflective surfaces.
* Modest scale appropriate to family dining.

---

# Brass Plates and Serving Bowls

Maintain:

* Brass plates, bowls, and serving vessels for guests, rituals, or festival meals when appropriate.
* Warm brass tone and maintained patina.
* No ornate palace-like luxury styling.

---

# Clay Cups and Small Bowls

Maintain:

* Simple small clay cups or bowls when story context makes them natural.
* Terracotta matte texture.
* Hand-made variation without random changing shapes.

---

# Serving Spoons and Ladles

Maintain:

* Wooden, clay, brass, or period-appropriate metal serving tools according to dish and occasion.
* These remain distinct from the cooking tools defined elsewhere in Kitchen_Tools.

---

# Dining Behaviour Rules

Objects should appear in logical combinations:

* Everyday meal: steel plate/bowl system.
* Festival or guest meal: banana leaf and/or brass serving context.
* Traditional beverage or occasional serving: clay cup only when natural.

Never mix modern disposable plates, plastic cutlery, or restaurant-style table settings into the Family Kitchen.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Family Kitchen serving and dining objects.

---

# Guiding Principle

Serving objects should turn food into care: simple on ordinary days, fuller on special days, and always rooted in the family's real kitchen life.
