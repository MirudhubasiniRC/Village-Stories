# Water Storage and Serving

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Family Kitchen's water-storage and drinking-serving objects.

Storage placement remains governed by `02_World/Locations/Kitchen/Storage.md`; this document defines physical object identity.

---

# Brass Water Pot

Maintain:

* Rounded hand-hammered brass body.
* Narrow neck and slightly flared rim.
* Warm aged-gold tone, clean and lightly polished.
* Stable hand-made proportions.

Used for stored drinking water or special serving where appropriate. It belongs on the Brass Vessel Shelf when not in use.

---

# Copper Water Vessel

Maintain:

* Warm reddish copper tone.
* Rounded vessel with practical neck and rim.
* Natural age patina, carefully cleaned.
* Not mirror-polished or decorative luxuryware.

Use only where a copper vessel is naturally appropriate; it must remain distinct from brass in colour and material.

---

# Clay Water Pot

Maintain:

* Rounded terracotta matka-style form.
* Wide belly, smaller neck, simple clay lid where appropriate.
* Natural porous matte surface.
* Low resting stand or stable floor position.

It cools water naturally and never becomes glazed modern décor.

---

# Tumblers and Water Ladle

Maintain:

* Stainless-steel tumblers for everyday family use.
* Brass tumblers for traditional, guest, or special contexts.
* Small long-handled water ladle, brass or period-appropriate metal.
* No plastic drinking cups as a default kitchen identity.

---

# Water Stand

Maintain:

* Simple low wooden or metal stand for the clay pot or vessel.
* Practical, sturdy, unadorned construction.
* Logical placement near the Kitchen water-storage zone.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always distinguish brass, copper, clay, and steel by their material colour and use.

Never replace this system with plastic dispensers, water coolers, or modern bottled-water imagery.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Family Kitchen water-storage and serving objects.

---

# Guiding Principle

Water objects should communicate care, coolness, and the quiet hospitality of a village home.
