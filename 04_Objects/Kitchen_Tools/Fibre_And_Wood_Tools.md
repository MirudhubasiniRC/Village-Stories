# Fibre and Wood Tools

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Coconut Scraper, Muram, Bamboo Baskets, and Wooden Ladles used in the Family Kitchen.

---

# Coconut Scraper

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Low traditional seated scraper with sturdy wooden base.
* Small serrated metal scraping head.
* Practical hand-made construction.
* No modern electric coconut scraper.

## Use

Fresh coconut is held and rotated against the scraping head. Coconut shavings collect in a bowl beneath or beside the tool.

## Audio Identity

* Fine scrape.
* Coconut shell movement.
* Bowl receiving shavings.

---

# Muram — Winnowing Tray

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Broad shallow hand-woven bamboo tray.
* Oval or gently curved rectangular silhouette.
* Natural pale-to-honey bamboo tone.
* Visible woven strips and use-softened edges.

## Use

Sorting rice, lentils, spices, or grains. The tray is held, tilted, and gently shaken in controlled movements.

Never render it as plastic, metal, or a flat modern tray.

---

# Bamboo Baskets

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Hand-woven bamboo in several stable sizes.
* Rounded or oval utility forms.
* Natural fibre variation and hand-bound rims.
* No colourful plastic baskets.

## Use

Carrying vegetables, herbs, coconuts, grain, prepared ingredients, and dry storage items.

They may show temporary soil, leaves, or food traces, then return cleanly to storage.

---

# Wooden Ladles

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Dark or warm native hardwood.
* Long comfortable handles.
* Rounded shallow bowls shaped for clay-pot stirring.
* Hand-made variation, smooth from repeated use.

## Use

Stirring curries, rice, sambar, rasam, and other clay-pot cooking.

## Audio Identity

* Soft wood against clay pot.
* Gentle scrape around pot curve.

---

# Continuity Rules

All fibre and wood tools must remain hand-made, naturally textured, and practical.

Never replace bamboo with plastic or wooden ladles with shiny modern utensils as default.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Family Kitchen's fibre and wood tools.

---

# Guiding Principle

These objects should feel warm in the frame: woven fibre, hand-smoothed wood, and tools that belong in skilled hands.
