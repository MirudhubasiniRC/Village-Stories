# Grinding Stones

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Ammikkal and Aatukkal, the Family Kitchen's two traditional stone-grinding tools.

---

# Ammikkal — Flat Grinding Stone

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Low rectangular dark-grey granite base stone.
* Smooth slightly concave grinding surface from years of use.
* Separate rounded hand stone, heavy and palm-length.
* Naturally irregular hand-cut edges.
* No polished decorative finish.

## Permanent Location

South preparation wall beneath the Large Garden Window, as defined by Kitchen canon.

## Use

Grinding coconut, ginger, garlic, green chilli, chutneys, and fresh masala pastes.

The hand stone moves in a slow forward-and-back or circular pressure motion, never like a modern appliance.

## Audio Identity

* Wet stone scraping.
* Ingredient crush.
* Occasional water drops.

---

# Aatukkal — Round Grinding Stone

## Physical Identity

Maintain:

* Round or oval dark granite grinding base.
* Deeper bowl-like working surface than the Ammikkal.
* Heavy rounded roller stone shaped for wet mixtures.
* Larger scale and greater visual weight than the Ammikkal.
* Stone remains visibly hand-worked, not machine-perfect.

## Permanent Location

Beside the Ammikkal. The pair always appears together in the Kitchen's grinding zone.

## Use

Grinding idli/dosa batter, spice pastes, wet mixtures, and larger festival preparations.

## Audio Identity

* Heavier wet stone roll.
* Slow batter movement.
* Soft scrape and water sound.

---

# Material and Wear Rules

Both stones:

* Remain dark granite.
* Show small ingredient traces only while in use.
* May be wet after washing.
* Never become glossy marble, cement, polished black stone, or modern grinder equipment.

---

# Scale and Handling Rules

* Both are heavy, floor-level tools.
* Mother uses grounded, practiced body mechanics.
* Children only observe or help under direct supervision.
* They are never lifted casually or placed on high counters.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always preserve the Ammikkal's rectangular flat silhouette and the Aatukkal's deeper rounded silhouette.

Never merge them into one generic stone grinder or replace them with mixer-grinders.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Family Kitchen grinding stones.

---

# Guiding Principle

The grinding stones should make patience visible: ingredients changing through weight, rhythm, and practiced hands.
