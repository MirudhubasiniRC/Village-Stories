# Vehicles

## Purpose

This folder permanently defines recurring transport objects in Village Stories.

---

# Family Bullock Cart

The family owns one traditional wooden bullock cart, permanently stored beside Hay Storage in the Livestock Shelter.

Maintain:

* Wooden frame and bed.
* Two large wooden wheels with metal rims where appropriate.
* Natural wood grain, repairs, and weathering.
* Coir rope, wooden yoke, and practical harness context.
* No tractor-cart identity.

Use:

* Carrying hay.
* Moving harvest baskets.
* Transporting farming tools.
* Short practical village journeys.

Bullocks are handled gently, rested appropriately, and never used as spectacle.

---

# Bicycle

Maintain:

* Simple early-1990s road bicycle.
* Steel frame.
* Black or muted paint.
* Metal bell.
* Practical carrier rack where appropriate.
* No branded modern mountain-bike or sports-cycle styling.

Bicycles lean against walls, trees, verandas, and known gathering places; there are no formal bicycle racks.

---

# Continuity Rules

Vehicles must follow established routes, period technology, and practical use.

Never introduce cars, motorcycles, tractors, modern buses in village lanes, or random vehicle redesigns without future canon.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Village Stories vehicles.

---

# Guiding Principle

Transport should feel slow enough for the audience to learn the village geography: a creaking cart, a bicycle bell, and familiar paths.
