# Bicycle

The recurring village bicycle is a simple early-1990s steel road bicycle.

Maintain a black or muted-painted frame, slim tyres, a metal bell, practical carrier rack, and light natural wear. It is parked by leaning it against a tree, wall, veranda, or gathering place; never in a formal rack.

Its audible identity is the small bell, wheel rattle, and pedal-chain movement. It is practical transport for adults, teachers, visitors, and older children only where story context permits.

Never render it as a mountain bike, sports cycle, modern branded bicycle, or motorised vehicle.
