# Clothing Objects

## Purpose

This folder defines non-character-specific clothing objects and textile items recurring across Village Stories.

Individual character wardrobe authority remains in that character's folder. This folder defines shared material culture.

---

# Canonical Shared Items

Maintain:

* Cotton thundu / shoulder cloth.
* Cotton towels.
* Woven cloth bags.
* Simple laundry cloths.
* Festival jasmine garlands where appropriate.
* Child school cloth bags.
* Traditional flat sandals.
* Rain-cover cloths used practically in monsoon scenes.

---

# Material Language

* Cotton and handloom cotton dominate.
* Colours are earthy, faded, practical, or festival-rich without neon.
* Natural fibre texture, slight wrinkles, repeated washing, and careful mending are normal.
* No synthetic fashion, logos, branded sportswear, or modern fast-fashion styling.

---

# Continuity Rules

Clothing must react to season and work:

* Damp cloth edges in rain.
* Light dust after farm work.
* Fresh clothes for festivals.
* Clean practical garments for school and formal occasions.

Temporary marks disappear naturally after washing.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines shared clothing objects and textile culture.

---

# Guiding Principle

Textiles should feel touched by daily life: folded, washed, worn, mended, and chosen for purpose rather than fashion.
