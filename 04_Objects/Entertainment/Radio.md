# Radio

The village radio is a modest early-1990s portable or tabletop transistor radio, used sparingly in appropriate household or gathering contexts.

Maintain a compact dark plastic or wood-look body, simple tuning dial, grille, and occasional antenna. Its sound may carry faint news, music, cricket commentary, or static, but it never replaces the natural village soundscape.

It must not become a smartphone, cassette boombox, television, or modern speaker system.
