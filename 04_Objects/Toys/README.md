# Toys

## Purpose

This folder permanently defines recurring childhood play objects in Village Stories.

They must support early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu childhood, village geography, and age-appropriate play.

---

# Canonical Toys and Play Objects

Maintain:

* Clay marbles.
* Glass marbles in small cloth pouch.
* Simple cloth ball.
* Hand-made wooden toy cart or animal.
* Paper boats during safe monsoon scenes.
* Kite made from paper and bamboo frame.
* Kite string spool.
* Tamarind seeds, shells, or pebbles used for simple games.
* Chalk or stone marks for ground games.

---

# Material and Appearance

* Hand-made, repaired, shared, and modest.
* Paper, bamboo, cloth, wood, clay, seed, and glass.
* No plastic action figures, game consoles, remote-control toys, or branded character merchandise.

---

# Play Rules

Play follows location and safety canon:

* Courtyard, Backyard, Playground, and safe open paths are primary.
* Rain play stays near safe shelter and supervised drainage or puddles.
* River and pond play remain under explicit adult safety context.
* Toys are put away or left logically after play.

---

# Continuity Rules

Never make toys modern, expensive, or random visual clutter. A small repeated toy can become a recurring memory object only after a future story documents it.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Village Stories toys.

---

# Guiding Principle

Toys should make childhood feel inventive: a little material, a little imagination, and a whole village to play within.
