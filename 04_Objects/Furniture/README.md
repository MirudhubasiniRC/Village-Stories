# Furniture

## Purpose

This folder permanently defines the Family Compound's recurring furniture and practical household fittings.

---

# Canonical Furniture Language

Maintain:

* Low wooden stools.
* Simple wooden benches.
* Woven charpoy / cot where a scene requires it.
* Wooden storage trunk.
* Low food-preparation table.
* Wall shelves.
* Floor mats.
* Traditional thinnai stone seating.

Furniture remains modest, hand-made, repaired when needed, and appropriate to each permanent location.

---

# Materials

* Local hardwood.
* Coir rope.
* Woven natural-fibre matting.
* Stone, lime, or red-oxide surfaces where fixed furniture is part of architecture.
* No modern modular furniture, sofas, glass tables, or synthetic showroom surfaces.

---

# Placement

Furniture must follow existing location canon:

* Front Thinnai: fixed seating and simple resting furniture.
* Courtyard: mats, low stools, and activity-specific arrangements.
* Kitchen: preparation surfaces and storage shelves.
* Bedroom: simple bedding and storage.

Never move permanent furnishings randomly between locations.

---

# Continuity Rules

Objects show gentle age, hand repair, and natural wear. They remain clean, useful, and never neglected for visual drama.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Village Stories furniture.

---

# Guiding Principle

Furniture should make every room feel lived in, not decorated: built to sit, store, prepare, rest, and welcome.
