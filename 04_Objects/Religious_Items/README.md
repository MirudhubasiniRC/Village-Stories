# Religious Items

## Purpose

This folder permanently defines recurring devotional objects used in the Family Pooja Corner, Vinayagar Shrine, and festival contexts.

---

# Canonical Items

Maintain:

* Brass oil lamp / kuthuvilakku.
* Small brass bell.
* Brass offering plate.
* Incense holder.
* Camphor holder.
* Flower basket or plate.
* Kumkum and turmeric containers.
* Simple framed devotional image where location canon establishes it.
* Clay diyas / oil lamps for festival use.

---

# Material and Handling Rules

* Brass is warm, aged, clean, and lightly polished.
* Clay diyas are hand-made terracotta.
* Flowers are fresh when offered, never artificial decoration.
* Lamps and flame require careful adult handling.
* Children participate only with age-appropriate guidance.

---

# Location Relationships

* Family Pooja Corner: daily household devotion.
* Vinayagar Shrine: modest shared village worship.
* Festival Ground: temporary festival lighting only.

Objects never migrate between locations without a story reason.

---

# Continuity Rules

Never introduce flashy temple décor, plastic devotional objects, or excessive gold spectacle. Devotional objects must remain humble, cared for, and culturally respectful.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Village Stories religious items.

---

# Guiding Principle

Religious objects should make faith visible through care, flame, flowers, and repetition—not through grandeur.
