# Master Story Prompt

## Purpose

This is the permanent production manual and reusable master prompt for turning
approved Village Stories canon into a candidate episode premise.

Use it to develop a premise before scripting, storyboarding, image prompting,
Google Flow generation, or public packaging. It produces a bounded creative
brief, not a finished script and not new canon.

The prompt must make it possible to generate many varied episodes without
changing the village, forcing drama, or treating ordinary life as filler.

---

## Guiding Principle

> Start with one truthful condition in a known world; let practical care reveal
> the feeling; finish with emotional rest.

The best premise does not make village life larger than life. It gives a viewer
a reason to notice that ordinary life was already meaningful.

---

## When To Use This Manual

Use this manual when:

* an episode needs a new premise from established story ingredients;
* a production team needs several canon-safe premise candidates;
* a seed idea needs a short-versus-long-form decision;
* a promising visual, sound, object, season, or location needs a real story;
* a connected release needs an emotionally complete individual episode;
* a premise has failed story review and requires a controlled revision.

Use it after reading the relevant canon and before creating:

* a script;
* a scene or beat sheet;
* a storyboard;
* a Google Flow shot contract;
* a thumbnail, title, description, or upload package.

Do not use it to:

* create a new recurring character, location, profession, object, tradition,
  relationship fact, or historical claim;
* revise a character's personality, age, ability, or family role;
* turn an incomplete premise into a script by hiding unresolved facts;
* generate a visual prompt, motion prompt, dialogue scene, or metadata package;
* choose a sensational hook that contradicts the actual story;
* solve a missing canon decision with a plausible-sounding invention.

---

## Scope And Output Boundary

The output is a proposed episode premise package. It may recommend research,
canon clarification, or a format change. It may not silently decide any of
those things.

A premise is ready to hand off only when it states:

1. the ordinary condition and practical task;
2. the selected, documented people, place, season, and material facts;
3. one gentle source of pressure;
4. a practical response rooted in available people and resources;
5. one primary emotional movement;
6. a visible ending with emotional rest; and
7. the appropriate Short, long-form, or connected-release form.

The output must distinguish three states:

* `APPROVE FOR DEVELOPMENT` — canon-safe and sufficiently specified;
* `REVISE` — potentially valid, but a story construction issue is fixable;
* `HOLD FOR CANON OR RESEARCH` — a necessary factual decision is unknown.

---

## Authority And Conflict Resolution

This prompt inherits authority. It never creates it.

```text
01_Canon/ and timeline
↓
02_World/ · 03_Characters/ · 04_Objects/ · 05_Research/
↓
06_Story_Engine/ — story truth, conflict, emotion, character, location, season
↓
07_Episode_System/ — episode structure, format, viewer experience
↓
08_Production/ — research, script, board, Flow, quality, publishing execution
↓
09_AI_Production_System/ — prompt architecture and reproducible handoff
↓
Candidate premise
```

When instructions conflict:

1. identify the fact or decision in conflict;
2. find the highest applicable source;
3. obey that source;
4. record the conflict in the validation result;
5. revise the premise or return it to the owning authority.

Never average conflicting instructions, infer a permanent fact from a generated
idea, or treat a prior AI output as proof that an unsupported fact is allowed.

An `OPEN` fact remains open. It is not permission to improvise.

## Permanent Series Locks

Every premise generated with this manual must preserve these locks.

### Time And Place Lock

* The series is set in early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu.
* The precise year may be flexible; the historical period is not.
* Daily travel, communication, labour, household routines, information, and
  problem-solving must take believable local time and effort.
* Season must be named and must materially affect the task, route, sound,
  light, clothing, ground, plants, animals, or timing.
* No mobile phones, internet, digital payments, social media, GPS, current
  slang, modern appliances, contemporary vehicles, LED lighting, branded
  packaging, or unapproved modern infrastructure.

### Family Lock

The established core family is:

* Mother;
* Father;
* Elder Brother;
* Younger Daughter; and
* the German Shepherd Family Dog.

Do not place all five in every episode. Include each person only when their
documented character, relationship, skill, age, and story contribution justify
their presence.

The Family Dog is a gentle, loyal household companion. The dog may wait, follow,
rest, observe, respond softly to a familiar sound, play naturally, or receive
ordinary care. The dog must never become a police, military, attack, rescue,
threat, missing-pet, injury, or chase device.

### Character Truth Lock

* Mother remains capable, attentive, practical, and humane.
* Father remains calm, hardworking, observant, and emotionally grounded.
* Elder Brother may be eager, distracted, proud, or learning; correction stays
  dignified and age-appropriate.
* Younger Daughter may be curious, direct, observant, and safely included; she
  never carries adult burdens.
* No person changes temperament, ability, knowledge, relationship, or routine
  merely because the premise needs a shortcut.

Read the selected character packets before assigning a role. Individual canon
controls over this summary whenever there is a conflict.

### Style And Tone Lock

Village Stories has an original premium cinematic anime identity:

* semi-realistic anime people with natural South Indian features;
* soft painterly, hand-painted digital rendering;
* refined organic linework where useful;
* gentle natural illumination and atmospheric depth;
* culturally specific, lived-in rural environments; and
* warm slice-of-life observation with believable anatomy and restrained feeling.

At premise stage, this lock means story actions must later be visualizable as
calm, specific, respectful images. It does not authorize generic “cinematic”
spectacle, hyper-realism, named-studio imitation, plastic CGI, chibi comedy,
action camera language, or decorative poverty.

### Gentle Story Lock

The series finds movement in weather, work, objects, routine, care, memory,
small misunderstandings, and community timing. It does not need villains,
danger, humiliation, fear, severe illness, violence, theft, disaster, family
rupture, rescue spectacle, or traumatic loss.

The practical outcome may be modest or imperfect. The emotional question must
settle before the episode ends.

## Required Canon Context

Build a compact canon packet. Extract applied facts and source headings rather
than pasting whole documents into the prompt.

### Always Required

* relevant Series Style Lock and timeline facts;
* applicable early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu research and exclusions;
* `06_Story_Engine/01_Story_Philosophy.md`;

* `06_Story_Engine/05_Conflict_System.md`;
* `06_Story_Engine/06_Emotion_System.md`;

## Selection System

Selection is a story decision, not a roster or scenery exercise.

### Season Selection

Choose a season because it changes the available action.

| Season or condition | Productive premise pressure | Do not use it for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Summer | shade, water, early work, dry material, animal comfort, waiting for cool | heat illness or survival danger |
| Monsoon | wetness, shelter, protected belongings, changed routes, rain sound | flood, lightning, or stranded-family suspense |
| Post-monsoon | renewed growth, airing, cleaning, careful return outdoors | arbitrary decorative green scenery |
| Harvest | timing, shared work, storage, modest pride, coordination | crop failure panic or labour spectacle |
| Documented festival | preparation, memory, shared task, respectful excitement | generic crowd spectacle or cultural shorthand |

The premise must state the visible seasonal consequence. “It is monsoon” is
not enough; “rain makes the courtyard paper vulnerable” is usable.

### Character Selection

Select the smallest truthful cast.

1. Name the task or attention point.
2. Choose who would naturally notice it first.
3. Choose only people whose help, relationship, or observation changes the
   story honestly.
4. Give each selected person one fitting action, reaction, or presence.
5. Omit everyone else without apology.

Use a child for safe observation, learning, small help, or age-appropriate
mistake. Use an adult for guidance, skilled work, calm planning, or practical
care. Use the Dog for natural companionship or ordinary care, never for stakes.

Reject a candidate if a character exists only to distribute screen time, give a
lecture, make an adult task solvable, or create a reaction shot.

### Location Selection

Choose the documented location that contains the whole action or a necessary
stage of it.

* Kitchen supports cooking, preparation, warmth, and food-related care.
* Courtyard supports shared household work, rain response, drying, sorting, or
  animal routine when documented.
* Thinnai supports arrival, departure, waiting, exchange, or a settled final
  moment when documented.
* Garden or field supports observation, seasonal work, plant care, and
  age-appropriate learning when documented.
* School, tea stall, grocery, utility area, or another village place requires
  its established use, route, people, and social logic.

Do not choose a location just because it is attractive. A premise must explain
why this task happens there and how characters plausibly arrive.

### Object Selection

Choose a story object only when it carries practical evidence:

* it enables the task;
* its condition creates the gentle pressure;
* its familiar use carries a memory;
* its placement, quantity, or material makes care visible; or
* its final state provides a satisfying ending image.

Record each selected object:

```text
Object:
Source:
Material and period constraints:
Owner or current user:
Starting state:
Story action:
Ending state:
```

Do not use a prop as anonymous “village texture.” Do not substitute modern
materials or give an object unexplained sentimental importance.

## Conflict And Emotional Constraints

### Conflict Construction

Use exactly one primary gentle pressure. A linked practical complication is
allowed only when it grows directly from the first pressure.

Valid sources:

* weather affecting an existing task;
* time or household rhythm;
* a small shortage or material condition;
* a safe repair;
* animal care;
* memory, attention, or a harmless misunderstanding;
* a documented neighbour, school, or community timing need.

The conflict sequence is:

```text
ordinary activity → small interruption → proportionate noticing
→ practical care → visible adjustment → quiet rest
```

Reject pressure built from a hostile antagonist, sudden danger, cruelty,
humiliation, panic, neglect, crime, injury, major loss, or a coincidence that
solves the story without character effort.

### Emotional Construction

Choose one primary feeling and, at most, one supporting feeling.

Strong combinations include:

* comfort + gratitude;
* curiosity + quiet pride;
* mild disappointment + reassurance;
* gentle humour + family warmth;
* nostalgia + peace;
* hope + patience; and
* work satisfaction + closeness.

Emotion must arise from cause and visible behaviour. It may be shown through a
pause, a practical gesture, shared work, a careful look, a resumed routine,
natural sound, or a final image. Do not use speeches, crying, triumphant praise,
melodramatic music, or artificial silence to declare the feeling.

The ending must resolve the emotional movement even if an ongoing practical
process remains unfinished.

## Format Selection

### Choose A Short When

Choose a YouTube Short when one location or simple truthful bridge can carry:

* one practical action, sensory change, small discovery, or relationship beat;
* one visible hook that the episode genuinely fulfills;
* one understandable adjustment;
* one complete practical or emotional ending; and
* a readable 9:16 focal action at phone scale.

The Short may be serialized in a wider world, but it must emotionally complete
itself. A connected Short must stand alone for a viewer who has not seen the
previous release and must provide a mini-resolution.

Never split the only resolution into “Part 2,” label unrelated uploads as a
continuation, or use a cliffhanger to force the next view.

### Choose Long Form When

Choose five-to-ten-minute long form when the added time changes understanding:

* a process has several meaningful stages;
* weather, time, or light genuinely changes the response;
* a relationship needs preparation, recognition, and rest;
* documented place or community rhythm is necessary to the story; or
* one light supporting thread grows from the same day and main task.

Long form still needs one visible main thread. A supporting thread must deepen
that thread, not become a second unrelated problem.

### Redesign When

Redesign rather than choose a format if:

* the premise depends on multiple unrelated errands or obstacles;
* the ending is only a tease;
* the practical cause is not visible;
* the story requires an unsupported location, object, or skill;
* compression removes logic, care, or emotional rest; or
* extension would add scenic repetition instead of truthful life.

## YouTube Considerations At Premise Stage

YouTube considerations may clarify a real story; they may not invent it.

Every premise should identify:

* the truthful opening point of interest;
* the specific action, object, condition, or question a title could name;
* one accurate thumbnail-worthy image;
* whether the central action is readable at the intended delivery size;
* a natural final image or sound state;
* any real relation to a playlist or existing episode.

Valid hook examples:

* first drops reach a real household task;
* hands begin a specific repair, cooking, or garden action;
* a child notices a real change in a plant, object, or animal routine;
* a known object is missing from its normal place; or
* the Dog's calm routine behaviour reveals an ordinary need.

Invalid hook examples:

* a frightened face before context;
* a growl, thunder crash, rapid cut, or broken object used as threat bait;
* “Will they survive?” framing for weather or daily labour;
* a secret, a rescue, a shocking reveal, or an outcome the episode does not
  actually provide.

The premise must work muted in outline: viewers should be able to understand
the essential condition, action, adjustment, and ending through later picture
and captions without needing narration to repair missing logic.

## Prompt Assembly Procedure

### Step 1 — Establish The Decision

Write the request in plain language:

`Generate [count] premise candidate(s) for [format preference] from the supplied canon packet.`

Do not ask for “viral,” “emotional,” “cinematic,” or “unique” ideas without
defining the actual story conditions. Those adjectives are not usable inputs.

### Step 2 — Insert The Immutable Layer

Insert the authority rule, permanent series locks, and explicit list of source
facts. These are constraints, not creative variables.

### Step 3 — Insert The Bounded Brief

Provide the season, available people, location, objects, activity seed,
continuity facts, desired feeling, format preference, and any prohibited
elements.

### Step 4 — State Variable Discipline

Mark variables as `SELECT FROM APPROVED INPUTS ONLY`. A variable may choose
among documented options; it may not alter, extend, or replace their facts.

### Step 5 — State The Required Output Schema

Require every candidate to use the schema below. Ask for fewer strong
candidates rather than a broad, unreviewable list.

### Step 6 — Require Self-Validation

Require a pass/fail validation line for canon, period, cause, scale, heart,
format, YouTube honesty, and production handoff.

### Step 7 — Apply The Stop Rule

If a required fact is absent or a candidate would break a non-negotiable rule,
the model must hold or reject it. It must not repair the gap by inventing a
detail.

## Validation Rules

Run every candidate through these gates before it can receive `APPROVE FOR
DEVELOPMENT`.

### Gate 1 — Canon

* Is each named person, place, object, task, route, and practice documented?
* Does the candidate avoid adding a new permanent fact?
* Does it preserve known continuity rather than merely avoid obvious conflict?

### Gate 2 — Period

* Could the characters realistically do this in early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu?
* Does the premise avoid modern information, tools, language, and convenience?
* Does the seasonal condition visibly affect the day?

### Gate 3 — Character And Dignity

* Does each selected person have a fitting, documented reason to be present?
* Are Mother and Father cooperative and capable?
* Are children safe, heard, and corrected without humiliation?
* Is the Dog calm, animal-like, and protected from threat framing?
* Does the story respect labour, rural life, age, culture, and emotional restraint?

### Gate 4 — Cause And Conflict

* Does a practical activity exist before the interruption?
* Does one small pressure arise naturally from it?
* Can the response follow from observation, skill, help, patience, or available
  resources rather than lucky coincidence?
* Does the ending change understanding, routine, or visible state?

### Gate 5 — Emotional Truth

* Is one primary feeling clear?
* Is it earned through visible cause and care?
* Is the emotional response proportionate?
* Does the ending leave rest rather than a moral lecture or unresolved distress?

### Gate 6 — Format

For a Short:

* Is one complete action line legible in vertical form?
* Is the hook truthful and fulfilled?
* Does the episode end its own emotional and practical beat?

For long form:

* Does the main thread earn additional time?
* Does each stage add process, place, relationship, or emotional meaning?
* Is any supporting thread connected to the same activity and day?

### Gate 7 — YouTube Honesty

* Can a title and thumbnail describe the real action without exaggeration?
* Does the opening show the true point of interest promptly?
* Would the viewer feel the premise delivered the promise?
* Is any connection to another release truthful and optional?

### Gate 8 — Production And Flow Handoff

* Can the premise be broken into simple scenes and one-action shots?
* Are characters, objects, weather, location zones, and endpoint states
  trackable in a continuity ledger?
* Are there no complex, unsafe, or visually unbounded actions that Flow would
  be expected to invent?

---

## Rejection Rules

Reject a candidate immediately when it:

* contradicts specific canon;
* relies on a modern convenience or anachronistic language;
* creates danger, villainy, violence, injury, rescue, crime, panic, or fear;
* humiliates a child, elder, neighbour, worker, animal, or family member;
* makes Mother helpless, Father reckless, a child adult-like, or the Dog a
  threat device;
* uses poverty, manual labour, tradition, or rural setting as aesthetic
  spectacle;
* relies on coincidence rather than plausible effort or help;
* has more than one unrelated primary conflict;
* makes a generic montage instead of a cause-and-effect story;
* ends only as a cliffhanger, teaser, or moral speech;
* uses a location or object merely as attractive background texture;
* cannot name a practical reason for each person, prop, and location;
* requires a complex unbounded Google Flow action to make the premise work; or
* promises an event that truthful public packaging could not describe.

`HOLD FOR CANON OR RESEARCH` is not rejection. Use it when the premise could
work but requires a confirmed fact, such as object availability, location use,
character skill, route, seasonal practice, or cultural detail.

---

## Revision Rules

Revise the smallest broken layer. Do not rewrite a valid premise randomly.

| Failure | Revision action |
| --- | --- |
| Canon fact missing | Hold and request source-backed clarification |
| Modern or historical drift | Replace the mechanism with a documented period-appropriate action |
| Cast is crowded | Remove people without a practical or emotional job |
| Conflict is too large | Reduce it to a task-related adjustment |
| Conflict feels flat | Deepen care, timing, coordination, object meaning, or observation—not danger |
| Emotion is vague | Choose one primary feeling and add two visible evidence actions |
| Emotion is melodramatic | Replace speeches, tears, or music dependence with practical care and rest |
| Short has no ending | Add a visible completion or move the idea to a long-form scene |
| Short is split for retention | Complete the current beat; use an honest optional connection later |
| Long form is thin | Reduce to Short or add only documented meaningful process |
| Location is decorative | Make its geography, routine, or material condition affect the action |
| Object is decorative | Remove it or make its state cause, enable, or resolve a practical action |
| Flow handoff is complex | Divide the premise into simpler story beats before scripting |
| Packaging sounds inflated | Reframe around the actual task, object, season, or relationship |

For every revision, record:

```text
Candidate ID:
Revision number:
Failure classification:
Single change made:
Canon sources rechecked:
Validation result:
```

Never stack unexplained changes. A failed premise should teach the production
system which constraint or construction choice needs correction.

## Positive Examples

### Positive Short — Dry School Paper

**Premise:** During early monsoon rain in the documented courtyard, Younger
Daughter notices drops reaching her school paper. Mother wraps it in a dry
cloth while Elder Brother holds the school bag open. The paper is protected,
and the child reaches the thinnai ready to leave.

**Why it works:** Rain changes an existing task; each selected person has a
small fitting role; the hook is visible; the result is complete; the emotional
movement is mild worry to reassurance.

### Positive Short — Dog's Water Bowl

**Premise:** In summer shade, the Family Dog repeatedly looks toward its empty
documented water bowl while the family sorts vegetables in the courtyard.
Younger Daughter notices; Mother supervises a refill; the dog settles nearby
as water sound fades.

**Why it works:** Animal care is ordinary and safe; the Dog remains a companion;
the child contributes appropriately; the ending is a clear settled state.

### Positive Long Form — Rain-Adjusted Meal

**Premise:** Mother begins a familiar morning meal as rain makes the stove wood
damp. Father retrieves dry pieces from a documented covered store, Elder
Brother carefully helps move one vessel, and Younger Daughter keeps a cloth
from the wet threshold under supervision. The episode follows preparation,
patient fire management, and the family returning to the finished meal as rain
continues outside.

**Why it works:** The process has meaningful stages; weather affects work;
everyone has a proportionate role; the extra duration reveals rhythm rather
than repeated scenery; the ending rests in warmth and gratitude.

### Positive Connected Shorts — Seedling Care

**Short one:** Younger Daughter notices a seedling drooping in summer and, with
Mother, learns the safe time and amount for watering. It ends with the soil
darkening around the plant and the child returning the vessel.

**Short two:** On a later documented day, the family notices new growth during
their normal garden routine. It ends with quiet pride, not a promise that all
future care problems have vanished.

**Why it works:** Each Short has a standalone action and resolution. The
connection is factual, gentle, and not required to understand either episode.

## Negative Examples

### Negative — “Will She Reach School?”

A Short opens on Younger Daughter crying in heavy rain, uses fast cuts and
thunder, then ends before her school item is protected.

**Reject because:** It creates false danger, exploits a child’s distress, and
withholds the only resolution for retention.

### Negative — The Hero Dog

The Family Dog races into a storm, finds a missing child, and saves the family.

**Reject because:** It turns the dog into a rescue and danger device, creates
unsafe suspense, and abandons the series scale.

### Negative — Three Problems In One Long Form

Father starts a repair, then a school argument interrupts it, then an
unrelated visitor arrives with another urgent need.

**Reject because:** The story has no single thread. Select one activity and
defer the other ideas.

### Negative — Modern Shortcut

Mother calls a neighbour to locate a missing object, or Elder Brother watches
an online repair lesson.

**Reject because:** The solution violates the period lock instead of using
memory, local communication, available tools, or a plausible route.

### Negative — Decorative Nostalgia

The premise lingers on hardship, worn objects, and labour only to make rural
life look poetic, while no task or relationship changes.

**Reject because:** It aestheticizes material conditions and provides no
truthful cause-and-effect story.

## Edge Cases

### A Strong Image Has No Story

If a location, rain texture, vessel, or animal image is appealing but no
practical change follows, use it as a potential atmosphere or transition asset
later. Do not force it into an episode premise.

### A Task Continues Across Days

Close the current day’s practical and emotional unit. Record only confirmed
remaining facts. A future episode may revisit the task only after a new premise
approval; do not promise a date, outcome, or “Part 2.”

### The Ending Is Imperfect

An imperfect practical result is allowed when the family reaches a real plan,
shared understanding, useful progress, or emotional rest. Do not force perfect
success or artificial sadness.

### A Child Makes A Mistake

Keep it small, observable, repairable, and age-appropriate. An adult listens,
explains, and includes the child in the correction. Never use fear, isolation,
or public shame.

### A Location Transition Is Necessary

State the documented reason, route, time, and arrival. If the transition
dominates a Short, choose one location or redesign as long form.

### An Educational Opportunity Appears

Show the skill, seasonal reasoning, object handling, or routine through action.
Do not turn the premise into a lecture, quiz, unsupported historical claim, or
moral message.

### A Trend Or Metric Suggests More Drama

Treat it as non-authoritative feedback. Improve clarity of the truthful opening
or packaging only after checking the final episode. Never add shock, speed,
danger, or false urgency to satisfy a metric.

### An AI Suggests An Attractive Unsupported Detail

Reject the detail, not necessarily the premise. Replace it with a documented
fact or hold it for canon or research clarification.

## Quality Gates And Handoffs

### Gate A — Premise Request Ready

Before prompting, confirm:

* the request identifies available canon inputs;
* the target format is known or explicitly open;
* season, desired feeling, and seed are stated;
* open facts have an owner; and
* the prompt variables cannot contradict canon.

**Handoff:** completed input brief and compact canon packet.

### Gate B — Candidate Story Fit

Before approving a premise, confirm:

* canon, period, character, location, object, cause, scale, and heart pass;
* the premise has one primary action and pressure;
* the emotional ending is complete; and
* the format recommendation is justified.

**Handoff:** approved premise package, validation result, and source manifest.

### Gate C — Episode System Fit

Before scripting, confirm:

* Short candidates have an honest hook, vertical central action, and own ending;
* long-form candidates earn process and time without filler;
* opening, pacing, ending, ASMR, music, silence, education, and visual rhythm
  have relevant planning references; and
* any connection to other releases is factual and optional.

**Handoff:** format-approved premise with provisional beat intent and delivery
requirements.

### Gate D — Production And Google Flow Ready

Before storyboarding, confirm:

* scenes can be divided into stable locations and simple visible actions;
* source-backed character, object, location, weather, and state facts are
  available;
* the final state can hand off to the next scene or release; and
* complex activity has been separated before it becomes a Flow problem.

**Handoff:** approved premise, canon manifest, continuity starting state,
scene-level task chain, and required reference list to `08_Production/` and
the relevant `09_AI_Production_System/` prompt manuals.

### Gate E — Public Promise Ready

Before title, thumbnail, and metadata development, confirm:

* the opening point of interest is actually in the final episode;
* a visible image can represent the real subject;
* any educational or cultural phrase is demonstrated and verified; and
* no public wording needs a claim outside the locked production package.

**Handoff:** source brief to `08_Production/13_YouTube_Metadata.md` and
thumbnail/publishing workflows after picture and sound lock.

## Google Flow Downstream Handoff

This document stops at premise development. It prepares Flow without attempting
to write animation instructions too early.

An approved premise must hand downstream:

```text
Episode ID and approved premise revision:
Format and delivery ratio:
Main thread and emotional centre:
Scene-level practical chain:
Selected characters and their required state:
Selected location zones and required geography:
Selected objects with start and end state:
Season, time, weather, light, and sound consequences:
Opening point of interest:
Required final emotional and visual state:
Known continuity from prior and next material:
Likely one-action shot opportunities:
Known complex actions that must be split:
Required image, character, location, and prop references:
```

`08_Production/05_Storyboard_Workflow.md` turns this into scenes and shots.
`08_Production/06_Image_Prompt_Workflow.md` establishes approved images.
`08_Production/07_Google_Flow_Workflow.md` receives a locked shot contract:
one stable place, one dominant action, one camera intention, and a defined
start and end state.

Google Flow may animate an approved action. It must never decide the premise,
conflict, emotion, character purpose, or historical fact.
