# 01_Global_Rules.md

# Purpose

This document defines the permanent laws of the Village Stories universe.

These rules apply to every story, every episode, every character, every prompt, every animation, and every future production.

Unless intentionally updated through the Canon, these rules must never be broken.

---

# Rule 1 — Persistent World

Village Stories exists within one continuous living world.

The village continues to exist even when an episode ends.

Characters continue their daily lives off-screen.

The audience should feel that they are visiting a real place rather than watching isolated stories.

---

# Rule 2 — Canon Is Permanent

Once a fact becomes Canon, it remains true unless intentionally updated.

Examples include:

* Village geography
* Character identities
* Permanent locations
* Family relationships
* Historical timeline
* Major life events

Canon should never change accidentally.

---

# Rule 3 — Continuity Before Creativity

Creative ideas must respect established continuity.

No new story may contradict existing canon without a documented change.

---

# Rule 4 — Authenticity First

Everything should represent authentic 1990s South Indian village life whenever reasonably possible.

If historical knowledge is uncertain, research should be preferred over assumption.

---

# Rule 5 — Every Place Has History

Every permanent location should feel as though it existed long before the current episode.

Locations accumulate memories rather than being recreated each time.

---

# Rule 6 — Every Character Has A Life

Characters exist beyond the current story.

When off-screen, they continue living naturally within the village.

No character should appear only because the audience expects them.

---

# Rule 7 — Nature Is A Character

Nature is an active part of the world.

Seasons,

Rain,

Wind,

Animals,

Plants,

Light,

Water,

and Time

all influence the story naturally.

---

# Rule 8 — Small Moments Matter

Village Stories celebrates ordinary life.

Simple actions can carry emotional meaning.

Examples include:

Cooking.

Fetching water.

Receiving a letter.

Waiting for rain.

Walking home.

Lighting an oil lamp.

Watching children play.

These moments deserve the same care as major story events.

---

# Rule 9 — Research Strengthens Storytelling

Whenever authentic historical knowledge exists, it should be incorporated into the world.

The Research Library exists to preserve forgotten traditions, occupations, customs, and everyday details.

---

# Rule 10 — The World Grows Organically

New locations,

new neighbours,

new traditions,

new memories,

and new stories

may be added over time,

provided they respect the existing world.

Village Stories should grow through discovery rather than constant reinvention.

---

# Rule 11 — Consistency Builds Trust

Viewers should gradually learn the village.

The same people,

the same houses,

the same paths,

the same trees,

the same river,

the same temple,

and the same traditions

should become familiar over time.

Consistency creates emotional attachment.

---

# Rule 12 — Quality Over Speed

The objective is not to produce the greatest number of episodes.

The objective is to create episodes that feel timeless,

authentic,

emotionally warm,

and worthy of becoming part of the Village Stories universe.

Every episode should strengthen the world.

# Rule 13 — Reward the Observant Viewer

Village Stories is designed to reward viewers who pay close attention.

The world should contain natural continuity and subtle recurring details that make the village feel alive over time.

These details should never distract from the main story, but they should quietly reinforce the feeling that the village continues to exist between episodes.

### Examples

* A familiar neighbour appears in the background performing their daily routine across multiple episodes.
* The village postman becomes a recognizable figure long before he plays an important role in a story.
* A jasmine plant slowly grows and blooms across several episodes.
* The river level rises and falls according to the current season.
* Paddy fields visibly progress through planting, growth, and harvest.
* A bicycle is usually parked outside the same neighbour's house.
* The family dog recognizes regular visitors and reacts differently to strangers.
* Festival decorations appear before the festival and disappear naturally afterwards.
* Children grow slightly taller over long periods of time if the series timeline advances.
* Household objects gradually show signs of age and regular use.

### Living Background

Background activity should feel independent of the main story.

Examples include:

* Someone sweeping their front yard.
* Clothes drying in the morning sun.
* A neighbour drawing a kolam.
* Villagers chatting at the River Ghat.
* Children walking home from school.
* Birds building nests.
* Butterflies visiting flowering plants.
* Smoke rising from distant kitchens.

These moments should happen because the village is alive, not because the story requires them.

### Continuity Through Time

The village should remember its own history.

Past events may be referenced naturally in future episodes.

Examples:

* "That mango tree was planted after the great storm."
* "The new stone steps at the River Ghat were built last summer."
* "The postman still stops for tea at the same house every morning."

These references create the feeling that the audience is revisiting a familiar place with its own shared memories.

### Guiding Principle

Do not add hidden details simply as Easter eggs.

Add them because they make the village feel like a real place where life continues beyond the edges of every episode.

The goal is for observant viewers to feel rewarded for returning to the Village Stories universe, while first-time viewers can still fully enjoy each story.
