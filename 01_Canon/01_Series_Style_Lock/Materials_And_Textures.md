# Materials And Textures

## Purpose

This document defines the physical appearance and surface qualities of every material used throughout the Village Stories universe.

Materials should feel handcrafted, naturally aged, and appropriate to an authentic 1990s South Indian village.

Every object, building, path, tool, and natural surface should consistently reflect its real-world material.

---

# Material Philosophy

Nothing should appear newly manufactured unless the story specifically requires it.

The village has been lived in for generations.

Materials should show age through gentle wear, careful maintenance, and everyday use.

Imperfections are signs of life.

Objects should feel valued rather than discarded.

---

# General Principles

Every material should display:

* Natural texture.
* Visible craftsmanship.
* Gentle imperfections.
* Signs of regular use.
* Appropriate ageing.
* Believable weight.
* Realistic surface behaviour.

Avoid perfectly smooth or artificial-looking surfaces.

---

# Clay

Used for:

* Cooking pots.
* Water pots.
* Clay stoves.
* Roof elements.
* Household containers.

Characteristics:

* Matte finish.
* Slight colour variation.
* Fine handmade imperfections.
* Light smoke staining where used for cooking.
* Small chips from years of use.

Clay should always feel handmade.

---

# Wood

Used for:

* Doors.
* Windows.
* Roof beams.
* Furniture.
* Agricultural tools.
* Veranda pillars.

Characteristics:

* Visible grain.
* Soft wear.
* Rounded edges from use.
* Small scratches.
* Weathering from sun and rain.

Wood should feel warm and alive.

---

# Stone

Used for:

* River Ghat steps.
* Wells.
* Grinding stones.
* Temple structures.
* Pathways.

Characteristics:

* Uneven surface.
* Weathered edges.
* Moss where moisture remains.
* Fine cracks.
* Natural colour variation.

Stone should suggest decades of use.

---

# Metal

Used for:

* Brass vessels.
* Copper utensils.
* Oil lamps.
* Bells.
* Traditional locks.

Characteristics:

* Soft reflections.
* Natural tarnish.
* Polished areas from frequent handling.
* Small dents from everyday use.

Avoid mirror-like finishes.

---

# Fabric

Used for:

* Sarees.
* Veshtis.
* Children's clothing.
* Towels.
* Curtains.
* Mats.

Characteristics:

* Natural cotton texture.
* Soft folds.
* Slight wrinkles.
* Gentle movement in the breeze.
* Signs of repeated washing.

Fabric should never appear stiff or synthetic.

---

# Earth

Used for:

* Village paths.
* Courtyards.
* Fields.
* Riverbanks.

Characteristics:

* Seasonal variation.
* Footprints.
* Wheel marks.
* Rain puddles.
* Dry cracks during summer.
* Soft mud during monsoon.

The ground should tell the story of recent weather.

---

# Water

Used for:

* River.
* Pond.
* Canal.
* Wells.
* Rain puddles.

Characteristics:

* Gentle reflections.
* Natural movement.
* Seasonal clarity.
* Soft ripples.
* Floating leaves when appropriate.

Water should always feel calm unless disturbed by people, animals, wind, or rain.

---

# Vegetation

Plants should feel alive.

Characteristics:

* Individual leaves.
* Natural growth patterns.
* Seasonal variation.
* Healthy imperfections.
* Wind movement.
* Moisture after rain.

Avoid repetitive or identical foliage.

---

# Walls

Traditional village walls should display:

* Lime wash.
* Mud plaster.
* Gentle weathering.
* Hairline cracks.
* Minor discoloration.
* Smoke stains near kitchens.

Walls should communicate age without appearing neglected.

---

# Roofs

Traditional tiled roofs should show:

* Terracotta variation.
* Moss where appropriate.
* Slight unevenness.
* Natural ageing.
* Fallen leaves after windy weather.

Roofs should never appear perfectly uniform.

---

# Kitchen Surfaces

The kitchen should naturally display:

* Smoke-darkened areas.
* Firewood ash.
* Clay dust.
* Water splashes.
* Flour residue.
* Grinding marks.
* Signs of frequent cooking.

A working kitchen should always feel actively used.

---

# Agricultural Tools

Farming equipment should show:

* Worn wooden handles.
* Earth stains.
* Metal wear.
* Rope fibres.
* Hand repairs.

Tools should reflect years of practical use.

---

# Natural Ageing

Age should appear gradually through:

* Sunlight.
* Rain.
* Daily work.
* Human touch.
* Smoke.
* Water.
* Time.

Avoid exaggerated decay unless required by the story.

The village is cared for.

It is not abandoned.

---

# Texture Consistency

The same object should maintain the same material identity across every episode.

Weather, lighting, and age may influence appearance.

The underlying material should never change.

---

# Guiding Principle

Every surface should tell a story.

The audience should instinctively feel that every object has been touched, used, repaired, and cared for by generations of people living in the Village Stories universe.
