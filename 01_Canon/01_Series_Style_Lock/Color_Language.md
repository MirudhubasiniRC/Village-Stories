# Color Language

## Purpose

This document defines how colour should be used throughout Village Stories.

Colour is a storytelling tool.

It communicates emotion, season, weather, time of day, culture, and atmosphere while maintaining a consistent visual identity across the series.

Every colour choice should strengthen the feeling of the world rather than simply making an image more attractive.

---

# Color Philosophy

Village Stories uses colours that feel naturally rich, emotionally warm, and true to life.

The world should feel vibrant because it is full of life, not because colours are artificially saturated.

Every colour should appear to belong to the environment.

---

# Colour Hierarchy

Every frame should naturally balance four groups of colours.

## Nature

The dominant colours of the world.

Examples:

* Coconut palms
* Banana plants
* Paddy fields
* River water
* Trees
* Flowers
* Earth

Nature should visually connect every location.

---

## Architecture

Warm traditional colours.

Examples:

* Whitewashed walls.
* Terracotta roof tiles.
* Red oxide floors.
* Weathered wood.
* Clay walls.
* Stone.

Buildings should always feel handcrafted and lived-in.

---

## People

Characters should stand out naturally without feeling disconnected from the environment.

Traditional clothing should complement the surrounding colours rather than overpower them.

Bright clothing should feel joyful, never distracting.

---

## Atmosphere

Sky, light, weather, mist, smoke, reflections, and seasonal conditions gently influence every colour in the frame.

Atmosphere should unify the entire image.

---

# Seasonal Colour Identity

## Summer

Colours should feel:

* Warm.
* Sunlit.
* Dry.
* Earthy.
* Bright without becoming harsh.

Examples include:

Golden grass.

Dry soil.

Warm wood.

Fresh green trees.

Bright blue skies.

---

## Monsoon

Colours should feel:

* Fresh.
* Moist.
* Deep.
* Lush.
* Soft.

Examples include:

Rain-darkened earth.

Wet leaves.

Grey skies.

River reflections.

Dense green vegetation.

---

## Post-Monsoon / Harvest

Colours should feel:

* Rich.
* Abundant.
* Mature.
* Celebratory.
* Full of life.

Examples include:

Golden paddy.

Healthy vegetation.

Warm evening skies.

Fresh produce.

Festival decorations.

**Greens (locked):** Post-Monsoon foliage must read **fresh and saturated** —
banana leaf, garden plant, palm, and path-edge grass stay clearly green
(`#5A9A4A`–`#4F8F3C`, coconut `#4A6B3D`). Do **not** render milky sage, cream-
washed, or heat-haze bleached leaves under soft morning light. Full lock:
`Color_And_Lighting_Formula.md` → **GREENS LOCK**.

Colour naturally changes throughout the day.

Morning

Fresh.

Gentle.

Soft.

Clean.

---

Afternoon

Balanced.

Natural.

Clear.

---

Evening

Warm.

Golden.

Comfortable.

Inviting.

---

Night

Deep.

Quiet.

Natural.

---

# Emotional Colour Use

Colour should reinforce emotion.

Examples:

Family warmth → warm natural earth tones.

Rain → cool greens and soft greys.

Celebration → brighter traditional clothing and decorations.

Reflection → muted natural colours.

Joy → colourful flowers, fruit, fabrics, and village life.

Colour should support emotion without becoming symbolic or exaggerated.

**Production hex values** for character skin, wardrobe, and key environments
are locked in `01_Canon/Character_Color_And_Light_Reference.md`.

---

# Traditional Colour Sources

The most beautiful colours should come naturally from village life itself.

Examples include:

* Turmeric.
* Kumkum.
* Jasmine.
* Marigolds.
* Banana leaves.
* Clay.
* Wood.
* Brass.
* Copper.
* Fresh vegetables.
* Fruits.
* Paddy.
* River water.
* Temple decorations.

Avoid introducing colours that have no natural place in the environment.

---

# Colour Balance

Every frame should feel balanced.

Avoid allowing one colour to dominate unnecessarily.

The viewer's eye should comfortably move throughout the composition.

Natural contrast is preferred over dramatic contrast.

---

# Avoid

Avoid:

* Neon colours.
* Oversaturation.
* Artificial filters.
* Modern colour grading.
* Excessive HDR effects.
* Unrealistic skin tones.
* Plastic-looking surfaces.
* Pure black shadows.
* Pure white highlights.

The world should always feel organic.

---

# Consistency

Characters, locations, vegetation, and recurring objects should maintain recognisable colour identities across episodes.

Natural variation caused by weather, season, and lighting is encouraged.

Random variation is not.

---

# Guiding Principle

Colour should never compete with the story.

Its role is to quietly strengthen emotion, authenticity, and immersion until the audience no longer notices the colours themselves—only the feeling they create.
