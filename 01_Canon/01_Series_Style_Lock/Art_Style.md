# Art Style

## Purpose

This document defines the official illustration style of Village Stories.

It establishes the permanent artistic language for all characters, environments, props, animals, and backgrounds.

Every visual asset produced for the series must follow these standards.

---

# Artistic Direction

Village Stories is illustrated in a **Studio Ghibli-inspired anime aesthetic**, adapted to authentically represent rural South India during the 1990s.

The artwork should feel handcrafted, emotionally warm, and timeless.

Every frame should resemble a carefully painted scene from a traditionally animated film rather than a digitally generated image.

The inspiration comes from the emotional sensitivity, environmental richness, and handcrafted quality associated with Studio Ghibli films, while remaining visually original and culturally authentic.

---

# Illustration Style

Maintain throughout the series:

* Soft painterly digital rendering.
* Hand-painted appearance.
* Watercolor and gouache inspired textures.
* Gentle cel-style shading blended with painted rendering.
* Fine organic linework.
* Natural brush texture.
* Rich environmental detail.
* Soft atmospheric depth.
* Subtle filmic softness.
* Handcrafted visual quality.

Avoid:

* Photorealism.
* CGI appearance.
* Plastic surfaces.
* Vector artwork.
* AI-generated glossy textures.
* Overly sharp digital edges.
* Heavy outlines.

---

# Character Design Language

Characters should appear:

* Warm and approachable.
* Naturally expressive.
* Softly stylized.
* Anatomically believable.
* Consistent throughout the series.

Maintain:

* Rounded facial forms.
* Gentle facial features.
* Expressive but proportionate eyes.
* Delicate noses.
* Soft natural smiles.
* Graceful body language.
* Traditional clothing.

Expressions should communicate emotion through subtle facial changes rather than exaggerated animation.

---

# Facial Proportions

Maintain consistent proportions across all recurring characters.

Eyes:

* Slightly larger than realistic.
* Approximately one-fifth of the face height.
* Expressive without becoming oversized.

Avoid:

* Chibi proportions.
* Shoujo-style oversized eyes.
* Hyper-realistic anatomy.
* Cartoon exaggeration.

Characters should remain recognisable in every episode.

---

# Linework

Use:

* Fine, organic linework.
* Slight natural variation in line thickness.
* Hand-drawn appearance.
* Soft edge transitions.

Avoid:

* Thick comic outlines.
* Uniform digital strokes.
* Harsh contour lines.

The drawing should feel crafted by an artist rather than generated mechanically.

---

# Painterly Rendering

Surfaces should display:

* Visible brush character.
* Layered colour transitions.
* Gentle gradients.
* Soft edge blending.
* Natural imperfections.

Do not create perfectly smooth digital surfaces.

Small imperfections help reinforce the handcrafted aesthetic.

---

# Environmental Artwork

Backgrounds should receive the same artistic attention as characters.

Maintain:

* Rich environmental detail.
* Botanical accuracy.
* Layered vegetation.
* Atmospheric perspective.
* Soft distance haze.
* Hand-painted skies.
* Natural terrain variation.

Every environment should feel like a place someone could explore.

---

# Botanical Detail

Plants should never appear as generic green shapes.

Render with:

* Individually defined leaves.
* Natural leaf variation.
* Layered foliage.
* Visible fruit where appropriate.
* Realistic growth patterns.
* Seasonal variation.

Special attention should be given to:

* Coconut trees.
* Banana plants.
* Mango trees.
* Paddy fields.
* Lotus plants.
* Village gardens.

---

# Animals

Animals should follow the same artistic language as the people.

Maintain:

* Soft expressive eyes.
* Natural proportions.
* Painterly fur and feathers.
* Gentle personality.
* Believable anatomy.

Animals should feel like members of the village rather than decorative props.

---

# Props

Traditional objects should be illustrated with care.

Examples include:

* Clay pots.
* Brass vessels.
* Wooden furniture.
* Grinding stones.
* Bamboo baskets.
* Farming tools.
* Oil lamps.
* Traditional kitchen utensils.

Objects should show craftsmanship, wear, and everyday use.

---

# Background Detail

Backgrounds should support immersion without competing for attention.

Foreground:

Highest detail.

Midground:

Primary story area.

Background:

Atmospheric simplification with preserved identity.

The audience should immediately understand the location while remaining focused on the story.

---

# Consistency

Every episode should feel like it belongs to the same animated film.

Do not change:

* Rendering quality.
* Character proportions.
* Brush style.
* Illustration technique.
* Artistic finish.

Visual evolution should occur only through intentional improvements that are applied consistently across the series.

---

# Guiding Principle

Every frame should look as though it was patiently painted by skilled artists who deeply respect the beauty of ordinary village life.

The artwork should never draw attention to itself through unnecessary complexity or artificial perfection.

Its purpose is to quietly support the world, the story, and the emotions of Village Stories.
