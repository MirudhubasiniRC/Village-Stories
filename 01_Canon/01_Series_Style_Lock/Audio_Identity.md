# Audio Identity

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent sound identity of Village Stories.

Audio should immerse the audience in the everyday life of a 1990s South Indian village.

Every sound should feel authentic, naturally motivated, and connected to the environment.

The goal is to make viewers feel as though they are physically present within the village.

---

# Audio Philosophy

The village should always sound alive.

Silence is never completely silent.

Instead, it is filled with subtle environmental sounds that naturally exist within the setting.

Audio should support the story without overwhelming it.

The audience should remember the feeling of being in the village rather than individual sound effects.

---

# Primary Sound Layers

Every scene should be built using four sound layers.

## Environmental Ambience

The constant sounds that define a location.

Examples:

* Wind through coconut trees.
* Flowing river water.
* Birds singing.
* Distant livestock.
* Insects.
* Leaves rustling.
* Rainfall.
* Temple bell in the distance.

These sounds create the foundation of the scene.

---

## Human Activity

Sounds created by everyday life.

Examples:

* Footsteps.
* Clothing movement.
* Grinding spices.
* Sweeping.
* Washing clothes.
* Water being poured.
* Chopping vegetables.
* Cooking.
* Laughter.
* Soft conversation.
* Breathing during physical work.

Human sounds should feel natural and never exaggerated.

---

## Object Sounds

Traditional materials produce distinctive sounds.

Examples:

* Brass utensils.
* Clay pots.
* Wooden doors.
* Bamboo baskets.
* Grinding stones.
* Firewood crackling.
* Oil lamps being lit.
* Rope moving over a well pulley.

Each object should sound true to its material.

---

## Nature

Nature constantly contributes to the soundscape.

Examples:

* Birds taking flight.
* Butterflies fluttering nearby.
* Rain hitting roof tiles.
* Water dripping from leaves.
* Frogs after rain.
* Crickets at night.
* Wind across paddy fields.

Nature should never feel like background noise.

It is part of the village's identity.

---

# Authentic ASMR

ASMR should arise naturally from everyday activities.

Examples include:

* Grinding chutney on a stone.
* Rice being washed.
* Firewood crackling.
* Steam escaping from a clay pot.
* Water filling a brass vessel.
* Banana leaves unfolding.
* Footsteps on wet earth.
* Paddy moving in the breeze.

Avoid artificial or exaggerated ASMR sounds.

---

# Dialogue

Dialogue should always feel natural.

Characters speak because conversation is part of life.

Not because information needs to be explained.

Silence is often more powerful than unnecessary speech.

---

# Music

Music should support emotion rather than control it.

Whenever possible, allow natural environmental sounds to carry the emotional weight of a scene.

If music is used, it should feel gentle, culturally appropriate, and unobtrusive.

The village itself should remain the dominant voice.

---

# Seasonal Audio

Every season has its own sound identity.

Summer:

* Dry leaves.
* Cicadas.
* Warm breeze.
* Distant farming activity.

Monsoon:

* Rain.
* Water flowing.
* Frogs.
* Wet footsteps.
* Thunder in the distance.

Post-Monsoon / Harvest:

* Birds.
* Harvest work.
* Wind across mature paddy.
* Community activity.

Seasonal sound should reinforce the passage of time.

---

# Time of Day

Morning:

Birdsong.

Roosters.

Temple bell.

Early household activity.

---

Afternoon:

Working sounds.

Livestock.

Gentle wind.

Insects.

---

Evening:

Children returning home.

Cooking.

Conversation.

Crickets beginning.

Temple bell.

---

Night:

Crickets.

Frogs.

Night breeze.

Occasional dog bark.

Distant flowing water.

---

# Spatial Audio

Sound should reflect distance.

Nearby sounds should feel detailed and immediate.

Distant sounds should feel softer and naturally blended into the environment.

The audience should instinctively understand the space through sound.

---

# Audio Continuity

Maintain consistency in:

* Weather.
* Time of day.
* Location.
* Character position.
* Environmental conditions.

Sound should transition naturally between shots.

Avoid abrupt changes that interrupt immersion.

---

# Avoid

Avoid:

* Modern electronic sounds.
* Artificial ambience.
* Overly loud sound effects.
* Repetitive looping.
* Constant background music.
* Sounds that do not belong to the location.
* Excessive dramatic audio cues.

Every sound should have a believable source.

---

# Guiding Principle

The sound of Village Stories should feel like a memory.

Without looking at the screen, the audience should be able to close their eyes and believe they are standing in a peaceful 1990s South Indian village, surrounded by the gentle sounds of everyday life.
