# Animation Principles

## Purpose

This document defines how movement behaves throughout the Village Stories universe.

Animation should always support realism, emotion, immersion, and storytelling.

The audience should feel that every person, animal, object, and natural element moves according to the laws of the real world.

---

# Animation Philosophy

Movement should feel observed rather than performed.

Characters should move because they are naturally living their lives, not because they are trying to entertain the audience.

Every action should have intention.

Every movement should have weight.

Every pause should feel natural.

---

# Movement Identity

Movement should always feel:

* Natural
* Believable
* Purposeful
* Relaxed when appropriate
* Energetic when appropriate
* Emotionally honest
* Physically consistent

The speed of movement should always match the activity.

---

# Physical Realism

Respect real-world physics.

Maintain:

* Natural balance.
* Realistic weight.
* Proper momentum.
* Appropriate acceleration and deceleration.
* Convincing body mechanics.

Avoid movements that feel weightless or mechanical.

---

# Human Behaviour

Characters should behave like real people.

Examples include:

* Adjusting clothing naturally.
* Looking toward someone before speaking.
* Wiping sweat after work.
* Stretching after sitting.
* Brushing away insects.
* Shifting weight while standing.
* Pausing to think before acting.

Small natural behaviours create believable characters.

---

# Emotional Movement

Emotion should influence body language.

Examples:

Joy:

* Relaxed posture.
* Open gestures.
* Light, energetic movement.

Concern:

* Slower movements.
* Focused attention.
* Subtle hesitation.

Tiredness:

* Reduced energy.
* Heavier footsteps.
* Gentle stretching.

Affection:

* Soft eye contact.
* Comfortable proximity.
* Calm gestures.

Emotion should be expressed through natural behaviour rather than exaggerated acting.

---

# Environmental Movement

The world should always feel alive.

Examples include:

* Coconut leaves moving in the wind.
* Banana leaves bending after rain.
* River water flowing gently.
* Smoke drifting upward.
* Steam rising from food.
* Birds flying.
* Butterflies changing direction.
* Grass responding to the breeze.

Nature should never appear frozen.

---

# Animal Behaviour

Animals should behave according to their species.

The family dog may:

* Follow family members.
* Rest in familiar places.
* Observe visitors.
* React to unusual sounds.
* Play with the children.

Chickens:

* Peck.
* Scratch the ground.
* Follow one another.
* Scatter when startled.

Cows:

* Graze.
* Chew slowly.
* Flick their tails.
* React calmly.

Animals should feel like permanent residents of the village.

---

# Object Movement

Objects move only when affected by natural forces.

Examples:

* Curtains move with the breeze.
* Clothes sway on the washing line.
* Water ripples after disturbance.
* Leaves fall during strong wind.
* Hanging lamps sway gently.

Avoid unnecessary motion.

Stillness is equally important.

---

# Rhythm

Different activities naturally have different rhythms.

Examples:

Cooking:

Steady.

Farming:

Purposeful.

Children playing:

Energetic.

Festival preparation:

Busy.

Prayer:

Calm.

Rain watching:

Quiet.

Allow each activity to establish its own natural rhythm.

---

# Continuity

Movement should remain continuous between shots.

Maintain:

* Walking direction.
* Body position.
* Hand placement.
* Object interaction.
* Environmental movement.

Avoid sudden pose changes or impossible transitions.

---

# Avoid

Avoid:

* Robotic movement.
* Floating motion.
* Instant acceleration.
* Instant stops.
* Excessive looping.
* Unmotivated gestures.
* Overacting.
* Cartoon exaggeration.

Movement should always feel grounded.

---

# Supporting the Story

Animation exists to strengthen the story.

It should never distract from it.

The audience should notice the characters and their emotions—not the animation technique itself.

---

# Guiding Principle

Movement should feel like life unfolding naturally.

Every gesture, every step, every breeze, every ripple, and every glance should contribute to the illusion that the Village Stories universe continues to exist whether the audience is watching or not.
