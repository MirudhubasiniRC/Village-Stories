# Lighting Style

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent lighting system for Village Stories.

Lighting is not only used for visibility but also for emotion, atmosphere, realism, continuity, and orientation within the persistent world.

Every scene must follow these standards.

---

# Lighting Philosophy

Lighting should always feel natural.

The audience should feel as though every scene was illuminated by the real environment rather than artificial studio lights.

Light should support emotion without drawing unnecessary attention to itself.

Every lighting decision should reinforce immersion.

---

# Primary Lighting Families

Village Stories uses two primary lighting families.

Neither is considered the default.

The story, weather, season, and mood determine which family should be used.

---

## Family A — Warm & Sunlit

Used for:

* Clear mornings
* Sunny afternoons
* Golden evenings
* Dry summer days
* Warm harvest days

Characteristics:

* Soft golden sunlight.
* Warm bounce light.
* Natural sun direction.
* Gentle contrast.
* Rich warmth.
* Comfortable atmosphere.

Avoid harsh midday brightness.

---

## Family B — Cool & Monsoon

Used for:

* Rainy days.
* Overcast weather.
* Misty mornings.
* Cloud-covered afternoons.
* Monsoon evenings.

Characteristics:

* Soft diffused light.
* Grey-blue ambient tones.
* Low contrast.
* Moist atmosphere.
* Gentle reflections.
* Soft shadows.

Avoid making monsoon scenes feel cold or lifeless.

Even cloudy days should remain emotionally inviting.

**Prompt labels:** `Lighting Family: Warm & Sunlit` or `Lighting Family: Cool &
Monsoon`. Character skin and scene colour hex values:
`01_Canon/Character_Color_And_Light_Reference.md`.

# Morning Lighting

Morning light enters from the east.

Typical characteristics:

* Soft.
* Warm (or diffused during monsoon).
* Long shadows.
* Fresh atmosphere.
* Gentle highlights.

Bedroom windows naturally receive morning light.

---

# Afternoon Lighting

Sun is high.

Characteristics:

* Balanced illumination.
* Shorter shadows.
* Clear environmental visibility.
* Soft natural bounce light.

Avoid excessive contrast.

---

# Evening Lighting

Sunlight enters from the west.

Characteristics:

* Warm orange tones.
* Long shadows.
* Soft golden atmosphere.
* Gentle rim lighting.

Kitchen and rear areas naturally receive evening light.

---

# Night Lighting

Night scenes should remain readable while preserving realism.

Possible light sources:

* Moonlight.
* Brass oil lamps.
* Clay lamps.
* Firewood stove.
* Lanterns.

Avoid modern lighting sources.

Darkness should feel peaceful rather than frightening.

---

# Rain Lighting

Rain naturally softens light.

Characteristics:

* Diffused sky.
* Wet reflections.
* Soft shadows.
* Moist atmosphere.
* Reduced contrast.

Rain should enhance immersion rather than reduce visibility.

---

# Interior Lighting

Interiors rely primarily on natural light.

Sources include:

* Windows.
* Courtyard openings.
* Doorways.
* Verandas.
* Oil lamps.
* Firelight.

Rooms should never feel artificially illuminated.

Natural light should define interior mood.

---

# Shadow Behaviour

Shadow direction must remain physically consistent.

Morning:

Long shadows toward the west.

Afternoon:

Shorter shadows.

Evening:

Long shadows toward the east.

Overcast:

Very soft shadows.

Rain:

Minimal visible shadows.

---

# Atmospheric Effects

Lighting may naturally include:

* Morning mist.
* Dust illuminated by sunlight.
* Smoke catching light.
* Steam.
* Rain haze.
* Soft reflections.
* Water shimmer.

Effects should support realism rather than become visual spectacle.

---

# Continuity

Lighting should remain consistent within a scene.

Avoid:

* Sudden sunlight changes.
* Inconsistent shadow direction.
* Unexplained weather shifts.
* Interior light changing between shots.

Lighting continuity is as important as character continuity.

---

# Emotional Use of Light

Lighting should gently reinforce emotion.

Examples:

Warm family meal → soft warm interior light.

First rain → cool diffused atmosphere.

Festival evening → warm lamp glow.

Early morning farming → fresh sunrise light.

Quiet conversation → gentle natural illumination.

Light should enhance emotion without overwhelming it.

---

# Soft Blue Sky (Post-Monsoon outdoor only)

One production add-on that does **not** change art style:

Post-Monsoon clear day / morning outdoor shots should show a **bluey clear
blue sky**, fresh greeny greens, and brick reddy terracotta accents — slight
morning warmth only. Do not use “soft warm / muted / airy” wording that
whitewashes pigment. Do not add HDR, neon, or heavy sunset amber.

**Series outdoor lock:** Warm & Sunlit early-morning Ghibli structure with
**GENTLE COLOR POP** (bluey · greeny · reddy · clear yellow). See
`Color_And_Lighting_Formula.md`.

Art style authority remains: `Art_Style.md` and Mother/Father canon references.

Full seasonal tables: `01_Canon/01_Series_Style_Lock/Color_And_Lighting_Formula.md`.

---

# Guiding Principle

Lighting should feel like a natural part of the village itself.

The audience should never consciously notice the lighting system.

They should simply believe they are witnessing real life unfolding beneath the changing sky of a 1990s South Indian village.
