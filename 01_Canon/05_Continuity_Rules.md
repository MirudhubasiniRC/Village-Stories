# 04_Continuity_Rules.md

# Purpose

This document defines the continuity standards for the Village Stories universe.

Its purpose is to ensure that every episode, every shot, and every scene feels like part of one continuous world.

Continuity should never be treated as optional.

The audience should never feel that the world has been reset between episodes unless the story intentionally introduces the passage of time.

---

# Rule 1 — Continuous World

The village never stops existing.

When an episode ends, life continues.

Characters continue their routines.

Animals continue living naturally.

Seasons continue progressing.

The world exists beyond what the audience sees.

---

# Rule 2 — Episode Continuity

Within a single episode:

Maintain continuity for:

* Character positions
* Body posture
* Walking direction
* Eye direction
* Clothing
* Hairstyles
* Expressions
* Objects
* Lighting
* Weather
* Time of day
* Season
* Camera geography

Each shot should begin exactly where the previous shot ended unless the storyboard intentionally indicates a time or location transition.

---

# Rule 2B — Sudden Rain Loud Face Reaction (CANON-2026-006)

When rain **suddenly** begins (first hard drops, burst, squall, or unexpected
downpour mid-scene), any visible person must get a **loud face reaction beat**
before or as they act.

**Required face read (close or medium-close):**

* Eyes widen sharply.
* Eyebrows lift.
* Mouth opens in gasp / surprise / shout shape (readable, not a tiny smile).
* Whole face commits — not a calm glance at the sky only.

**Edit pattern (Growth Sprint):**

1. Rain hit / first hard drops (macro or wide).
2. **Face reaction cut** (loud — 0.7–2 s).
3. Practical next action (run under roof, grab clothes, cover food, laugh + dash).

Kids may react louder (delighted shriek-face or startled shout-face). Adults stay
in character but still show clear startle. Reject blank calm faces on sudden rain.

Does not apply to scenes that **open already raining** with no sudden onset.

---

# Rule 3 — Geographic Continuity

Permanent locations never move.

Maintain:

* Village layout
* Walking routes
* River Ghat
* Banyan Tree & Shrine
* Family House
* Paddy Fields
* Field Rest Area (paddy edge)
* Pond
* Irrigation Canal
* Waterfall Hamlet House via the northern upland route
* Upland cascade joining the river upstream outside the village boundary

Characters should always travel using logical routes through the established geography.

---

# Rule 4 — Environmental Continuity

The environment should remain consistent.

Maintain:

* River water level
* Pond condition
* Paddy growth stage
* Ground wetness
* Sky condition
* Vegetation
* Seasonal flowers
* Tree growth
* Shadows
* Wind direction when visible

Weather should not change suddenly without narrative justification.

---

# Rule 5 — Character Continuity

Recurring characters must remain recognizable.

Maintain:

* Appearance
* Height
* Relative scale
* Clothing logic
* Personality
* Habits
* Relationships
* Emotional state
* Physical condition

Characters should react naturally to previous events.

---

# Rule 6 — Object Continuity

Objects remain where they logically belong.

Examples:

* Cooking utensils remain in the kitchen.
* Farming tools return to storage after use.
* Bicycles are parked in familiar locations.
* The brass oil lamp stays in the puja corner unless moved.
* The grinding stone remains in its permanent position.

Objects should not disappear or relocate without explanation.

---

# Rule 7 — Seasonal Continuity

Every episode belongs to one defined season.

Maintain consistency for:

* Crops
* River flow
* Pond level
* Trees
* Flowers
* Rainfall
* Clothing
* Farming activities
* Animal behaviour

Season changes should occur gradually or be explicitly shown through the passage of time.

---

# Rule 8 — Historical Continuity

The historical setting remains the early 1990s.

Avoid introducing:

* Modern technology
* Contemporary vehicles
* Modern packaging
* Recent architecture
* Current fashion
* Present-day social habits

Every episode should preserve the historical identity of the series.

---

# Rule 9 — Emotional Continuity

Characters should remember meaningful experiences.

Previous events may influence:

* Relationships
* Behaviour
* Confidence
* Traditions
* Daily routines

The village should feel capable of remembering its own history.

---

# Rule 10 — Living Background

Background life should remain believable.

Examples include:

* Neighbours following familiar routines.
* Children taking the same route home from school.
* The postman delivering mail on his regular route.
* Villagers gathering at the River Ghat.
* The family dog resting in its favourite places.
* Chickens wandering around the courtyard.

Background activity should support the illusion of a living village.

---

# Rule 11 — Passage of Time

Time should leave visible traces.

Examples:

* Trees slowly mature.
* Buildings weather naturally.
* Objects become worn through use.
* Festivals return annually.
* Crops follow seasonal cycles.
* Children grow if the series timeline advances.

The world should evolve naturally rather than resetting.

---

# Rule 12 — Canon Updates

When a permanent change occurs, update the appropriate Canon documents.

Examples include:

* A new recurring neighbour.
* A newly built bridge.
* A permanent location.
* A major family milestone.
* A recurring tradition.

Canon should evolve intentionally, never accidentally.

---

# Continuity Checklist

Before finalizing an episode, verify:

✓ Characters remain consistent.

✓ Geography is correct.

✓ Lighting matches the time of day.

✓ Season is correct.

✓ Objects are logically placed.

✓ Background activity feels alive.

✓ Historical setting remains authentic.

✓ Previous story events are respected.

---

# Guiding Principle

Continuity is one of the foundations of immersion.

The audience should feel they are returning to the same village each time they watch, where every path, every home, every tree, every neighbour, and every memory still belongs.
