# 05_Timeline.md

# Purpose

This document records the official timeline of the Village Stories universe.

It ensures that stories, seasons, recurring events, character development, and permanent world changes remain historically consistent.

The timeline represents what has happened within the world—not the order in which episodes are published.

---

# Timeline Philosophy

Village Stories follows a continuous timeline.

Life moves forward naturally.

Days become weeks.

Weeks become months.

Seasons change.

Festivals return each year.

Children gradually grow.

Trees mature.

Buildings weather.

The village remembers its own past.

---

# Timeline Units

The universe is organized using the following structure:

Year

↓

Season

↓

Month (optional)

↓

Episode

↓

Scene

This allows both short-term continuity and long-term world building.

---

# Current Era

**Historical Period**

Early–Mid 1990s

The entire series takes place during this period unless an intentional time jump is introduced.

All stories, objects, clothing, occupations, transportation, and technology should remain historically appropriate for this era.

## Seasons

Each episode belongs to one season.

Available seasons:

* Summer
* Monsoon
* Post-Monsoon / Harvest

Seasonal details are maintained in the Season Tracker.

---

## Festivals

Festivals occur in their natural annual order.

Examples include:

* Pongal
* Tamil New Year
* Deepavali
* Vinayagar Chaturthi
* Karthigai Deepam

Festival stories should align naturally with the season and historical calendar.

---

# Passage of Time

Not every episode occurs on consecutive days.

Time may pass naturally between episodes.

Possible transitions include:

* Same day
* Next day
* A few days later
* Several weeks later
* New season
* New year (rare)

Whenever time passes significantly, it should be clear within the story.

---

# Character Growth

Characters may develop naturally over time.

Examples include:

* Learning new skills.
* Building friendships.
* Taking on new responsibilities.
* Forming new traditions.
* Gradually aging if the timeline advances sufficiently.

Growth should always be gradual and believable.

---

# World Events

Permanent events become part of the village's shared history.

Examples:

* A new bridge is constructed.
* A recurring neighbour moves into the village.
* The River Ghat receives new stone steps.
* A banyan tree branch falls during a storm.
* The family adopts a new calf.

Once established, these events become part of the official timeline.

# Timeline Rules

* Events cannot happen before they are introduced.
* Permanent changes remain permanent unless another story changes them.
* Character memories should respect previous events.
* Seasons should progress naturally.
* Festivals should occur only once per year.
* Historical accuracy must always be maintained.

---

# Future Time Jumps

If the series ever advances by months or years:

Update:

* Character ages.
* Season Tracker.
* Village Map (if required).
* Canon documents affected by the change.

Large time jumps should be rare and intentional.

---

# Guiding Principle

The timeline exists to preserve the feeling that Village Stories is one continuous life unfolding over time.

Every episode adds another chapter to the history of the village rather than existing as an isolated event.
