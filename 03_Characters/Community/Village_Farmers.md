# Village Farmers

## Purpose

This document defines the recurring broader farmer group of Village Stories.

Father and Neighbour Father retain individual canon; this document defines other farmers who make the agricultural world feel active and cooperative.

---

# Identity

Village Farmers are working adults shaped by seasonal land, water, livestock, field paths, and cooperative labour.

They are not interchangeable silhouettes. Vary faces, ages, builds, clothing colours, and tasks while preserving the same cultural world.

---

# Typical Locations

* Paddy Fields.
* Irrigation Canal.
* Coconut Grove.
* Grain Drying Yard when established.
* Livestock routes.
* Tea Stall mornings.
* Blacksmith Forge before major field work.

---

# Clothing and Appearance

Maintain:

* Cotton shirts or bare upper bodies only where heat and task make it natural and modest.
* Practical veshtis/dhotis, thundus, simple sandals or bare feet depending on work.
* Sun-warmed medium-brown complexions and real working bodies.
* No gym physiques, designer clothes, or modern machinery aesthetics.

---

# Behaviour

They naturally:

* Read weather and water.
* Share work during planting and harvest.
* Carry tools correctly.
* Rest in shade.
* Help a neighbour when a task requires more hands.
* Treat animals and land with practical care.

---

# Seasonal Behaviour

Summer: early starts, shade breaks, water awareness.

Monsoon: rain-led scheduling, canal and crop vigilance.

Harvest: more cooperative labour, grain movement, tired pride.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain farming realism, safety, and mutual-aid culture.

Never depict modern industrial farming, random machinery, or exaggerated hardship spectacle.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Village Farmers as a community group.

---

# Guiding Principle

Village Farmers should make the land feel worked by generations of knowledge, not used as a decorative background.
