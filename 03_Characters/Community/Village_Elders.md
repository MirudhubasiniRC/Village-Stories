# Village Elders

## Purpose

This document defines the recurring elder group of Village Stories.

Individual recurring elders, including Neighbour Family 01 and Lakshmi Paati, retain their own canon. This document defines the broader village elder presence.

---

# Identity

Typical age:

**60–85 years old**

Village Elders carry memory of older seasons, customs, routes, farming patterns, and community changes. They are respected but not treated as automatically correct in every matter.

---

# Typical Locations

* Sacred Banyan Tree platform.
* Village Square shade.
* Front thinnais.
* Festival Ground edge seating.
* Tea Stall bench when appropriate.

---

# Appearance and Clothing

Maintain:

* Natural elder bodies, slower but capable movement.
* Traditional cotton sarees for elder women; cotton shirts and veshtis for elder men.
* Grey hair, natural age lines, simple sandals or bare feet where appropriate.
* No stylized frailty, modern fashion, or identical faces.

---

# Behaviour

They naturally:

* Rest in shade.
* Discuss weather, crops, and village memory.
* Watch children with quiet affection.
* Offer advice when asked.
* Participate in prayers and festivals at an age-appropriate pace.

They do not exist merely to deliver exposition or settle every conflict.

---

# Sounds and Atmosphere

* Soft conversation.
* Occasional warm laugh.
* Walking-stick tap only if an individual canon establishes one.
* Rustle of cotton and leaves overhead.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always preserve dignity, variety, and lived knowledge.

Never make all elders identical, helpless, grumpy, or comic.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Village Elders as a community group.

---

# Guiding Principle

Village Elders should feel like living memory beneath the Banyan Tree: present, varied, and quietly woven into every season.
