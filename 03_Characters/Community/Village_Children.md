# Village Children

## Purpose

This document defines the recurring broader child group of Village Stories.

Elder Brother, Younger Daughter, and Neighbour Son retain individual canon; this document defines their schoolmates, playmates, and other village children.

---

# Identity

Typical age range:

**5–13 years old**

Village Children are curious, active, respectful of adults, and naturally shaped by school, paths, courtyards, fields, rain, animals, and seasonal celebrations.

---

# Typical Activities

* Walking to and from Primary School.
* Marbles, hide-and-seek, running games, and kite flying.
* Safe courtyard and playground play.
* Watching adults work.
* Paper boats in appropriate monsoon scenes.
* Festival excitement.
* Small age-appropriate chores.

River, pond, tree-climbing, and field-edge play always remain within established safety and adult-supervision rules.

---

# Appearance and Clothing

Maintain:

* Healthy varied South Indian child appearances.
* Simple early-1990s cotton shirts, shorts, frocks, pavadai-style clothing, and sandals/bare feet appropriate to activity.
* No branded sportswear, modern toys, or identical crowd designs.

---

# Behaviour

They naturally:

* Ask questions.
* Include younger children safely.
* Return home when called.
* Respect teachers and elders.
* Learn through observation.

They may make small mistakes, but cruelty and reckless danger are never default childhood traits.

---

# Sounds

* Laughter.
* Footsteps in packed earth.
* School recitation.
* Marbles clicking.
* Kite paper and string.
* Short excited calls.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain age-appropriate play, village geography awareness, and cultural respect.

Never turn children into unsupervised modern urban kids or constant slapstick chaos.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Village Children as a community group.

---

# Guiding Principle

Village Children should make every path, courtyard, and school ground feel like tomorrow is already growing there.
