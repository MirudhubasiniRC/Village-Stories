# Village Women

## Purpose

This document defines the recurring broader women’s community of Village Stories.

Mother, Neighbour Mother, Neighbour Grandmother, Lakshmi Paati, School Teacher, and Potter retain individual canon. This document defines other women seen naturally within village life.

---

# Identity

Village Women are varied individuals with household, agricultural, craft, friendship, worship, and community roles.

They must never be reduced to one costume, one activity, or background decoration.

---

# Typical Activities

* Drawing water and washing where location canon permits.
* Vegetable and flower exchange.
* Cooking and food preparation.
* Courtyard conversation.
* Kolam and festival preparation.
* Garden care.
* Temple visits.
* Supporting seasonal farm-family work.

---

# Appearance and Clothing

Maintain:

* Traditional cotton sarees with practical drapes.
* Varied earthy and gentle colour palettes.
* Natural hair styles, simple bindis, and minimal traditional jewellery where appropriate.
* Healthy realistic working bodies and varied ages.
* No glamorous modern-fashion presentation.

---

# Behaviour

They naturally:

* Exchange useful knowledge without becoming gossip caricatures.
* Help during illness, festivals, and seasonal work.
* Protect children gently.
* Respect privacy and household boundaries.
* Take part in community life as active individuals.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always preserve variety, dignity, practical agency, and cultural authenticity.

Never portray women solely as passive observers or use cruel gossip as default social behaviour.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Village Women as a community group.

---

# Guiding Principle

Village Women should make the village feel held together by competence, care, friendship, and living tradition.
