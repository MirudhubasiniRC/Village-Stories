# Village Vendors

## Purpose

This document defines the recurring broader vendor group of Village Stories.

The Tea Stall Owner and Grocery Shopkeeper retain individual canon. This document defines passing sellers and small local vendors who appear only when a story needs them.

---

# Identity

Village Vendors are practical small traders moving through familiar village routes with goods that suit early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu.

They remain occasional community texture, not a permanent market crowd unless a future Weekly Market location is established.

---

# Typical Goods

Depending on season and story:

* Flowers.
* Seasonal fruit.
* Small snack items.
* Baskets.
* Cloth or household essentials.
* Festival decorations.
* Simple toys at festival events.

All goods must remain period-correct and culturally local.

---

# Typical Locations

* Village Square.
* Main Village Path.
* Tea Stall approach.
* Festival Ground only during events.
* Banyan Tree edge when appropriate.

---

# Appearance and Behaviour

Maintain:

* Simple cotton work clothing.
* Baskets, cloth bundles, bicycle loads, or hand-carried goods appropriate to the vendor.
* Polite calls at modest volume.
* Respect for homes, shrine space, and village paths.
* Short appearances that do not replace established shopkeepers.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always preserve small-scale village trade and period authenticity.

Never introduce modern retail branding, loud commercial spectacle, or a permanent weekly market before that location is created.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Village Vendors as a community group.

---

# Guiding Principle

Village Vendors should arrive like a small change in the day’s rhythm—useful, local, and gone again without disturbing the village's permanence.
