# Character Behaviour Rules

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent behavioural principles governing every recurring character in the Village Stories universe.

It establishes how characters think, move, interact, express emotions, solve problems, and respond to everyday situations.

All individual character documents inherit these behavioural rules unless a documented exception exists.

---

# Philosophy

Behaviour reveals character more deeply than appearance.

Viewers should understand who a character is through their everyday actions rather than through dialogue or narration.

Small habits, quiet gestures, and familiar routines should communicate personality naturally.

---

# Core Behaviour Principles

Every recurring character should behave with:

* Patience.
* Kindness.
* Practicality.
* Humility.
* Respect.
* Emotional honesty.

Behaviour should emerge naturally from the character's personality and life experience.

---

# Natural Behaviour

Characters should behave as though they have lived in the village their entire lives.

They should:

* Know where things belong.
* Understand daily routines.
* Move confidently within familiar places.
* Interact naturally with neighbours.
* Respect village traditions.

Nothing should feel forced or artificial.

---

# Purposeful Actions

Every action should have a reason.

Examples:

* Carrying vegetables to the Kitchen.
* Watering plants before sunrise.
* Feeding livestock after breakfast.
* Cleaning tools after work.

Avoid unnecessary movement or repetitive actions without purpose.

---

# Movement

Movement should be:

* Relaxed.
* Efficient.
* Familiar.
* Unhurried.
* Confident.

Characters rarely rush unless a story specifically requires urgency.

---

# Emotional Behaviour

Characters express emotions naturally.

Typical emotional responses include:

* Happiness.
* Curiosity.
* Pride.
* Concern.
* Gratitude.
* Gentle sadness.
* Relief.

Emotions should develop gradually and remain appropriate to the situation.

Avoid exaggerated emotional reactions.

---

# Communication

Most communication is calm and respectful.

Maintain:

* Soft conversations.
* Patient listening.
* Eye contact when appropriate.
* Natural pauses.
* Gentle humour.

Silence is also an important form of communication.

---

# Problem Solving

Characters usually solve problems through:

* Experience.
* Cooperation.
* Observation.
* Patience.
* Traditional knowledge.

Conflict should rarely become dramatic.

---

# Family Behaviour

Family members naturally:

* Help one another.
* Share responsibilities.
* Respect elders.
* Encourage children.
* Eat together.
* Work together.

Family cooperation should appear effortless.

---

# Community Behaviour

Villagers naturally:

* Greet neighbours.
* Share food.
* Offer assistance.
* Exchange advice.
* Respect community traditions.

Hospitality should feel genuine rather than formal.

---

# Behaviour Around Children

Adults should:

* Teach patiently.
* Encourage curiosity.
* Protect without over-controlling.
* Allow children to learn through experience.

Children should be treated with warmth and respect.

---

# Behaviour Around Animals

Maintain:

* Gentle handling.
* Respect for livestock.
* Kindness toward the Family Dog.
* Natural interaction with wildlife.

Animals should never be mistreated for entertainment.

---

# Behaviour During Work

While working, characters should appear:

* Focused.
* Skilled.
* Calm.
* Efficient.

Work should feel familiar rather than difficult.

---

# Behaviour During Meals

Meals are shared experiences.

Maintain:

* Waiting for everyone.
* Serving others before oneself.
* Calm conversation.
* Appreciation for food.

Meals strengthen family relationships.

---

# Behaviour During Festivals

Characters become:

* More joyful.
* More social.
* More generous.
* More expressive.

Despite celebration, behaviour remains respectful and grounded.

---

# Behaviour During Rain

Rain naturally changes behaviour.

Examples:

* Watching the rain from the veranda.
* Bringing clothes indoors.
* Enjoying warm food.
* Slowing outdoor work.

Rain should create comfort rather than inconvenience.

---

# Quiet Moments

Characters should occasionally:

* Watch birds.
* Observe children.
* Sit beneath a tree.
* Listen to rain.
* Enjoy a cup of tea.
* Look across the fields.

These moments create emotional depth.

---

# Decision Making

Characters make decisions based on:

* Family wellbeing.
* Practical experience.
* Community values.
* Compassion.
* Respect for nature.

They should never behave only to advance the plot.

---

# Character Consistency

Each character should retain:

* Personal habits.
* Preferred routines.
* Emotional tendencies.
* Typical reactions.
* Individual strengths.

Behaviour should remain recognizable across episodes.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Calm pacing.
* Practical behaviour.
* Respectful interaction.
* Emotional authenticity.
* Cooperative relationships.

Never:

* Create unnecessary drama.
* Introduce modern urban attitudes without narrative reason.
* Make characters behave inconsistently.
* Force conflict for entertainment.

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the behavioural standards for every recurring character in the Village Stories universe.

Every story, image prompt, storyboard, animation, and production document should follow these principles unless a documented canonical exception exists.

---

# Guiding Principle

The people of Village Stories should feel real because of how they live, not because of what they say.

Their kindness, patience, quiet routines, and everyday choices should reveal their character, allowing viewers to feel they are spending time with genuine neighbours rather than fictional personalities.
