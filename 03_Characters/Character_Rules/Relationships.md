# Character Relationships

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent relationship principles governing all recurring characters in the Village Stories universe.

It establishes how family members, neighbours, villagers, visitors, and animals naturally interact with one another.

Rather than defining individual personalities, this document defines the social structure of the village.

All individual character relationship documents inherit these principles.

---

# Philosophy

Relationships are built through years of shared experiences.

Trust develops gradually.

Respect is earned naturally.

Friendships are maintained through everyday kindness rather than dramatic events.

The village functions as an extended family where cooperation is valued above competition.

---

# Relationship Principles

Every recurring relationship should be built upon:

* Respect.
* Trust.
* Kindness.
* Patience.
* Cooperation.
* Shared responsibility.

Relationships should strengthen slowly over time.

---

# Family Relationships

The central family forms the emotional heart of the series.

Their relationships are based on:

* Love.
* Mutual respect.
* Daily cooperation.
* Shared meals.
* Shared responsibilities.

Conflict should remain rare, gentle, and quickly resolved.

---

# Parent–Child Relationships

Parents should:

* Teach through example.
* Encourage curiosity.
* Offer gentle correction.
* Protect without limiting independence.
* Celebrate learning.

Children should:

* Respect parents.
* Ask questions.
* Learn through observation.
* Gradually accept responsibility.

Learning happens naturally during everyday life.

---

# Husband–Wife Relationship

The relationship between Father and Mother is based upon:

* Partnership.
* Mutual trust.
* Quiet understanding.
* Shared work.
* Emotional support.

Affection is shown primarily through actions rather than dramatic expressions.

Examples:

* Preparing favourite meals.
* Helping with difficult tasks.
* Sharing responsibilities.
* Looking after one another during illness.

---

# Extended Family

Relatives may occasionally visit.

Maintain:

* Warm hospitality.
* Shared meals.
* Respect for elders.
* Children playing together.

Visitors should feel naturally welcomed.

---

# Neighbour Relationships

Neighbours are trusted members of the community.

Typical interactions include:

* Borrowing ingredients.
* Sharing vegetables.
* Helping during harvest.
* Caring for children.
* Casual conversations.
* Visiting without formal invitation.

Neighbourhood relationships should feel relaxed and familiar.

---

# Village Relationships

Villagers generally know one another.

Maintain:

* Friendly greetings.
* Cooperative work.
* Community support.
* Respect for occupations.
* Shared celebrations.

The village functions through mutual trust.

---

# Teacher Relationships

Teachers are respected by:

* Children.
* Parents.
* Villagers.

Teaching extends beyond the classroom.

Teachers may gently guide children during everyday village life.

---

# Elder Relationships

Older villagers receive:

* Respect.
* Patience.
* Assistance.
* Attention.

Elders naturally share:

* Stories.
* Advice.
* Traditional knowledge.
* Cultural practices.

---

# Children's Relationships

Children naturally:

* Play together.
* Learn together.
* Explore together.
* Help adults.
* Care for animals.

Competition remains friendly.

Curiosity is encouraged.

---

# Relationship With Animals

Animals are respected as part of village life.

Examples:

Family Dog

* Trusted companion.
* Family member.
* Protective but gentle.

Cows

* Cared for daily.
* Handled calmly.

Chickens

* Move freely around the home.
* Familiar to children.

Wildlife

* Observed with curiosity.
* Not disturbed unnecessarily.

---

# Hospitality

Guests are welcomed with:

* Water.
* Tea or coffee.
* Fresh food.
* Conversation.
* Respect.

Hospitality is considered an important cultural value.

---

# Community Cooperation

Villagers naturally help one another.

Examples:

* Harvest work.
* House repairs.
* Festival preparation.
* Carrying heavy loads.
* Sharing seasonal produce.

Helping others is treated as ordinary rather than exceptional.

---

# Conflict

Conflict exists but remains realistic.

Typical disagreements involve:

* Misunderstandings.
* Small mistakes.
* Everyday inconveniences.

Conflicts should:

* Remain respectful.
* Avoid personal hostility.
* Be resolved through conversation, patience, or shared understanding.

Avoid prolonged interpersonal drama.

---

# Relationship Growth

Relationships may gradually deepen through:

* Shared experiences.
* Repeated cooperation.
* Seasonal traditions.
* Family milestones.
* Community events.

Growth should always feel earned.

---

# Emotional Support

During illness, loss, or difficulty:

* Family members assist one another.
* Neighbours offer help.
* Meals may be shared.
* Practical support is prioritized over dramatic speeches.

Care is demonstrated through actions.

---

# Storytelling Influence

Relationships should create stories naturally.

Examples:

* Sharing a harvest.
* Helping repair a roof.
* Cooking together.
* Teaching a child.
* Visiting an elderly neighbour.
* Caring for a sick family member.

The strongest stories often emerge from ordinary relationships.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Stable relationships.
* Respectful behaviour.
* Community cooperation.
* Gradual relationship growth.
* Consistent family dynamics.

Never:

* Introduce sudden hostility without narrative justification.
* Contradict established relationships.
* Force unnecessary interpersonal conflict.
* Break trust casually.

# Guiding Principle

The village is held together by relationships rather than buildings.

Families, neighbours, elders, children, and even animals form a quiet network of trust and care, where everyday acts of kindness create a sense of belonging that lasts across generations.
