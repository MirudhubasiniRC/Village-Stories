# Character Canon

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent canonical rules governing every recurring character in the Village Stories universe.

It establishes how characters maintain consistency across appearance, personality, behaviour, relationships, growth, and continuity.

All character documents inherit these rules unless a documented exception exists.

---

# Philosophy

Characters are permanent individuals.

They should feel like real people who continue living between episodes.

Their personality should remain recognizable.

Their habits should remain familiar.

Their relationships should develop naturally without contradicting established canon.

The audience should feel they are revisiting people they already know.

# Permanent Identity

Every recurring character possesses a permanent identity.

This includes:

* Family role.
* Appearance.
* Personality.
* Skills.
* Relationships.
* Daily routines.
* Values.

These traits should remain stable across stories.

---

# Character Consistency

Maintain consistency in:

* Facial features.
* Body proportions.
* Hair.
* Clothing style.
* Behaviour.
* Voice.
* Emotional responses.
* Physical abilities.
* Age progression.

Characters should never appear as different people between episodes.

---

# Growth

Characters may grow through experience.

Growth should be:

* Gradual.
* Believable.
* Consistent.
* Emotionally earned.

Core personality should remain recognizable.

---

# Memory

Recurring characters remember previous experiences.

Examples:

* Lessons learned.
* Family traditions.
* Important celebrations.
* Neighbour relationships.
* Personal preferences.

However, stories should remain understandable even for first-time viewers.

---

# Relationships

Relationships evolve slowly.

Examples:

* Trust increases.
* Children mature.
* Neighbours become closer.
* Family traditions strengthen.

Relationships should never change suddenly without narrative development.

---

# Skills

Each character develops specialized knowledge.

Examples include:

* Cooking.
* Farming.
* Gardening.
* Traditional crafts.
* Caring for animals.
* Household management.

Skills improve gradually through practice.

---

# Emotional Behaviour

Characters express genuine emotions.

Typical emotions include:

* Happiness.
* Curiosity.
* Concern.
* Gratitude.
* Pride.
* Sadness.
* Surprise.

Emotional reactions should always match the situation and the character's personality.

---

# Decision Making

Characters act according to:

* Personal values.
* Family traditions.
* Village culture.
* Practical experience.
* Compassion.

They should not behave merely to move the plot forward.

---

# Daily Routine

Each recurring character follows a predictable daily rhythm.

Individual routines are documented separately.

Stories should naturally fit within these established patterns.

---

# Cultural Consistency

Every character respects the cultural framework established in:

* 02_World/Culture/
* 02_World/Time/
* 02_World/Nature/

Characters should never behave in ways that contradict the established world.

---

# Physical Continuity

Maintain consistency in:

* Height.
* Build.
* Skin tone.
* Hair.
* Clothing preferences.
* Accessories.

Minor seasonal clothing changes are acceptable.

Permanent physical characteristics should not change.

---

# Story Participation

Not every recurring character must appear in every story.

Characters should appear only when their presence feels natural.

Forced appearances should be avoided.

---

# Temporary Changes

Temporary changes may include:

* Festival clothing.
* Rain protection.
* Minor injuries.
* Carrying tools.
* Seasonal activities.

After the story concludes, characters return to their established canonical state unless a permanent story event states otherwise.

---

# Canonical Changes

Permanent changes require meaningful narrative justification.

Examples:

* A child growing older.
* A new recurring neighbour.
* A permanent home renovation.
* Adoption of a new family animal.

Such changes must update the relevant character documentation.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Stable identity.
* Consistent behaviour.
* Logical growth.
* Natural relationships.
* Respect for established world rules.

Never:

* Redesign characters without reason.
* Change personalities between episodes.
* Ignore established relationships.
* Contradict previous canon.

# Canon Authority

This document is the highest authority governing recurring characters within the Village Stories universe.

Every character document, story, storyboard, animation, and production asset must follow these canonical rules unless a permanent, documented change is introduced.

---

# Guiding Principle

Characters should feel like lifelong neighbours.

The audience should recognize them instantly—not because they are repeatedly explained, but because they consistently look, behave, speak, and care in ways that remain true to who they are.
