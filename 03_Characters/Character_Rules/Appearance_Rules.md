# Character Appearance Rules

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent visual rules governing every recurring character in the Village Stories universe.

It establishes consistent standards for physical appearance, clothing, grooming, ageing, seasonal variation, and visual continuity.

All individual character documents inherit these rules unless a documented exception exists.

---

# Philosophy

Characters should always feel like the same people.

Their appearance should become instantly recognizable across hundreds of episodes.

Small changes may reflect weather, work, festivals, or the passage of time, but their core identity should never change unexpectedly.

Viewers should recognize a character even from a silhouette.

---

# Design Principles

Every recurring character should appear:

* Natural.
* Healthy.
* Practical.
* Authentic.
* Timeless.
* Consistent.

Avoid exaggerated proportions or overly stylized features.

The visual style should support the grounded atmosphere of Village Stories.

---

# Age Representation

Characters should always appear appropriate for their established age.

Maintain:

* Natural facial proportions.
* Appropriate body proportions.
* Realistic posture.
* Age-appropriate movement.

Do not artificially age or de-age characters between stories.

---

# Facial Consistency

Maintain consistent:

* Face shape.
* Jawline.
* Nose shape.
* Lip shape.
* Eye shape.
* Eyebrow shape.
* Ear shape.

These features define character identity.

---

# Eyes

Eyes should appear:

* Expressive.
* Calm.
* Warm.
* Natural.

Avoid exaggerated anime-style proportions.

Eye colour should remain naturally dark brown unless otherwise specified.

---

# Hair

Maintain consistency in:

* Hairstyle.
* Hair texture.
* Hair length.
* Hairline.
* Parting.

Hair should only change when a story explicitly requires it.

---

# Skin

Maintain:

* Consistent skin tone.
* Natural texture.
* Outdoor lifestyle appearance.

Avoid unrealistic smoothness or cosmetic perfection.

Characters who work outdoors may show gentle signs of sun exposure.

---

# Body Proportions

Maintain realistic proportions.

Characters should never appear:

* Overly muscular.
* Extremely thin.
* Cartoonishly exaggerated.

Their physique should reflect their lifestyle.

---

# Hands

Hands should reflect daily work.

Examples:

* Farmers may have stronger hands.
* Cooks may have experienced, practiced movements.
* Children have softer, smaller hands.

Hands should appear expressive during close-up scenes.

---

# Feet

Characters typically walk barefoot inside the house.

Outside:

* Barefoot when appropriate.
* Traditional sandals when required.

Modern footwear should remain uncommon.

---

# Clothing Principles

Clothing should always be:

* Practical.
* Clean.
* Traditional.
* Comfortable.
* Appropriate for weather and work.

Clothing should support movement rather than fashion.

---

# Seasonal Clothing

Summer

* Lightweight cotton.
* Light colours.

Monsoon

* Slightly darker fabrics.
* Clothing appropriate for damp conditions.

Cool Season

* Additional shawls or light wraps when appropriate.

Seasonal adjustments should remain subtle.

---

# Festival Clothing

During festivals:

Maintain:

* Freshly washed traditional clothing.
* Brighter colours.
* Flowers.
* Simple jewellery.

Festival clothing should remain consistent with village traditions.

---

# Accessories

Accessories should remain minimal.

Examples:

* Bangles.
* Earrings.
* Traditional chains.
* Hair flowers.
* Waist towel.
* Cloth shoulder towel.

Avoid modern fashion accessories.

---

# Cleanliness

Characters should appear:

* Well cared for.
* Naturally clean.
* Appropriate for their activity.

Temporary dirt from farming or cooking is acceptable.

After work, characters generally clean themselves before family meals.

---

# Physical Changes

Temporary changes may include:

* Wet clothing after rain.
* Flour while cooking.
* Mud after farming.
* Ash near the stove.
* Sweat during summer.

These changes disappear naturally after the activity.

---

# Permanent Features

Permanent features should never change without a documented canonical event.

Examples:

* Height.
* Build.
* Facial features.
* Hair style.
* Skin tone.
* Distinguishing marks.

These define long-term visual identity.

---

# Silhouette Recognition

Each recurring character should have a recognizable silhouette.

Recognition should be possible through:

* Hairstyle.
* Clothing shape.
* Body posture.
* Movement.

Silhouette consistency strengthens visual storytelling.

---

# Animation Consistency

Every image and animation should preserve:

* Facial proportions.
* Body proportions.
* Clothing style.
* Hair.
* Colour palette.

Avoid gradual visual drift across episodes.

---

# Camera Considerations

Close-ups should preserve:

* Natural facial expressions.
* Skin texture.
* Hair details.
* Hand movements.
* Eye direction.

Wide shots should preserve:

* Body proportions.
* Clothing silhouette.
* Character posture.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Consistent appearance.
* Traditional clothing.
* Natural ageing.
* Realistic proportions.
* Practical presentation.

Never:

* Redesign characters between episodes.
* Introduce modern fashion without narrative reason.
* Change physical identity unexpectedly.
* Exaggerate anatomy.

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the visual standards for every recurring character in the Village Stories universe.

All future stories, image prompts, animations, storyboards, and production assets must preserve these appearance rules unless a documented canonical change is introduced.

---

# Guiding Principle

A character's appearance should tell the quiet story of their life.

Their face, clothing, posture, and hands should reflect years of familiar routines, honest work, and deep connection to the village, allowing viewers to recognize them immediately and believe they have always belonged there.
