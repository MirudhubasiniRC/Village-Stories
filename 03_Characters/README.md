# Character Documentation

## Purpose

This folder contains the complete character documentation for the Village Stories universe.

It defines every recurring character, their appearance, personality, relationships, behaviour, clothing, routines, expressions, and continuity rules.

Rather than treating characters as isolated individuals, this documentation ensures they remain consistent across every story, image prompt, storyboard, animation, and future production document.

---

# Philosophy

Characters are the emotional heart of Village Stories.

The world gives them a place to live.

The Kitchen gives them work to do.

The stories give them purpose.

Each character should feel like a real person with consistent habits, emotions, strengths, weaknesses, and relationships.

Viewers should recognize a character immediately—not because they are introduced, but because they always behave like themselves.

## Family

Defines the main family featured throughout the series.

Members include:

* Father
* Mother
* Elder Brother
* Younger Daughter
* Family Dog (German Shepherd)
* Maternal Grandmother / Maternal Paati — recurring extended family in the
  Waterfall Hamlet

These characters appear most frequently and receive the most detailed documentation.

### Family Children Canon

The family has two children:

* Elder Brother — older son; outdoor learning with Father; school-going village boy.
* Younger Daughter — younger sister; household and garden learning with Mother; curious and gentle.

They appear together often, learn differently by age, and remain visually and behaviourally distinct forever.

### Family Dog Canon

The Family Dog is a German Shepherd.

He is a loyal village companion and gentle guardian of the family compound—never a police, military, or attack-dog portrayal.

# Character Design Principles

Every recurring character should maintain:

* Consistent appearance.
* Consistent personality.
* Consistent clothing style.
* Consistent body language.
* Consistent relationships.
* Consistent speech style.
* Consistent daily routine.

Characters should never change simply because the story changes.

---

# Storytelling Philosophy

Stories should emerge from the characters rather than happen to them.

Small actions should reveal personality.

Examples:

* The way Mother prepares vegetables.
* The way Father carries harvested crops.
* The way Elder Brother helps with outdoor work.
* The way Younger Daughter watches and learns.
* The way the Family Dog patiently waits outside the Kitchen.

These recurring behaviours build emotional connection over time.

---

# Growth Philosophy

Characters may grow through experience.

However, their core identity should remain stable.

Growth should feel gradual, believable, and rooted in everyday life rather than dramatic transformation.

---

# Canon Authority

This folder permanently defines every recurring character in the Village Stories universe.

Every future story, image prompt, storyboard, animation, and production document should follow these character specifications unless a deliberate canonical change is introduced.

---

# Guiding Principle

The people of Village Stories should feel like neighbours rather than fictional characters.

Their kindness, routines, traditions, and quiet moments should invite the audience into a world that feels familiar, comforting, and timeless.
