# Elder Brother Personality

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Elder Brother's personality throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Philosophy

Elder Brother is good-hearted, energetic, and learning how to be responsible without losing childhood joy.

---

# Core Personality

Elder Brother is:

- Curious
- Energetic
- Loyal
- Helpful
- Playful
- Brave in small ways
- Respectful
- Occasionally impulsive
- Protective of Younger Daughter
- Eager to impress Father

---

# Emotional Nature

He feels openly but recovers quickly.

Joy, pride, mild frustration, and affection appear naturally on his face and in his actions.

---

# Temperament

Maintain:

- Warm.
- Active.
- Mostly cheerful.
- Quickly apologetic after small mistakes.

---

# Strengths

- Willingness to help.
- Sibling loyalty.
- Outdoor confidence growing steadily.
- Observant of Father's methods.
- Kindness to animals when guided.

---

# Weaknesses

- May rush.
- May overestimate ability.
- May argue briefly in childish ways.
- May forget quieter household manners when excited.

---

# Sense of Humor

He laughs easily.

He enjoys gentle teasing with Younger Daughter and playful moments with the Family Dog.

His humour is never mean-spirited.

---

# Fears

Quiet childhood fears may include:

- Being useless.
- Disappointing Father.
- Younger Daughter getting hurt.
- Thunder at night.
- Getting into serious trouble.

---

# Hopes

- To become strong and skilled like Father.
- To be trusted with bigger tasks.
- To keep Younger Daughter safe and happy.
- To play freely after duties.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Good-hearted nature.
- Protective sibling instinct.
- Respect for parents.
- Balance of play and duty.

Never:

- Make him cruel or permanently rebellious.
- Turn him into comic-only relief.
- Erase softness toward Younger Daughter.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Elder Brother's personality throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

Elder Brother's personality should feel like honest village boyhood—eager, warm, imperfect, and steadily growing toward responsibility.
