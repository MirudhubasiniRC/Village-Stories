# Elder Brother Voice & Body Language

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Elder Brother's voice, speech, body language, and signature sounds throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Philosophy

Elder Brother communicates with youthful energy and growing self-control.

---

# Voice Characteristics

- Clear boyish voice.
- Medium-high child pitch appropriate to age 9–10.
- Warm.
- Quick when excited.
- Softer when speaking to Mother or comforting Younger Daughter.

---

# Speaking Style

- Short energetic sentences.
- Questions often.
- Respectful address to adults.
- Playful talk with friends and Younger Daughter.

He rarely sounds adult or formal.

---

# Walking and Movement

- Quick steps.
- Occasional running in compound.
- Sudden stops when called.
- Squatting beside Father.
- Restless hands when excited.

---

# Signature Gestures

- Wiping sweat like Father with a small cloth imitation.
- Holding Younger Daughter's hand on busier paths.
- Pointing toward fields with pride.
- Scratching the Family Dog.
- Standing straight when given a task.

---

# Signature Sounds

- Bare feet on courtyard earth.
- Laughing with friends.
- Calling Younger Daughter by familiar sibling habit.
- Soft commands or play-sounds with the German Shepherd.
- School-path chatter.

---

# Google Flow Performance Rules

Maintain:

- Natural child movement speed.
- Energetic but believable motion.
- Clear age in voice and gesture.

Avoid:

- Adult body language.
- Overacted slapstick as default.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain boyish voice, quick movement, and respectful speech to adults.

Never deepen his voice into teenage range without a documented time jump.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Elder Brother's voice and body language throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

Elder Brother should be recognizable by his quick footsteps, eager voice, and the way his energy softens around family.
