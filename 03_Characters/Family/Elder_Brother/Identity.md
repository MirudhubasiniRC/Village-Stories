# Elder Brother Identity

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the canonical identity of Elder Brother within the Village Stories universe.

It establishes who he is as a person, his role within the family, his values, motivations, responsibilities, and narrative purpose.

---

# Core Identity

Elder Brother is one of the primary recurring family characters of Village Stories.

He is the older son of Father and Mother and the elder sibling of Younger Daughter.

His identity is built upon curiosity, growing responsibility, loyalty, playfulness, and respect.

He wants to be useful.

He wants to be brave in small ways.

He still needs guidance and rest like any child.

---

# Narrative Role

Elder Brother represents:

* Boyhood.
* Apprenticeship to Father.
* Sibling protection.
* School life.
* Outdoor courage in safe measures.
* The bridge between play and duty.

---

# Family Role

Within the family, Elder Brother is the older child who:

* Helps Father with age-appropriate outdoor work.
* Learns household respect from Mother.
* Watches over Younger Daughter.
* Shares space and affection with the Family Dog.
* Brings school stories home.

He is not a second adult.

He is a child learning responsibility.

---

# Personal Values

Elder Brother consistently values:

* Family.
* Fairness.
* Courage in small acts.
* Helping.
* Learning.
* Play.
* Respect for parents and elders.
* Protecting Younger Daughter.

---

# Life Philosophy

Elder Brother believes useful children make their parents proud.

He finds satisfaction in:

* Completing a small task well.
* Being trusted with tools.
* Making Younger Daughter laugh.
* Playing after work.
* Hearing Father's quiet approval.
* Mother's warm food after school or chores.

---

# Strengths

* Quick observation.
* Willingness to help.
* Physical energy.
* Loyalty.
* Growing outdoor confidence.
* Affectionate sibling nature.

---

# Limitations

He may occasionally:

* Overestimate what he can carry or do.
* Become impatient.
* Get distracted by play.
* Feel jealous of attention in small childish ways.
* Become tired and quiet after long days.

These remain gentle and age-appropriate.

---

# Motivations

* To help Father.
* To make Mother proud.
* To protect Younger Daughter.
* To learn village skills.
* To play with friends and the Family Dog.
* To feel capable.

---

# Relationship With Work

Work is becoming meaningful.

He still loves play, but feels pride when trusted with real tasks.

---

# Relationship With Learning

He learns from:

* Father outdoors.
* Mother at home.
* School teachers.
* Watching elders.
* Trying and correcting mistakes.

---

# Story Function

Elder Brother often anchors stories involving:

* Outdoor learning.
* Sibling care.
* School.
* Village play.
* Small adventures near home.
* Helping during harvest or rain preparations.

---

# Character Arc

He grows gradually through:

* New skills.
* Better judgment.
* Deeper care for Younger Daughter.
* Seasonal experience.

His core identity as a good-hearted village boy remains constant.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Age-appropriate responsibility.
* Respect for parents.
* Protective warmth toward Younger Daughter.
* Curiosity.
* Playfulness balanced with duty.

Never:

* Make him cruel.
* Turn him into an adult surrogate.
* Erase his need for guidance.
* Create harsh sibling rivalry as a default.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Elder Brother's identity within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

Elder Brother is becoming himself through ordinary days.

His greatest strength is not perfection, but the honest wish to help, protect, learn, and belong.
