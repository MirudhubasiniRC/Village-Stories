# Maternal Grandmother — Maternal Paati

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Mother's mother, the children's maternal
grandmother, for Village Stories.

Authority: `CANON-2026-001`.

## Identity

- Production identity: **Maternal Paati**
- Personal name: intentionally open until a story earns and records it
- Age: approximately **62–70**
- Residence: Waterfall Hamlet House, north of the main village
- Relationship: Mother's mother; maternal grandmother of Elder Brother and
  Younger Daughter

She is not Lakshmi Paati of House N-04 and not the Neighbour Grandmother of
House N-02.

## Character

Maternal Paati is warm, capable, observant, and practically affectionate. She
shows love by preparing food, keeping small things ready for expected visitors,
drying wet hair, sharing household knowledge, and giving children safe useful
tasks.

She is active for her age and never framed as helpless, mystical, or dependent
merely to create emotion.

Default everyday clothing locked by the approved reference:

- muted indigo / slate-blue handloom cotton saree;

- matching short-sleeved blouse;
- natural matte cotton texture;
- minimal jewellery.

Other muted traditional colours may appear in later episodes only when
story-justified and clearly the same woman. Do not redesign her face, bun,
build, or default indigo–maroon identity casually.

No heavy makeup, glamorous jewellery, modern clothing, or cosmetically youthful
face.

## Skills

She naturally knows:

- clay-stove cooking;
- managing coconut husk, kindling, and firewood in wet weather;
- fermented batter and traditional kuzhi paniyaram;
- coconut chutney grinding;
- garden and hill-hamlet household routines;
- safe observation of rain and stream conditions.

She is not a miracle healer or magical remedy source.

## Relationships

### Mother

Their relationship is familiar, respectful, and practical. Mother knows
Maternal Paati's routines and anticipates ordinary needs without treating her as
incapable.

### Elder Brother

She trusts him with age-appropriate tasks such as protecting a dry bundle,
arranging kindling under supervision, carrying a covered basket, and helping
younger children safely.

### Younger Daughter

She gives the girl small tactile tasks, dries her hair after rain, and keeps
familiar bowls or snacks ready when visits are expected.

### Father

They share respectful family warmth. Father assists with heavy repairs or travel
when needed but does not control her household.

### Family Dog

The dog is a familiar visitor. He waits at the veranda edge, remains calm around
the stove, and never runs toward the waterfall or stream.

## Voice And Body Language

- mature Tamil cadence; minimal dialogue by default;
- warm small laugh;
- clear practical instruction;
- precise cooking hands;
- gentle towel-drying or serving gestures;
- affection shown through preparation rather than speeches.

Signature sounds:

- cotton saree movement;
- bowl placed on wood or stone;
- coconut husk crackle;
- paniyaram batter sizzle;
- wooden pick turning cooked paniyaram.

## Continuity Rules

Always:

- preserve her maternal-family identity;
- preserve the Waterfall Hamlet residence;
- keep her distinct from existing village elder women;
- maintain dignity and practical independence;
- use an adult escort when the children visit during heavy rain.

Never:

- relocate her into N-02 or N-04;
- make the waterfall supernatural;
- use illness, abandonment, or poverty as emotional bait;
- send the children across floodwater or a dangerous cascade;
- treat wet-weather competence as magical knowledge.
