# Family Dog Appearance

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the canonical physical appearance of the Family Dog throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Identity

The Family Dog is a healthy adult German Shepherd living as a rural family companion in early-1990s Tamil Nadu.

He should look strong, intelligent, and lived-in—not like a polished show dog or cinematic action dog.

---

# Estimated Age

Approximately:

**3–5 years old**

Prime adult dog years—energetic but settled, fully grown.

---

# Sex

Male.

Consistent across all episodes.

---

# Size

Maintain:

- Medium-large German Shepherd build.
- Strong but not oversized monstrous proportions.
- Clear size above the children in presence, never threatening.

Approximate feel:

- Shoulder height consistent with a real German Shepherd.
- Longer than he is tall.
- Powerful gait with natural elegance.

---

# Head

Maintain:

- Classic German Shepherd head.
- Intelligent dark eyes.
- Alert but soft expression with family.
- Black muzzle common to the breed look.
- Erect ears, mobile and expressive.

---

# Body

Maintain:

- Strong chest.
- Straight athletic back appropriate to a working-family dog.
- Healthy weight from village life and care.
- Neither starved nor overfed.
- Tail with natural shepherd carriage; expressive but not constantly hyper.

---

# Coat Overview

Full coat details are locked in Coat_And_Markings.md.

Summary:

- Classic black-and-tan German Shepherd pattern.
- Medium outdoor coat.
- Natural village texture—clean enough from family care, not salon-perfect.

---

# Eyes

- Dark brown.
- Intelligent.
- Soft with family.
- Alert toward unfamiliar sounds.

---

# Recognizable Features

The Family Dog should remain instantly recognizable through:

- German Shepherd silhouette.
- Black-and-tan saddle pattern.
- Erect ears.
- Intelligent eyes.
- Calm compound presence.
- Loyal orientation toward family members.

Even in distant shots he should read as the same dog.

---

# Environmental Appearance

After play:

- Light dust on paws and lower legs.

After rain:

- Damp coat.
- Natural drying in shade.

After rest:

- Occasional grass or straw bits.

Temporary changes disappear naturally.

---

# Animation Consistency

Preserve:

- Breed silhouette.
- Coat pattern.
- Ear shape.
- Size relative to family.
- Facial identity.

Avoid visual drift into Indie dog, Labrador, or wolf-like redesigns.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain adult male German Shepherd appearance.

Never:

* Change breed.
* Make him a puppy permanently after establishment.
* Over-stylize with armor, harness fashion, or police gear.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Family Dog's physical appearance throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Family Dog should look like a real German Shepherd who belongs to a village family—noble, familiar, and softened by love rather than hardened by training spectacle.
