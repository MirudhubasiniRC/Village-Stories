# Family Dog Coat & Markings

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Family Dog's coat, colour, markings, grooming state, and seasonal coat behaviour throughout the Village Stories universe.

This is the dog equivalent of a clothing continuity document.

---

# Philosophy

His coat should communicate breed identity immediately and remain identical across episodes.

Village life may add temporary dust or dampness, but the underlying pattern never changes.

---

# Breed Coat Type

Maintain:

- Classic German Shepherd double-coat feel.
- Medium length suitable for outdoor Tamil Nadu life.
- Natural weather response.
- Healthy sheen from care—not oily, not glamorous.

---

# Permanent Colour Pattern

Canonical pattern:

- Black-and-tan German Shepherd marking.
- Black saddle across the back.
- Tan to rich warm brown on legs, chest sides, and facial points as breed-typical.
- Black muzzle.
- Darker ears.
- Lighter tan on lower legs and eyebrows points as natural breed detail.

This pattern is permanent.

---

# Marking Stability

Never randomly:

- Flip saddle coverage.
- Recolour him solid black.
- Recolour him solid brown.
- Add white patches not previously established.
- Change him to sable-only or unrelated patterns between episodes.

If a tiny unique marking (for example a subtle chest detail) is introduced, document it here before use and keep forever.

Default canon uses classic clean black-and-tan without special unique scars unless a story permanently adds one.

---

# Grooming State

Everyday:

- Naturally maintained by family care and outdoor living.
- Not tangled into neglect.
- Not brushed into show perfection.

Festival-adjacent clean scenes:

- May appear freshly bathed and fluffier.
- Pattern remains identical.

---

# Seasonal Coat Behaviour

Summer:

- Coat may appear slightly flatter.
- Heat panting common in shade.

Monsoon:

- Coat darkens when wet.
- Dries in sheltered areas.
- Mud on paws and lower legs is temporary.

Cooler mornings:

- Coat looks fuller in soft light.

---

# Dirt and Temporary Effects

Allowed temporary effects:

- Dust.
- Water darkening.
- Straw bits.
- Wet paw prints.

These must reset naturally.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain classic black-and-tan German Shepherd coat identity.

Never redesign colourway for aesthetic variety.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Family Dog's coat and markings throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

His coat should be as recognizable as Mother's hairstyle or Father's silhouette—same black saddle, same tan points, same dog, every episode.
