# Family Dog Continuity

## Purpose

This document acts as the final continuity checklist for the Family Dog.

---

# Permanent Identity

Always preserve:

- Family companion and gentle compound guardian.
- German Shepherd.
- Male.
- Adult age approximately 3–5.
- Rural Tamil Nadu early-1990s household dog.

---

# Permanent Appearance

Always preserve:

- Classic German Shepherd silhouette.
- Black-and-tan saddle pattern.
- Erect ears.
- Intelligent dark eyes.
- Medium-large healthy build.
- Village-real coat texture.

---

# Permanent Personality

Always preserve:

- Loyal.
- Intelligent.
- Gentle with family.
- Calmly alert.
- Playful in bursts.
- Peaceful with known livestock.

---

# Permanent Behaviour

Always preserve:

- Kitchen-entrance waiting.
- Father-return greeting.
- Child companionship modulated by age.
- Shade resting in Backyard / Thinnai / Courtyard.
- No default chicken chasing.
- No police/military portrayal.

---

# Permanent Relationships

Always preserve:

- Strong bond with Father.
- Respectful closeness to Mother.
- Active play bond with Elder Brother.
- Soft comfort bond with Younger Daughter.
- Peaceful familiarity with family livestock.

---

# Never List

Never:

- Change breed.
- Turn him into an Indie street dog redesign.
- Make him vicious.
- Add attack training aesthetic.
- Give fashion accessories as identity.

---

# AI Consistency Rule

Google Flow must always generate the same German Shepherd.

Do not alter breed, coat pattern, size, face, or temperament between episodes.

---

# Canon Authority

This document is the final continuity reference for the Family Dog.

---

# Guiding Principle

No matter how many stories are told, the Family Dog should always feel like the same loyal German Shepherd at the edge of every family scene—waiting, watching, and belonging.
