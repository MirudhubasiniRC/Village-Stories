# Family Dog Voice & Body Language

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Family Dog's sounds, movement, and physical communication throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Voice / Sound Characteristics

Canonical sounds include:

- Soft pant.
- Low friendly woof greeting.
- Occasional deeper warning bark at night unknowns.
- Content sigh when lying down.
- Light collar-free fur movement and paw sounds on earth.
- Quiet whine only for strong need or storm unease.

He is not constantly barking.

Silence and breathing are part of his audio identity.

---

# Movement Style

Maintain:

- Strong shepherd trot.
- Efficient compound walking.
- Sudden stillness when listening.
- Playful bursts that settle quickly.
- Careful paws near Kitchen and garden edges.

---

# Tail Language

- Soft wag for family greeting.
- Neutral rest carriage when calm.
- Higher alert attention when watching something new.
- Never manic constant wagging.

---

# Ear Language

- Forward for interest.
- Softly angled for ease.
- Highly mobile and expressive.

---

# Signature Body Habits

- Waiting with front paws aligned at Kitchen entrance.
- Circling once before lying down.
- Leaning lightly against Elder Brother after play.
- Placing himself between Younger Daughter and uncertain space without aggression.
- Escorting Father to the thinnai edge then resting.

---

# Google Flow Performance Rules

Natural quadruped motion, believable weight, consistent size, no slapstick default.

---

# Audio Identity

Viewers should recognize him through:

- Paw sounds on packed earth.
- Soft pant in heat.
- Warm greeting woof.
- Quiet presence under dialogue.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain restrained vocal behaviour and shepherd-true movement.

Never make nonstop barking his personality.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Family Dog's voice and body language throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should be recognizable in sound and motion alone—the steady trot home, the soft wait at the Kitchen, the warm greeting when Father returns.
