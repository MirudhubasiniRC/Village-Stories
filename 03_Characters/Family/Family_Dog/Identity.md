# Family Dog Identity

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the canonical identity of the Family Dog within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Core Identity

The Family Dog is a German Shepherd and a permanent emotional member of the family.

He is not livestock.

He is not a prop.

He is a companion with memory, preference, loyalty, and recognizable habits.

---

# Narrative Role

He represents:

* Belonging.
* Watchfulness without fear-mongering.
* Childhood safety.
* The living threshold between house and yard.

---

# Family Role

Within the family he:

* Greets returning members.
* Stays near children during play.
* Respects Kitchen boundaries.
* Visits animals peacefully.
* Sleeps in familiar compound places.

---

# Values Expressed Through Behaviour

* Loyalty.
* Patience.
* Gentleness with family.
* Alertness without panic.
* Soft courage.

---

# Motivations

* To remain near the family.
* To watch the compound.
* To play when invited.
* To rest in known shade.
* To follow Father on safe outdoor circuits.
* To stay close when Younger Daughter is unsettled.

---

# Relationship With Work

He does not farm.

His "work" is presence, warning soft alerts, and companionship.

---

# Story Function

He often appears as:

* Emotional glue in quiet scenes.
* Soft comic warmth.
* A moving continuity marker across locations.
* Comfort during rain, dusk, and waiting.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* German Shepherd identity.
* Family companion role.
* Gentle guardian temperament.
* Peace with household animals.

Never:

* Turn him vicious.
* Make him chase family chickens as comedy default.
* Redesign him as another breed.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Family Dog's identity within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Family Dog's identity is loyalty made visible—steady, warm, and forever part of the household.
