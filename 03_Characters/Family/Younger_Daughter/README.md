# Younger Daughter

## Purpose

This folder contains the complete canonical documentation for the Younger Daughter, one of the primary recurring family characters in the Village Stories universe.

She is the younger child of Father and Mother, and the beloved little sister of Elder Brother.

Her documentation defines her identity, appearance, personality, behaviour, relationships, routines, skills, and continuity.

Every image prompt, animation, storyboard, and story involving Younger Daughter should reference this documentation.

---

# Character Role

Younger Daughter is the family's younger child and Mother's primary household apprentice.

She learns cooking observation, gardening, kindness, and traditional home life through everyday closeness.

Her curiosity, gentleness, wonder, and affection for Elder Brother and the Family Dog shape many tender stories.

---

# Narrative Function

Younger Daughter represents:

* Childhood wonder.
* Gentleness.
* Learning through watching.
* Sibling trust.
* Kitchen and garden discovery.
* Emotional softness of the home.

She often becomes the emotional lens through which viewers rediscover ordinary village beauty.

---

# Primary Activities

Younger Daughter naturally:

* Watches Mother cook.
* Helps with tiny safe tasks.
* Visits the Vegetable Garden.
* Plays in the Courtyard and Backyard.
* Follows Elder Brother when safe.
* Sits with the Family Dog.
* Asks many questions.

# Continuity Note on Older Documents

Some earlier documents refer simply to "Daughter."

In current canon, those references indicate **Younger Daughter**, unless a passage clearly means children in general.

Mother remains appearance-locked; her relationship language may still say "Daughter," which maps to Younger Daughter.
