# Younger Daughter Clothing

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Younger Daughter's wardrobe throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Philosophy

Younger Daughter dresses with simple rural childhood practicality and modest traditional charm.

Clothes support play, tiny chores, and family life—not fashion.

---

# Everyday Clothing

Canonical everyday clothing:

- Simple cotton frock or skirt-and-blouse common to rural Tamil Nadu girls in the early 1990s.
- Soft practical fabrics.
- Easy movement for play.
- Bare feet in compound; sandals outside.

---

# Colour Palette

Preferred:

- Soft yellow
- Cream
- Light green
- Soft pink
- Sky blue
- Mild floral or simple prints
- Earth-gentle tones

Avoid neon colours and modern character prints.

---

# Festival Clothing

- Cleaner brighter traditional frock or pavadai-style dressing appropriate to age and period.
- Neat hair with flowers possible.
- Still childlike and modest.
- No heavy jewellery.

Small earrings may appear if already established as normal for her; keep minimal and traditional.

---

# Play and Helping Adjustments

- Dress may wrinkle.
- Hem may show dust after courtyard play.
- Sleeves or skirt carefully managed near Kitchen under Mother's watch.

---

# Footwear

- Barefoot at home/compound when practical.
- Simple sandals for school-adjacent or village walking scenes if she accompanies family.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Simple cotton childhood clothing.
- Period-correct early 1990s village look.
- Modest traditional styling.

Never:

- Modern fashion dresses as default.
- Makeup.
- Heavy jewellery.
- Western branded kidswear.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines Younger Daughter's wardrobe throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

Younger Daughter's clothing should feel soft, practical, and remembered—like a real village little girl's everyday frock after play and before dinner.
