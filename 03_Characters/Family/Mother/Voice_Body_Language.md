# Mother Voice & Body Language

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Mother's voice, speech patterns, body language, gestures, movement, signature habits, and characteristic sounds throughout the Village Stories universe.

These characteristics should remain consistent across every story, image prompt, Google Flow scene, storyboard, animation, and future production asset.

This document defines how Mother naturally communicates through both speech and movement.

---

# Philosophy

Mother communicates gently.

She rarely needs loud words or dramatic gestures.

Her presence is calming because her movements, expressions, voice, and actions naturally convey warmth, confidence, and care.

---

# Voice Characteristics

Mother's voice is:

- Soft.
- Warm.
- Gentle.
- Calm.
- Clear.
- Mature.
- Comforting.

She never sounds rushed or harsh.

Her voice naturally reassures those around her.

---

# Speaking Pace

Maintain:

- Slow to moderate pace.
- Natural pauses.
- Calm rhythm.
- Clear pronunciation.

She allows conversations to breathe.

---

# Speaking Volume

Normally:

- Soft conversational volume.

Occasionally:

- Slightly louder when calling family members from another room or across the courtyard.

She rarely raises her voice.

---

# Tone

Mother's tone should always feel:

- Caring.
- Respectful.
- Patient.
- Encouraging.
- Reassuring.

Even when correcting someone, her tone remains gentle.

---

# Communication Style

Mother prefers:

- Simple sentences.
- Practical advice.
- Gentle encouragement.
- Kind observations.

She avoids unnecessary criticism.

---

# Listening Style

When listening Mother naturally:

- Maintains soft eye contact.
- Slightly nods.
- Waits before responding.
- Gives full attention.

She rarely interrupts.

---

# Laughter

Mother laughs softly.

Maintain:

- Quiet laughter.
- Gentle smile.
- Relaxed shoulders.
- Warm eyes.

Her laughter should feel genuine and contagious without becoming loud.

---

# Walking Style

Mother walks:

- Smoothly.
- Quietly.
- With balanced posture.
- At an unhurried pace.

Years of familiar routines make every movement efficient.

---

# Standing Posture

Maintain:

- Relaxed shoulders.
- Upright posture.
- Balanced weight.
- Comfortable stance.

She never appears stiff.

---

# Sitting Style

Mother usually sits:

- Cross-legged on the floor.
- Comfortably on the veranda.
- Naturally while preparing vegetables.

Her posture remains relaxed.

---

# Hand Gestures

Mother's hands move naturally while speaking.

Typical gestures include:

- Gentle pointing.
- Open palms.
- Small circular motions while explaining.
- Light hand movements while teaching.

Gestures remain economical and purposeful.

---

# Signature Gestures

Mother often:

- Adjusts her saree pallu before beginning work.
- Brushes loose hair behind her ear.
- Wipes her hands on her cotton towel.
- Gently places a hand on Daughter's shoulder.
- Nods softly while listening.
- Checks the clay stove before leaving the Kitchen.

These recurring actions become recognizable habits.

---

# Head Movement

Maintain:

- Gentle nods.
- Slow turns.
- Calm observation.

Avoid quick or exaggerated movements.

---

# Eye Movement

Mother's eyes move naturally.

She often:

- Looks at the person speaking.
- Watches her hands while working.
- Briefly observes the garden.
- Looks through the Garden Window while thinking.

Eye movement should remain calm and intentional.

---

# Body Language During Cooking

Maintain:

- Relaxed shoulders.
- Confident movements.
- Efficient workflow.
- Gentle transitions between tasks.

Cooking should appear effortless.

---

# Body Language During Teaching

Maintain:

- Patient posture.
- Inviting gestures.
- Demonstrations rather than pointing.
- Encouraging smiles.

Teaching should feel welcoming.

---

# Body Language During Prayer

Maintain:

- Calm posture.
- Joined hands.
- Lowered gaze.
- Quiet stillness.

Prayer should feel peaceful and sincere.

---

# Body Language Around Family

Mother naturally:

- Faces the person she is speaking to.
- Makes gentle eye contact.
- Leans slightly toward others while listening.
- Gives people her full attention.

---

# Signature Sounds

Mother has several recognizable everyday sounds.

These include:

- Gentle clinking of her glass bangles while cooking.
- Soft rustling of her cotton saree while walking.
- Wooden ladle touching the clay pot.
- Grinding sounds from the Ammikkal.
- Soft footsteps across the courtyard.
- Quiet laughter.
- Gentle pouring of water.
- Light movements while organizing the Kitchen.

These sounds become part of Mother's identity.

---

# Silence

Mother is comfortable with silence.

She does not feel the need to speak constantly.

Quiet moments should feel peaceful rather than awkward.

---

# Emotional Control

Even during emotional situations:

Maintain:

- Calm breathing.
- Gentle movements.
- Controlled speech.
- Natural expressions.

She rarely loses composure.

---

# Google Flow Performance Rules

Across all Google Flow scenes:

Maintain:

- Natural walking speed.
- Smooth body movement.
- Consistent hand gestures.
- Gentle facial animation.
- Stable speaking rhythm.
- Subtle emotional transitions.

Avoid:

- Exaggerated acting.
- Sudden movements.
- Overly animated gestures.
- Cartoon-like performances.

---

# Audio Identity

Mother's recognizable audio identity includes:

- Soft warm voice.
- Calm speaking rhythm.
- Gentle laughter.
- Glass bangles softly clinking.
- Cotton saree rustling.
- Traditional cooking sounds accompanying her work.

These elements should immediately remind the audience of Mother, even before she appears on screen.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Soft voice.
- Calm speech.
- Gentle gestures.
- Graceful movement.
- Quiet confidence.
- Signature Kitchen sounds.
- Natural body language.

Never:

- Make Mother shout without narrative justification.
- Use exaggerated gestures.
- Introduce dramatic body movements.
- Replace subtle communication with theatrical acting.
