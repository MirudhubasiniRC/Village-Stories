# Mother Behaviour

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Mother's observable behaviour throughout the Village Stories universe.

It establishes how she moves, works, communicates, teaches, reacts, solves problems, and interacts with people, animals, and her environment.

This document describes what others can see.

It does not define Mother's inner personality, which is documented separately.

---

# Philosophy

Mother's behaviour is calm, purposeful, and deeply rooted in habit.

Nothing she does feels rushed or theatrical.

Years of experience have made her movements efficient, gentle, and confident.

Every action should communicate care.

---

# General Behaviour

Mother naturally behaves in a way that is:

- Calm.
- Gentle.
- Patient.
- Efficient.
- Observant.
- Respectful.
- Quietly confident.

She rarely performs unnecessary movements.

Every action has a purpose.

---

# Pace

Mother moves at a steady rhythm.

She never appears:

- Rushed.
- Careless.
- Restless.

Even when many tasks remain, she works one step at a time.

Her calmness influences those around her.

---

# Body Language

Maintain:

- Relaxed shoulders.
- Soft posture.
- Gentle hand movements.
- Smooth transitions between tasks.
- Comfortable standing positions.

Her body language communicates experience rather than effort.

---

# Walking

Mother walks:

- Quietly.
- Smoothly.
- With confidence.
- At a relaxed pace.

She naturally avoids unnecessary noise.

---

# Working Style

Mother performs household work with practiced efficiency.

Examples include:

- Preparing ingredients before cooking.
- Returning tools to their proper place.
- Cleaning as she works.
- Organizing tasks logically.
- Finishing one task before beginning another.

Her workflow should always appear organized.

---

# Cooking Behaviour

While cooking Mother naturally:

- Washes ingredients carefully.
- Inspects vegetables before cutting.
- Uses traditional tools confidently.
- Tastes food only when necessary.
- Adjusts seasoning through experience.
- Works without hesitation.

Cooking appears effortless because of years of practice.

---

# Gardening Behaviour

Mother naturally:

- Checks plants daily.
- Removes damaged leaves.
- Harvests only what is needed.
- Waters carefully.
- Appreciates healthy growth.

She treats every plant with care.

---

# Teaching Behaviour

Mother teaches through demonstration.

She often:

- Invites Daughter to help.
- Explains while working.
- Encourages observation.
- Allows mistakes.
- Corrects gently.

Teaching happens naturally during everyday activities.

---

# Listening

Mother is an attentive listener.

When others speak she:

- Maintains eye contact.
- Stops unnecessary work if appropriate.
- Listens fully before responding.
- Responds thoughtfully.

She never interrupts unnecessarily.

---

# Speaking

Mother speaks:

- Calmly.
- Clearly.
- Softly.
- Respectfully.

She rarely raises her voice.

She prefers encouraging words over criticism.

---

# Helping Others

Mother notices when someone needs help.

She often:

- Assists without being asked.
- Shares food.
- Offers practical advice.
- Comforts quietly.
- Solves problems through action.

Helping others feels natural to her.

---

# Behaviour Around Father

Mother treats Father as an equal partner.

She naturally:

- Shares responsibilities.
- Consults him on family decisions.
- Offers support.
- Appreciates his work.
- Expresses affection through everyday actions.

Their relationship is built on mutual respect.

# Behaviour Around Daughter

Mother is:

- Encouraging.
- Protective.
- Patient.
- Warm.

She allows Daughter to learn independently while remaining nearby for guidance.

# Behaviour Around The Family Dog

Mother naturally:

- Greets the dog warmly.
- Notices its needs.
- Speaks gently.
- Allows it to rest near the family.
- Occasionally shares appropriate food.

The Family Dog is treated as part of the household.

---

# Behaviour Around Neighbours

Mother naturally:

- Greets neighbours warmly.
- Shares vegetables.
- Exchanges recipes.
- Offers assistance.
- Welcomes visitors.

Community relationships remain relaxed and genuine.

---

# Behaviour During Meals

Meals are never rushed.

Mother naturally:

- Serves others before herself.
- Ensures everyone has enough food.
- Notices if someone eats less than usual.
- Waits until everyone is comfortable.

Food is an expression of care.

---

# Behaviour During Festivals

Festivals make Mother:

- Slightly more expressive.
- More social.
- More joyful.
- More generous.

Despite celebration, she remains composed and graceful.

---

# Behaviour During Rain

Rain slows Mother's pace slightly.

She may naturally:

- Watch the rain for a moment.
- Move clothes indoors.
- Prepare warm food.
- Listen to rainfall while cooking.

Rain creates comfort rather than inconvenience.

---

# Behaviour During Illness

When someone is unwell Mother naturally:

- Prepares comforting food.
- Makes herbal remedies.
- Checks on them frequently.
- Speaks reassuringly.
- Remains calm.

Care is shown through actions rather than dramatic words.

---

# Problem Solving

Mother approaches problems by:

- Observing first.
- Remaining calm.
- Considering practical solutions.
- Asking for help when appropriate.
- Acting patiently.

She avoids impulsive decisions.

---

# Daily Habits

Mother consistently:

- Begins work early.
- Keeps the Kitchen organized.
- Returns tools after use.
- Cleans while working.
- Checks the garden daily.
- Notices small household details.

These habits should remain consistent across the series.

---

# Signature Habits

Viewers should gradually recognize Mother through recurring behaviours.

Examples:

- Gently adjusting her saree before beginning work.
- Lightly wiping her hands on her cotton towel.
- Brushing loose strands of hair behind her ear.
- Softly smiling before serving food.
- Checking the fire before leaving the stove.
- Looking toward the Garden Window while thinking.
- The gentle clinking of her glass bangles while cooking.

These habits become part of her identity.

---

# Behaviour Under Pressure

Even during stressful moments Mother:

- Slows down rather than rushes.
- Speaks gently.
- Focuses on practical solutions.
- Reassures others.

Her calmness helps the family remain composed.

---

# Behaviour Consistency

Mother's behaviour should always feel:

- Familiar.
- Predictable.
- Genuine.
- Comfortable.

Viewers should immediately recognize her through her actions alone.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Calm movement.
- Purposeful actions.
- Gentle communication.
- Organized workflow.
- Patient teaching.
- Quiet confidence.
- Signature habits.

Never:

- Make Mother hurried without narrative reason.
- Create exaggerated reactions.
- Replace gentle guidance with harsh discipline.
- Contradict her established routines.
