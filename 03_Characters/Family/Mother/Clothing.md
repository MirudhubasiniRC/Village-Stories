# Mother Clothing

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Mother's wardrobe throughout the Village Stories universe.

It establishes her everyday clothing, seasonal variations, festival attire, accessories, footwear, and continuity rules.

This document defines only what Mother wears.

Her physical appearance is documented separately in Appearance.md.

---

# Philosophy

Mother dresses with simplicity, dignity, and practicality.

Her clothing supports everyday village life rather than fashion.

Every garment should feel authentic to rural Tamil Nadu during the 1990s.

Comfort, functionality, and tradition always take priority over decoration.

---

# Everyday Clothing

Mother's canonical everyday clothing consists of:

- Traditional cotton saree.
- Matching short-sleeved blouse.
- One simple gold chain carrying her traditional thali pendant.
- Traditional **gold jhumka earrings** — bell-shaped, dangling, ~1.5–2 inches
  (3.5–5 cm); soft painterly warm gold; gentle sway; not oversized chandelier.
- Small gold nose stud.
- Thin glass bangles (green / red / gold).
- Small red or maroon tilak bindi.

This is Mother's default appearance in most stories.

---

# Saree

Maintain:

- Traditional Tamil cotton saree.
- Practical drape.
- Securely tucked for household work.
- Comfortable movement.
- Natural folds.
- Slight wrinkles from daily use.

The saree should never appear perfectly pressed or overly formal.

---

# Saree Fabric

Preferred fabrics:

- Cotton.
- Handloom cotton.
- Soft woven cotton.

Avoid:

- Satin.
- Polyester.
- Glossy synthetic fabrics.
- Modern designer materials.

---

# Everyday Colour Palette

Mother prefers calm earthy cotton colours (approved hex):

- Maroon `#7A1F2B`
- Mustard `#D4A017`
- Dark green `#3A6B3A`
- Cream `#F5E6C8`
- Indigo `#2A3A5A`
- Also: rust, olive, brown, navy, off-white, soft teal when scene needs variety

Bright neon colours should never appear.

---

# Saree Borders

Maintain:

- Thin traditional woven borders.
- Small geometric designs.
- Temple borders.
- Simple stripes.

Avoid:

- Large floral prints.
- Glitter.
- Heavy embroidery.
- Modern fashion patterns.

---

# Blouse

Maintain:

- Cotton fabric.
- Short sleeves.
- Comfortable fit.
- Traditional neckline.
- Matching or complementary colour.

The blouse should always allow comfortable movement.

---

# Jewellery

Mother wears the same small collection every day.

Maintain:

- One simple, modest gold chain carrying the traditional thali pendant.
- Traditional **gold jhumkas** (~1.5–2 inches) with restrained gentle sway.
- Small gold nose stud.
- Thin glass bangles (green / red / gold).
- Occasionally a simple finger ring.

Jewellery should never dominate the character.

Never add multiple chains, heavy necklaces, large chandelier earrings, jewellery sets, or contemporary fashion jewellery to Mother's everyday appearance.

---

# Glass Bangles

Mother almost always wears:

- Thin traditional glass bangles.
- Usually 6–10 on each wrist.
- Colours coordinated with the saree.

Typical colours:

- Green
- Red / maroon
- Gold / amber
- Transparent
- Dark green

(Coordinate with saree; green + red + gold is the preferred everyday mix.)

The bangles naturally produce soft glass sounds while:

- Cooking.
- Grinding.
- Stirring.
- Gardening.
- Serving food.
- Walking.

This gentle sound is one of Mother's signature audio characteristics.

---

# Hair Flowers

Daily

Usually none.

Occasionally

- Small strand of fresh jasmine.

Festivals

- Full jasmine garland.

Special ceremonies

- Larger floral arrangements.

Flowers should always be fresh.

---

# Cooking Adjustments

While cooking Mother may:

- Tuck the saree pallu securely.
- Fold sleeves slightly if necessary.
- Tie back loose hair when working near fire.

These adjustments are temporary.

---

# Gardening Adjustments

During gardening:

- Saree tucked slightly for movement.
- Feet often barefoot.
- Minor soil marks are natural.

Gardening clothing remains the same traditional saree.

---

# Rainy Season

During rain:

- Saree edge may become wet.
- Cotton fabric becomes slightly heavier.
- Hair may become slightly damp.

The clothing should react naturally to weather.

---

# Summer

Preferred:

- Lightweight cotton.
- Breathable fabrics.
- Lighter earthy colours.

Comfort becomes the priority.

---

# Cool Season

Occasionally add:

- Thin cotton shawl during early mornings.

The shawl is removed as the day warms.

---

# Festival Clothing

Festivals are one of the few occasions where Mother dresses more formally.

Maintain:

- Traditional silk saree or premium handloom saree.
- Rich but elegant colours.
- Fresh jasmine flowers.
- Slightly more glass bangles.
- Traditional gold jewellery.
- Decorative bindi.

Despite the celebration, Mother remains modest and graceful.

---

# Wedding Clothing

For weddings and major ceremonies:

Maintain:

- Silk saree.
- Traditional gold jewellery.
- Decorative hair ornament.
- Fresh flowers.
- Elegant but authentic styling.

Wedding attire should remain culturally accurate for rural Tamil Nadu.

---

# Footwear

Inside house

- Barefoot.

Courtyard

- Usually barefoot.

Garden

- Barefoot when practical.

Outside village

- Traditional flat sandals.

Modern footwear should rarely appear.

---

# Clothing Movement

Clothing should move naturally.

Maintain:

- Gentle saree folds.
- Natural fabric weight.
- Soft movement while walking.
- Slight flutter in the breeze.

Avoid exaggerated flowing fabric.

---

# Wear and Age

Clothing should show gentle signs of everyday use.

Examples:

- Soft wrinkles.
- Slight fading after repeated washing.
- Natural cotton texture.

Clothing should feel loved rather than worn out.

---

# Animation Consistency

Every image, Google Flow scene, storyboard, and animation should preserve:

- Traditional cotton saree.
- Practical drape.
- Matching blouse.
- Glass bangles.
- Small gold jhumkas.
- Gold nose pin.
- One simple gold chain carrying her traditional thali pendant.
- Barefoot indoors.

Avoid wardrobe drift across episodes.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Traditional Tamil clothing.
- Cotton everyday sarees.
- Practical draping.
- Minimal jewellery.
- Thin glass bangles.
- Traditional accessories.
- Barefoot inside the home.

Never:

- Western clothing.
- Modern fashion trends.
- Heavy makeup.
- High heels.
- Flashy jewellery.
- Designer handbags.
- Modern wristwatches.

# Guiding Principle

Mother's clothing should feel like an extension of her life.

Every cotton saree, every softly chiming glass bangle, every gently moving small jhumka, one simple gold thali chain, and every practical adjustment for cooking or gardening should quietly communicate warmth, humility, and generations of village tradition.
