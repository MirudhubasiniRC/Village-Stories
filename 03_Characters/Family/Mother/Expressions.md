# Mother Expressions

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Mother's facial expressions, eye expressions, smiles, emotional reactions, and subtle mannerisms throughout the Village Stories universe.

These expressions should remain consistent across every image prompt, Google Flow scene, storyboard, animation, and illustration.

This document defines **how Mother expresses emotion**, not **what she feels internally**.

---

# Philosophy

Mother rarely displays exaggerated emotions.

Her face is naturally calm and expressive.

Even strong emotions are communicated through subtle changes in her eyes, smile, posture, and gentle movements.

The audience should recognize Mother's feelings immediately without dramatic acting.

---

# Expression Principles

Mother's expressions should always feel:

- Genuine.
- Warm.
- Gentle.
- Calm.
- Natural.
- Understated.

Avoid exaggerated acting.

### Exception — Sudden Rain (CANON-2026-006)

When rain **suddenly** hits while Mother is outdoors or on the thinnai, her face
must show a **loud readable startle** for one short beat:

- Eyes snap wide.
- Brows lift.
- Mouth opens in a clear gasp / “Ah!” shape.
- Cheeks and whole face commit — not a subtle eye-flick.

She remains Mother (warm, not cartoon-villain melodrama), but the reaction must
read instantly on a phone screen. Then cut to her practical next move (call
children, gather clothes, pull them under roof).

# Neutral Expression

Mother's resting expression is peaceful.

Maintain:

- Relaxed eyebrows.
- Soft eyes.
- Relaxed lips.
- Gentle facial muscles.

She should never appear angry or emotionless by default.

---

# Gentle Smile

This is Mother's signature expression.

Maintain:

- Closed-mouth smile.
- Slight upward curve of the lips.
- Warm eyes.
- Relaxed cheeks.

This smile appears while:

- Cooking.
- Gardening.
- Watching Daughter.
- Serving meals.
- Greeting neighbours.
- Looking at the Family Dog.

This should be Mother's most frequently used expression.

---

# Happy Smile

When genuinely happy:

Maintain:

- Brighter eyes.
- Wider but natural smile.
- Relaxed face.
- Slight laugh lines.

Never exaggerate the smile.

---

# Laugh

Mother laughs softly.

Maintain:

- Warm smile.

- Natural eye movement.
- Gentle body movement.

Laughter should feel sincere rather than loud.

---

# Pride

- Gentle smile.
- Bright eyes.

# Curiosity

Maintain:

- Slight eyebrow lift.
- Soft attentive eyes.
- Relaxed lips.

Curiosity should appear thoughtful rather than surprised.

# Surprise

Mother rarely shows dramatic surprise.

Maintain:

- Slightly widened eyes.
- Small eyebrow lift.
- Slight pause.

Avoid exaggerated reactions.

---

# Concern

When worried:

Maintain:

- Soft furrow between eyebrows.
- Gentle eye focus.
- Slightly pressed lips.

Concern should communicate care rather than panic.

---

# Sadness

Sadness remains quiet.

Maintain:

- Lowered gaze.
- Gentle expression.
- Calm breathing.
- Minimal tears unless the story genuinely requires them.

Mother rarely loses emotional control.

---

# Relief

Maintain:

- Gentle exhale.
- Soft smile.
- Relaxed shoulders.
- Warm eyes.

Relief often follows concern.

---

Gratitude is usually expressed through actions as much as expressions.

- Subtle emotional transitions.

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Gentle expressions.
- Calm eyes.
- Warm smiles.
- Natural emotion.
- Understated reactions.

Never:

- Overact.
- Use exaggerated cartoon expressions.
- Display extreme emotional swings without strong narrative justification.
- Sacrifice authenticity for visual drama.
