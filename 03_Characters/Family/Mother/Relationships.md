# Mother Relationships

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Mother's personal relationships with the recurring characters of the Village Stories universe.

It expands upon the global relationship rules by describing Mother's unique connection with each individual.

---

# Philosophy

Mother builds relationships through everyday acts of care rather than words.

She expresses affection through cooking, listening, helping, teaching, and quietly supporting others.

Trust is earned over time through consistency.

---

# Relationship With Father

Father is Mother's life partner.

Their relationship is built on:

- Mutual respect.
- Trust.
- Shared responsibilities.
- Quiet understanding.
- Teamwork.

Mother naturally:

- Supports Father's farming work.
- Prepares nourishing meals after long days.
- Discusses family decisions together.
- Notices when Father is tired or worried.
- Shows affection through thoughtful actions rather than dramatic words.

Their marriage should always feel stable, loving, and respectful.

---

# Relationship With Daughter

Mother is Daughter's first teacher.

She teaches through:

- Daily routines.
- Cooking together.
- Gardening.
- Observation.
- Gentle correction.
- Encouragement.

Mother allows Daughter to learn by doing rather than simply watching.

She celebrates effort as much as success.

---

# Relationship With the Family Dog

Mother considers the Family Dog a member of the household.

She naturally:

- Speaks gently.
- Provides food and fresh water.
- Notices its needs.
- Smiles at its playful behaviour.
- Allows it to rest near the Kitchen and courtyard.

The dog trusts Mother completely.

---

# Relationship With Neighbours

Mother shares warm relationships with nearby families.

She naturally:

- Exchanges vegetables.
- Shares recipes.
- Helps during festivals.
- Visits when someone is ill.
- Welcomes neighbours into the home.

These friendships are long-standing and built on trust.

---

# Relationship With Village Elders

Mother treats elders with great respect.

She:

- Listens carefully.
- Learns from their experience.
- Offers assistance whenever needed.
- Serves food before herself.
- Preserves traditions they pass down.

---

# Relationship With Children

Mother naturally becomes a caring figure for village children.

She:

- Welcomes them kindly.
- Shares simple snacks.
- Encourages curiosity.
- Protects them gently.
- Treats every child with patience.

---

# Relationship With Visitors

Every guest is welcomed warmly.

Mother usually:

- Offers drinking water immediately.
- Prepares tea or filter coffee.
- Serves fresh food.
- Makes visitors feel comfortable.
- Encourages them to stay and rest.

Hospitality is one of her defining qualities.

---

# Relationship With Nature

Mother feels deeply connected to nature.

She respects:

- Trees.
- Plants.
- Birds.
- Animals.
- Rain.
- Rivers.
- The changing seasons.

Nature guides many of her daily decisions.

---

# Relationship With Food

Food is Mother's way of expressing love.

Every meal represents:

- Care.
- Comfort.
- Healing.
- Gratitude.
- Togetherness.

Cooking is never simply a chore.

---

# Relationship With Home

Mother sees the family home as a place of warmth and safety.

She naturally keeps it:

- Clean.
- Organized.
- Welcoming.
- Peaceful.

Every room reflects her quiet care.

---

# Relationship With the Village

Mother feels she belongs to the village.

She values:

- Community.
- Tradition.
- Cooperation.
- Shared celebrations.
- Helping others.

The village is an extension of her family.

---

# Relationship Consistency

Mother's relationships should always feel:

- Warm.
- Stable.
- Respectful.
- Genuine.
- Long-lasting.

She rarely holds grudges.

Forgiveness comes naturally.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Loving partnership with Father.
- Patient guidance for Daughter.
- Kindness toward the Family Dog.
- Respect for elders.
- Hospitality toward visitors.
- Strong neighbourly bonds.
- Deep connection with nature.

Never:

- Introduce unnecessary family conflict.
- Contradict established relationships.
- Make Mother indifferent toward others.
- Reduce relationships to dialogue alone.
