# Mother Daily Routine

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Mother's typical daily routine throughout the Village Stories universe.

It establishes the rhythm of her day from waking before sunrise until preparing the home for the night.

While individual stories may focus on only one activity, this document defines Mother's normal lifestyle.

---

# Philosophy

Mother's daily routine follows the natural rhythm of the village.

Her day is shaped by:

- Sunrise.
- Family needs.
- Seasonal changes.
- Traditional cooking.
- Gardening.
- Household care.

Although activities may vary slightly depending on weather, festivals, or special occasions, the overall rhythm remains consistent.

# Before Sunrise

Mother usually wakes before the rest of the family.

She naturally:

- Folds her bedding.
- Opens windows for fresh morning air.
- Washes her face, hands, and feet at the Family Well.
- Enjoys a few quiet moments before beginning the day.

Morning birds and cool air often accompany this peaceful routine.

---

# Morning Prayer

After freshening up, Mother visits the Pooja Room.

She:

- Lights the traditional oil lamp.
- Offers flowers when available.
- Says a quiet prayer.
- Expresses gratitude for the new day.
- Prays for her family's health and well-being.

This peaceful ritual prepares her mind before beginning household work.

---

# Morning Tea

Mother prepares fresh tea for the family.

She naturally:

- Lights the clay stove if needed.
- Boils fresh water.
- Prepares traditional tea.
- Serves Father and other family members.

Morning tea marks the beginning of the family's daily activities.

---

# Breakfast Preparation

Mother prepares breakfast using fresh ingredients.

Typical activities include:

- Washing vegetables.
- Grinding spices.
- Preparing chutney.
- Cooking on the clay stove.
- Serving breakfast.

Breakfast is prepared calmly without rushing.

---

# Kitchen Cleaning

After breakfast Mother naturally:

- Washes cooking utensils.
- Cleans the clay stove.
- Organizes traditional tools.
- Sweeps the Kitchen floor.

The Kitchen is left ready for the next meal.

---

# Vegetable & Utensil Washing

Fresh vegetables and cooking utensils are usually washed at the Well Utility Area.

Mother:

- Draws fresh water from the well.
- Washes vegetables carefully.
- Cleans clay pots and utensils.
- Leaves everything neatly organized.

This activity often becomes a quiet, peaceful moment.

---

# Garden Inspection

Mother visits the Family Vegetable Garden.

She naturally:

- Checks vegetable growth.
- Waters plants if needed.
- Harvests fresh vegetables.
- Removes weeds.
- Collects herbs.
- Observes seasonal changes.

She harvests only what the family needs for the day.

---

# Mid-Morning Household Work

Typical activities include:

- Sweeping.
- Cleaning.
- Organizing storage.
- Preparing ingredients.
- Drying spices.
- Folding clothes.

Mother works steadily without feeling hurried.

---

# Laundry

Most daily clothes are washed at the Well Utility Area.

Occasionally, larger household items such as:

- Bedsheets.
- Blankets.
- Large cloths.

are washed at the riverside during suitable weather.

Laundry is usually completed before the afternoon heat.

---

# Bathing

Mother traditionally bathes during the morning after completing the first round of household work.

When conditions permit:

- She bathes at the family's private riverside bathing area.

During heavy rain, illness, or when remaining at home:

- She bathes using water drawn from the Family Well.

Bathing remains a private daily routine.

---

# Lunch Preparation

Lunch preparation begins well before noon.

Mother:

- Harvests fresh vegetables if required.
- Grinds fresh masalas.
- Prepares rice.
- Cooks seasonal dishes.
- Serves the family.

Lunch is the main family meal of the day.

---

# Afternoon

After lunch Mother usually:

- Washes utensils.
- Cleans the Kitchen.
- Waters plants if needed.
- Performs light household work.
- Sorts vegetables.
- Prepares ingredients for dinner.

The afternoon is generally calmer than the morning.

---

# Teaching Daughter

Throughout the day Mother naturally teaches Daughter by:

- Inviting her to help.
- Demonstrating techniques.
- Explaining traditions.
- Answering questions.
- Encouraging observation.

Teaching happens naturally during work rather than through formal lessons.

---

# Caring for Animals

Mother regularly checks on:

- The Family Dog.
- Chickens.
- Cows.
- Goats.

She ensures they have:

- Food.
- Water.
- Gentle attention.

---

# Evening Coffee

As the family begins returning home, Mother prepares traditional filter coffee.

This becomes a quiet family moment.

Occasionally, tea may be prepared instead depending on the family's preference or the day's activities.

---

# Evening Garden Visit

Before sunset Mother often:

- Waters plants if necessary.
- Collects herbs.
- Observes flowers.
- Enjoys the cooler evening air.

This is one of her favourite quiet moments of the day.

---

# Dinner Preparation

Mother prepares a lighter evening meal.

She works calmly while the family gradually gathers together.

Dinner preparation often includes conversation with family members.

---

# Night Kitchen Routine

After dinner Mother:

- Washes utensils.
- Cleans the clay stove.
- Organizes ingredients.
- Covers stored food.
- Prepares the Kitchen for the next morning.

The Kitchen is always left clean before bedtime.

---

# Night Prayer

Before sleeping Mother returns to the Pooja Room.

She:

- Offers a short prayer.
- Gives thanks for the day.
- Prays for the family's safety.

This quietly completes her daily routine.

---

# Rest

Mother usually sleeps after ensuring:

- The Kitchen is clean.
- Doors are secured.
- The family is comfortable.
- Tomorrow's work is mentally prepared.

Her day ends with the same calmness with which it began.

---

# Seasonal Variations

Summer

- Begins work earlier.
- Additional watering.
- Lighter meals.

Monsoon

- More indoor work.
- Rain influences gardening.
- Warm comfort food.

Post-Monsoon

- More harvesting.
- Garden at its most productive.

Festival Days

- Earlier start.
- Special cooking.
- Home decoration.
- Temple visits.

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Early rising.
- Morning prayer.
- Face washing at the Family Well.
- Morning tea.
- Evening filter coffee.
- Cooking with seasonal ingredients.
- Garden care.
- Well Utility Area for washing vegetables and utensils.
- Regular household organization.
- Calm working rhythm.
- Night prayer.

Never:

- Skip established routines without narrative reason.
- Rush daily activities.
- Contradict the world's seasonal rhythm.
