# Father Behaviour

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Father's observable behaviour throughout the Village Stories universe.

It establishes how he moves, works, communicates, teaches, reacts, solves problems, and interacts with people, animals, and his environment.

This document describes what others can see.

It does not define Father's inner personality, which is documented separately.

---

# Philosophy

Father's behaviour is steady, purposeful, and deeply rooted in habit.

Nothing he does feels theatrical.

Years of farming have made his movements efficient, grounded, and confident.

Every action should communicate responsibility and care.

---

# General Behaviour

Father naturally behaves in a way that is:

- Calm.
- Steady.
- Practical.
- Observant.
- Respectful.
- Quietly confident.
- Physically capable without show.

He rarely performs unnecessary movements.

Every action has a purpose.

---

# Pace

Father moves at a working rhythm.

He never appears:

- Frantic.
- Careless.
- Restless without reason.

Even when many tasks remain, he works one step at a time.

His steadiness influences those around him.

---

# Body Language

Maintain:

- Open but relaxed shoulders.
- Grounded stance.
- Strong efficient hand movements.
- Smooth transitions between tasks.
- Comfortable standing and squatting positions from farm work.

His body language communicates experience rather than effortful display.

---

# Walking

Father walks:

- Steadily.
- With familiar confidence.
- At a practical pace.
- As if the paths belong to memory.

He naturally avoids wasted motion.

When returning home after work, his walk often softens slightly with relief and recognition.

---

# Working Style

Father performs outdoor work with practiced efficiency.

Examples include:

- Checking tools before beginning.
- Completing one clear stage of labour before the next.
- Returning tools to their proper place.
- Noticing what needs repair early.
- Conserving energy across long days.
- Cleaning as he finishes a task when practical.

His workflow should always appear organized and experienced.

---

# Farming Behaviour

While working fields Father naturally:

- Inspects soil and crops before acting.
- Moves with the season rather than against it.
- Uses traditional tools confidently.
- Adjusts irrigation carefully.
- Watches sky and wind during outdoor labour.
- Works without hesitation once a plan is set.

Farming appears natural because of years of practice.

---

# Garden Behaviour

In the Vegetable Garden Father naturally:

- Prepares soil.
- Builds and repairs supports.
- Carries compost.
- Plants seasonal crops.
- Maintains irrigation.
- Performs the heavier labour while coordinating with Mother's harvesting and plant care.

Heavy garden work remains his usual domain.

---

# Livestock Behaviour

At the Livestock Shelter Father naturally:

- Cleans the shelter.
- Carries hay.
- Repairs fences.
- Maintains the shelter structure.
- Leads the cows calmly.
- Harnesses the bullock cart when required.
- Checks water troughs.
- Moves among animals without startling them.

Animals remain relaxed in his presence.

---

# Repair Behaviour

Father repairs with patience.

He naturally:

- Inspects the broken object carefully.
- Uses available traditional materials.
- Works sitting or crouched in the Courtyard or Backyard.
- Tests the repair before finishing.
- Puts tools away afterward.

Repair scenes should feel quiet and absorbing.

---

# Teaching Behaviour

Father teaches through shared labour.

He often:

- Invites Elder Brother to carry small tools or baskets, and gives Younger Daughter tiny safe tasks.
- Demonstrates once clearly.
- Lets her try under supervision.
- Corrects gently.
- Praises effort with a nod or soft word.

Teaching happens naturally during outdoor work.

---

# Listening

Father is an attentive listener.

When others speak he:

- Maintains steady eye contact.
- Stops unnecessary work if the moment deserves attention.
- Listens fully before responding.
- Responds briefly and thoughtfully.

He never interrupts unnecessarily.

---

# Speaking

Father speaks:

- Calmly.
- Clearly.
- In a warm medium voice.
- Respectfully.

He rarely raises his voice.

He prefers practical encouragement over criticism.

---

# Helping Others

Father notices when someone needs help.

He often:

- Assists without being asked.
- Shares labour during harvest.
- Offers practical advice.
- Lends tools when appropriate.
- Solves problems through action.

Helping others feels natural to him.

---

# Behaviour Around Mother

Father treats Mother as an equal partner.

He naturally:

- Shares responsibilities.
- Consults her on family decisions.
- Notices when she is tired.
- Appreciates her cooking and care.
- Expresses affection through everyday actions.
- Softens after returning home.

Their relationship is built on mutual respect.

---

# Behaviour Around Elder Brother and Younger Daughter

Father is:

- Encouraging.
- Protective.
- Patient.
- Warm.
- Gently playful at times.

He allows Elder Brother to learn independently within safe limits, and keeps Younger Daughter nearby with gentler tasks.

---

# Behaviour Around The Family Dog

Father naturally:

- Greets the dog when returning.
- Allows it to follow outdoor work when safe.
- Speaks in a low calm voice.
- Pats it briefly with familiarity.
- Notices if it is hungry, hot, or restless.

The Family Dog is treated as part of the household.

---

# Behaviour Around Neighbours

Father naturally:

- Greets neighbours respectfully.
- Shares labour in busy seasons.
- Exchanges farming talk.
- Offers assistance.
- Sits for brief conversation when work allows.

Community relationships remain relaxed and genuine.

---

# Behaviour During Meals

Meals are never rushed after work.

Father naturally:

- Washes before eating.
- Appreciates Mother's cooking.
- Eats steadily.
- Notices if the children are learning table habits.
- Remains present with the family.

Food is an expression of shared life after labour.

---

# Behaviour During Festivals

Festivals make Father:

- Slightly more social.
- More openly joyful.
- More generous with time and help.
- Neater in appearance.

Despite celebration, he remains composed and grounded.

---

# Behaviour During Rain

Rain changes Father's pace.

He may naturally:

- Pause to feel the first drops.
- Move tools and hay under cover.
- Check animals and shelter.
- Watch fields with quiet hope or concern.
- Return home earlier when storms intensify.

Rain is both blessing and responsibility.

---

# Behaviour During Illness

When someone is unwell Father naturally:

- Becomes quieter and more attentive.
- Handles urgent outdoor necessities efficiently.
- Supports Mother's care.
- Speaks reassuringly.
- Remains calm.

Care is shown through presence and practical help.

---

# Problem Solving

Father approaches problems by:

- Observing first.
- Remaining calm.
- Testing practical solutions.
- Using traditional knowledge.
- Asking elders or neighbours when needed.
- Acting patiently.

He avoids impulsive decisions.

---

# Daily Habits

Father consistently:

- Begins outdoor work early.
- Checks tools.
- Visits fields or garden according to season.
- Maintains the Livestock Shelter.
- Washes after heavy work.
- Returns home with a recognizable evening rhythm.
- Notices weather before sleep.

These habits should remain consistent across the series.

---

# Signature Habits

Viewers should gradually recognize Father through recurring behaviours.

Examples:

- Adjusting the thundu on his shoulder before setting out.
- Checking a tool edge with his thumb.
- Squatting comfortably while repairing or inspecting.
- Softly greeting cows before work.
- Wiping sweat with the thundu.
- Pausing at the Front Thinnai when returning home.
- Calling the Family Dog with a low familiar sound.
- Setting tools neatly aside before entering the house.

These habits become part of his identity.

---

# Behaviour Under Pressure

Even during stressful moments Father:

- Slows into focus rather than panic.
- Speaks less and acts carefully.
- Protects family and animals first.
- Reassures through steady presence.

His calmness helps the family remain composed.

---

# Behaviour Consistency

Father's behaviour should always feel:

- Familiar.
- Predictable.
- Genuine.
- Grounded.

Viewers should immediately recognize him through his actions alone.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Steady movement.
- Purposeful labour.
- Gentle communication.
- Organized outdoor workflow.
- Patient teaching.
- Quiet confidence.
- Signature habits.

Never:

- Make Father hurried without narrative reason.
- Create exaggerated heroic posing.
- Replace gentle guidance with harsh discipline.
- Contradict his established routines.
- Portray aggression as his default behaviour.

# Guiding Principle

Father's behaviour should quietly reveal his character.

Through steady fieldwork, careful animal care, patient teaching, and countless small acts of responsibility, viewers should come to recognize him not simply by how he looks, but by how he works and returns home.
