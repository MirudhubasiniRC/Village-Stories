# Father

## Purpose

This folder contains the complete canonical documentation for the Father, one of the four primary recurring characters in the Village Stories universe.

He is the family's primary farmer and outdoor worker, and appears frequently throughout stories involving land, animals, seasons, and daily village life.

His documentation defines his identity, appearance, personality, behaviour, relationships, routines, skills, and continuity.

Every image prompt, animation, storyboard, and story involving Father should reference this documentation.

---

# Character Role

Father is the primary caretaker of the family's land, livestock, and outdoor work.

He preserves traditional farming knowledge through everyday practice.

His strength, patience, quiet humour, and dependable presence shape the family's sense of security.

He teaches not through formal instruction but through shared work, calm demonstration, and steady example.

---

# Narrative Function

Father represents:

* Strength without aggression.
* Honest labour.
* Reliability.
* Quiet kindness.
* Farming knowledge.
* Seasonal wisdom.
* Family protection.
* Partnership with Mother.

He is rarely theatrical, yet his return home, his work in the fields, and his care for animals often become emotional anchors in a scene.

---

# Primary Responsibilities

Father naturally manages:

* Paddy field work.
* Vegetable garden heavy labour.
* Livestock shelter maintenance.
* Bullock cart use and care.
* Tool repair.
* Irrigation and canal work.
* Seasonal planting and harvest.
* Supporting Mother and teaching Elder Brother and Younger Daughter through outdoor and home-linked work.

He performs these responsibilities steadily and without complaint.

---

# Relationships

Father shares important relationships with:

* Mother.
* Elder Brother.
* Younger Daughter.
* Family Dog.
* Neighbours.
* Fellow farmers.
* Village elders.
* Village children.

Individual relationship details are documented separately.

---

# Typical Locations

Father most frequently appears in:

* Paddy Fields.
* Vegetable Garden.
* Livestock Shelter.
* Backyard.
* Well Utility Area.
* Irrigation Canal.
* Front Thinnai.
* Courtyard.

He also appears along village paths, at the tea stall, grain drying yard, and other agricultural locations when the story requires it.

---

# Story Themes

Stories involving Father commonly explore:

* Farming and harvest.
* Seasonal work.
* Caring for cows, goats, and chickens.
* Repairing tools.
* Teaching Elder Brother outdoor skills.
* Sharing gentle learning moments with Younger Daughter.
* Returning home after work.
* Rain, drought worry, and recovery.
* Quiet partnership with Mother.
* Everyday village labour.

These themes should arise naturally rather than feeling instructional.
