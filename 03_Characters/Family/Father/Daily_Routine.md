# Father Daily Routine

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Father's typical daily routine throughout the Village Stories universe.

It establishes the rhythm of his day from waking near sunrise until securing outdoor work and resting for the night.

While individual stories may focus on only one activity, this document defines Father's normal lifestyle.

---

# Philosophy

Father's daily routine follows the natural rhythm of the village and the land.

His day is shaped by:

- Sunrise.
- Season.
- Weather.
- Crop needs.
- Animal care.
- Family meals.
- Traditional outdoor labour.

Although activities may vary depending on planting, harvest, rain, festivals, or special occasions, the overall rhythm remains consistent.

# Before / Near Sunrise

Father usually wakes near sunrise, often shortly after Mother has begun the earliest household quiet.

He naturally:

- Folds or arranges bedding simply.
- Steps into cool morning air.
- Washes face, hands, and feet at the Family Well.
- Observes sky colour and wind before planning the day.

Morning birds, roosters, and cool air often accompany this beginning.

---

# Morning Prayer

Father may join morning prayer at the Pooja Corner when time and season allow.

On especially busy field mornings, his prayer may be brief and sincere before outdoor work.

He expresses gratitude for land, family, animals, and the coming day's labour.

---

# Morning Tea

Father shares morning tea prepared by Mother.

He naturally:

- Sits briefly on the Front Thinnai or in a familiar family place.
- Discusses the day's likely work in a few practical words.
- Notices the children if they are awake.
- Greets the Family Dog if it is nearby.

Morning tea marks the transition from home quiet to outdoor labour.

---

# Livestock Morning Check

Before leaving for distant fields, Father usually visits the Livestock Shelter.

He naturally:

- Checks cows, calf, and goats.
- Ensures water access.
- Moves hay if needed.
- Inspects gates and shelter condition.
- Notes any animal behaviour that needs later attention.

Mother often handles feeding and milking routines; Father supports with maintenance and heavier tasks.

---

# Morning Field or Garden Work

Father begins primary outdoor labour according to season.

Typical activities include:

- Walking to paddy fields.
- Preparing soil.
- Irrigation work.
- Weeding or planting tasks.
- Heavy Vegetable Garden labour.
- Carrying tools and baskets.
- Repairing supports or fences when required.

Work begins early to use cooler hours.

---

# Mid-Morning Continuity

As the sun rises higher Father naturally:

- Uses the thundu for sweat.
- Drinks water when available.
- Paces labour to avoid exhaustion.
- Watches weather changes.
- Continues the most urgent seasonal task.

He works steadily rather than racing.

---

# Midday Return and Lunch

Around midday Father usually returns home or pauses near home for lunch.

He naturally:

- Washes hands and feet.
- Leaves dusty tools in a proper outdoor place.
- Shares the main family meal prepared by Mother.
- Rests briefly if the heat is severe.

Lunch restores him for the second half of the day.

---

# Afternoon

Afternoon behaviour depends on season.

Hot summer afternoons

- Lighter tasks.
- Shelter repairs in shade.
- Tool maintenance in the Courtyard or Backyard.
- Brief rest.

Milder seasons / urgent farm periods

- Continued field work.
- Irrigation.
- Harvest support.
- Garden labour.

Monsoon afternoons

- Indoor-adjacent repairs.
- Protecting hay and tools.
- Checking drainage and animals.

The afternoon is often more adaptive than the morning.

---

# Teaching Moments

Throughout the day Father naturally teaches Elder Brother, and sometimes Younger Daughter, when they join him by:

- Giving small safe tasks.
- Demonstrating tool use.
- Explaining why a job is done a certain way.
- Watching her carefully.
- Ending a lesson before fatigue or danger appears.

Teaching remains woven into real work.

---

# Evening Animal Care

Before sunset Father often returns to the Livestock Shelter.

He naturally:

- Ensures animals are settled.
- Carries remaining hay if needed.
- Checks water trough.
- Secures gates.
- Prepares bullock cart area if used that day.
- Notices chickens returning toward familiar evening patterns.

This closes the outdoor animal day.

---

# Wash and Return

After heavy work Father washes at the Well Utility Area or Family Well.

He naturally:

- Cleans hands, feet, face, and arms.
- Refreshes before entering the main living spaces.
- Leaves the heaviest dust outside.

This transition marks the return from labourer to family presence.

---

# Evening Coffee and Family Time

As the family gathers, Father shares evening filter coffee or tea.

He may sit on the Front Thinnai or Courtyard edge.

This becomes a quiet family moment after work.

Conversation is simple: weather, fields, the children's day, village news.

---

# Dinner

Father joins the family evening meal.

He eats calmly, appreciates Mother's cooking, and remains present.

Dinner is lighter than midday but emotionally important.

---

# Final Outdoor Check

Before full night rest Father often makes a brief final check of:

- Livestock Shelter security.
- Backyard gate conditions when relevant.
- Tools under cover.
- Sky signs for the next morning.

Then he returns indoors.

---

# Night Rest

Father usually sleeps after ensuring:

- Animals are secure.
- Outdoor tools are not left carelessly.
- The family is settled.
- Tomorrow's likely work is understood.

His day ends with the same grounded calm with which outdoor labour began.

---

# Seasonal Variations

Summer

- Earlier start.
- Reduced midday field intensity.
- Extra attention to animal water.
- Lighter clothing.

Monsoon

- Weather-led schedule.
- More shelter and drainage work.
- Careful travel on wet paths.
- Heightened crop and animal protection.

Post-Monsoon / Harvest periods

- Longer outdoor hours.
- Shared labour with neighbours when needed.
- Grain movement and drying support.
- Deep physical tiredness after successful days.

Festival Days

- Reduced routine labour when appropriate.
- Clean clothes.
- Temple or community participation.
- Help with practical festival preparations.

# Storytelling Opportunities

Father's routine naturally creates stories involving:

- Early field departures.
- Rain arriving mid-work.
- A repaired fence or tool.
- Teaching the children outdoors.
- A calf needing attention.
- Returning home to Mother's cooking.
- Bullock cart journeys.
- Quiet evening thinnai rest.
- Seasonal harvest pressure and relief.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Early outdoor orientation.
- Morning tea with family.
- Livestock awareness.
- Seasonal field or garden labour.
- Midday meal rhythm.
- Evening animal check.
- Washing before full return indoors.
- Evening coffee / family presence.
- Final outdoor security awareness.

Never:

- Skip established routines without narrative reason.
- Keep Father away from land and animals so long that his role feels false.
- Rush the day into modern schedule patterns.
- Contradict the world's seasonal rhythm.
