# Father Skills

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Father's knowledge, practical abilities, traditional skills, and life experience throughout the Village Stories universe.

These skills have been developed through years of observation, practice, family tradition, and everyday village farming life.

This document defines what Father knows and what he is capable of doing.

---

# Philosophy

Father is highly skilled, but never boastful.

His abilities are the result of years of outdoor labour rather than formal education.

He performs most tasks naturally without drawing attention to his expertise.

---

# Core Skills

Father possesses advanced practical knowledge in:

- Paddy cultivation.
- Seasonal farming.
- Vegetable garden heavy labour.
- Livestock shelter maintenance.
- Animal handling.
- Bullock cart use.
- Tool repair.
- Irrigation.
- Weather observation.
- Outdoor family teaching.

---

# Paddy Farming

Father is an experienced paddy farmer.

He understands:

- Field preparation.
- Transplanting rhythms.
- Water management.
- Weed control through traditional methods.
- Harvest timing.
- Grain handling after harvest.

Paddy work is one of Father's central lifelong skills.

---

# Soil and Land Knowledge

Father naturally understands:

- Soil moisture.
- Soil tiredness.
- Compost needs.
- Slope and water flow.
- When land should rest or be worked.

He treats land as a living responsibility.

---

# Irrigation Skills

Father is proficient with:

- Canal water awareness.
- Directing water into fields.
- Avoiding overwatering.
- Protecting young plants.
- Adjusting methods by season.

Irrigation decisions are careful and observational.

---

# Vegetable Garden Skills

Father regularly:

- Prepares soil.
- Builds trellises and supports.
- Plants seasonal crops.
- Carries compost.
- Maintains irrigation.
- Repairs garden structures.

Heavy garden labour is usually performed by Father, while Mother leads much of the harvesting and delicate plant care.

---

# Livestock Care

Father confidently maintains:

- The Livestock Shelter.
- Hay storage access.
- Stone water trough filling and cleaning support.
- Fence and gate repair.
- Cow leading and calm handling.
- Goat enclosure practical care.
- Bullock cart harnessing and basic maintenance.

He works alongside Mother, who frequently handles feeding, milking support, egg collection, and gentle daily animal attention.

---

# Bullock Cart Skills

Father knows how to:

- Harness animals calmly.
- Balance loads.
- Drive at a safe village pace.
- Care for wooden cart parts.
- Rest animals appropriately.

The bullock cart remains a practical family tool, never a spectacle.

---

# Tool Skills

Father is proficient with traditional farming and repair tools.

He understands:

- Sharpening and care.
- Safe handling.
- Field use.
- Small wooden and metal repairs within village means.
- Knowing when a tool must go to the blacksmith or carpenter.

He respects tools and returns them after use.

---

# Weather Reading

Father notices:

- Cloud colour and movement.
- Wind shifts.
- Humidity before rain.
- Heat stress signs in animals and crops.
- Seasonal transition cues.

Weather knowledge guides when he works, waits, or protects shelter and harvest.

---

# Physical Labour Skills

Father can confidently:

- Carry harvest baskets and hay.
- Lift and move practical farm loads.
- Dig and prepare ground.
- Walk long field distances.
- Work through a full outdoor day with paced endurance.

His strength is functional and sustainable.

---

# Repair and Maintenance

Father repairs and maintains:

- Fences.
- Simple garden supports.
- Shelter details.
- Tool handles when possible.
- Household outdoor fixtures.

He prefers durable practical fixes over temporary carelessness.

---

# Child Guidance Outdoors

Father teaches through:

- Demonstration.
- Shared tasks.
- Safety awareness.
- Gentle correction.
- Encouragement.

He rarely lectures.

Elder Brother and Younger Daughter learn by participating in age-appropriate outdoor work.

---

# Community Labour Skills

Father participates naturally in:

- Shared harvest help.
- Neighbour field assistance.
- Festival ground practical setup when needed.
- Cooperative village labour traditions.

Community work is part of rural life.

---

# Observation

Father notices:

- Crop stress.
- Animal behaviour changes.
- Broken fence lines.
- Canal level changes.
- Tool wear.
- Family fatigue after long days.

His careful observation helps prevent problems before they become serious.

---

# Problem Solving

Father solves everyday problems through:

- Experience.
- Patience.
- Practical thinking.
- Traditional knowledge.
- Physical testing of solutions.
- Asking skilled villagers when needed.

He rarely panics.

---

# Skills Under Pressure

During unexpected situations Father remains:

- Calm.
- Organized.
- Practical.
- Protective.

He focuses on immediate safety and workable next steps.

---

# Continuous Learning

Father never stops learning.

He continues improving through:

- Seasonal experience.
- Conversations with elders and farmers.
- Observing outcomes in fields and gardens.
- Teaching the children.
- Repair challenges.

Knowledge grows naturally over time.

---

# Limitations

Father does not know everything.

He may:

- Ask the blacksmith, carpenter, or elders for specialized help.
- Learn improved traditional methods.
- Adapt when weather disrupts plans.
- Rely on Mother's household and cooking expertise.

His willingness to learn and cooperate is one of his strengths.

---

# Signature Skills

Father is especially recognized for:

- Paddy field work.
- Heavy vegetable garden labour.
- Livestock shelter maintenance.
- Calm animal handling.
- Bullock cart use.
- Tool repair.
- Reading weather and soil.
- Teaching outdoor skills through shared work.
- Returning home with the quiet competence of a man who has completed honest labour.

These skills define his role within the family.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- High farming skill.
- Practical outdoor intelligence.
- Livestock maintenance ability.
- Tool competence.
- Calm problem solving.
- Seasonal knowledge.
- Lifelong learning through practice.

Never:

- Portray Father as inexperienced on the land.
- Replace traditional methods with modern machinery without narrative reason.
- Give him cinematic superhuman strength.
- Contradict established skills.
