# Father Clothing

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Father's wardrobe throughout the Village Stories universe.

It establishes his everyday clothing, seasonal variations, festival attire, work adjustments, footwear, and continuity rules.

This document defines only what Father wears.

His physical appearance is documented separately in Appearance.md.

---

# Philosophy

Father dresses with simplicity, dignity, and practicality.

His clothing supports farming, livestock care, and everyday village movement rather than fashion.

Every garment should feel authentic to rural Tamil Nadu during the early 1990s.

Comfort, durability, and tradition always take priority over decoration.

---

# Everyday Clothing

Father's canonical everyday clothing consists of:

- Traditional cotton shirt or half-sleeve shirt.
- Traditional cotton veshti / dhoti, or practical cotton work veshti.
- Simple cloth towel or thundu often carried on the shoulder.
- Occasional simple waist cloth adjustment for fieldwork.
- Bare feet or traditional sandals depending on location.

This is Father's default appearance in most stories.

---

# Shirt

Maintain:

- Cotton fabric.
- Light, breathable weave.
- Half sleeves preferred for work days.
- Full sleeves acceptable for cooler mornings, temple visits, or festivals.
- Simple solid colours or very subtle checks.
- Comfortable fit that allows lifting and carrying.
- Natural wrinkles from work.

The shirt should never appear perfectly pressed or fashion-tailored.

---

# Shirt Colour Palette

Father prefers calm practical colours.

Examples:

- White
- Off white
- Cream
- Light beige
- Soft brown
- Faded sky blue
- Light grey
- Pale olive
- Soft mustard

Avoid:

- Neon colours
- Loud prints
- Designer patterns
- Glossy synthetic sheen

---

# Veshti / Dhoti

Maintain:

- Traditional white or off-white cotton veshti for many everyday and evening scenes.
- Practical cotton work veshti for field and shelter labour.
- Secure traditional wrapping.
- Natural folds.
- Slight dust, dampness, or crease after work is acceptable and realistic.

The veshti should never appear like a costume or fashion drape.

---

# Work Adjustments

During heavy labour Father may:

- Fold the veshti slightly higher for movement.
- Leave the shirt slightly open at the collar after heat.
- Place the thundu over one shoulder.
- Roll sleeves if wearing a full-sleeve shirt.
- Remove the shirt temporarily only when heat and hard labour make it natural, remaining modest and culturally appropriate.

These adjustments are temporary and practical.

He never dresses to display physique.

---

# Shoulder Cloth / Thundu

Father often carries a simple cotton thundu.

Uses include:

- Wiping sweat.
- Softening a shoulder while carrying loads.
- Casual resting cloth on the Front Thinnai.
- Light head cover in harsh sun when needed.

Maintain:

- Cotton.
- White, cream, checked, or softly coloured.
- Soft from repeated washing.
- Never decorative fashion scarf styling.

---

# Festival Clothing

Festivals are one of the few occasions where Father dresses more formally.

Maintain:

- Clean white or premium cotton / silk-cotton shirt.
- Freshly washed white veshti.
- Neat grooming.
- Simple traditional appearance.
- Optional simple sandal footwear.

Despite celebration, Father remains modest and grounded.

No flashy jewellery, no modern formal suits, no cinematic styling.

---

# Temple and Ceremony Clothing

For temple visits, weddings, and major ceremonies:

Maintain:

- Clean traditional shirt.
- White veshti.
- Neat hair.
- Subtle moustache grooming.
- Calm respectful presentation.

Ceremony attire should remain culturally accurate for rural Tamil Nadu.

---

# Rainy Season

During rain:

- Cotton fabric becomes slightly heavier.
- Shirt and veshti edges may darken with water.
- Thundu may be used for light rain cover when practical.
- Footwear may appear more often on wet paths.

Clothing should react naturally to weather.

---

# Summer

Preferred:

- Lightweight cotton.
- Half-sleeve shirts.
- Breathable white or light colours.
- Thundu for sweat.

Comfort and sun practicality become priorities.

---

# Cool Season

Occasionally add:

- Slightly thicker cotton shirt in early mornings.
- Thundu wrapped more closely around shoulders before sunrise warmth arrives.

Heavier modern jackets should not appear.

---

# Footwear

Inside house

- Often barefoot.

Courtyard / compound

- Barefoot when practical.

Fields and longer outdoor work

- Traditional sandals or simple rural work footwear when needed.

Village visits

- Traditional flat sandals.

Modern sports shoes, branded footwear, and fashion sandals should rarely or never appear.

---

# Accessories

Father wears almost no jewellery.

Acceptable rare items:

- Simple functional wristwatch only if ever introduced with strong continuity justification.
- No fashion chains.
- No rings worn for style.
- No modern sunglasses as a character signature.

By default, Father's everyday look has no jewellery.

---

# Clothing Movement

Clothing should move naturally.

Maintain:

- Soft cotton folds.
- Natural fabric weight.
- Slight lift while walking.
- Realistic dampness or dust after work.
- Thundu shifting on the shoulder.

Avoid exaggerated flowing fabric or heroic cape-like movement.

---

# Wear and Age

Clothing should show gentle signs of everyday use.

Examples:

- Soft wrinkles.
- Slight fading after repeated washing.
- Faint work marks that disappear after laundry.
- Natural cotton texture.

Clothing should feel lived-in and respected rather than torn or glamorous.

---

# Animation Consistency

Every image, Google Flow scene, storyboard, and animation should preserve:

- Traditional cotton shirt.
- Traditional veshti / dhoti.
- Practical rural drape.
- Optional shoulder thundu.
- Simple colour palette.
- Barefoot or traditional sandals according to place.
- No modern fashion layering.

Avoid wardrobe drift across episodes.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Traditional Tamil rural men's clothing.
- Cotton everyday garments.
- Practical work-ready draping.
- Minimal or no jewellery.
- Barefoot indoors when appropriate.
- Authentic early-1990s village appearance.

Never:

- Western shirts with logos.
- Jeans.
- Modern track pants.
- Designer clothing.
- Gym wear.
- Movie-hero open-shirt styling as a default look.
- Fashion accessories.
- Flashy footwear.

# Guiding Principle

Father's clothing should feel like an extension of his work and character.

Every cotton shirt, every practical veshti fold, every sweat-softened thundu on the shoulder, and every dusty edge after fieldwork should quietly communicate humility, strength, and generations of village farming tradition.
