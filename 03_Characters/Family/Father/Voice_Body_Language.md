# Father Voice & Body Language

## Purpose

This document permanently defines Father's voice, speech patterns, body language, gestures, movement, signature habits, and characteristic sounds throughout the Village Stories universe.

These characteristics should remain consistent across every story, image prompt, Google Flow scene, storyboard, animation, and future production asset.

This document defines how Father naturally communicates through both speech and movement.

---

# Philosophy

Father communicates with grounded calm.

He rarely needs loud words or dramatic gestures.

His presence feels secure because his movements, expressions, voice, and actions naturally convey strength, warmth, and reliability.

---

# Voice Characteristics

Father's voice is:

- Warm.
- Medium-low.
- Steady.
- Clear.
- Mature.
- Calm.
- Reassuring.

He never sounds shrill, rushed, or harsh by default.

His voice naturally settles those around him.

---

# Speaking Pace

Maintain:

- Moderate pace.
- Natural pauses.
- Practical rhythm.
- Clear pronunciation.

He allows conversations to breathe, especially after long work.

---

# Speaking Volume

Normally:

- Comfortable conversational volume.

Occasionally:

- Slightly louder when calling across fields, courtyard, or livestock area.

He rarely shouts.

When he must call out, the sound remains practical rather than angry.

---

# Tone

Father's tone should always feel:

- Respectful.
- Warm.
- Steady.
- Encouraging.
- Practical.

Even when correcting someone, his tone remains controlled and kind.

---

# Communication Style

Father prefers:

- Short clear sentences.
- Practical advice.
- Quiet encouragement.
- Observational remarks about weather, land, and work.

He avoids unnecessary criticism and long speeches.

---

# Listening Style

When listening Father naturally:

- Maintains steady eye contact.
- Nods once or twice.
- Waits before responding.
- Gives full attention when the moment matters.

He rarely interrupts.

---

# Laughter

Father laughs softly to moderately.

Maintain:

- Warm low laughter.
- Gentle smile.
- Relaxed shoulders.
- Genuine eyes.

His laughter should feel sincere and grounding without becoming loud spectacle.

---

# Walking Style

Father walks:

- Steadily.
- With balanced weight.
- At a working man's pace.
- With familiar path memory.

Years of fields and compound routes make every step efficient.

When tired, his pace slows naturally without theatrical exhaustion.

---

# Standing Posture

Maintain:

- Broad relaxed shoulders.
- Upright but unforced posture.
- Balanced stance.
- Ready-for-work body ease.

He never appears stiff like a posed statue.

---

# Sitting and Squatting Style

Father commonly:

- Sits on the Front Thinnai.
- Squats comfortably while repairing or inspecting.
- Sits cross-legged during some family or rest moments.
- Rests with forearms on knees after labour.

His resting postures should look practiced and rural.

---

# Hand Gestures

Father's hands move naturally while speaking and working.

Typical gestures include:

- Open-hand explanations.
- Brief pointing toward fields, canal, or tools.
- Measuring gaps with fingers during repairs.
- Calm palm signals to animals.
- Resting one hand on a tool handle while talking.

Gestures remain economical and purposeful.

---

# Signature Gestures

Father often:

- Adjusts the thundu on his shoulder before leaving.
- Checks a tool with his thumb.
- Wipes sweat from brow or neck with the thundu.
- Rests hands on hips briefly while surveying a field.
- Pats the Family Dog once or twice in greeting.
- Sets tools down neatly before entering home spaces.
- Places a reassuring hand near Elder Brother's shoulder while teaching, and offers gentler guidance to Younger Daughter.

These recurring actions become recognizable habits.

---

# Head Movement

Maintain:

- Steady turns.
- Practical sky checks.
- Gentle nods.
- Calm observation of land and people.

Avoid quick or exaggerated movements.

---

# Eye Movement

Father's eyes move naturally.

He often:

- Looks at the person speaking.
- Scans crops and soil.
- Checks animals.
- Glances at the sky for weather.
- Softens when looking at Mother or the children.

Eye movement should remain intentional and unhurried.

---

# Body Language During Farm Work

Maintain:

- Efficient strength.
- Balanced lifting.
- Confident tool use.
- Smooth transitions between tasks.
- Natural breath and sweat after effort.

Farm work should appear authentic, never like fitness display.

---

# Body Language During Teaching

Maintain:

- Side-by-side positioning with Elder Brother, and careful closeness with Younger Daughter.
- Demonstrations before correction.
- Patient stillness while she tries.
- Encouraging small gestures.

Teaching should feel safe and collaborative.

---

# Body Language During Prayer

When praying Father maintains:

- Calm posture.
- Joined hands.
- Lowered or softly focused gaze.
- Quiet sincerity.

Prayer should feel humble and brief or steady according to the moment.

---

# Body Language Around Family

Father naturally:

- Faces the person he is speaking to.
- Softens his working intensity at home.
- Makes warm eye contact.
- Gives people his attention after washing off the workday.

---

# Signature Sounds

Father has several recognizable everyday sounds.

These include:

- Soft thudu of sandals or bare feet on packed earth.
- Hay rustle while carrying feed.
- Wooden tool handles against soil or fence.
- Bullock cart wood creaking when used.
- Low calm voice calling animals or family.
- Water pouring at the well after work.
- Quiet laughter.
- Cloth of the thundu wiping sweat.
- Gate wood moving at the Livestock Shelter.

These sounds become part of Father's identity.

---

# Silence

Father is comfortable with silence.

He does not feel the need to speak constantly.

Quiet shared presence on the Front Thinnai or in the fields should feel peaceful rather than empty.

---

# Emotional Control

Even during emotional situations:

Maintain:

- Controlled breathing.
- Grounded movement.
- Measured speech.
- Natural expressions.

He rarely loses composure.

---

# Google Flow Performance Rules

Across all Google Flow scenes:

Maintain:

- Natural walking speed.
- Grounded body movement.
- Consistent hand gestures.
- Subtle facial animation.
- Stable speaking rhythm.
- Realistic work effort without exaggeration.

Avoid:

- Heroic slow-motion posing.
- Sudden dramatic movements.
- Overly animated gestures.
- Cartoon-like performances.
- Fashion-model body language.

---

# Audio Identity

Father's recognizable audio identity includes:

- Warm medium-low voice.
- Steady speaking rhythm.
- Soft practical laughter.
- Outdoor work sounds accompanying him.
- Thundu and cotton cloth movement.
- Occasional animal responses to his calm calls.
- Familiar footsteps returning home.

These elements should immediately remind the audience of Father, even before he fully appears on screen.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

- Warm medium-low voice.
- Steady speech.
- Grounded gestures.
- Practical movement.
- Quiet confidence.
- Signature outdoor work sounds.
- Natural body language.

Never:

- Make Father shout without narrative justification.
- Use exaggerated gestures.
- Introduce theatrical body movements.
- Replace subtle communication with heroic performance.
- Make his body language glamorous or stylized.
