# Neighbour Grandmother

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Grandmother of Neighbour Family 01 throughout the Village Stories universe.

She is a recurring elder woman living at House N-02, the Ramasamy Family home northwest of the Main Family.

---

# Identity

The Neighbour Grandmother is approximately **65–70 years old**.

She is a warm, capable South Indian village elder whose knowledge comes from a long life of cooking, gardening, prayer, family care, and observing village seasons.

She treats the Main Family children with grandparent-like affection while respecting their parents' authority.

---

# Narrative Role

She represents:

* Traditional memory.
* Patient affection.
* Garden and flower knowledge.
* Gentle household wisdom.
* The continuity between generations.

---

# Appearance

Maintain:

* Warm medium-brown South Indian complexion.
* Naturally lined face that communicates health, warmth, and age.
* Soft dark-brown eyes.
* Silver-grey or salt-and-pepper hair, always in a simple low bun or practical traditional braid.
* Small traditional red or muted bindi.
* Small gold earrings or simple traditional studs.
* Comfortable, slightly slower but steady movement.

She should appear active for her age, never frail by default and never cosmetically youthful.

---

# Clothing

Everyday:

* Traditional cotton saree.
* Simple short-sleeved blouse.
* Earthy, faded, or gentle colours: cream, soft green, maroon, muted blue, mustard, or brown.
* Minimal jewellery.
* Barefoot in her home and garden when practical; simple sandals outside.

Festivals:

* Cleaner handloom or cotton saree.
* Fresh flowers if appropriate.
* Modest traditional presentation.

Never use modern fashion, heavy makeup, or elaborate jewellery as her default.

---

# Personality

She is:

* Kind.
* Patient.
* Observant.
* Quietly humorous.
* Practical.
* Respectful.
* Generous in small everyday ways.

She does not lecture constantly. Her wisdom appears through remembered details, useful suggestions, stories, and caring action.

---

# Skills and Knowledge

She naturally knows:

* Flower and small-garden care.
* Jasmine and prayer-flower handling where available.
* Simple garland-making.
* Traditional snacks and food sharing.
* Everyday herbal home-remedy knowledge.
* Festival preparation.
* Old village stories and seasonal memory.

She understands that serious illness requires proper medical care.

---

# Behaviour

She naturally:

* Tends flowers or sits at the thinnai during cooler hours.
* Offers a flower, small vegetable bundle, or snack without ceremony.
* Listens fully before speaking.
* Smiles when Younger Daughter brings a question.
* Corrects children softly and only when needed.
* Speaks respectfully with Mother as a neighbour, not an authority over her.

---

# Relationship With Main Family

## Mother

They share neighbourly trust through recipes, flowers, festival help, and practical conversation.

## Father

She respects his work and may share remembered weather or crop observations gently, without pretending to direct him.

## Elder Brother

She encourages patience, respectful speech, and small acts of responsibility. He sees her as a trusted elder.

## Younger Daughter

She gives the child warm grandparent-like attention: stories, flowers, small safe helping tasks, and reassurance.

## Family Dog

She recognizes the German Shepherd as a familiar family companion. The dog remains calm and respectful near her.

---

# Voice and Body Language

Voice:

* Soft, warm, mature Tamil cadence.
* Calm, deliberate pace.
* Light laugh rather than loud amusement.

Body language:

* Gentle nods.
* Slow open-palmed teaching gestures.
* One hand resting on lap, walking stick, or flower basket only if a future visual lock establishes it.
* No exaggerated frailty acting.

Signature sounds:

* Saree rustle.
* Flower stems being sorted.
* Soft low laughter.
* Quiet storytelling voice.

---

# Daily Rhythm

She generally:

* Begins with simple prayer and household freshness.
* Tends a flower patch or small home garden in the morning.
* Completes light household tasks.
* Rests during harsh midday heat.
* Receives neighbours or shares small items in late afternoon.
* Participates in festival preparation when appropriate.

She is never expected to perform heavy work.

---

# Expressions

Maintain:

* Gentle closed-mouth smile.
* Patient eyes.
* Mild curiosity brow lift.
* Quiet concern when a child is upset.
* Deep but restrained emotion during memories.

Avoid cartoon elderly expressions or constant sadness.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Age 65–70 range.
* Traditional elderly South Indian village appearance.
* Flower/garden and household wisdom.
* Grandmother-like warmth toward the children.
* Respectful equality with Mother as a neighbour.
* House N-02 residence.

Never:

* Make her a replacement mother.
* Give her modern styling.
* Make her physically helpless without a specific story event.
* Use her wisdom as magic or unquestionable authority.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Neighbour Grandmother within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Neighbour Grandmother should feel like a living store of warmth: a woman whose flowers, stories, and small acts of care make the neighbourhood feel older, safer, and more connected.
