# Neighbour Grandfather

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Grandfather of Neighbour Family 01 throughout the Village Stories universe.

He is a recurring elder man living at House N-02, the Ramasamy Family home northwest of the Main Family.

---

# Identity

The Neighbour Grandfather is approximately **68–73 years old**.

He is a respected village elder with a long memory of fields, rain, livestock, village routes, and changing seasons.

He is not a formal village leader in every situation. His authority comes from age, calm judgment, and a lifetime of ordinary participation in village life.

---

# Narrative Role

He represents:

* Village memory.
* Calm elder perspective.
* Respect for land and seasons.
* Gentle mentorship.
* Quiet neighbourhood watchfulness.

---

# Appearance

Maintain:

* Warm medium-brown South Indian complexion with natural age lines.
* Lean-to-medium elderly build.
* Silver-grey hair, kept short and simple.
* Neat grey moustache.
* Dark brown eyes, alert and kind.
* Upright but age-appropriate slower posture.

He should look healthy and active within his age range, never artificially young and never helpless by default.

---

# Clothing

Everyday:

* White, cream, or lightly coloured cotton shirt.
* Traditional white or off-white veshti.
* Soft thundu on shoulder when useful.
* Simple sandals outside; barefoot within the home when practical.

Festivals:

* Clean white veshti and fresh cotton shirt.
* Neater grooming, still modest.

Never use modern formalwear, fashion accessories, or cinematic elder styling.

---

# Personality

He is:

* Calm.
* Patient.
* Observant.
* Mildly humorous.
* Fair-minded.
* Unhurried.
* Respectful toward younger adults.

He gives advice only when useful or asked. Silence is comfortable for him.

---

# Skills and Knowledge

He naturally understands:

* Seasonal weather patterns.
* Older farming practices.
* Basic tool care.
* Livestock and field observations.
* Traditional village customs.
* Local walking routes and landmarks.
* Storytelling from lived experience.

His knowledge complements Father's working expertise; it does not replace it.

---

# Behaviour

He naturally:

* Sits on the thinnai or beneath shade during cooler hours.
* Watches the Main Village Path without gossiping harshly.
* Greets passing neighbours.
* Shares a brief observation about weather or crops.
* Lets children ask questions.
* Offers simple help within his strength.

He does not need to appear in every public gathering.

---

# Relationship With Main Family

## Father

Father treats him with respect. Their conversations may include rain, soil, crops, animals, and village memory.

The Grandfather does not command Father; he offers perspective.

## Mother

He respects her household work and greeting rituals, and accepts her hospitality with modest gratitude.

## Elder Brother

He gives the boy patience, small practical lessons, and stories that teach attention without long lectures.

## Younger Daughter

He responds gently to her questions and gives her a sense of safety, never expecting adult-like behaviour.

## Family Dog

The German Shepherd recognizes him as a familiar neighbour and remains calm at House N-02.

---

# Voice and Body Language

Voice:

* Low, steady, aged but clear Tamil cadence.
* Few words, chosen carefully.
* Soft chuckle rather than loud laughter.

Body language:

* Slow nod.
* Hands resting on knees while seated.
* Small measured pointing gesture when explaining a route or field detail.
* A walking stick may be used only if future visual canon deliberately establishes it; it is not required by default.

Signature sounds:

* Veshti cotton movement.
* Sandal steps on packed earth.
* Quiet greeting.
* Soft clearing of throat before a story or observation.

---

# Daily Rhythm

He generally:

* Wakes early and observes the morning air.
* Completes light personal routines and prayer.
* Walks short familiar distances or sits in shade.
* Checks the neighbourhood rhythm without intruding.
* Rests during heat.
* Receives neighbours in the later afternoon.
* Participates in festivals at an age-appropriate pace.

---

# Expressions

Maintain:

* Calm resting face.
* Small knowing smile.
* Attentive eyes when listening to children.
* Quiet concern during difficult weather.
* Soft pride when observing younger generations learn.

Avoid exaggerated sternness, comic grumpiness, or constant melancholy.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Age 68–73 range.
* Traditional elderly South Indian village appearance.
* Calm elder wisdom without domination.
* Respectful relationship with Father and Mother.
* Patient presence around children.
* House N-02 residence.

Never:

* Turn him into an all-powerful village chief.
* Give him modern styling.
* Treat him as physically helpless by default.
* Use his age only for jokes.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Neighbour Grandfather within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Neighbour Grandfather should feel like a familiar shade tree: quiet, grounded, and full of lived knowledge when someone pauses long enough to listen.
