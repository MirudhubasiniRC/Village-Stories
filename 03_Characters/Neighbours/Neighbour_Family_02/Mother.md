# Neighbour Mother

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Mother of Neighbour Family 02 throughout the Village Stories universe.

She is a recurring young village woman living at House N-03, the Kumar Family home northeast of the Main Family.

---

# Identity

The Neighbour Mother is approximately **28–33 years old**.

She is a capable village homemaker and farming-family partner. She shares practical friendship with Mother through food, festival work, vegetable exchange, and everyday neighbour support.

She is not a duplicate of Mother. Her warmth is slightly more conversational and openly social, while remaining culturally grounded and respectful.

---

# Narrative Role

She represents:

* Neighbourly women’s friendship.
* Recipe exchange.
* Festival cooperation.
* Shared everyday care.
* A second young household rhythm within the village.

---

# Appearance

Maintain:

* Warm medium-brown South Indian complexion.
* Healthy medium build shaped by household and garden work.
* Dark brown expressive eyes.
* Naturally black hair in a simple braid or low bun for work.
* Small traditional bindi.
* Minimal traditional gold earrings or studs.
* Practical, recognizably different face shape and hairstyle language from Mother.

Avoid glamour styling, heavy makeup, and modern salon presentation.

---

# Clothing

Everyday:

* Traditional cotton saree with simple blouse.
* Practical drape for household and festival preparation work.
* Earthy or soft colours: green, rust, muted blue, cream, maroon, or mustard.
* Minimal jewellery.
* Barefoot in home spaces; simple sandals outside as needed.

Festival:

* Cleaner handloom or silk-cotton saree.
* Fresh flowers when appropriate.
* Still modest and local.

---

# Personality

She is:

* Friendly.
* Capable.
* Generous.
* Sociable without gossip cruelty.
* Practical.
* Encouraging with children.
* Calm in busy preparation periods.

She enjoys shared work because it makes ordinary tasks lighter.

---

# Skills

She naturally knows:

* Traditional home cooking.
* Recipe variation and ingredient exchange.
* Festival food preparation.
* Garden-produce sorting.
* Household organization.
* Childcare and gentle guidance.
* Kolam and simple flower preparation when appropriate.

She does not replace Mother's established cooking authority in the Main Family; she brings her own capable household knowledge.

---

# Behaviour

She naturally:

* Visits Mother at a reasonable neighbourly rhythm.
* Exchanges vegetables, recipes, or festival ingredients.
* Helps without taking over.
* Welcomes children into safe shared play.
* Keeps her own household orderly and independent.

Her presence should feel like friendship, not constant access to the Main Family home.

---

# Relationship With Main Family

## Mother

They are trusted friends. They exchange recipes, vegetables, festival tasks, and practical support.

Their conversations are warm but never written as endless gossip.

## Father

She treats Father respectfully as her husband's farming friend and a familiar neighbour.

## Elder Brother and Younger Daughter

She is a safe familiar adult who welcomes them during age-appropriate neighbour visits and play.

## Family Dog

The German Shepherd knows her as a trusted neighbour and behaves calmly.

---

# Voice and Body Language

Voice:

* Warm clear Tamil cadence.
* Slightly more animated than Mother's, without becoming loud.
* Encouraging with children.

Body language:

* Open hand gestures when sharing or explaining a recipe.
* Efficient sari adjustments before work.
* Relaxed neighbourly doorstep posture.

Signature sounds:

* Cotton saree movement.
* Vegetable basket or small bowl handling.
* Light friendly laugh.

---

# Daily Rhythm

She generally:

* Begins with home prayer and breakfast work.
* Manages her own household.
* Exchanges small practical visits with neighbours when appropriate.
* Supports seasonal farm-family rhythms.
* Prepares evening food and gathers her family at House N-03.

---

# Expressions

Maintain:

* Friendly greeting smile.
* Focused food-preparation concentration.
* Warm amusement at children playing.
* Mild concern during neighbour difficulty.
* Festival-day brightness without glamour.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Age 28–33 range.
* Young farming-family mother identity.
* Friendly, independent relationship with Mother.
* Distinct appearance and manner from Mother.
* House N-03 residence.

Never:

* Turn her into a copy of Mother.
* Make gossip or conflict her defining trait.
* Modernize her clothing or household life.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Neighbour Mother within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Neighbour Mother should feel like the friend who arrives with a bowl, a useful idea, and enough warmth to make shared work feel lighter.
