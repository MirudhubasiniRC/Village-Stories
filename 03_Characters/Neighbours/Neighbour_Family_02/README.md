# Neighbour Family 02 — Kumar Young Farming Family

## Purpose

This folder contains the canonical documentation for Neighbour Family 02: the young farming household living at House N-03, northeast of the Main Family.

The Village Neighbourhood map already identifies this residence as the Kumar Family. That established house identity remains permanent; this folder defines its recurring members through functional production identities.

---

# Household Identity

Neighbour Family 02 is a young farming family whose everyday rhythm closely parallels the Main Family's outdoor and household life.

They are neighbours, friends, and seasonal work partners—not duplicates of the Main Family. Their relationship adds shared harvest help, recipe exchange, child friendship, and lived-in community cooperation to the village.

---

# Residents

* Neighbour Father — approximately 32–36
* Neighbour Mother — approximately 28–33
* Neighbour Son — approximately 8–10

Personal names are not required in regular production dialogue. Use functional identities unless a future story earns a permanent name.

---

# Narrative Function

The Kumar Family represents:

* Farming friendship.
* Neighbourly cooperation.
* Shared harvest labour.
* Everyday parent friendship.
* Childhood play between households.

---

# Relationship With Main Family

* Neighbour Father and Father help one another during seasonal farm work when needed.
* Neighbour Mother and Mother exchange recipes, vegetables, and festival preparation help.
* Neighbour Son is an age-near peer of Elder Brother and a safe familiar play companion for Younger Daughter.
* Both families share ordinary greetings, seasonal concerns, and quiet mutual aid.

No relationship should feel forced or over-present in every episode.

---

# Typical Story Use

Natural story settings include:

* Shared harvest work.
* Seedling or irrigation help.
* Recipe and vegetable exchange.
* Festival preparation.
* Children fishing only under safe adult supervision.
* Kite flying, marbles, tree-edge play, and school-route friendship.
* Rain disrupting a neighbourly plan.

# Household Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Young farming-family identity.
* House N-03 / Kumar Family map continuity.
* Equal, friendly relationship with the Main Family.
* Seasonal farming cooperation.
* Child play that stays culturally and physically safe.

Never:

* Make them a duplicate replacement for the Main Family.
* Move them to another house.
* Turn neighbour friendship into default dramatic conflict.
* Make the child an unsupervised river or field adventurer.
