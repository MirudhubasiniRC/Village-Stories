# Neighbour Son

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Son of Neighbour Family 02 throughout the Village Stories universe.

He is a recurring village child living at House N-03, the Kumar Family home northeast of the Main Family.

---

# Identity

The Neighbour Son is approximately **8–10 years old**.

He is an age-near friend of Elder Brother and a familiar, safe older play companion to Younger Daughter.

He is a child—not an adult helper, not a reckless adventurer, and not a replacement sibling within the Main Family.

---

# Narrative Role

He represents:

* Village childhood friendship.
* Outdoor play.
* Shared small adventures.
* Learning fair play and responsibility.
* The world beyond one household.

---

# Appearance

Maintain:

* Warm medium-brown South Indian complexion.
* Lean active child build.
* Dark brown curious eyes.
* Naturally black short-to-medium rural schoolboy hair.
* Face shape and smile clearly distinct from Elder Brother.
* Similar child scale to Elder Brother, never confused with him.

He should look like a healthy village boy, never like a styled child actor.

---

# Clothing

Everyday:

* Simple cotton shirt or banian with shirt depending on activity.
* Practical shorts or child-appropriate cotton bottoms.
* Bare feet in familiar compound spaces; simple sandals on village paths and for school.

School:

* Clean simple school-appropriate clothing consistent with the Primary School period canon.

Festival:

* Fresh simple shirt and neat child clothing.

No branded sportswear, modern graphic tees, or city-fashion styling.

---

# Personality

He is:

* Energetic.
* Friendly.
* Curious.
* Slightly adventurous.
* Fair-minded.
* Loyal to friends.
* Sometimes competitive in harmless games.
* Quick to apologize after small mistakes.

His energy differs from Elder Brother's protective responsibility: he is more likely to suggest a game, while Elder Brother may be more likely to consider safety or duty.

---

# Skills

He naturally knows:

* Village paths near home and school.
* Common outdoor games.
* Basic kite flying.
* Marbles and hide-and-seek rules.
* Safe, supervised pond-edge or river-edge observation only.
* Small age-appropriate household or farm chores.

He may learn fishing observation or simple line handling only with adult supervision. He never swims, fishes, climbs, or roams in hazardous places alone.

---

# Behaviour

He naturally:

* Calls for Elder Brother from the path or courtyard edge.
* Includes Younger Daughter in safe, slower games.
* Runs, plays, and returns home before family routines require.
* Listens when an adult stops unsafe play.
* Carries a small school bag or simple play item rather than modern toys.

---

# Relationship With Main Family

## Elder Brother

They are age-near friends and recurring play companions.

They may:

* Fly kites.
* Play marbles.
* Race along safe open paths.
* Help briefly with light chores.
* Walk school routes.
* Learn from mistakes with adult guidance.

Their friendship is warm, not competitive rivalry.

## Younger Daughter

He is a familiar older child, not her replacement brother.

He includes her in safe games, slows down when needed, and treats her kindly. Her strongest sibling bond remains with Elder Brother.

## Mother and Father

Both see him as a known neighbour child. They guide him gently when he joins household play or safe chores.

## Family Dog

The German Shepherd recognizes him as a regular play visitor and remains friendly under adult-established compound boundaries.

---

# Voice and Body Language

Voice:

* Clear energetic boy voice.
* Natural age-appropriate Tamil speech.
* Fast when excited; respectful when answering adults.

Body language:

* Quick runs and sudden pauses.
* Sideways grin before proposing a game.
* Squatting with Elder Brother to inspect something interesting.
* Careful smaller movements when Younger Daughter joins.

Signature sounds:

* Footsteps on packed earth.
* Kite paper or string handling.
* Marbles clicking.
* Child laughter and path-side calls.

---

# Daily Rhythm

He generally:

* Follows school-day routine when school is in session.
* Plays after school with village children.
* Helps his parents with tiny safe tasks.
* Returns to House N-03 for meals and evening routine.
* Participates in festival play at an age-appropriate distance from crowds.

---

# Expressions

Maintain:

* Wide friendly smile.
* Curious forward-leaning attention.
* Small competitive focus during games.
* Quick concern if Younger Daughter becomes upset.
* Sheepish apology after childish mistakes.

Avoid adult hardness, mockery, or cartoon exaggeration.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Age 8–10 range.
* Friendly village-boy identity.
* Distinct face and clothes from Elder Brother.
* Peer friendship with Elder Brother.
* Safe, kind inclusion of Younger Daughter.
* House N-03 residence.

Never:

* Make him a reckless danger source.
* Turn him into a bully.
* Replace Elder Brother's unique family role.
* Give him adult labour responsibilities.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Neighbour Son within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

The Neighbour Son should feel like the friend waiting just beyond the gate: an invitation to play, a familiar laugh, and a reminder that childhood belongs to the whole village.
