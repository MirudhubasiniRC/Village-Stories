# Grocery Shopkeeper Voice & Body Language

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Grocery Shopkeeper's voice, speech patterns, body language, and signature sounds throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Voice Characteristics

Maintain:

* Calm medium-low male voice.
* Clear rural Tamil cadence.
* Unhurried practical speech.
* Softer respectful tone with elders.
* Patient clarity with children.

He does not need to speak loudly for the shop to feel active.

---

# Speaking Style

He uses:

* Short useful questions.
* Clear quantity confirmations.
* Familiar greetings.
* Gentle practical advice.
* Quiet humour when appropriate.

---

# Body Language

Maintain:

* Counter-centred stance.
* Confident scale handling.
* Small precise hand movements.
* Slight forward lean while listening to a customer.
* Relaxed shoulders during quiet hours.

---

# Signature Gestures

* Tapping a weight gently into position.
* Pressing a packet fold flat.
* Running one hand over the account page before writing.
* Reaching automatically to the correct shelf.
* Nodding once after confirming an order.

---

# Signature Sounds

The audio identity includes:

* Metal scale weights clicking.
* Grain pouring into a bowl or packet.
* Paper rustling and folding.
* Glass jar lids opening.
* Pencil scratching in the account notebook.
* Soft greeting from behind the counter.
* Shutter movement at opening and closing.

These sounds should identify him and the store together.

---

# Google Flow Performance Rules

Maintain natural work speed, accurate hand-scale movements, restrained gestures, and consistent voice warmth.

Avoid slapstick spills or exaggerated bargaining performance.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain calm voice, precise hands, and modest counter-centred movement.

Never make him harshly shout, gesture theatrically, or move like a modern retail salesperson.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Grocery Shopkeeper's voice and body language throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should be recognizable by sound and motion before his face appears: scale click, grain pour, paper fold, calm greeting.
