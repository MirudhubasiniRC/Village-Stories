# Grocery Shopkeeper Clothing

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Grocery Shopkeeper's wardrobe throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Philosophy

He dresses for measured work, shelf dust, village heat, and respectful customer contact.

His clothes must feel authentic to early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu, never like retail-uniform fashion.

---

# Everyday Clothing

Canonical everyday clothing:

* Simple light cotton shirt, usually half-sleeved.
* Traditional cotton veshti / dhoti in a practical wrap.
* Optional soft cotton thundu over shoulder or nearby for dust and sweat.
* Barefoot inside the shop or simple sandals at the threshold.

---

# Colour Palette

Preferred colours:

* Off white.
* Cream.
* Faded pale blue.
* Light brown.
* Soft grey.
* Subtle checks.

Avoid logos, neon colours, designer cuts, and modern retail uniforms.

---

# Work Adjustments

During stock work, he may:

* Fold veshti slightly for lifting sacks.
* Roll shirt sleeves when dusting shelves.
* Carry the thundu over one shoulder.
* Show light flour, grain, or spice dust on clothing.

---

# Festival Days

For festivals:

* Clean pressed-looking cotton shirt without artificial perfection.
* Fresh white or cream veshti.
* Same modest moustache and rural grooming.

The shop may be busier, but he is never dressed as a modern businessman.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain simple cotton shirt, practical veshti, and humble provisions-shop identity.

Never:

* Add trousers, jeans, branded polo shirts, or modern shop aprons as default.
* Introduce flashy jewellery.
* Use a modern corporate uniform.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Grocery Shopkeeper's wardrobe throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

His clothes should look ready for the scale, the ledger, and the next cloth bag handed over the counter.
