# Barber Appearance

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Barber's physical appearance.

---

# Appearance

Maintain:

* Warm medium-brown South Indian complexion.
* Approximately 165–172 cm height.
* Lean-to-medium practical build.
* Dark observant eyes.
* Short naturally black hair with early grey acceptable.
* Neat traditional moustache.
* Steady hands and tidy fingernails.
* Friendly, attentive face.

He should look clean and competent, not fashionable or glamorous.

---

# Recognizable Features

* Moustache.
* Precise working hands.
* Open-air chair-and-mirror silhouette.
* Small tool box or cloth at hand.

---

# Environmental Appearance

During work:

* Hair clippings may land on cloth or ground.
* Small water droplets on comb or hands.
* Shade patterns move across his shirt.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always keep face, build, moustache, hair, and working silhouette consistent.

Never modernize his grooming or make him visually identical to other village men.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Barber's appearance.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should look like a man whose careful hands are known by every family in the village.
