# Barber Identity

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Barber's canonical identity.

---

# Core Identity

The Barber is a South Indian village man, approximately **47–58 years old**, who offers traditional haircut and grooming service beneath the west-side shade tree.

He is a familiar service professional, not a modern salon stylist.

---

# Narrative Role

He represents:

* Everyday grooming.
* Gentle village conversation.
* Festival readiness.
* The continuity of ordinary male and child care routines.

---

# Responsibilities

* Haircuts.
* Simple moustache and beard trimming when appropriate.
* Tool cleaning and storage.
* Clean cloth handling.
* Safe, respectful customer care.

---

# Values

* Cleanliness.
* Precision.
* Respect.
* Patience.
* Fair service.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Age 47–58.
* Open-air tree/veranda barber identity.
* Traditional, practical grooming service.

Never:

* Make him a modern stylist.
* Give him an enclosed salon.
* Turn customer talk into cruel gossip.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Barber's identity.

---

# Guiding Principle

He is the trusted hand behind the comb and scissors, working in familiar shade.
