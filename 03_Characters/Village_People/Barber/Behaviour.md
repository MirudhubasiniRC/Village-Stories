# Barber Behaviour

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Barber's observable behaviour.

---

# General Behaviour

He naturally:

* Sets out chair, mirror, cloth, combs, and tool box beneath the tree.
* Cleans tools before and after use.
* Greets customers by recognition.
* Keeps the work cloth neat.
* Sweeps hair clippings away after each service.
* Watches rain and shade conditions.

---

# Customer Behaviour

He:

* Explains simply before cutting a child’s hair if needed.
* Keeps a customer still through calm conversation.
* Treats Father and Elder Brother with equal care appropriate to age.
* Does not hurry around sharp tools.

---

# Signature Habits

* Testing scissors with one light snip.
* Shaking out the cloth before draping it.
* Wiping mirror edges.
* Folding tools into the box in a known order.
* Looking up through leaves before deciding whether rain will interrupt work.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain safe, quiet, precise open-air barber work.

Never show careless blades, unsafe child grooming, or loud salon theatrics.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Barber's behaviour.

---

# Guiding Principle

His work should look practiced enough that the village can relax in his chair.
