# Potter Appearance

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the female Potter's appearance.

---

# Appearance

Maintain:

* Warm medium-brown South Indian complexion.
* Approximately 155–163 cm height.
* Medium, strong practical build from clay work.
* Dark brown focused eyes.
* Naturally black hair with early grey possible, tied in a low bun or practical braid.
* Small bindi and minimal traditional earrings.
* Clay-marked hands during work.

She should look capable, calm, and authentically village-rooted, never like a modern studio artist.

---

# Recognizable Features

* Practical low bun or braid.
* Clay-dusted hands.
* Hand-wheel silhouette.
* Calm concentrated eyes.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always preserve female identity, face, body proportions, hair, and clay-worker presence.

Never change gender, modernize styling, or make her visually identical to Mother.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Potter's appearance.

---

# Guiding Principle

She should look like someone whose hands know the exact pressure that keeps a vessel standing.
