# Potter Behaviour

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Potter's observable behaviour.

---

# General Behaviour

She naturally:

* Prepares clay before forming.
* Centres clay patiently on the hand wheel.
* Shapes vessels with steady pressure.
* Sets finished pieces carefully on drying boards.
* Checks outdoor kiln safety before firing.
* Keeps children away from kiln heat.

---

# Signature Habits

* Wetting hands before shaping.
* Looking closely at a rim before setting a pot down.
* Brushing clay from fingers on a work cloth.
* Checking sky and humidity before planning drying.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain calm hand-wheel work, outdoor-kiln safety, and traditional clay process.

Never create rushed production or unsafe child access.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Potter's behaviour.

---

# Guiding Principle

Her movements should make the pottery process feel slow because it matters, not because it lacks energy.
