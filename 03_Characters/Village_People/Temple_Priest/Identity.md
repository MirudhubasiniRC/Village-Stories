# Temple Priest Identity

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's canonical identity.

---

# Core Identity

The Temple Priest is a South Indian village man, approximately **58–68 years old**, who tends the Vinayagar Shrine beneath the Sacred Banyan Tree.

He serves through daily ritual, care of the modest shrine, and calm welcome to villagers. He is a caretaker of tradition, not an untouchable or theatrical figure.

---

# Responsibilities

* Morning and evening pooja.
* Lighting and tending brass lamps.
* Keeping flowers, incense, kumkum, turmeric, and offering items orderly.
* Ringing the small shrine bell at appropriate ritual moments.
* Supporting festival prayer preparation.
* Receiving villagers with quiet respect.

---

# Values

* Humility.
* Compassion.
* Ritual care.
* Cleanliness.
* Patience.
* Community peace.

---

# Narrative Function

He appears in:

* Morning prayer scenes.
* Festival worship.
* Family blessings.
* Quiet reflection.
* Flower and garland preparation.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Age 58–68 range.
* Gentle spiritual caretaker identity.
* Vinayagar Shrine location lock.
* Humble service, never magical power.

Never:

* Make him judgmental, greedy, or an all-powerful village authority.
* Turn rituals into spectacle without cultural purpose.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's identity.

---

# Guiding Principle

His presence should feel like a lamp kept burning: steady, modest, and comforting.
