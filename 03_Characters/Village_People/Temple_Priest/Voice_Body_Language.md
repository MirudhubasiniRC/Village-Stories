# Temple Priest Voice & Body Language

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's voice, body language, and signature sounds.

---

# Voice

Maintain:

* Soft mature male Tamil voice.
* Calm deliberate ritual cadence.
* Gentle conversational tone.
* Clear but never forceful public guidance.

---

# Body Language

Maintain:

* Relaxed upright posture.
* Folded hands before ritual.
* Precise lamp and flower handling.
* Small nod to greet villagers.
* Slow careful movement around the shrine.

---

# Signature Sounds

* Small brass bell at ritual moments.
* Oil lamp flame.
* Incense holder movement.
* Flower garland handling.
* Quiet prayer voice.
* Banyan leaves moving above the shrine.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain restrained voice and careful ritual movement.

Never make him loudly perform, shout, or use exaggerated spiritual gestures.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's voice and body language.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should be recognizable by bell, lamp, and a voice quiet enough to let the Banyan Tree remain part of the scene.
