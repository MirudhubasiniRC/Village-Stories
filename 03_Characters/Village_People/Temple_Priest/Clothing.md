# Temple Priest Clothing

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's wardrobe.

---

# Everyday Shrine Clothing

Maintain:

* Clean white or cream cotton veshti / dhoti.
* Simple cotton upper cloth or bare upper body only during appropriate traditional ritual service.
* Modest angavastram / shoulder cloth when appropriate.
* Bare feet within shrine service space.

His appearance must remain respectful, culturally grounded, and never costume-like.

---

# Ritual Adjustments

During pooja:

* Freshly washed cloth.
* Vibhuti marking.
* Flowers, lamps, and offering items handled carefully.

Festival:

* Cleaner traditional cotton or silk-cotton veshti.
* Same humble restraint.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain simple traditional priest clothing and shrine-service practicality.

Never use modern formalwear, flashy jewellery, dramatic robes, or theatrical costumes.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's clothing.

---

# Guiding Principle

His clothing should serve the ritual and disappear into the shrine's quiet dignity.
