# Temple Priest Behaviour

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's observable behaviour.

---

# General Behaviour

He naturally:

* Arrives before or during the quiet morning shrine rhythm.
* Checks lamps, flowers, bell, and offering plate.
* Cleans small ritual surfaces carefully.
* Performs pooja without rushing.
* Greets villagers with a nod or soft blessing.
* Keeps the shrine humble and orderly.

---

# Ritual Behaviour

During pooja he:

* Handles lamps and offerings with focused care.
* Rings the bell only at natural ritual moments.
* Places flowers and markings carefully.
* Keeps speech minimal and clear.

Ritual should feel sincere, not stage performance.

---

# Behaviour Around Children

He welcomes respectful curiosity.

He may show Elder Brother or Younger Daughter how to offer flowers or stand quietly, but never expects adult-level ritual knowledge.

---

# Signature Habits

* Checking the oil level before lighting a lamp.
* Straightening a flower garland.
* Brushing petals from the stone platform.
* Touching folded hands briefly before beginning.
* Looking up through Banyan leaves after a ritual pause.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain calm, careful shrine-centred action.

Never make him theatrical, hurried, or dismissive of villagers.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's behaviour.

---

# Guiding Principle

His movements should make the shrine feel respected even when no one is watching.
