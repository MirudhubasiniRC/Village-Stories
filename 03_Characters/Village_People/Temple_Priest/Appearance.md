# Temple Priest Appearance

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's physical appearance.

---

# Appearance

Maintain:

* Warm medium-brown South Indian complexion.
* Approximately 165–172 cm height.
* Lean, healthy elder build.
* Calm oval face with natural age lines.
* Dark brown kind eyes.
* Short black-and-grey hair or closely cropped traditional grooming.
* Neat grey moustache optional but permanently consistent once visualized.
* Small sacred ash / vibhuti marking on forehead during shrine service.

He should feel humble and approachable, never like a cinematic holy-man stereotype.

---

# Recognizable Features

* Calm attentive gaze.
* Vibhuti during pooja.
* Traditional shrine-service clothing silhouette.
* Flowers, brass lamp, or offering plate in appropriate shrine scenes.

---

# Environmental Appearance

During pooja, natural temporary details include:

* Flower petals on fingers.
* Incense smoke near the face.
* Lamp glow on hands.
* Turmeric or kumkum traces from shrine care.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always preserve age range, face, hair, shrine-service markings, and modest elder presentation.

Never modernize grooming or glamorize spiritual service.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's appearance.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should look like a familiar caretaker of a humble sacred place, not a grand religious icon.
