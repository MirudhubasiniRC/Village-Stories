# Temple Priest Daily Routine

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's typical daily rhythm.

---

# Daily Timeline

```text
Morning shrine preparation

↓

Morning pooja

↓

Light shrine care / village greetings

↓

Midday quiet

↓

Evening lamp and pooja

↓

Festival preparation when needed
```

---

# Morning

He:

* Cleans and checks the shrine.
* Prepares flowers, lamp oil, incense, and offering items.
* Performs morning pooja.
* Receives brief prayers from passing villagers.

---

# Midday

The shrine is quieter.

He completes light care, rests, or prepares materials for later service. He does not create constant public activity.

---

# Evening

He:

* Relights lamps.
* Performs brief evening pooja.
* Supports villagers stopping after work.
* Secures ritual items respectfully.

---

# Festival Variations

Festival periods bring:

* Extra garlands and lamps.
* Community prayer.
* Decoration support.
* Coordination with Festival Ground events where relevant.

The shrine remains humble even at its most decorated.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain morning and evening shrine rhythm.

Never leave the shrine uncared for during ordinary active days without a story reason.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Temple Priest's daily routine.

---

# Guiding Principle

His day should give the shrine its dependable rhythm from first lamp to evening glow.
