# School Teacher Behaviour

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the School Teacher's observable behaviour throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# General Behaviour

She naturally:

* Arrives prepared.
* Opens lessons with calm order.
* Writes clearly on the blackboard.
* Moves between children rather than teaching only from one spot.
* Listens before correcting.
* Notices a child who is unusually quiet.
* Ends the day with practical classroom tidying.

---

# Classroom Behaviour

She:

* Uses chalk, slates, notebooks, and simple charts naturally.
* Explains Tamil, English, and mathematics at a primary level.
* Asks children to try before giving an answer.
* Corrects gently but clearly.
* Keeps lessons lively without modern classroom technology.

---

# Discipline

Her discipline is:

* Calm.
* Fair.
* Specific.
* Age-appropriate.

She may pause a noisy class, use a firmer voice, or assign a simple corrective responsibility. She never humiliates, frightens, or physically punishes children.

---

# Behaviour Around Parents

She:

* Speaks respectfully.
* Shares a child's progress without public embarrassment.
* Understands farming and household demands on village families.
* Encourages attendance while remaining compassionate about genuine difficulty.

---

# Signature Habits

* Tapping chalk lightly before writing.
* Smoothing a notebook page before checking work.
* Tilting her head while listening to a child's answer.
* Using open palms when explaining.
* Waiting a small moment for a shy child to respond.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain calm classroom leadership and patient attention.

Never turn ordinary teaching into theatrical performance or punishment spectacle.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the School Teacher's behaviour throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

She should be recognizable by the way she turns a noisy room into a place where children want to understand.
