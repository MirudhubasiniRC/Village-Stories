# School Teacher Voice & Body Language

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the School Teacher's voice, speech, body language, and signature sounds throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Voice

Maintain:

* Clear warm female Tamil voice.
* Moderate, patient teaching pace.
* Slightly firmer projection during class instruction.
* Softer respectful tone with parents and shy children.

Her voice should carry across a modest classroom without becoming harsh.

---

# Speaking Style

She uses:

* Short clear instructions.
* Gentle questions.
* Encouraging repetition.
* Simple examples from daily village life.
* Respectful adult conversation.

---

# Body Language

Maintain:

* Upright relaxed stance.
* Open palms while explaining.
* Small chalk gestures at the board.
* Calm movement between desks or mats.
* Bending slightly to speak at child height when appropriate.

---

# Signature Gestures

* Straightening a child's notebook gently.
* Tapping chalk once before writing.
* Holding a hand open to invite an answer.
* Nodding after effort.
* Adjusting her saree pallu before assembly or class.

---

# Signature Sounds

* Chalk on blackboard.
* Notebook pages turning.
* Soft classroom call-and-response.
* Bell or assembly sound nearby.
* Cloth bag and lesson materials.
* Warm clear instructions.

---

# Google Flow Performance Rules

Maintain natural teacher movement, restrained gestures, and voice clarity.

Avoid modern presentation-style performance, theatrical scolding, or cartoon classroom acting.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain clear calm voice, modest gestures, and classroom-centred physical language.

Never use aggressive shouting or modern corporate speech patterns.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the School Teacher's voice and body language throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

She should be recognizable in sound before sight: chalk, a patient question, and a voice that makes a classroom settle.
