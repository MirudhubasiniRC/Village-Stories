# School Teacher Appearance

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the School Teacher's physical appearance throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Identity

She is a healthy South Indian village teacher whose appearance communicates practical dignity, warmth, and professional steadiness in early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu.

She should be recognizably distinct from Mother and Neighbour Mother.

---

# Appearance

Maintain:

* Warm medium-brown South Indian complexion.
* Approximately 158–165 cm height.
* Medium healthy build.
* Soft oval-to-rectangular face with intelligent dark-brown eyes.
* Naturally black hair, often in a neat low bun or single braid suitable for a teaching day.
* Small traditional bindi.
* Simple small earrings.
* Calm upright posture.

Her face should look attentive and approachable, never glamorous or stern by default.

---

# Hair

Maintain:

* Thick naturally black hair.
* Neatly tied low bun or single braid.
* No salon styling, modern cut, or loose fashion hair.
* Festival flowers only when story appropriate.

---

# Recognizable Features

She should remain instantly recognizable through:

* Neat teacher braid or bun.
* Small bindi.
* Steady intelligent eyes.
* Simple cotton-saree silhouette.
* Upright classroom posture.
* Calm encouraging expression.

---

# Environmental Appearance

At school:

* Chalk dust may lightly touch fingertips.
* A notebook, slate, or chalk may be in hand.
* Hair remains neat despite a full day.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain face, hair style family, skin tone, age range, teacher silhouette, and modest appearance.

Never:

* Make her visually identical to Mother.
* Modernize her into a city-office appearance.
* Apply heavy makeup or fashion styling.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the School Teacher's physical appearance throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

She should look like the familiar person children see at the blackboard: composed, kind, and ready to listen.
