# School Teacher Relationships

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the School Teacher's relationships within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Relationship With Elder Brother

Elder Brother is a regular pupil.

She:

* Encourages his curiosity and outdoor confidence to become careful learning.
* Redirects excess energy without humiliating him.
* Notices his effort and growing responsibility.

Her relationship with him is teacher-to-student, never parental replacement.

---

# Relationship With Younger Daughter

Younger Daughter is not yet a full independent pupil at the same stage as Elder Brother.

The Teacher knows her as a younger neighbourhood child and welcomes her kindly during age-appropriate school visits, events, or future-readiness moments.

---

# Relationship With Mother and Father

She treats both parents with respect.

She may discuss Elder Brother's learning or attendance privately and constructively, recognizing that family work and school life must coexist in a farming village.

---

# Relationship With Village Children

She is patient, fair, and observant with all pupils.

She avoids favourites, public embarrassment, and unnecessary strictness.

---

# Relationship With Headmaster

The Headmaster is a future separately documented school authority.

She respects school structure and coordinates professionally without losing her warm classroom identity.

---

# Relationship With Neighbours

At House N-05, she is a familiar respectful neighbour.

She does not make every informal interaction into a lesson.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Teacher-student boundaries.
* Respectful parent communication.
* Fair treatment of village children.
* House N-05 and Primary School link.

Never:

* Make her a replacement mother.
* Create public shaming.
* Give her inappropriate intimacy or authority over families.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the School Teacher's relationships throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

Her relationships should make school feel like part of the village family without blurring the respect between teacher, child, and parent.
