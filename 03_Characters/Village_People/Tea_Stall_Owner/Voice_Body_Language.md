# Tea Stall Owner Voice & Body Language

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's voice, speech, body language, and signature sounds throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Voice

Maintain:

- Warm medium male voice.
- Clear village Tamil cadence.
- Friendly conversational volume.
- Slightly brighter in morning greetings.
- Softer when speaking to elders.

He calls orders and greetings practically, never shouting harshly.

---

# Speaking Style

- Short hospitable phrases.
- Easy questions about the day.
- Practical replies.
- Gentle humour.
- Comfortable silence while pouring.

---

# Body Language

Maintain:

- Counter-centered stance.
- Efficient hand movement between kettle, milk, and glasses.
- Quick eye-checks toward the path.
- Relaxed shoulders during lulls.
- Forward lean when listening to a customer story.

---

# Signature Sounds

Audio identity includes:

- Kettle simmer.
- Tea pouring into glass.
- Tumblers clinking.
- Spoon against glass.
- Soft wiping cloth.
- Friendly greeting voice.
- Occasional laughter with regulars.
- Stall wood/bench creak nearby.

These sounds should identify him and the stall together.

---

# Google Flow Performance Rules

Natural work speed, believable pouring motion, consistent voice warmth, no slapstick default.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain warm voice and efficient stall body language.

Never make him silently blank or aggressively loud as default.

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's voice and body language throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should be recognizable in steam and sound—the pour, the clink, the greeting—before the camera even settles on his face.
