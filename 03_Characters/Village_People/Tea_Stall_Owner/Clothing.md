# Tea Stall Owner Clothing

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's wardrobe throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Philosophy

He dresses for heat, work, and humility.

Clothes support stall labour, not fashion.

---

# Everyday Clothing

Canonical everyday clothing:

- Simple cotton shirt, often half-sleeve.
- Traditional cotton veshti / dhoti, or practical work veshti.
- Optional shoulder cloth / thundu for sweat and glass wiping support.
- Barefoot or simple sandals behind/near the stall.

This is his default look in most scenes.

---

# Colour Palette

Preferred:

- White
- Off white
- Light cream
- Faded light blue
- Soft beige
- Simple checks

Avoid neon colours and logos.

---

# Work Adjustments

While working he may:

- Fold veshti practically.
- Keep shirt sleeves clear of flame/steam.
- Tuck a wiping cloth at waist or over shoulder.
- Appear slightly tea-splashed or damp at the front cloth edge.

These are temporary work signs.

---

# Festival Days

- Cleaner shirt and veshti.
- Neater grooming.
- Still modest.
- Stall may be busier; clothing remains practical.

---

# Footwear

- Simple sandals common.
- Barefoot possible on familiar stall earth when practical.

No modern sports shoes as signature.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain simple early-1990s rural work clothing.

Never:

- Cafe apron branding as identity.
- Jeans / modern pants.
- Designer shirts.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's wardrobe throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

His clothes should look ready for steam, sugar, and sunrise customers.
