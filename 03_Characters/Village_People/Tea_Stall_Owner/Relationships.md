# Tea Stall Owner Relationships

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's relationships within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Relationship With Father

Father is a respected familiar customer.

When Father visits, the Tea Stall Owner:

- Greets him warmly.
- Serves without fuss.
- Shares brief practical talk about weather, fields, or village news.
- Never becomes overly familiar or disrespectful.

Their bond is friendly, steady, and roadside-true.

---

# Relationship With Other Farmers and Elders

He treats them as core regulars.

He listens, serves, and keeps atmosphere respectful.

Elders receive natural deference.

---

# Relationship With Travellers

He offers:

- Quick hospitality.
- Clear practical service.
- No exploitation.

Travellers leave warmed and continue on.

---

# Relationship With Children

He is kindly but careful.

He may offer a biscuit smile when an adult is present.

He does not encourage children to crowd the stove area.

Elder Brother may pass with curiosity; Younger Daughter rarely becomes a stall regular.

---

# Relationship With Mother and Family

He knows the family as part of village life.

Direct deep household friendship is unnecessary.

Recognition and respectful greeting are enough.

---

# Relationship With the Stall Itself

The stall is his workplace and public identity.

He cares for it as a trusted village place.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain hospitable customer relationships and special familiarity with regulars like Father.

Never create hostile shopkeeper drama as default.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's relationships throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

His relationships are poured one glass at a time—remembered faces, repeated mornings, quiet trust.
