# Tea Stall Owner Identity

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the canonical identity of the Tea Stall Owner within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Core Identity

The Tea Stall Owner is a recurring village man of rural Tamil Nadu in the early 1990s.

He runs the roadside Tea Stall as both livelihood and social duty.

His identity is built upon hospitality, routine, practical humour, and steady work.

He knows many faces.

He remembers how regulars take their tea.

---

# Narrative Role

He represents:

* Public warmth.
* Village conversation culture.
* Small-business dignity.
* Continuity of morning and evening roadside life.

He supports stories without becoming the emotional centre of the family.

---

# Life Role

He naturally:

* Opens and closes the Tea Stall.
* Prepares tea and filter coffee.
* Serves biscuits and simple snacks.
* Talks with farmers, elders, and travellers.
* Keeps the stall clean and ready.
* Watches the path while working.

---

# Personal Values

* Hard work.
* Fairness in small trade.
* Respect for regulars.
* Clean service.
* Simple living.
* Community belonging.

---

# Strengths

* Excellent tea-making consistency.
* Memory for customers.
* Calm multitasking.
* Friendly conversation.
* Reliable daily presence.

---

# Limitations

He may:

* Become tired by late evening.
* Worry about supply shortages.
* Speak more than he works during very slow hours.
* Feel the heat of the stall stove strongly in summer.

These remain human and gentle.

---

# Motivations

* Keep the stall respected.
* Serve good hot tea.
* Earn honestly.
* Remain useful to the village rhythm.
* Enjoy familiar morning company.

---

# Story Function

He often appears in scenes involving:

* Father's morning pause.
* Farmer talk before fields.
* Bus-approach waiting.
* Soft village humour.
* Rain shelter under the stall roof.
* Festival-day special busyness.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Permanent link to the Tea Stall.
* Hospitable working identity.
* Early-1990s village trader authenticity.

Never:

* Turn him into comic cruelty.
* Modernize him into a cafe barista identity.
* Detach him from his stall as if he were a random extra.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's identity within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

He is not merely a man who sells tea.

He is the reason the stall feels like a known place rather than a temporary setup.
