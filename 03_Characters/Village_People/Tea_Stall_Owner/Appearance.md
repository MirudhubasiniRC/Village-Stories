# Tea Stall Owner Appearance

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the canonical physical appearance of the Tea Stall Owner throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Identity

He is a healthy middle-aged South Indian village man shaped by years of roadside work, heat, steam, and early mornings.

He should look familiar, practical, and kind—never glamorous.

---

# Estimated Age

Approximately:

**42–48 years old**

Clearly older than Father, clearly younger than the eldest village elders.

---

# Height

Approximately:

**165–172 cm**

Average village male height.

---

# Build

Maintain:

- Medium build.
- Slight belly possible from settled adult life, never exaggerated.
- Strong practical arms from daily stall work.
- Neither thin frailty nor cinematic muscular styling.

---

# Skin Tone

Maintain:

- Warm medium-brown to slightly deeper sun-worked brown.
- Natural outdoor weathering on face and forearms.
- Healthy real skin texture.

---

# Face

Maintain:

- Rounded-oval to softly rectangular face.
- Friendly open features.
- Light smile lines from years of talking with customers.
- Practical, approachable look.

Optional canonical detail:

- Thin traditional moustache, neat and modest.

If the moustache is used, keep it forever.

---

# Eyes

Maintain:

- Dark brown.
- Alert.
- Warm.
- Quick to notice arriving customers.

---

# Hair

Maintain:

- Black hair with early natural greying at temples acceptable.
- Simple short rural cut.
- Neat in morning; slightly damp/textured by midday heat and steam.
- No modern hairstyle.

---

# Hands

Hands should appear:

- Experienced.
- Heat-used.
- Capable with kettle and glasses.
- Occasionally tea-stained fingertips or damp from washing glasses.

---

# Recognizable Features

Instantly recognizable through:

- Stall-side posture behind the counter.
- Warm medium-brown working complexion.
- Friendly alert eyes.
- Simple rural haircut.
- Subtle moustache if established.
- Kettle-and-glass handling silhouette.

---

# Environmental Appearance

While working:

- Light sweat in heat.
- Steam near face during pouring.
- Occasional tea splash marks on cloth.
- Damp cloth over shoulder possible.

These reset with ordinary cleaning and do not redesign him.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain the same face, age range, build, and stall-worker look.

Never:

- Youthify him into Father's peer identically.
- Give movie-hero styling.
- Change him into a different vendor between episodes.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's physical appearance throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should look like the man who has always been there when the kettle sings.
