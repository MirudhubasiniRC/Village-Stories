# Tea Stall Owner Behaviour

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's observable behaviour throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# General Behaviour

He naturally:

- Stands or sits ready at the counter.
- Greets familiar customers by recognition.
- Works kettle, milk, and glasses in practiced order.
- Wipes the counter.
- Watches the path between servings.
- Listens as much as he talks.

---

# Serving Behaviour

While making tea he:

- Moves efficiently.
- Pours with confidence.
- Hands glasses carefully.
- Adjusts sugar/strength from memory for regulars when appropriate.
- Accepts payment simply and honestly.

Tea service should look effortless from experience.

---

# Social Behaviour

He:

- Opens conversation about weather, crops, buses, and village news.
- Gives elders respectful attention.
- Treats Father as a familiar respected customer when Father visits.
- Remains kind to children without encouraging unsafe lingering near the stove.
- Welcomes travellers briefly and practically.

---

# Signature Habits

- Tapping the kettle rim lightly before pouring.
- Wiping a glass with a practiced twist.
- Glancing down the path for the next customer.
- Nodding while listening.
- Resting both hands on the counter during lulls.
- Calling out a soft greeting as regulars approach.

---

# Behaviour Under Rush

During busy spells he:

- Speaks shorter.
- Moves faster but not frantically.
- Keeps quality steady.
- Returns to conversation when the rush eases.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always keep him stall-centered, hospitable, and practically efficient.

Never show careless hygiene or harsh customer treatment.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's behaviour throughout the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should be recognized by the rhythm of his stall work alone—greet, brew, pour, listen, wipe, repeat.
