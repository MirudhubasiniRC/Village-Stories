# Tea Stall Owner Daily Routine

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Tea Stall Owner's typical daily routine throughout the Village Stories universe.


---

# Early Morning

He usually:

- Reaches the stall early.
- Sweeps the earth/stall area.
- Checks water, milk, tea leaves, sugar.
- Lights the stove.
- Arranges glasses.
- Prepares for farmers and early walkers.

---

# Morning Peak

- Continuous tea and coffee service.
- Conversation with regulars.
- Possible snack service.
- Watching Entrance / Bus Stop approach movement.

This is his most important social period.

---

# Midday

- Slower customers.
- Washing and resetting glasses.
- Restocking.
- Brief personal rest in shade.
- Light meals when possible.

---

# Evening

- Returning workers stop for tea.
- Stronger talk energy.
- Final sales.
- Gradual wind-down as light fades.

---

# Closing

- Washes remaining glasses.
- Secures tins and vessels.
- Covers or shuts the stall.
- Leaves the path quiet again.

---

# Seasonal Variations

Summer: earlier heavy shade reliance, more midday quiet.

Monsoon: roof shelter value rises, muddy-path customers, steam more visible.

Festival periods: busier spells, possible special snack demand, longer evenings.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain early opening, morning peak, evening wind-down, and clean closing.

Never keep the stall randomly abandoned during normal daytime without story reason.
