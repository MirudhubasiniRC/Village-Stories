# Blacksmith Daily Routine

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's daily working rhythm.

---

# Daily Timeline

```text
Morning forge preparation

↓

Tool repair and shaping

↓

Midday heat pause

↓

Afternoon customer work

↓

Forge cooling and tool storage
```

---

# Morning

He:

* Checks hearth, bellows, water, anvil, and tools.
* Starts forge work in cooler hours.
* Receives farmers' tools for repair.

---

# Midday and Afternoon

Heavy heat slows work. He completes safer lighter tasks, resumes major forging when conditions allow, and handles seasonal demand.

---

# Closing

He:

* Lets the forge cool safely.
* Stores hammers and tools.
* Clears metal fragments.
* Leaves the open forge orderly.

---

# Seasonal Variations

Pre-planting and harvest periods bring tool demand. Monsoon requires dry storage. Festival periods may add small lamp or metal-fitting repair work.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain morning setup, heat-aware pacing, and safe closing.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's daily routine.

---

# Guiding Principle

His day should follow the practical cycle of fire: built carefully, used with focus, and left safe at dusk.
