# Blacksmith Relationships

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's relationships.

---

# Relationship With Father

Father is a familiar working customer.

The Blacksmith repairs or sharpens Father’s agricultural tools with practical respect. Their conversation centres on tool condition, crop work, rain, and timing.

---

# Relationship With Elder Brother and Younger Daughter

Both children are kept at a safe distance from the active forge.

He may explain a cooled tool or basic craft principle to Elder Brother under supervision. Younger Daughter may observe only from a safe place with an adult.

---

# Relationship With Other Farmers

He supports the village through fair, dependable repairs. He does not favour one household unfairly.

---

# Relationship With Other Artisans

He respects the Carpenter, Potter, and Barber as fellow village workers. Their crafts remain distinct.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain respectful work relationships and firm safety boundaries.

Never use the forge as an unsafe child-adventure setting.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's relationships.

---

# Guiding Principle

His relationships are practical trust: farmers bring what is worn, and he returns it ready for work.
