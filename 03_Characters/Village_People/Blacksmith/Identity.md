# Blacksmith Identity

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's canonical identity.

---

# Core Identity

The Blacksmith is a South Indian village man, approximately **48–60 years old**, whose open forge keeps farmers' and households' metal tools useful.

He is a skilled artisan, not a dramatic warrior or industrial worker.

---

# Responsibilities

* Sharpening sickles and blades.
* Repairing metal farming tools.
* Making small practical iron items.
* Repairing cart fittings when appropriate.
* Maintaining hearth, bellows, anvil, and tools.

---

# Values

* Safety.
* Patience.
* Durable work.
* Fair dealing.
* Respect for tools and labour.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Age 48–60.
* Traditional open-forge artisan identity.
* West-side Blacksmith Forge location lock.
* Practical village service.

Never:

* Make him a cinematic weaponsmith.
* Give him industrial machinery or supernatural crafting.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's identity.

---

# Guiding Principle

He is the person who gives worn tools their next season of life.
