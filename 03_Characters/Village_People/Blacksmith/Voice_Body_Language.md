# Blacksmith Voice & Body Language

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's voice, body language, and signature sounds.

---

# Voice

Maintain:

* Low practical male Tamil voice.
* Few clear words over forge noise.
* Calm safety instructions.
* Respectful customer conversation.

---

# Body Language

Maintain:

* Grounded stance at anvil.
* Balanced controlled hammer motion.
* Clear hand signal to keep others safely back.
* Slow inspection posture after cooling metal.

---

# Signature Sounds

* Bellows breath.
* Hammer striking anvil.
* Fire crackle.
* Water hiss.
* Metal tool rack movement.
* Low practical voice.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain controlled forge movement and restrained voice.

Never use reckless hammer flourishes or action-film performance.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's voice and body language.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should be recognized through sound before sight: bellows, iron, anvil, then a quiet voice.
