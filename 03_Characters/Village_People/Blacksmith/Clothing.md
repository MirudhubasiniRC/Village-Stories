# Blacksmith Clothing

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's wardrobe.

---

# Everyday Forge Clothing

Maintain:

* Simple durable cotton shirt, often half-sleeved or sleeves rolled for heat.
* Practical cotton veshti / dhoti securely wrapped for work.
* Cotton thundu for sweat.
* Bare feet or simple sturdy sandals according to safe work position.

Colours remain practical: cream, faded brown, grey, off-white, or dull blue.

---

# Work Adjustments

* Shirt may show temporary soot or sweat.
* Veshti remains safely secured away from hearth.
* No modern protective industrial uniform as default.

---

# Festival Clothing

* Fresh simple shirt and clean veshti.
* Same humble traditional identity.

---

# Continuity Rules

Never use modern workshop uniforms, jeans, branded clothing, or cinematic armour-like styling.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's clothing.

---

# Guiding Principle

His clothing should look made for heat, dust, and the honest weight of tools.
