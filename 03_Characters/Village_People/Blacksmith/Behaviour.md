# Blacksmith Behaviour

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's observable behaviour.

---

# General Behaviour

He naturally:

* Inspects a tool before heating it.
* Works in deliberate stages: heat, shape, strike, cool, test.
* Keeps customers away from the hottest work area.
* Returns tools to a rack after use.
* Cleans the forge space at the end of work.

---

# Customer Behaviour

He:

* Explains a repair plainly.
* Gives Father and other farmers honest estimates of what a worn tool can still do.
* Avoids rushing unsafe work.
* Treats children with care around forge heat and sharp tools.

---

# Signature Habits

* Testing metal edge carefully after cooling.
* Pausing to judge colour of heated iron.
* Checking bellows before a job.
* Setting hammer down in the same known place.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain safe, deliberate hand-forge behaviour.

Never create reckless hammering, unsafe child access, or industrial assembly-line work.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Blacksmith's behaviour.

---

# Guiding Principle

His movements should make the forge feel controlled even when fire and hammer are loud.
