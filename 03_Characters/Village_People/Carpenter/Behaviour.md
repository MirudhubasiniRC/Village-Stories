# Carpenter Behaviour

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the Carpenter's observable behaviour.

---

# General Behaviour

He naturally:

* Inspects wood grain and damage before cutting.
* Measures twice before shaping.
* Uses hand saw, plane, chisel, and mallet carefully.
* Keeps sharp tools beyond child reach.
* Sorts useful offcuts.
* Clears shavings at the end of work.

---

# Customer Behaviour

He explains a repair simply and does not promise impossible work. He treats Father and other customers fairly.

---

# Signature Habits

* Running thumb along a timber edge.
* Checking a joint before final fitting.
* Sweeping shavings into a neat pile.
* Returning tools to a known rack order.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain patient hand-tool work and safety boundaries.

Never create careless cutting, unsafe child access, or electric factory work.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Carpenter's behaviour.

---

# Guiding Principle

His work should make wood feel patient: inspected, measured, shaped, and fitted without hurry.
