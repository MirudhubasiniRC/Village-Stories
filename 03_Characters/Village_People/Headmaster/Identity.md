# Headmaster Identity

## Purpose

This document permanently defines the canonical identity of the Headmaster within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Core Identity

The Headmaster is a recurring South Indian village man, approximately **52–60 years old**, who leads the Primary School with fairness, dignity, and practical discipline.

He is not a distant bureaucrat. He knows the children, understands village families, and sees education as a shared responsibility.

---

# Responsibilities

He naturally:

* Oversees school routine.
* Leads or supervises assembly.
* Maintains school standards and records.
* Organizes national-day and annual-day activities.
* Supports teachers.
* Meets parents when appropriate.
* Handles important school decisions calmly.

---

# Values

* Education.
* Fairness.
* Discipline with dignity.
* Effort.
* Respect.
* Community responsibility.

---

# Strengths

* Measured decision-making.
* School administration.
* Clear public speaking.
* Recognizing student effort.
* Calm authority.

---

# Limitations

He may:

* Carry institutional concerns privately.
* Become serious when attendance or resources suffer.
* Expect punctuality more strongly than some children enjoy.

He never becomes cruel or inflexible for spectacle.

---

# Continuity Rules

Always maintain:

* Male Headmaster identity.
* Age 52–60 range.
* Strict but fair authority.
* Primary School location lock.

Never:

* Make him a comic tyrant.
* Humiliate children.
* Give him modern corporate-school administration language.

---

# Canon Authority

This document permanently defines the Headmaster's identity within the Village Stories universe.

---

# Guiding Principle

He should make children stand straighter without making them afraid to learn.
