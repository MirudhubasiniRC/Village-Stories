# Story Engine

## Purpose

This folder permanently defines how Village Stories creates emotionally consistent episodes from its established world, people, locations, objects, seasons, and culture.

`02_World/`, `03_Characters/`, and `04_Objects/` answer:

> What exists in the Village Stories universe?

`06_Story_Engine/` answers:

> How can a writer, director, or AI turn those established elements into a truthful, engaging episode?

This is not an idea bank. It is a production decision system for creating hundreds of episodes without losing the series' calm identity, visual continuity, or emotional honesty.

---

# Story Engine Philosophy

Village Stories does not depend on spectacle, villains, danger, or escalating drama.

The series finds story in:

* A meal made with care.
* A field task completed patiently.
* A child learning from an ordinary mistake.
* Rain changing the family's plans.
* A neighbour arriving with a small need.
* A family memory returning through an object, scent, or song.
* A quiet repair after a disagreement.

The engine protects that promise at every stage of development.

---

# Operating Formula

Generate an episode from established canon:

```text
Season
+ Location
+ Character need or skill
+ Gentle conflict
+ Practical action
+ Honest emotion
= Village Stories episode
```

For example:

```text
Monsoon
+ Family Kitchen
+ Mother needs to complete a meal
+ Damp firewood slows the stove
+ Family adapts the cooking process together
+ Patience and warmth
= A rain-day cooking episode
```

The formula generates a beginning. The documents in this folder determine whether that beginning becomes a proper Village Stories episode.

---

# Canon Priority

The Story Engine cannot override specific canon.

When a story idea conflicts with a permanent document:

1. Individual character documentation governs character identity and behaviour.
2. Location documentation governs geography, layout, atmosphere, and use.
3. Object documentation governs materials, handling, and period accuracy.
4. World and Culture documentation govern season, village life, and social context.
5. This Story Engine governs story construction within those limits.

A story must adapt to canon. Canon must never be altered merely to make a convenient plot work.

---

# Core Production Principles

Every episode should:

* Begin with a truthful sensory or emotional point of interest.
* Follow a practical activity or real relationship need.
* Use conflict that is gentle, proportionate, and solvable.
* Reveal personality through choices and actions.
* Respect early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu life.
* Give location, weather, objects, and animals a meaningful role.
* Use visual detail, ambient sound, and silence with intention.
* End with emotional rest rather than artificial escalation.

# Using This Folder

Use documents in the following order:

1. Read `01_Story_Philosophy.md` to confirm the episode belongs to the series.
2. Read `02_Story_Rules.md` before developing the premise.
3. Use `03_Story_Structure.md` and `04_Episode_Formula.md` to shape the episode.
4. Use the relevant system documents for conflict, emotion, pace, characters, location, season, learning, and humour.
5. Use visual and ASMR documents while planning scenes and Google Flow generations.
6. Use the checklists and forbidden-patterns documents before approval.
7. Return to `19_Magic_Of_Everyday_Life.md` whenever a premise feels too small.

---

# Relationship With Episode System

`06_Story_Engine/` defines how stories should work.

`07_Episode_System/` defines how a Short or long-form episode is physically organised for viewers: hook, timing, opening, ending, retention, sound placement, and review.

The Story Engine comes first. Episode System never sacrifices an honest story merely to gain a click or force a next part.

---

# Google Flow Considerations

When producing with Google Flow:

* Build a scene only from canonically established people, objects, weather, and locations.
* Preserve clothing state, object position, time of day, and emotional progression across shots.
* Use the specific visual and ASMR manuals to prevent random cinematic choices.
* Prompt actions rather than abstract emotions whenever possible.
* Treat continuity as part of storytelling, not as a final technical check.

---

# Common Misuse

Do not use this folder to:

* Add a new location, object, profession, or character without canon documentation.
* Justify modern technology or a historical inaccuracy.
* Turn a gentle conflict into a crisis for attention.
* Make children, animals, or elders act unnaturally for comedy.
* Repeat a familiar formula without a fresh emotional reason.

# Canon Authority

This folder is the permanent story-construction authority for Village Stories.

It does not replace the specific authority of World, Character, Location, Object, Culture, or Episode System documents.

---

# Guiding Principle

The best Village Stories episode does not make village life larger than life.

It helps the audience notice that ordinary life was already meaningful.
