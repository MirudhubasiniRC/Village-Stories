# Seasonal Engine

## Purpose

This manual turns the annual rhythm of early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu into usable,
consistent story pressure.

Season changes what the family needs, notices, protects, prepares, wears, hears,
and hopes for.

It is never a decorative colour grade placed over an otherwise interchangeable
episode.

Use the documented World and Culture canon as the authority for local practices,
festival details, crops, weather effects, food, clothing, and location state.

---

## Canon Authority

Village Stories takes place in early-1990s rural Tamil Nadu.

The permanent family is Mother, Father, Elder Brother, Younger Daughter, and the
Family Dog.

Seasonal stories may use only established locations and documented cultural
events.

Do not import a festival, crop practice, weather extreme, modern appliance, or
location simply because it is familiar from another region or time.

Do not create a Rice Mill or any undocumented worksite as a seasonal shortcut.

If a Culture, World, or location document specifies a condition, it overrides
this general engine.

---

## Guiding Principle

Let the season create a real practical need before it creates a visual.

---

## Seasonal Philosophy

The season should be felt before it is named.

Dry feet seeking shade, a vessel watched carefully, damp clothes delayed from
drying, green shoots checked at dawn, or grain handled with care can tell the
viewer more than an explanatory line.

Seasonal variation refreshes recurring spaces without changing their identity.

The Kitchen remains the Kitchen, but its fire, food, storage, and pace may
change.

The field path remains the field path, but dust, mud, puddles, grass, or light
may change how a family member moves through it.

The emotional scale remains gentle.

Weather may delay, complicate, or invite cooperation; it may not become a
disaster spectacle.

---

## Core Principles

1. Season changes visible behaviour.
2. Season changes the practical value of locations.
3. Season changes sound, light, texture, and tempo.
4. Weather should create manageable pressure, not melodrama.
5. Agricultural work must follow documented seasonal logic.
6. Food, clothing, and household routines must support the conditions.
7. Animals need the same practical care as people.
8. A seasonal event needs a real community or family purpose.
9. A transition between seasons requires evidence, not a title card alone.
10. Every seasonal choice must remain consistent across the episode.

---

## The Seasonal Decision Framework

Start each episode with this sequence:

1. Select the seasonal window from established canon.
2. Identify one practical consequence for the family.
3. Identify one changed condition in the primary location.
4. Identify one sensory signal for image and sound.
5. Identify one task that cannot be performed exactly as usual.
6. Choose a gentle emotional response.
7. Confirm the ending respects the condition rather than erasing it.

For example, light rain can make a school departure slower, but it should not
turn into a dangerous rescue.

### Seasonal Story Formula

```text
Seasonal condition + ordinary need + family adjustment + small shared action
= grounded seasonal episode
```

The adjustment is the story engine.

The weather itself is not the protagonist.

## Summer

### Seasonal Character

Summer brings heat, brightness, dry surfaces, thirst, tired animals, shade, and
the measured waiting for rain.

It rewards early starts, slower midday movement, water awareness, and practical
restraint.

### Useful Story Pressures

* A task must be completed before the stronger heat.
* Water must be used thoughtfully.
* A meal or drink needs cooling, covering, or timing.
* The Family Dog seeks a sensible shaded place.
* A child learns why a plant or animal needs regular care.
* A journey is shortened, delayed, or moved to a cooler hour.

### Location Applications

The Kitchen can show cooler preparations, careful water use, and a slower fire
rhythm.

The Courtyard can show shade patterns, drying work, and quiet family movement.

The Vegetable Garden can show watering, observation, and patient care.

The Livestock Shelter can show attention to animals' comfort.

The Front Thinnai can hold an afternoon pause, not an idle crowd scene.

Field work and paths should favour early or late-day logic where canon supports
it.

### Positive Example

Father plans an early field-related task. Elder Brother wakes in time to help,
but Younger Daughter worries that the Family Dog is restless in the heat.
Mother calmly creates shade and water care at home. The episode ends with the
family regrouping before the day becomes too hot.

### Avoid

Do not make summer an excuse for exhausting children, careless animal treatment,
or a crisis caused by an implausible lack of preparation.

## Monsoon

### Seasonal Character

Monsoon brings rain, wet cloth, slippery ground, changed routes, protected
fires, shelter, renewed soil, and attentive listening.

Rain should have weight, sound, and effect.

### Useful Story Pressures

* Laundry cannot dry as planned.
* A path is muddy and requires a slower walk.
* A cooking fire needs careful protection.
* A school item or household object must stay dry.
* Rain changes when animals are fed or checked.
* A family plan moves from outdoors to a safe covered space.
* A small leak, puddle, or drainage concern invites practical cooperation.

### Location Applications

The Kitchen supports protected cooking, steam, dampness at the threshold, and
quiet preparation.

The Courtyard shows rain direction, sheltered waiting, and changed play.

The Front Thinnai provides a threshold from which rain can be observed safely.

The Livestock Shelter requires practical, calm animal care.

Field paths, Stone Bridge, and the River Ghat must reflect wet conditions and
never be used for reckless suspense.

### Positive Example

Younger Daughter has prepared something for school, but rain begins before the
family leaves. Mother helps protect it. Elder Brother adjusts the carrying
method, and Father judges the departure time. The family solves one small
problem together without pretending the rain has stopped.

### Avoid

Do not use flooded danger, frightening thunder, stranded children, or storm
damage as entertainment.

Do not leave clothing, fire, paths, or animal behaviour unchanged in rain.

## Post-Monsoon

### Seasonal Character

Post-monsoon brings refreshed growth, cleaner light, damp earth settling, path
repair, outdoor renewal, and gratitude expressed through work rather than
speeches.

### Useful Story Pressures

* A familiar place needs a small repair or tidying.
* New growth invites a careful observation.
* A route is usable again but still needs attention.
* A household item is aired or checked after damp weather.
* The family notices the difference between waiting and renewal.

### Location Applications

The Backyard and Vegetable Garden can reveal growth through specific, documented
plants and work.

The Courtyard can show cleaning, airing, and resumed routines.

Field paths can show a slower return to ordinary use.

The paddy field may show renewed work only as supported by agricultural canon.

### Positive Example

Elder Brother wants to rush along a newly usable path. Father asks him to slow
down and notice where the ground still needs care. Later, Younger Daughter finds
a healthy new leaf in the garden. The lesson remains in the shared observation.

### Avoid

Do not show every surface suddenly clean, dry, and unchanged immediately after
rain.

## Harvest

### Seasonal Character

Harvest stories centre on cooperation, timing, practical labour, stored food,
shared meals, and quiet appreciation for work completed together.

They should respect labour rather than turning the season into a colourful
montage.

### Useful Story Pressures

* A task needs several family contributions.
* Food or materials need careful sorting, carrying, or storage.
* An expected helper has another necessary responsibility.
* A child wants to join grown-up work and is given a safe, meaningful role.
* A family meal acknowledges effort without a formal moral.

### Location Applications

Use the paddy fields and related routes only with plausible work, time, and
family roles.

Use the Kitchen for food, storage, and return-home care.

Use the Courtyard for sorting or shared domestic follow-through only when the
location canon supports the materials and staging.

Do not invent processing facilities or undocumented agricultural sites.

### Positive Example

Mother prepares a simple meal for returning workers. Elder Brother wants to do
the hardest task, but Father assigns him a safer useful one. Younger Daughter
helps keep a small household responsibility moving. The ending values each
contribution.

### Avoid

Do not turn harvest into an unending crowd scene, a wealth display, or an
unexplained commercial transaction.

---

## Festival and Community Seasons

Pongal, Deepavali, temple events, and local celebrations may appear only when
supported by existing Culture canon and the documented Festival Ground or other
established relevant settings.

### Event Rules

* The event must affect a real family preparation or relationship.
* Use established ritual and location details accurately.
* Show preparation, participation, or aftermath rather than a generic spectacle.
* Keep devotion respectful and non-comic.
* Do not invent elaborate competitions, crowds, vendors, or entertainments.
* A festival does not excuse broken household, seasonal, or travel logic.

### Positive Example

The family prepares one modest documented festival task. Younger Daughter is
impatient, Elder Brother takes a practical role, and the parents guide the
timing. A quiet shared moment at the end carries the celebration.

## Character Applications

### Mother

Mother often translates season into household rhythm: food, cloth, fire, care,
planning, and calm adjustment.

Avoid making her the sole person responsible for every seasonal task.

### Father

Father often connects seasonal work, routes, repairs, and measured judgment.

His response should be steady rather than heroic.

### Elder Brother

Elder Brother can learn timing, preparedness, effort, and how to offer useful
help without being asked to carry adult burdens.

### Younger Daughter

Younger Daughter can notice a small seasonal change first: a dry leaf, wet
shoe, new sprout, cooler breeze, or changed animal behaviour.

Her curiosity opens action; it does not require a lecture.

### Family Dog

The Family Dog can seek shade, shelter, a dry resting spot, or a familiar family
member. Use these behaviours gently and safely.

## Seasonal Continuity

Declare the seasonal window in the episode brief.

Then carry it through every scene.

Check:

* ground condition;
* sky and light;
* clothing practicality;
* food and fire behaviour;
* water use;
* animal care;
* plant and field state;
* route difficulty;
* soundscape;
* time-of-day choices.

Do not begin in heavy rain and end in dry dust unless a justified time passage is
shown.

Do not let a character arrive dry after a wet journey without evidence of
shelter or protection.

## Google Flow

Season must be specified in every image or motion prompt where it changes the
frame.

Include:

* seasonal window and time of day;
* exact established location;
* weather condition and intensity;
* ground, roof, cloth, plant, and animal effects;
* character action adapted to the condition;
* stable canonical spatial features;
* restrained emotional tone.

Example prompt logic:

`Early monsoon morning, established Front Thinnai of the family house, soft
steady rain beyond the threshold, Mother folds a dry cloth around Younger
Daughter's school paper, Elder Brother holds the bag open, Family Dog resting
under cover, wet courtyard visible as documented, calm preparation.`

Do not use prompts that add storms, floodwater, lightning drama, new crops,
modern rain gear, or unrecorded structures.

---

## Common Mistakes

Treating season as a background filter.

Putting the same clothes, work, and animal behaviour in every condition.

Using rain only for sadness or danger.

Using summer only for visual glare.

Skipping the practical work that makes harvest meaningful.

Adding unsupported festival customs.

Forgetting that a season persists across scenes and episodes.

Turning weather into a villain that requires rescue.

Season affects every visible detail and every practical decision.

If an episode would play exactly the same in another season, the seasonal engine
has not yet been used.
